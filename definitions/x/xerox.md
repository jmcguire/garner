# xerox

xerox is a registered trademark that nevertheless came to be used in the 1960s as a common noun <he made a xerox of the document>, an adjective <a xerox copy>, and a verb <to xerox an article>. Sometimes the word is capitalized, but nearly as often not—e.g.: "Several readers *xeroxed* my Sept. 14 column." Alex Beam, "Looking Backward," *Boston Globe*, 21 Dec. 1992, at 19. Careful writers and speakers tend to use *photocopy* or some other similar word. *\*Zerox* is a common misspelling.

Current ratio in print \(*Xeroxed* vs. *xeroxed*\): 1.5:1
