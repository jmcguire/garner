# tonnage

tonnage \(= \[1\] the total number of tons that something weighs; \[2\] the size of a ship, or its cargo capacity as measured in tons; \[3\] a duty or tax on ships based on cargo; or \[4\] the total amount of shipping in which a country engages, as measured in tons\), a French/Latin loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has always been predominantly so spelled. **Tunnage* is a variant.

In BrE, *ton* is officially spelled *tonne* \(originally a French spelling\) whenever possible to denote a metric ton of 1,000 kilograms. In the U.S., a *ton* is 2,000 pounds; in Great Britain, a *ton* is legally 2,240 pounds. A *tonne* \(or metric ton\) is 2,204.6 pounds.

 - Current ratio in print \(*tonnage* vs. \**tunnage*\): 245:1
