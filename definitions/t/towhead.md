# towhead

towhead /**toh**-hed/ (= \[1\] a head of pale-yellow, flaxen hair; \[2\] a person having such a head of hair; or \[3\] a sandbar or shoal in a river), an Americanism dating from the early 1800s, is so spelled. The adjective is *towheaded*. The metaphor derives from *tow* in the sense of a fiber of flax, hemp, etc. used for spinning yarn, twine, or threads. Since the 1960s, writers have sometimes misspelled the word \**toe-head* or \**toe-headed*.

 - Current ratio in print (*towheaded* vs. \**toe-headed*): 64:1
