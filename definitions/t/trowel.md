# trowel

trowel. This word is most often a noun denoting either a handheld garden tool resembling a small spade or a flat-bladed tool for spreading mortar or plaster. The word can also function as a verb, however, meaning "to use such a tool"—and that's where the point of usage arises. In AmE, the inflected forms are *troweled* and *troweling* (both standard forms since the late 1800s); in BrE, the inflected forms have always been *trowelled* and *trowelling*. See spelling (c).
