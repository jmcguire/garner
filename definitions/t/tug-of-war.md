# tug-of-war

tug-of-war (= \[1\] a game that tests collective strength by having two teams pull against each other at opposite ends of a rope; or \[2\] a situation in which two people or factions try hard to get or keep the same finite thing) has been predominantly so hyphenated in BrE since about 1907 and in AmE since about 1967. The unhyphenated form, *tug of war*, has been losing ground. In any event, the hyphens seem mimetically appropriate.

 - Current ratio in print (*tug-of-war* vs. *tug of war* in World Englishes): 1.5:1
