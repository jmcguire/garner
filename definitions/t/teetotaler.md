# teetotaler

teetotaler; **teetotaller**. The first is AmE; the second is BrE. This geographic distinction was fixed by the early 20th century. But in all varieties of English, *teetotalism* is the standard spelling. Cf. spelling \(c\).

*T-total* and *T-totally* began in the early 19th century as emphatic forms, singling out the first letter in a reduplicative way. By the 1830s, when *teetotaling* came to refer to absolute abstinence from alcohol, the cognate terms were uniformly spelled *tee*-. But perhaps through folk etymology \(see etymology \(d\)\), some people came to think that the temperance movement had something to do with *tea* as opposed to spirits. Thus were born the misspellings \**teatotaler* and \**teatotaller*.

Current ratio in print \(*teetotaler* vs. \**teatotaler* in AmE\): 544:1 2017 ratio in print \(*teetotaller* vs. \**teatotaller* in BrE\): 2,818:1
