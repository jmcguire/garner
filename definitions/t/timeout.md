# timeout

timeout (= \[1\] a short break during a sporting competition; \[2\] an imposed period of still and quiet used esp. as a way of disciplining children; or \[3\] the purposeful cessation of a usu. romantic relationship, esp. for a stated period), a compound dating from the late 1800s, has been predominantly so spelled, as a solid word, since the late 1980s. *Time-out* and *time out* are variants.

The plural is *timeouts*, not *\*timesout*. See plurals (g).

 - Current ratio in print (*timeouts* vs. *time-outs* vs. *time outs*): 6:4:1
