# tempestuous

tempestuous; **tempestive**. *Tempestuous* \(a common word\) = stormy <Jane has a tempestuous relationship with her mother>. *Tempestive* \(an extremely arcane word\) = timely, seasonable <a tempestive delivery>. The latter is sometimes misused for the former—e.g.: "In the past year, competition has become *tempestive* \[read *tempestuous*\], said Dominique Decoudray, M & S chief buyer for fresh products in Europe." Suzanne Lowry, "Paris Life," *Daily Telegraph*, 2 June 1994, at 15.

## Language-Change Index

*tempestive* misused for *tempestuous*: Stage 1
