# teasel

teasel \(= \[1\] an Old World prickly herb having cylindrical heads of yellow or purple flowers; \[2\] a flower head of such an herb, dried and used to raise a nap on cloth such as wool; or \[3\] any similar device for raising a nap on cloth\), an Old English word dating from the 1100s, has been predominantly so written only since about 1885. Before that, \**teazle* and \**teazel* were common spellings. The spelling \**teasle* has always been uncommon.
