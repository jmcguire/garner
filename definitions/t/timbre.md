# timbre

timbre; **timber**. These are different words in both BrE and AmE. *Timbre* (/**tim**-bәr/ or /**tam**-bәr/) is primarily a musical term meaning "tone quality." E.g.: "Nor was his voice, when he spoke, of a *timbre* calculated to lull any apprehensions which his aspect might have inspired." P.G. Wodehouse, *The Return of Jeeves* 116 (1954). *Timber* /**tim**-bәr/ is the correct form in all wood-related senses. Cf. -er (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*timbre of the voice* vs. *\*timber of the voice*): 20:1
