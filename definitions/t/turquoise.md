# turquoise

turquoise (= \[1\] a semiprecious greenish-blue stone often containing some copper; or \[2\] a light greenish-blue color), a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, has been predominantly so spelled since the mid-1700s. **Turquois* is a rare variant of less value.

 - Current ratio in print (*turquoise* vs. \**turquois*): 390:1
