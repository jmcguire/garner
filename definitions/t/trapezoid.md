# trapezoid

trapezoid; **trapezium**. These words are traps for the geometrically unwary. In AmE, a *trapezoid* is a shape with four sides, two of which are parallel; in BrE, it is a four-sided shape with no parallel sides. In AmE, *trapezium* is the word for the latter sense \(a four-sided shape with no parallel sides\), while in BrE it bears the former sense \(a four-sided shape with two parallel sides\). The predominant plural of *trapezium* is *trapeziums* in both AmE and BrE \(a recent development in both\)—though across World Englishes *trapezia* has predominated since about 1970. The two plurals have been locked in rivalry since the 1700s.
