# then

then.

**A. As an Adjective.** *Then* should not be hyphenated when alone as an adjective meaning "that existed or was so at that time" <the then mayor of San Diego>. When the word is part of a phrasal adjective, the phrase should be hyphenated <then-mayor Pete Buttigieg>, but not when it occurs after the noun <Rudolph Giuliani, then mayor of New York, said . . .>. Cf. **once** & **often \(b\)**.

**B. For *than*.** This is a fairly common error, especially in newsprint—e.g.: "He enjoyed much more autonomy with ‘Face/Off' *then* \[read *than*\] he did with his other movies." Douglas J. Rowe, "Director Woo Puts a New Face on American Family Values," *Salt Lake Trib.*, 4 July 1997, at C8. For the reverse error, see **than \(b\)**.
