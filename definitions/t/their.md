# their

their.

**A. And *they're.*** A book like this one need not explain such elementary distinctions. So it will not. But: "Liberals are again trying to explain why they lost their fifth presidential election in 20 years. They've been talking about what *they're* \[read *their*\] party should be for." "What's a Liberal For?," *Wall Street J.*, 13 Jan. 1989, at A6. For still another common mistake, see **there**. See also spelling \(a\).

**B. Singular Antecedent with *their*.** See concord \(b\).
