# tinker's damn

tinker's damn \(= something valueless\) is the standard spelling. The phrase's mid-19th-century origin is unknown, but it probably alludes to the tinkers' reputation for profanity. *\*Tinker's dam* is a variant spelling, said to have originated from the tinker's little dam of bread to keep solder from running through a hole in a pot being mended. See etymology \(d\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*tinker's damn* vs. \**tinker's dam*\): 3:1
