# tubercular

tubercular; **tuberculous**; **tuberculoid**; **tuberculated**. *Tubercular* = (1) of, relating to, or involving tuberculosis \<tubercular disease\>; (2) caused by the tubercle bacillus \<tubercular meningitis\>; (3) having lesions that are or seem like tubercles \<tubercular matter\>; or (4) of, relating to, involving, or resembling a tubercle \<tubercular deposit\>. *Tuberculous* = (1) involving or having tuberculosis \<tuberculous patients\>; or (2) caused by the tubercle bacillus \<tuberculous meningitis\>. *Tuberculoid* = resembling or mimicking tuberculosis \<tuberculoid leprosy\>. *Tuberculated* = having or covered by tubercles \<tuberculated skin\>.

In short, *tubercular* is the broadest, most multifaceted term, and it was the most popular of the terms in the 1800s. In its two senses, *tuberculous* has been more commonly used in medical literature since 1900—hence *tuberculous meningitis* was nearly 25 times as common as *tubercular meningitis* in 1960 and about 8 times as common in 2019. The most specific and least common terms are *tuberculoid* and *tuberculated*.

As long as this bacterial disease (formerly known as *consumption*) inhabits the globe, we are going to need all four adjectives.
