# taboo

taboo; **\*tabu**. Although the two forms were close rivals throughout the 1700s and 1800s, beginning in the 1890s *taboo* established itself as the standard spelling.

For the verb *taboo* \(= to forbid or prohibit because of tradition or custom\), the past tense is *tabooed* rather than *\*taboo'd*. E.g.: "In golf's rubric, the serious mention of those two words \[Grand Slam\] had long held the same *tabooed* rank as a discourse on the shanks." Barker Davis, "Old Blue Gives a Wake-Up Call," *Wash. Times*, 17 June 1997, at B1.

Current ratio in print \(*taboo* vs. *\*tabu*\): 11:1
