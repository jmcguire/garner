# transship

transship (= to transfer from one ship or vehicle to another) is so spelled—without a hyphen. The word is often misspelled *\*tranship*—e.g.: "Japanese intelligence sources speculated that the shipment may have originated in China and been *transhipped* \[read *transshipped*\] through the North Korean port of Chongjin by a middleman." Richard Lloyd Parry, "North Korea: A Nation Exporting Food While Its Children Starve," *Independent*, 18 July 1997, at 16. Cf. **transonic**.

 - Current ratio in print (*transship* vs. \**tranship*): 1.1:1
