# trousseau

trousseau /troo-**soh**/ or /**troo**-soh/ \(= the personal possessions that a woman brings with her into marriage\) is an archaic word, to say the least. It has traditionally formed the French plural *trousseaux* in AmE and BrE alike. But since about 1970, *trousseaus* has made headway in AmE, while *trousseaux* has been somewhat in decline.

Current ratio in print \(*trousseaux* vs. *trousseaus*\): 1.8:1
