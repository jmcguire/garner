# threshold

threshold. So spelled. *\*Threshhold*, a common misspelling that emerged in the 1800s, occasionally appears in print—e.g.: "We dared to cross the *threshhold* \[read *threshold*\] from sophisticated, drawing-room, strangulated drollery to the wilderness where we not only faced the lion's roar but smelled the breath of their bad habits." Letter of Richard Harris \(the actor\), "A Sharp Kick from a Man Called Horse," *Sunday Times* \(London\), 6 Aug. 1995, § 3, at 8. The word is not a compound of *thresh* and *hold*, but rather a modern form of the Old English *thaerscwold* \("doorsill"\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*threshold* vs. *\*threshhold*\): 857:1
