# tricorne

tricorne \(= \[1\] a cocked hat with three corners, or \[2\] a muscle having three terminations\), dating in English from about 1800, is the traditional noun—whereas *tricorn* is the traditional adjective. Since about 1980, however, the spelling *tricorn* has commonly been used as a noun. Although it might be possible to maintain the distinction between *tricornes* and *tricorn hats*—using, as in BrE today, *tricorne* as a noun and *tricorn* as an adjective—the nicety seems unlikely to persist. Cf. **bicorne**.
