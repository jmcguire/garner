# to(-)wit

to(-)wit. The ordinary progression in such common phrases is from two words, to a hyphenated form, to a single word. Though some writers have experimented with the single-word version, and the hyphenated form was once common, today it seems that *to wit* is destined to remain two words—if indeed its destiny is not oblivion. *To wit* is a legal archaism in the place of which *namely* is almost always an improvement. Cf. **namely** & **viz**.
