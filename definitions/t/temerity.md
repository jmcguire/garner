# temerity

temerity (= rash boldness) is sometimes, through word-swapping, confused with *timidity*. In the following example, the writer's meaning isn't at all clear: "There's a wonderful moment when Hal actually has the *temerity* to place his hand on his father's shoulder, a *timid* gesture of affection that he immediately is made to regret." Lloyd Rose, " ‘Henry IV': Shortened and Sweet," *Wash. Post*, 27 Sept. 1994, at D1.
