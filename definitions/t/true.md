# true

true. Normally an adjective, of course, this word has also, since the early 1800s, functioned as a verb meaning "to fit, place, or form in a way that is perfectly flat, correct, straight, or level." In that sense, the present participle raises a ticklish question: is it *truing* or \**trueing*? The answer is that *truing* has always predominated in all varieties of English.

 - Current ratio in print \(*truing* vs. \**trueing* in World Englishes\): 5:1
