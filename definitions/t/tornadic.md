# tornadic

tornadic (= of, relating to, or involving a tornado or tornadoes) was coined in the late 1800s as an adjective corresponding to the 16th-century word *tornado*. The newer word can be seen as a pomposity because *tornado* generally serves as its own adjective \<tornado activity\>. But some writers seem to like the mock-technicality of *tornadic*—e.g.: "The peak of the storm hit Brockton and Mary D in Schuylkill Township, where *tornadic* \[read *tornado*\] damage was a quarter-mile wide." Kristen Klick, "Schuylkill Was Ripped by Twister," *Morning Call* (Allentown, Pa.), 15 May 1996, at B1. The term is especially common in the phrases *tornadic activity*, *tornadic blast*, *tornadic storms*, and *tornadic winds*.

*Tornadic* is pronounced either /tor-**nay**-dik/ or /tor-**nad**-ik/.
