# tularemia

tularemia (= an infectious rodent disease transmissible to humans and other animals by insect bites or the handling of infected flesh, characteristic symptoms being inflammation of lymph nodes, irregular fevers, and general achiness), a modern Latin form first appearing in the early 20th century, was originally spelled \**tularaemia*. But within a decade of its being coined (after Tulare County, California), the digraph *ae* had been reduced in AmE to *e*: hence *tularemia*. Not until about 2000 was the simplified spelling predominant in BrE.

 - Current ratio in print (*tularemia* vs. \**tularaemia*): 10:1
