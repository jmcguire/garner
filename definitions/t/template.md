# template

template /**tem**-plәt/ \(= \[1\] something used as a model for something else; or \[2\] a document used for purposes of formatting or drafting similar documents\), seemingly a French/Latin loanword, dates in English from the 1600s. It has been predominantly spelled *template*, however, only since about 1925. Before that, the spelling *templet* \(the same as the French etymon\) was equally common. An unfortunate byproduct of settling on the standard spelling *template* is the overdone "spelling pronunciation" /**tem**-playt/. See pronunciation \(f\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*template* vs. *templet*\): 91:1
