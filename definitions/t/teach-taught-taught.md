# teach > taught > taught

teach > taught > taught. So inflected. *\*Teached* is a form that isn't taught anywhere and is no part of standard english. But it sometimes appears in print—e.g.:

 - "Bert John Berghorst worked with the Hope Summer Repertory Theatre and formerly *teached* \[read *taught*\] at the West Ottawa schools." "Teacher, Theater Figure Discovered Dead in Home," *Grand Rapids Press*, 26 Sept. 1995, at A10 \(story deck\).

 - "Royston said he *teached* \[read *taught*\] youngsters and adults to either cover a grease fire before it spreads or put it out with baking soda." Caryl R. Lucas, "Belleville Kids Honored for Saving Their Moms in Separate House Fires," *Star-Ledger* \(Newark\), 11 Nov. 1998, at 43.

 - "In addition to traditional subjects, the school *teached* \[read *taught*\] functional skills and has a vocational job program." "Specialty Schools," *Enquirer-J.* \(Monroe, N.C.\), 25 June 2015, at A31.
