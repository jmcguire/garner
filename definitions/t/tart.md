# tart

tart (= \[1\] having a sharp, sour taste; or \[2\] marked by sarcasm, comeuppance, or acrimony), an Old English word dating from about 1000, makes the comparative *tarter* and the superlative *tartest*. The comparative appears frequently in the phrases *tarter flavor* and *tarter taste*. In reference to sauces, however, the comparative is ill-advised because of the inevitable suspicion that you mean *tartar sauce* (AmE) or *tartare sauce* (BrE). (See **tartar.)** So if you mean a sauce that's more tart than another, the better course is to use some other phrasing such as *sharper sauce* or (through circumlocution) *sauce that's more pungent*.
