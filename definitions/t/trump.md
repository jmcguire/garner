# trump

trump (= to defeat or vanquish by superior means) originated as a card-playing term in the 1500s. In the same century, the verb acquired senses relating to fabrication and imposition <to trump up charges>. By the 1700s, the phrasal adjective *trumped-up* (always so hyphenated) had gained currency. Since the elevation of Donald J. Trump to the U.S. presidency in 2017, the word has inevitably become associated, in some minds, with his name. But many people continue to use the word as if there were no such overtones.
