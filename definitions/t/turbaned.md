# turbaned

turbaned \(= wearing a long piece of cloth wound tightly around the head, as is customary among men in parts of North Africa and South Asia, and sometimes among women as a matter of style\), a past-participial adjective made from a Persian/Turkish loanword (the noun *turban*\), has been predominantly so spelled in all varieties of English since the 1700s. \**Turbanned* and \**turban'd* are variants.

Current ratio in print \(*turbaned* vs. \**turbanned* vs. \**turban'd*\): 35:1.8:1
