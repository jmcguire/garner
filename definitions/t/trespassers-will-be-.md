# trespassers will be prosecuted

trespassers will be prosecuted. This set phrase, which most readers would construe as referring to criminal proceedings, usually expresses an untruth. In most states (Louisiana is a notable exception), trespass to land is ordinarily a tort—not a crime. Although the landowner can sue, the district attorney won't prosecute. But a trespasser who causes damage, as by trampling crops or breaking windows, can be criminally prosecuted. The warning *Trespassers will be prosecuted* came into widespread use in the 1840s.
