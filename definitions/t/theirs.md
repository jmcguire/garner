# theirs

theirs, an absolute possessive that takes no apostrophe, is sometimes wrongly written *\*their's*—e.g.:

 - "The Badgers read Bennett's intensity. He read *their's* \[read *theirs*\]." Vic Feuerherd, "Bennett's Gift," *Wis. State J.*, 14 Nov. 2000, at D1.

 - "Apparently, it can happen even in a marriage such as *their's* \[read *theirs*\], which lasted 33 years." Anna L. Bisol, "Montachusett People," *Telegram & Gaz.* \(Worcester\), 25 Mar. 2001, at 2.

 - "The soon-to-be expanded dump's operators offer assurance that the stink isn't *their's* \[read *theirs*\]." "What's That Smell?," *Daily News* \(L.A.\), 25 Apr. 2001, at N14.

See possessives \(c\).

## Language-Change Index

*\*their's* for *theirs*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*theirs* vs. *\*their's*\): 252:1
