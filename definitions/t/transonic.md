# transonic

transonic \(= of, relating to, or involving travel at a speed that progresses from subsonic to supersonic velocity\), a modern adjective made from Latin elements and dating from the 1940s, has been predominantly so spelled from the beginning. In this way, it's anomalous to have lost an *s* in *trans* \+ *sonic*—unlike, say, *transsexual*, *transshape*, and *transship*. Since the 1940s, \**transsonic* and \**trans-sonic* have been variants. Cf. **transsexual** & **transship**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*transonic* vs. \**transsonic* vs. \**trans-sonic*\): 130:1.5:1
