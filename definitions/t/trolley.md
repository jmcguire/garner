# trolley

trolley. AmE and BrE agree in the spelling of this word—*trolley*, as opposed to the variant \**trolly* \(which was standard BrE till about 1890\). But the two major varieties of English diverge over the meaning. AmE uses *trolley* where BrE would use *tram*; BrE uses *trolley* where AmE would use *cart*. So in AmE, a *trolley* is an electric vehicle that moves along the street on metal tracks and carries passengers from one point to another <took a trolley to the museum district>. In BrE, a *trolley* is either a shopping cart or a small table on wheels used for serving food <pushing a supermarket trolley> <the trolleys at a dim sum restaurant>.

Current ratio in print \(*trolley* vs. \**trolly* in World Englishes\): 166:1
