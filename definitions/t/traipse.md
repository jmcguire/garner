# traipse

traipse (= \[1\] to walk in a leisurely, carefree way; or \[2\] to walk in a slow and aimless way from boredom or fatigue), a verb of unknown origin dating from the 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1915. This standard spelling was established first in AmE (1915) and only later in BrE (1939). \**Trapes* and \**trapse* are variants.

 - Current ratio in print (*traipsing* vs. \**trapesing* vs. \**trapsing*): 161:6:1
