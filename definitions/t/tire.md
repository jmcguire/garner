# tire

tire; **tyre**. In AmE, *tire* is both \(1\) the noun denoting the thick rubber fitted onto a wheel; and \(2\) the verb meaning "to fatigue" or "to weary." In BrE, sense 1 has predominantly been spelled *tyre* since the early 20th century. Although there is no etymological justification for this *y* spelling, it is firmly ensconced in BrE.

Current ratio in print \(*car tires* vs. *car tyres* in AmE\): 7:1
Current ratio in print \(*car tyres* vs. *car tires* in BrE\): 3:1
