# try and

try and is, in AmE, a casualism for *try to*—e.g.: "Mr. Kemp, who seemed intent on slowing his normally rapid speaking pace, accused the Administration of ‘demagoguery' in using ‘fear' to *try and* \[read *try to*\] panic older voters with charges that Republicans endanger the health of the Medicare program." Francis X. Clines, "Candidates Stick to the Issues, Not Ducking the Touchy Ones," *N.Y. Times*, 10 Oct. 1996, at A15. In BrE, however, *try and* is a standard idiom.

Current ratio in print \(*try to* vs. *try and*\): 7:1
