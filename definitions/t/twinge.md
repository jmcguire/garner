# twinge

twinge \(= \[1\] to cause to have a sudden feeling of slight pain; or \[2\] to experience a sudden sharp but slightly local pain\), an Old English verb dating from about 1000, predominantly makes the present participle *twinging*, not \**twingeing*. \(Cf. **swinge**.\) The noun *twinge* \(= a sudden feeling of slight physical or emotional pain\) is much less old, dating from the 1500s. Yet the noun is considerably more common today.
