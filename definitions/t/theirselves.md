# \*theirselves

\*theirselves is confined to nonstandard dialects. With origins in the late 1700s, it seldom appears in print—e.g.: "He encouraged those in attendance to be leaders, but to go beyond devoting *theirselves* \[read *themselves*\] to their careers to devote time to their family and personal lives." "Brentwood Honors 5 Leaders," *Tennessean*, 7 Oct. 1996, at F6. See pronouns \(e\).

Language-Change Index

*\*theirselves* for *themselves*: Stage 1   
Current ratio in print \(*themselves* vs. *\*theirselves*\): 2,416:1
