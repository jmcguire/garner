# take

take.

**A. Inflected Forms: *take* > *took* > *taken*.** The form *\*tooken* is low dialect that occasionally shows up in quoted speech—e.g.:

 - "And how he absolutely hated ‘to get *tooken* out of a ball game.' " Garret Mathews, "Ol' Diz Would Have Struck Out in Broadcasting Today," *Evansville Courier & Press*, 22 Jan. 1999, at B1 \(quoting Dizzy Dean\).

 - "Testimony showed Sokolowski told another man he ‘had *tooken* care of' Ellwood." "Sentence Upheld in Grisly Murder," *News & Record* \(Greensboro\), 4 Dec. 1999, at B6C.

 - "‘We just had a tornado up there in Clinton. I got my house *tooken* away,' said 22-year-old Johnnie Arnold." Jon Gambrell, "Risky Business," *St. Louis Post-Dispatch*, 20 Apr. 2008, at D4.

Though unusual, the form *\*tooken* isn't new: it first appeared in print in the 1700s and has persisted as nonstandard. See irregular verbs.
