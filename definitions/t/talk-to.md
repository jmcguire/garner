# talk to

talk to; **talk with**. These have nuanced connotations. The first suggests a superior's advising or reprimanding or even condescending <I want to talk to you about the work you're doing> <a movie star wants to talk to me?>. The second suggests a conversation between equals, with equal participation <I want to talk with you about our project> <I can always talk with my husband after class>. The distinction is chiefly relevant when the parties have different levels of power, authority, or prestige. Among equals, *talk with* conveys greater respect and tact.
