# thorough

thorough. This adjective began life in the 1100s as a variant spelling of the preposition *through*—a sense still visible in the word *thoroughfare*. By the 1400s, the term was becoming primarily adjectival in either of two broad senses: (1) "so exhaustive as to include every detail" \<thorough research\>; and (2) "painstakingly careful so as to avoid mistakes" \<a thorough investigator\>. Beginning in the 1600s, the nonstandard spelling \**thoro* and \**thorow* appeared with some frequency. The *OED* quite properly labels them obsolete—along with many other variants that occurred earlier, during the Middle English period.

The word is pronounced /**thәr**-oh/ in AmE and /**thәr**-ә/ in BrE. British speakers are often surprised at the final syllable as Americans say it.

 - Current ratio in print (*thorough* vs. \**thorow* vs. \**thoro*): 427:1.6:1
