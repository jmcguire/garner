# dishabille

dishabille /dis-ә-**beel**/ \(= a state of careless or partial dress\) is so spelled in AmE—though beginning in 20th-century BrE the usual spelling has been *deshabille*. The word was anglicized in the 1600s and in fairly widespread use through the late 1800s. There was little cause for reverting to the gallicism *déshabillé* in the 1900s, even toward the end of that period when more and more people were dressing in a casual and even careless way. To keep things tidy, *dishabille* should be printed in roman type \(not italic\) and without diacritical marks.

 - Current ratio in print \(*dishabille* vs. *deshabille*\): 2.2:1
