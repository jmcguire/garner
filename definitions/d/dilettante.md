# dilettante

dilettante (= \[1\] a devotee of the fine arts; or \[2\] someone who dabbles in a subject or skill but never masters it), an Italian loanword dating in English from 1748, primarily made the Italian plural *dilettanti* through the mid-20th century. The anglicized plural *dilettantes* had become predominant in AmE by 1940 and in BrE by 1975. It has been standard across World Englishes since the mid-1950s.

As for pronouncing the singular noun, it's /**dil**-i-tahnt/ in AmE but /dil-i-**tahn**-tee/ or /dil-i-**tahn**-tay/ in BrE.

 - Current ratio in print (*dilettantes* vs. *dilettanti*): 1.7:1
