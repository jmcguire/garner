# defamation

defamation; **libel**; **slander**. These three terms are distinguished in law. *Defamation* = an attack on the reputation of another. It encompasses both *libel* \(in permanent form, especially in writing\) and *slander* \(in transitory form, especially by spoken words\). See **libel**.

*Defamation of character*, a slightly verbose phrase that is essentially synonymous with *defamation*, has been in frequent use since about 1800. Since the late 1800s, it has occasionally been mangled into \**deformation of character*—a malapropism.

 - Current ratio in print \(*defamation of character* vs. \**deformation of character*\): 19:1
