# destruct

destruct, vb., originated in the 1600s as a need-less variant of *destroy*. By the early 1900s, it had largely fallen into disuse. In the 1950s, however, the word arose once again—this time as a high-tech espionage term arrived at as a back-formation from the noun *destruction*. Today, it is confined mostly to the phrase *self-destruct*.
