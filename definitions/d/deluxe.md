# deluxe

deluxe. Although this adjective was originally \(from the early 1800s\) written as two words—*de luxe*—it became predominantly solidified in AmE by 1950, in World Englishes by 1955, and in BrE by the late 1980s. Of course, the word means "outstandingly luxurious and high-quality." It has undergone some downgrading in sense because of hyperbolic uses—most notably in the description of some very ordinary hotel rooms as "deluxe rooms." It is preferably pronounced /di-**lәks**/—not /di-**looks**/, /di-**lʊks**/, or /**dee**-lәks/. The term is sometimes misspelled *\*delux*.
