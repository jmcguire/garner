# decision-making

decision-making, n., is a generic term for *deciding* and, though useful in some contexts, is much overworked in modern prose—especially in the redundant bureaucratese *decision-making process*. The word smacks of business jargon: after all, when one makes decisions, one decides.

It is now frequently spelled as one word, even by the U.S. Supreme Court. One sees the same one-wordism tendency at work in the terms *\*budgetmaking* and *\*policymaking*. These compounds are too bulky to look like anything but jargonistic English; a simple hyphen does a lot.

 - Current ratio in print \(*decision-making* vs. *decisionmaking*\): 8:1
