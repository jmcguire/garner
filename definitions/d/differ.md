# differ

differ. When it comes to one's approach to a subject, to differ *from* a person is merely to be unlike the other <the two kids differ from each other in so many ways>. But to differ *with* a person is to take issue explicitly with the other <he was looking for opportunities to differ with his rival>.

The phrasing *differ from* typically appears when two things are unalike—e.g.: "Assisted suicide *differs from* euthanasia, in which the doctor may take an active role in the death, such as by administering lethal injection." Bob Groves, "Doctor-Assisted Suicide Called an Obligation," *Record* (N.J.), 20 Sept. 1996, at A5.
