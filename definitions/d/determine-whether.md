# determine whether

determine whether; \***determine if**. Although *\*determine if* has historically been used by reputable writers, it has never been as common as *determine whether* in edited prose. The *whether* form is considered better because *if* erroneously suggests a conditional statement as opposed to a neutral hypothetical. E.g.:

 - "Tests were being done to *determine if* \[read *determine whether*\] the bees were of the Africanized variety." "Man, 85, Survives More than 300 Bee Stings," *Record* (N.J.), 20 Sept. 1996, at L6.

 - "Without a clear, consistent method of evaluating the performances of the chief attorney and county administrator, it's difficult, if not impossible, for the commissioners and the public to *determine if* \[read *determine whether*\] these two top employees are meeting their goals and objectives." "Value of Evaluations," *Sarasota Herald-Trib.*, 20 Sept. 1996, at A14.

 - "A seasoned duck hunter and cook can always *determine if* \[read *determine whether*\] a fowl is taken east or west of I-35." "Editorial," *Abilene Rptr.-News* (Tex.), 23 Aug. 2015, at G12.

For a similar problem with *\*doubt if* for *doubt whether*, see **doubt (a)**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*determine if* for *determine whether*: Stage 4
 - Current ratio in print (*determine whether* vs. *\*determine if *): 2.2:1
