# dotcom

dotcom. This word from the computer age refers to a company that does its primary business on the internet or provides goods or services to other companies that do. Written as one word, it derives from the *.com* at the end of some commercial internet addresses. E.g.: "It seems like only yesterday that *dotcoms* were hotter than hot." Rem Reider, "At the Crossroads," *Am. Journalism Rev.*, Mar. 2001, at 6. It once connoted a company that was raking in investors yet never making a profit, and after the tech bubble burst in 2000 the word was often replaced by *dot bomb*.
