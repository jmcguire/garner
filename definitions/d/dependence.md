# dependence

dependence; **dependency**. These variants have undergone differentiation. *Dependence*, more common since the 1500s, is the general word meaning \(1\) "the quality or state of being dependent"; or \(2\) "reliance." *Dependency* is a geopolitical term meaning "a territory under the jurisdiction of, but not formally annexed by, a nation." \(See **territory**.\) These words are commonly misspelled *\*dependance* and *\*dependancy.*

Sometimes *dependency* is misused for *dependence*—e.g.: "Citing the Army's *dependency* \[read *dependence*\] on reservists, Mr. Cohen last month postponed a planned reduction of 25,000 Guard and Reservist positions, a 4 percent cut that would have saved $900 million over the next five years." Steven Lee Myers, "New Role of Guard Transforming Military," *N.Y. Times*, 24 Jan. 2000, at A1, A22.

Unfortunately, the distinction is not complete: *dependency* is also the term for drug habituation. See **addicted**.
