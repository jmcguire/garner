# dissolvable

dissolvable; **dissoluble**. The word meaning "capable of being dissolved" was traditionally *dissoluble* \(dating from the 1530s\). But *dissolvable*, dating from the 1540s, is almost as old. The two have long competed. Since the late 1980s in AmE and the late 1990s in BrE, *dissolvable* has become the predominant form. It seems here to stay.

Current ratio in print \(*dissolvable* vs. *dissoluble*\): 2.5:1
