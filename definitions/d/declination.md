# declination

declination; **\*declinature**; **declension**. All three words are used in denoting the act of courteously refusing, but *declination* outstrips the other two in frequency of use. In referring to the act of declining, *\*declinature* and *declension* ought to be considered needless variants of *declination*.

But even *declination* is open to possible ambiguity because it also means \(1\) "a downward bend or slope"; \(2\) "a deterioration; falling off"; or \(3\) "a deviation." *Declension* has the same problem because it may mean \(1\) "a downhill slope"; \(2\) "a decline or decrease"; or \(3\) "the grammatical inflections of a noun or pronoun." *Declinature* is best known \(when it is known at all\) as a historical plea in Scots law.

 - Current ratio in print \(*his* *declination* vs. *his declension* vs. *\*his declinature*\): 21:6:1
