# damp

damp, v.t.; **dampen**. Both may mean "to make damp, moisten," but each word carries at least one additional sense. *Damp* = (1) to stifle or extinguish \<damp the furnace\>; or (2) to deaden the vibration of (a piano string). *Dampen* = to check, diminish, or depress \<the news of Mr. Ratliff's illness dampened our spirits\>. Although the words sometimes overlap in these additional senses, they are best kept distinct.
