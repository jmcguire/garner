# dog-eat-dog

dog-eat-dog \(= fiercely competitive\), a phrasal adjective dating from the 1850s, most often appears in the phrase *dog-eat-dog world*. The phrase derives from a Latin proverb *canis caninam non est* \("dog does not eat dog"\), found in the works of Marcus Terentius Varro \(*De Lingua Latina* VII.32\). The mondegreen \**doggy-dog world* is traceable to the 1970s.

Current ratio in print \(*dog-eat-dog* vs. \**doggy-dog*\): 72:1
