# delirium

delirium (= \[1\] a disordered mental state, often hallucination, in which the affected person talks nonstop in a confused, anxious way; or \[2\] loosely, frenzied excitement or emotion), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has had the anglicized plural *deliriums* since the mid-1700s. That form has predominated for most of the period since then. But the Latinate \**deliria* has existed alongside *deliriums*, mostly as a secondary variant. Both words appear primarily in medical texts <deliriums can be etiologically classified into two types>. Doubtless the anglicized plural is more immediately comprehensible to more readers and listeners. See plurals (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*deliriums* vs. \**deliria*): 1.4:1
