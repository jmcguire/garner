# dock

dock, n. Since the late 19th century, usage commentators have occasionally criticized the landlubber's use of *dock* for *wharf* or *pier*. But this sense has been common since that time. In fact, nautically speaking, *dock* has two senses: (1) "a large structure or excavated basin for receiving ships, equipped with gates to keep water in or out"; and (2) "a landing pier; wharf" (*WNWCD*). Although Theodore Bernstein and Wilson Follett both disapproved of sense 2, most commentators today accept it. It should be considered standard.
