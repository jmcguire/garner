# difference

difference; **discrepancy**; **disparity**. A difference—not really a discrepancy or a disparity—exists between these words, but it's largely one of connotation. *Difference* is the most general of the three, meaning "the quality or state of being unalike or dissimilar" \<a difference of less than $20\> \<a difference of opinion\>. *Discrepancy* suggests that the difference is between things that should be consistent \<a discrepancy between the bank statements and the business ledger\>. *Disparity* usually implies that the difference is inequitable or unfair \<a disparity in the treatment of men and women\>. See **differential (a)**.
