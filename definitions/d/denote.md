# denote

denote \(= to indicate\) for *denominate* \(= to assign a name\) is a common error—e.g.: "Teenagers whose parents are home at what the researchers *denoted* \[read *denominated* or *called*\] ‘key times'—in the morning, after school, at dinner and at bedtime—are less likely to smoke, to drink or to use marijuana." "What Teenagers Need, Etc.," *Wash. Times*, 14 Sept. 1997, at B2.

For the difference between *denote* and *connote*, see **connote \(a\)**.

Language-Change Index

*denote* misused for *denominate*: Stage 1
