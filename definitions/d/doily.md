# doily

doily \(= a small decorative napkin or mat on which an object is to be placed\), a word derived from a proper noun and dating in English from the late 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled since the latter part of that century. The London draper who gave his name to this word spelled his name either *Doyley* or *Doiley*—scholars can't say with certainty which one it was. Today \**doyley*, \**doyly*, and \**doilie* are variant spellings that appear only infrequently.

Current ratio in print \(*doily* vs. \**doyley* vs. \**doilie* vs. \**doyly*\): 363:6:1.2:1
