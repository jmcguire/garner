# dormouse

dormouse \(= a small European squirrel-like rodent with a long furry tail\), a word of uncertain origin, dates from the 1400s. The word is thought to derive from OF *dormir* "to sleep" and perhaps from *dormeuse* "sluggish," altered through folk etymology to -*mouse*. The spelling had been settled by the early 1700s. One thing is certain: the word has nothing to do with doors. Yet since about 1897, writers have sometimes misspelled the word \**doormouse*—applying folk etymology to both syllables.

The plural \**dormouses* was common during the 1500s and 1600s. But today, as the result of folk etymology, the established plural is *dormice*. See etymology \(d\).

Current ratio in print \(*dormouse* vs. \**doormouse*\): 133:1
Current ratio in print \(*dormice* vs. \**dormouses*\): 438:1
