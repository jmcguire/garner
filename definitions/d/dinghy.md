# dinghy

dinghy /**deeng**-ee/ or /**ding**-ee/ \(= a small open boat used either for pleasure or for transporting people between ship and shore\), a term dating in English from the late 1700s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1885. Before that, the spellings \**dingey* and \**dinghee* were about equally common. The term is also sometimes spelled, or misspelled, *dingy*; that spelling, however, should be reserved for the adjective pronounced /**din**-jee/, meaning "dirty and shabby; dusky from uncleanliness" <a dingy bar in the rough part of town>.
