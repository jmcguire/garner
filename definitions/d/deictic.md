# deictic

deictic /**dīk**-tik/ is a literary term meaning "proving or showing by direct demonstration." Even in scholarly works, writers feel obliged to explain the term—e.g.: "If we accept \[Marcel\] Duchamp's part objects as signifiers in this sense, they appear as ‘*deictic*,' that is to say, as directly pointing to—as demonstrative of—the subject." Erika Naginski, "Eros-en-Valise," *Art J.*, 22 Sept. 2007, at 122. For a discussion of deictic terms from a grammatical point of view, see pointing words.
