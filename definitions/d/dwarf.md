# dwarf

dwarf. Pl. *dwarfs*. The form *\*dwarves*, a variant, occasionally appears—e.g.:

 - "The sculptors of Benin long have made their finest objects—their regal heads and life-size *dwarves* \[read *dwarfs*\], their animals and birds and ceremonial swords—out of molten brass." Joseph McLellan et al., "Here & Now," *Wash. Post*, 25 Sept. 1994, at G3.

 - "The painting depicts a variety of fey creatures such as elves, *dwarves* \[read *dwarfs*\], gnomes, and pixies in a forest." Justin Reutter, "Adventure Awaits," *Chillicothe Gaz.* \(Ohio\), 8 Oct. 2021, at A1.

When he released his famous movie in 1938, Walt Disney got it right: *Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs*. See plurals \(c\). Cf. **wharf**.

## Language-Change Index

*\*dwarves* for *dwarfs*: Stage 3
Current ratio in print \(*dwarfs* vs. *\*dwarves*\): 1.3:1
