# dado

dado /**day**-doh/ (= \[1\] an architectural feature on a column or wall—either the part of a pedestal between the base and the cap or the decorated part of a wall beneath a chair rail; or \[2\] a rectangular groove in woodworking), an Italian loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has two possible plurals: *dados* and *dadoes*. Although *AHD* and *W11* list only *dadoes*, the plural *dados* has generally predominated, as it does today. See plurals (d).

 - Current ratio in print (*cutting dados* vs. *cutting dadoes*): 5:1
