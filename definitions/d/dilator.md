# dilator

dilator; \***dilatator**; **\*dilater**. *Dilator* \(= someone or something \[as a muscle or a medical instrument\] that causes a body part, wound, etc. to expand or widen\) has been the predominant term since the early 1800s. Although the first two editions of the *OED* called the form "less correct" than \**dilatator* and \**dilater*, the etymological justification proffered for this statement is flimsy. And in any event, usage has simply superseded those by-forms. See -er \(a\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*dilator* vs. \**dilatator* vs. \**dilater*\): 347:7:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*dilator muscle* vs. \**dilatator muscle*\): 49:1
