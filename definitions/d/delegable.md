# delegable

delegable is the word, not *\*delegatable*. But many writers mistakenly use the latter form—e.g.: "The reality is that oversight simply is not *delegatable* \[read *delegable*\]." Warren F. McFarlan & Richard L. Nolan, "How to Manage an IT Outsourcing Alliance," *Sloan Mgmt. Rev.*, Jan. 1995, at 9. See -able \(d\) & -atable.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*delegatable* for *delegable*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*delegable* vs. *\*delegatable*\): 12:1
