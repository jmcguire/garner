# dissimilar

dissimilar has always preferably taken the preposition *to*, not *from* on the analogy of *similar to*. E.g.:

 - "In the '60s, the blue-collar neighborhoods were dominated by gangs of youths not at all *dissimilar from* \[read *dissimilar to*\] the warring clans of ‘Braveheart' and ‘Rob Roy,' " Gary Dretzka, " ‘Small Faces' Is Another Grim Picture of Scottish Life," *Chicago Trib.*, 3 Sept. 1996, at C3.

 - "On a holiday a few years ago, Alam and family stayed in a house not *dissimilar to* the one in Leave the World Behind." Jason Steger, "Rumaan Alam," *Sydney Morning Herald* (Aus.), 14 Aug. 2021, at 10.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*dissimilar from* for *dissimilar to*: Stage 2
 - Current ratio in print (*dissimilar to* vs. *\*dissimilar from*): 3:1
