# daemon

daemon; **demon**. Both are pronounced /**dee**-mәn/. The spelling *daemon* distinguishes the Greek-mythology senses of supernatural being, indwelling spirit, etc. from the modern sense of devil's helper (*demon*). E.g.: "The *daemon* in him played the game just as it wrote the poems." Jeffrey Meyers, "Poets and Tennis—Drop Shots and Tender Egos," *N.Y. Times*, 2 June 1985, § 7, at 24. *Daemon* is also a term of art in the field of information technology, referring to a background process or thread (primarily in Unix).
