# divulge

divulge, v.t., = to make known \(esp. something secret\); reveal. E.g.: "Yet Mattel executives wouldn't *divulge* any details of the coming job cuts." David Robinson, "Mattel Puts Profits Before Morale During Holidays," *Buffalo News*, 25 Dec. 1994, at 11. The verb should not be used where *say* or *tell* would suffice—e.g.: "What Dunton, a portly man who was dressed in jeans and a striped shirt Wednesday, *divulged* \[read *said*\] to investigators is unclear." Matt Krasnowski, "Witness Takes Jail over Talking in Cowlings' Case," *State J.-Register* \(Springfield, Ill.\), 8 Sept. 1994, at 2. The accent is on the second syllable: /di-**vәlj**/ or, esp. in BrE, /dī-**vәlj**/.
