# determiner

determiner; **determinant**. Both mean "that which determines," but only *determiner* denotes the person who determines. \(For the grammatical sense of *determiner*, see p. 1204.\) In the general sense, preference might be given to the native-English suffix *-er*, but you couldn't be faulted for using either term: euphony should be the determiner. And *determinant* often simply sounds better—e.g.: "The use of the traditional business concept of value as the *determinant* of choices would set the environmental debate back decades." "The Challenge of Going Green," *Harv. Bus. Rev.*, July–Aug. 1994, at 37.
