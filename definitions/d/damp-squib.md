# damp squib

damp squib \(= something intended or expected to be exciting, effective, or powerful but proving to be quite the opposite\). A *squib* is either a small firework or, in a literary sense, a published attack on someone or something. The firework sense led originally to the metaphorical phrase *damp squib*, dating from the mid-1800s.

Beginning about 1960, some people began either toying with the phrase or \(more often\) misunderstanding it \(see mondegreens\), thereby making it \**damp squid*, itself a fishy phrase given that, in their natural habitats, squids are more than just damp. It's hard to conjure up a context in which the phrase might correctly appear except when complaining in a restaurant, after one has ordered fried squid—or perhaps in an Asian market if one were complaining about the improper storage of the delicacy known as dried squid.

Current ratio in print \(*damp squib* vs. \**damp squid*\): 36:1
