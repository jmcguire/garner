# disenthrall

disenthrall; **disenthral**. Today, this word isn't an antonym of *enthrall* \(= to hold spellbound or thoroughly engrossed\). Instead, *disenthrall* means "to free from the bondage of slavery; to disenslave." Actually, though, an archaic sense of *enthrall* \(BrE *enthral*\) is "to enslave." Few now think of *thralldom* in that sense. So perhaps it's understandable that the usual spellings in BrE are *enthral* but *disenthrall*. \(Please don't be disillusioned: you'd wander long and far to find a BrE speaker who has used the term *disenthrall*.\) In AmE, of course, the standard forms are *enthrall* and *disenthrall*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*to disenthrall* vs. *to disenthral* in BrE\): 1.5:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*to disenthrall* vs. *to disenthral* in AmE\): 5:1
