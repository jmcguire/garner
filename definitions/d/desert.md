# desert

desert, vb.; **abandon**, vb. You can either desert or abandon a place or a person, but only *abandon* works for plans or efforts. E.g.: "Attempts were also made to use the shredded currency as insulation, mattress filling, and ‘drilling mud' for oil exploration, but again the material was not suitable and those efforts were *deserted* \[read *abandoned*\]." Calvin Sims, "In Recycling of Greenbacks, New Meaning for Old Money," *N.Y. Times*, 22 May 1994, at 1, 12.

## Language-Change Index

 - *desert* misused for *abandon*: Stage 1
