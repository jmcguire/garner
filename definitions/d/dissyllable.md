# dissyllable

dissyllable \(= a word of two syllables\) has been predominantly so spelled in all varieties of English since the 1700s. Beginning in the 1970s, there was a concerted effort among philologists to use *disyllable*—"the better spelling," according to Robert W. Burchfield \(*FMEU3* at 221\)—but the gallicism-like spelling with the *-ss-* has persisted and predominated.

Current ratio in print \(*dissyllable* vs. *disyllable*\): 1.03:1
