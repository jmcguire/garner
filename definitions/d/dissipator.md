# dissipator

dissipator \(= \[1\] someone or something that causes a dispersion or scattering; \[2\] the terminal part of a glacier that continually melts; or \[3\] an instrument for measuring the rate of dispersion\), an agent noun dating from the 1500s, has always been predominantly so spelled. *Dissipater*, though long given as the main headword in the *OED*, has come to rival the *-or* spelling only since the year 2000. See -er \(a\).

1979 ratio in print \(*dissipator* vs. *dissipater*\): 8:1   
Current ratio in print \(*dissipator* vs. *dissipater*\): 1.4:1
