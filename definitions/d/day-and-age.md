# day and age

day and age. This folksy pairing, in widespread use since the late 1800s, appears most commonly in the phrases *in our day and age*, *in that day and age, in their day and age*, *in the modern day and age*, etc. Because in speech the *and* is often phonetically reduced to *'n'*, some people mishear it as *in*—and therefore write \**day in age*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*this day and age* vs. \**this day in age*\): 154:1
