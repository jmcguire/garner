# debonair

debonair (= suave, urbane, and confident), a term dating across World Englishes from the 1200s, has been predominantly so spelled in Modern English. The variant forms \**debonnaire* and \**debonaire*, though more in accord with Modern French, are simply by-forms of this long-ago-anglicized word.

 - Current ratio in print (*debonair* vs. \**debonnaire* vs. \**debonaire*): 28:1.4:1
