# divorcement

divorcement is now obsolete for *divorce* in the sense "the dissolution of a marriage," although it persisted in this sense up to the mid-1900s. For example, David O. Selznick directed Katharine Hepburn and John Barrymore in the 1932 film *A Bill of Divorcement*. But even with the legal term, *bill of divorce* has been standard since the 1700s.

*Divorcement* survives in the general figurative sense "the severance or complete separation of any close relation, esp. a business relation"—e.g.: "1941–42 . . . Justice Dept. pursues *divorcement* of theaters from studios." Keithe Collins, "Reeling in the Years," *Daily Variety*, 8 Mar. 2001, Special §, at 6.

 - Current ratio in print \(*a divorce* vs. *a divorcement*\): 2,642:1
