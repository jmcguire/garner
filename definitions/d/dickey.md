# dickey

dickey (= a false shirt front or collar worn under a shirt, sweater, suit, or dress), an adaptation of the nickname for *Richard*, dates in this sense from the early 1800s. It has been predominantly spelled *dickey* in all varieties of English since the mid-1960s. Before that the variant \**dicky*, and to a lesser extent \**dickie*, vied for dominance.

 - Current ratio in print (*starched dickey* vs. \**starched dicky*): 30:1
