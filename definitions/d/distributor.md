# distributor

distributor \(= \[1\] a company or person that supplies businesses and stores with goods; or \[2\] the part of an engine that sends an electric current to spark plugs\), an agent noun dating from the early 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1775. Before that, \**distributer* was the more usual spelling. During the 1800s, the *-or* spelling predominated moderately, and then about 1900 the rate of frequency diverged markedly as the *-er* spelling fell into obscurity. See -er \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*distributor* vs. \**distributer*\): 76:1
