# diarrheal

diarrheal \(= of, relating to, or involving diarrhea\), an adjective dating from the 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since about 1895. The competing spelling *diarrhoeal* has always predominated in BrE. Among the needless variants are \**diarrheic*, \**diarrhetic*, \**diarrhoeic*, and \**diarrhoetic*. Cf. **diuretic**.

Current ratio in print \(*diarrheal* vs. *diarrhoeal* vs. \**diarrheic* vs. \**diarrhetic* vs. \**diarrhoeic* vs. \**diarrhoetic* in World Englishes\): 451:127:57:22:10:1
