# dike

dike; **dyke**. *Dike* \(= \[1\] a wall or bank to keep back water and prevent flooding; or \[2\] a protective barrier or obstacle\) is so spelled in AmE—though it is *dyke* in BrE. On this point as on so many others, though, BrE is moving toward the AmE spelling: *finger in the dike* \(= a small action to prevent a catastrophe\) is the vastly preponderant spelling in both AmE and World Englishes, and it threatens to become so in BrE.

In AmE, *dyke* is also a dysphemism for *lesbian*. It is best abstained from if one seeks to avoid giving offense. See prejudiced and prejudicial terms.
