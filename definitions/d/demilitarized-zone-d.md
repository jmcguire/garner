# Demilitarized Zone (DMZ)

Demilitarized Zone (DMZ). This phrase—referring to any neutral territory between two hostile military powers where military installations are forbidden—is sometimes thought of as a prime example of doublespeak. Today we usually associate the phrase with the international border between North and South Korea. "Why do they call it Demilitarized Zone?" asked Christopher Hitchens in a 2014 speech. "Words lose their meaning so fast. It's the most militarized zone on the planet. There are nuclear mines sown in that DMZ. There's a concentration of force and violence on either side of it that is unequaled anywhere." The misnomer has been widely remarked in the literature of international relations and peacekeeping.
