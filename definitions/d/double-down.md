# double down

double down, a phrasal verb dating from the 1940s, derives from the card game blackjack. To *double down* in that game is to double the bet after you've seen the cards initially dealt, with the stipulation that you may draw one and only one additional card. In an extended sense, to *double down* is to reassert a risky position or to persist even more forcefully in a course of conduct that one has already embarked on. Since about 2000, the phrase has become a vogue term, especially during the presidency of Donald Trump, who was often characterized as doubling down on some policy that his detractors considered reckless or dangerous.
