# diseased

diseased; **deceased**. The first describes a body that is racked with illness, usually a bacterial or viral infection. The second describes a person or thing that has died. Although a *diseased* person may become *deceased*, it is surprising that either word would ever be misused for the other. It does happen, however, perhaps as a result of computer spell-checkers—e.g.:

 - "It is the only charity golf tournament that raises money for treatment of a *decease* \[read *disease*\] that affects African Americans." Charles L. Griggs, "Sickle Cell Tourney Got Me Involved," *Jacksonville Free Press*, 27 May 1998, at 5.

 - "But hypocrisy is a contagious *decease* \[read *disease*\], especially in the Clinton White House, and why should Richardson be immune?" Miguel Perez, "Opinion: Clinton's Appointees," *Record* \(N.J.\), 19 June 1998, at L11.
