# disincentivize

disincentivize, jargon for *discourage* or *deter*, became popular in the mid-1980s and remains so—e.g.: " ‘We're competing with Los Angeles and New York firms for talent,' Bochner said. ‘We don't want to *disincentivize* people from coming here because there are huge gaps in salary.' " Jessica Guynn, "Wilson Sonsini Opens Its Purse for Associates: $5,000 Raises," *S.F. Daily J.*, 12 Oct. 1995, at 2.

Whereas the clipped verb \**incent* is fairly well known—though far less common than *incentivize*—the negative \**disincent* is virtually unknown. See **incentivize**.
