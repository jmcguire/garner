# dyed-in-the-wool

dyed-in-the-wool (= having strong beliefs, convictions, or opinions that aren't subject to change) is a phrase that dates back to the 1300s in the literal sense of fabrics and to the 1500s in the metaphorical sense. Since the 1960s, the phrase has sometimes been mistakenly written \**died-in-the-wool.*

 - Current ratio in print (*a dyed in the wool* vs. *a died in the wool* \[necessarily searched, alas, without hyphens—even though the phrase preferably contains them\]): 19:1
