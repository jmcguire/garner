# *de rigueur*

*de rigueur*  \(= proper; required by custom or etiquette\) is sometimes misspelled *\*de rigeur* or *\*de riguer*—e.g.:

 - "If such affirmation of a transcendent order via the assertion of human dignity is *de rigeur* \[read *de rigueur*\] for tragedy, *Timon* does not qualify." Rolf Soellner, *Timon of Athens* 22 \(1979\).

 - "I was tired of absorbing abuse while paying premium prices and receiving less service and courtesy than are *de riguer* \[read *de rigueur*\] in most small towns." Susan D. Haas, "Surly Hosts Want Traveling Wallets, Not Real Visitors," *Morning Call* \(Allentown, Pa.\), 31 Aug. 1996, at A35.

The phrase is pronounced /dә ri-**gәr**/.

Language-Change Index

*de rigueur* misspelled *\*de rigeur* or *\*de riguer*: Stage 2
Current ratio in print \(*de rigueur* vs. *\*de rigeur*\): 9:1
