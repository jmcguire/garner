# dilettantish

dilettantish \(= having only a superficial dabbler's knowledge of a subject\), an adjective dating from the mid-1800s, has always been predominantly so spelled. \**Dilettanteish* is a variant that was moderately common from about the 1870s to the 1920s. A similar pattern held true, though over a longer span of years \(1850–1940\), for *dilettantism* and \**dilettanteism*.

Current ratio in print \(*dilettantish* vs. \**dilettanteish*\): 138:1   
Current ratio in print \(*dilettantism* vs. \**dilettanteism*\): 13:1
