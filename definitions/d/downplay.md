# downplay

downplay (= to de-emphasize), an Americanism dating from the 1950s, became a vogue term after about 1985 and remains so. Like the phrasal verb *play down* (dating from the 1860s), it's a casualism—e.g.: "Theiss also *downplayed* the importance of police groups altogether, saying many of them are ‘vintage labor unions' that typically back Democrats." Marc Lacey, "Sherman Endorsed by Police Groups," *L.A. Times*, 21 Sept. 1996, at B2.

## Language-Change Index

 - *downplay* for *play down*: Stage 5
 - Current ratio in print (*downplay* vs. *play down*): 4:1
