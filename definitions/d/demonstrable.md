# demonstrable

demonstrable /di-**mon**-strә-bәl/ is the word, not *\*demonstratable*, a needless variant—e.g.:

 - "On the issue of funding, there is a very strong, statistically *demonstratable* \[read *demonstrable*\] correlation between the quality of a public university and the extent to which it is supported by the state." G.A. Clark, "Low Pay for Professors Devastates State Schools," *Ariz. Republic/Phoenix Gaz.*, 29 Mar. 1995, Tempe Community §, at 4.

 - "Banks still want a solid track record of experience in the business field and, preferably, a *demonstratable* \[read *demonstrable*\] ability to turn a profit." Neil Orman, "Improved Lending Still Rides on Prospects for Big Returns," *Austin Bus. J.*, 13 Oct. 1995, § 1, at 18.

 - "In some cases, she said, she gives herself a challenge. For example, can she come up with a skill-and-action game that's fast-paced and *demonstratable* \[read *demonstrable*\] on TV?" Don Dodson, "Woman Creates Children's Games," *Courier* \(Lincoln, Ill.\), 25 May 2012, at 6.

See -able \(d\) & -atable.
