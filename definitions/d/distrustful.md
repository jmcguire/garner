# distrustful

distrustful; **mistrustful**. The difference here is subtle because both terms mean "having or showing doubt; lacking confidence" \(in someone or something\). But *distrustful* implies suspicion or wariness based on an informed judgment, whereas *mistrustful* suggests a more general uncertainty or uneasiness. So you might be *distrustful* of a used-car dealer's puffing, yet *mistrustful* of a stranger's advice. Note that both adjectives take the preposition *of*.
