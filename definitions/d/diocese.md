# diocese

diocese = a jurisdictional subdivision, esp. of (1) a bishop or (2) a province under control of the Roman Empire after Constantine. Today the word is best pronounced /**dī**-ә-sees/ or /-sis/, not /-seez/. The second pronunciation is dominant in BrE and is preferred by some AmE authorities. The third is recorded in *W3* but, in the words of one commentator, "has little authority." Charles Harrington Elster, *BBBM* at 150.

The plural is regular in form (*dioceses*) and is pronounced with four syllables: /**dī**-ә-sees-iz/ or /**dī**-ә-sis-iz/. But some speakers (most notably newscasters) mistakenly use the three-syllable variant /**dī**-ә-seez/ as a plural.

The adjectival form is *diocesan*, pronounced /dī-**os**-i-sin/ or /-zәn/.

For more on the term *diocese*, see **bishopric**.
