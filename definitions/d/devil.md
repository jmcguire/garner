# devil

devil, vb., meaning either (1) "to season highly" <deviled eggs> or (2) "to tease or annoy" <the kids deviled each other during the road trip>, dates from about 1800. For sense 2, the more usual word is *bedevil*. The AmE inflections are *deviled* and *deviling*; the BrE inflections are *devilled* and *devilling*. See spelling (c).
