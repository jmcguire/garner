# dominatrix

dominatrix /dom-i-**nay**-triks/ (= \[1\] a domineering woman; or \[2\] a woman who plays the sadistic role in sadomasochistic acts, usu. involving sex), a latinism dating in English from the 1500s, went through centuries of disuse until it was revived in sense 2 during the 1960s. The two plurals *dominatrices* and *dominatrixes* are both used, the former being a little more common—e.g.: "In 2008 . . . the now-defunct News of the World published secretly filmed video of Mosley being whipped and engaging in sex acts in a London flat with five *dominatrices*, one wearing a German Luftwaffe jacket and another in a striped prison-style uniform." "Max Mosley, 81" (obit.), *Record-J.* (Meriden, Conn.), 25 May 2021, at A5. See plurals (b) & sexism (d).

 - Current ratio in print (*dominatrices* vs. *dominatrixes*): 1.2:1
