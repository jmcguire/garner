# disrupter

disrupter \(= someone or something that prevents continuation in the usual way\), a term dating from the late 1880s, was predominantly so spelled until 1995 in AmE and 2007 in BrE—when *disruptor* suddenly gained predominance. What happened? In various *Star* *Trek* films, handheld weapons called "Klingon Disruptors" were used—and they are still sold online as "officially licensed Star Trek collectibles." Meanwhile, the *-or* spelling was adopted for medical innovations such as *endocrine disruptors*. So the spelling of the word, once thought to be settled on the analogy of *corrupter*, has been thrown into uncertainty. The new spelling *disruptor* has proved to be self-referential. See -er \(a\).
