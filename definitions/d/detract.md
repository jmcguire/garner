# detract

detract; **distract**. Although *detract* can be transitive \(meaning "to divert"\) as well as intransitive \(meaning "to take something away"\), the transitive use—by far less common—encroaches on territory better served by *distract*. So instead of using the phrase *\*detract attention from*, careful writers usually say *distract attention from* or just *distract from*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*distract attention from* vs. \**detract attention from*\): 6:1
