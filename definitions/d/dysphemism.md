# Dysphemism

Dysphemism /**dis**-fә-miz-әm/ = \(1\) the substitution of a disagreeable word or phrase for a neutral or even positive one; or \(2\) a word or phrase so substituted. Dysphemism is the opposite of euphemism. Examples usually fall into the realm of slang—e.g.:

 - accountant bean-counter
 - athlete \(dumb\) jock, bull neck
 - baker dough roller
 - bassoon farting bedpost
 - cadaver, corpse stiff
 - carpenter termite
 - Christian fundamentalist Bible-thumper, Bible-beater
 - cigarette cancer stick
 - civil servant paper-shuffler, paper-pusher
 - clarinet licorice stick
 - conservative reactionary
 - crawfish mudbug
 - detective dick, gumshoe
 - doctor pill-pusher, quack
 - environmentalist tree-hugger
 - estate tax, inheritance tax death tax
 - executive fatcat
 - fashion model clothes-peg
 - fraternity member frat rat, frat boy
 - intellectual egghead, nerd, bookworm
 - investigative journalist snoop, scoop
 - jailer screw
 - lawyer mouthpiece, shyster, pettifogger
 - liberal bleeding heart, granola head, pinko
 - Marine jarhead, grunt
 - mason mud dauber
 - mechanic grease monkey
 - newscaster talking head
 - newspaper \(paper copy\) dead-tree edition
 - newspaper editor inkstained wretch
 - office worker pencil-pusher
 - pilot flyboy
 - plumber turd herder

 - printer ink dauber
 - psychiatrist shrink, spook, wig picker

Many dysphemisms, of course, are hardly printable, as when the word *man* or *woman* is reduced in reference to the low word for a sexual organ. Most racist terms likewise illustrate the phenomenon of dysphemism.

Although they frequently appear in dialogue, dysphemisms rarely find a place in well-edited prose that is not intended to be jocular or to replicate casual speech. Cf. spin conjugations.
