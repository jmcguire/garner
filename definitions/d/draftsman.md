# draftsman

draftsman \(= \[1\] someone who draws architectural plans or machinery specifications; \[2\] an artist who excels in sketches or other drawings; or \[3\] someone who composes legal instruments or similar documents\), a term dating from the mid-1600s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since about 1911. But in BrE, senses 1 and 2 are predominantly spelled *draughtsman*—and always have been. In sense 3, *drafter* has become somewhat more frequent than *draftsman* just since 2000. See sexism \(c\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*legal drafters* vs. *legal draftsmen* vs. *legal draughtsmen* in World Englishes\): 17:8:1
