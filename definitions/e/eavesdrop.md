# eavesdrop

eavesdrop \(= to secretly listen to other people's conversation\), a verb dating from the early 1600s, derives from the practice in the Middle Ages or even before of standing under the eaves of a house or building with the purpose of overhearing what people inside are saying. The word is a back-formation from the much earlier agent noun *eavesdropper* \(1400s\). The verb is sometimes mangled into the malapropism \**ease* *drop*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*eavesdrop on* vs. \**ease drop on*\): 348:1
