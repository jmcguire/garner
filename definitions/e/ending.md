# end\(ing\)

end\(ing\), n. *End*, not *ending*, corresponds most idiomatically to the noun *beginning*. E.g.: "The turnover of the Sinai is a beginning, not an *ending* \[read *end*\]." In print sources since 1700, the phrase *beginning and end* has consistently appeared more frequently than *beginning and ending*—today by an almost 3:1 ratio. See parallelism.
