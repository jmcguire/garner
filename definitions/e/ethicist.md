# ethicist

ethicist; **\*ethician**. *\*Ethician* is more than two centuries older \(dating from the early 1600s\) and is therefore given precedence in many dictionaries. But *ethicist* overwhelmingly predominates in modern usage—being more than 250 times as common in print—so *\*ethician* is now a needless variant. E.g.: "George Annas, a Boston University medical *ethicist*, opposes the legislation and predicted it will never pass in Massachusetts." Liz Kowalczyk, "Curren Case Shows Need for Regulations," *Patriot Ledger* \(Quincy, Mass.\), 24 Aug. 1996, at 1. The matter was settled even more soundly when Randy Cohen's popular column, "The Ethicist," was established in *The New York Times Magazine* in February 1999.
