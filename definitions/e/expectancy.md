# expectancy

expectancy; **expectation**. Despite an overlap in actual use, the differentiation is clear-cut. *Expectancy* = that which is anticipated or forecast; something expected <a life expectancy of 70 years>. *Expectation* = the act or state of anticipating the occurrence of something <an expectation of high profits>. The most common error is to misuse *expectation* for *expectancy*—e.g.: "The impairment must adversely affect life *expectation* \[read *expectancy*\] and not just the quality of life." "Cash in the Magnificent 70s," *Fin. Times*, 19 July 1997, at 21. *Life expectancy* is a question of longevity; it's the usual phrase. *Life expectation\(s\)* would presumably relate to what you think you'll probably experience in life.
