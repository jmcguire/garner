# expresser

expresser \(= \[1\] someone who states an idea; \[2\] someone or something that presses \[a liquid, juice, etc.\] out from a gland, from pulp, etc.; or \[3\] someone who sends something by special means to arrive quickly\), an agent noun dating from the 1500s, is traditionally so spelled. From about 1940 to 2000, *expressor* came to rival and sometimes even surpass the -*er* spelling, especially in BrE. But since 2005, *expresser* has once again become slightly predominant in print. See -er \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*expresser* vs. *expressor*\): 1.2:1
