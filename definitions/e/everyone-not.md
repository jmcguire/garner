# \*everyone . . . not

\*everyone . . . not, in place of *not everyone*, is just as illogical as *\*all . . . not*. E.g.: "But if richness needs gifts with which *everyone is not endowed* \[read *not everyone is endowed*\], simplicity by no means comes by nature." W. Somerset Maugham, "Lucidity, Simplicity, Euphony," in *The Summing Up* 321, 322 \(1938\). \(Otherwise, the sense is that no one is endowed with gifts.\) See illogic \(b\). Cf. **all \(b\)**.

Language-Change Index

*\*everyone . . . not* for *not everyone*: Stage 2   
Current ratio in print \(*not everyone is* vs. *\*everyone is not*\): 6:1
