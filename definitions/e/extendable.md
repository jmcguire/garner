# extendable

extendable; **\*extendible**; **extensible**. Though dictionaries are split between *extendable* and \**extendible*, the *-able* spelling is much more natural-looking and much more common in everyday contexts <extendable ladder>. But *extensible*, surprisingly enough, has predominated for most of the period since 1800. It is especially common in intangible senses <extensible markup> and technical ones <extensible membranes>. See -able \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*extensible* vs. *extendable* vs. \**extendible*\): 6:3:1
Current ratio in print \(*extendable ladder* vs. *extensible ladder*\): 10:1
