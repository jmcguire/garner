# existential

existential; **existentialist**, adj. *Existential* (= \[1\] of, relating to, or involving existence, esp. continued existence; or \[2\] having corporeal or spiritual reality), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1600s, became a vogue term in the late 1900s and early 2000s <existential threat>. Although the term is sometimes used as the adjective corresponding to *existentialism* (= the philosophical belief that the world is without meaning and that the plight of the individual is to assume responsibility for exercising free will without any clear certainty about what is good or bad, right or wrong), that function is better served by *existentialist*. In short, the two adjectives have undergone differentiation.
