# email

email; **e-mail**; **E-mail**. From the early 1990s until about 2010, *e-mail* remained the prevalent form in print sources. The letter *e*—short for *electronic*—was sometimes capitalized, but that didn't last long. The unhyphenated *email*, though unsightly to many when first introduced, became so predominant in the 2010s that the hyphenated form soon came to seem quaint. The solid form was aided and abetted by tech companies that used it daily in billions of messages.

A few writers have objected to *email* as a shortened form of *email message* \<send me an email\>—and also to using it as a count noun \<I had three emails\>. Both these uses are now standard. Others have questioned whether *email* should function as a verb \<please email me with the answer\>, but that question has long since been answered affirmatively.

## Language-Change Index

 - *email* as a solid word: Stage 5
 - Current ratio in print (*email* vs. *e-mail*): 2.1:1
