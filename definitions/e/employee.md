# employe(e)

employe(e). Although *employé*, the French masculine form, might logically be thought to be better as a generic term, *employée* (which in French denotes the feminine gender) is so widespread—minus the accent mark, of course—as to be thoroughly established as standard. Although *The Wall Street Journal* and *U.S. News and World Report* were longtime holdouts for *employe*, a spelling that will strike many if not most readers as quirky. See -ee. See also **independent contractor**.

 - Current ratio in print (*employees* vs. *employes*): 737:1
