# exorbitant

exorbitant \(lit., "having departed or deviated from one's track \[*orbit*\] or rut"\) is sometimes mistakenly spelled *\*exhorbitant*—perhaps because it is confused with *exhort.* E.g.: "The developers and tenants, however, insist the costs are far from *exhorbitant* \[read *exorbitant*\]." Geeta Anand, "Mission Main Funds in Jeopardy," *Boston Globe*, 6 May 1997, at B1. See **exuberant \(a\)**.

## Language-Change Index

*exorbitant* misspelled *\*exhorbitant*: Stage 1
