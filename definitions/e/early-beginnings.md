# \*early beginnings

\*early beginnings is a redundancy that was especially common during the mid-20th century. By the 1990s it had fallen in frequency but still sometimes appeared—e.g.: "One of the nation's most skilled neurosurgeons, Dr. Carson candidly recalls his *early beginnings* \[read *beginnings* or *early days*\] in poverty." Joy Bennett Kinnon, "10 Who Beat Welfare," *Ebony*, Nov. 1996, at 166.
