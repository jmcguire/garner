# ensheath

ensheath \(= to put into a sheath—that is, a close-fitting cover or protective layer\), a verb dating from the 1500s, is a curious word. The spelling *ensheath* has always predominated over \**ensheathe*. Yet almost all English-language dictionaries, apart from the Oxford dictionaries, record the word exclusively as \**ensheathe*—doubtless from the influence of the synonym *sheathe*. Oxford has it right: standard verb forms, then, are *ensheath* and *sheathe*.

The spellings \**insheath* and \**insheathe* are variants.

Current ratio in print \(*to* *ensheath* vs. \**to* *ensheathe*\): 3:1
Current ratio in print \(*sheathed* vs. *ensheathed*\): 73:1
