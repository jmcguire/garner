# errata

errata. Like *addenda* and *corrigenda*, the plural form *errata* \(= errors; corrections to be made\) should be used only when listing more than one item. E.g.: "It is important to clarify some of its abundant *errata*." Letter of Mark A. Caldwell, "Flat Wrong," *Chattanooga Times*, 29 July 1995, at A4. See plurals \(b\).

If there is only one mistake \(rarely the case, of course\), the singular *erratum* is called for—e.g.: "Professor Esmond Wright, doyen of British Americanists and former holder of the chair of modern history at Glasgow University, insists his ‘embarrassing *erratum*' was totally unintentional." Rob Brown, "Apology as History Repeats Itself," *Scotland on Sunday*, 16 June 1996, at 3. The English plural *erratums* is not used. See **corrigendum**.

The pronunciation is /i-**rah**-tә/ or /i-**ray**-tә/—not /i-**rat**-ә/.
