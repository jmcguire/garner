# exemplary

exemplary has two almost contradictory connotations, but both deal with setting examples: *exemplary damages* \(better known as *punitive damages*\) make an example out of a wrongdoer, whereas *exemplary behavior* is model behavior. *Exemplary* is sometimes misunderstood as meaning "severe" in phrases such as *exemplary punishment*. The meaning is always that the accompanying noun should set an example for others' behavior.
