# elocute

elocute, meaning "to speak in a grand, affected manner," is a usually facetious back-formation from *elocution*—e.g.: "Simon Russell Beale plays Oswald, the afflicted Alving son. He's an actor who *elocutes* so well that talk no longer reveals character but effectively conceals it." Vincent Canby, "A Crowd Under the National's Umbrella," *N.Y. Times*, 12 June 1994, § 2, at 5. The verb dates from the early 1880s.
