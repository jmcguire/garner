# endear

endear, v.t., is properly a reflexive verb. President John F. Kennedy endeared himself to the press. Wanglers endear themselves to the people they'd like to manipulate. But in the following sentence, *endear* is used incorrectly as a regular transitive verb—as a near-synonym of *charm*—instead of as a reflexive: "Roberts, whose Columbo-like, disheveled personality has *endeared* \[read *endeared him to* or *charmed*\] reporters for decades—he once set his desk on fire with a cigarette—is likely to help morale." Larry Reibstein, "It's Back to the Future," *Newsweek*, 18 Apr. 1994, at 41.
