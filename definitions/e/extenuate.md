# extenuate

extenuate (= to lessen the seriousness of \[something bad\] by partial excuse) should apply only to the fault that is minimized, not the person who committed it <please don't extenuate our predicament>. The *OED* cites improper uses (so labeled) such as "The pursuer's steward . . . *extenuated* himself calmly enough," in which the word is used as if it meant "to extenuate the guilt of; to plead partial excuses for" (*OED*).
