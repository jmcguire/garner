# extortionate

extortionate; \***extortive**; **\*extortionary**; **\*extorsive**. *Extortionate* (= \[1\] given to or characterized by extortion; or \[2\] \[of prices\] exorbitant) has been the standard term since the late 1700s. The others are needless variants. E.g.:

 - "Phanor Arizabaleta, considered No. 5 in the \[Cali\] syndicate, was convicted and sentenced for *extorsive* \[read *extortionate*\] kidnapping Tuesday by a regional judge in Cali, the chief prosecutor's office said." "Datelines," *Deseret News* (Salt Lake City), 16 Apr. 1997, at A4.

 - "The alleged misconduct includes mismanagement of funds, *extortive* \[read *extortionate*\] conduct and abuse of power." Richard Marosi, "Police Officer Files Whistle-Blower Lawsuit Against the LAPD," *L.A. Times*, 9 Oct. 2002 (Home ed.), Cal. Metro §, pt. 2, at 3.

 - "But last Saturday, the first day the museum was open to the public, attendees paid an *extortionary* \[read *extortionate*\] fee for an experience that, for many, was probably not all that different from their first sexual encounter—lots of buildup, and just like that, it's over." Kevin Canfield, "That Empty Feeling After New York's MoSex," *Hartford Courant*, 10 Oct. 2002, at D1.

 - Current ratio in print (*extortionate* vs. \**extortive* vs. \**extortionary* vs. \**extorsive*): 115:4:3:1
