# euphuism

euphuism /**yoo**-fyoo-iz-әm/ derives from John Lyly's play entitled *Euphues* \(1578\), in which the characters speak in an affected, highly ornate style. The term now denotes a convoluted, embellished prose style. But it's sometimes misused for *euphemism*—e.g.:

 - "Twice, in 1987 and 1991, under duress to win at a more brisk clip, Brewer bought extra time by overhauling his staff—a time-honored *euphuism* \[read *ruse*\] for transferring blame." Jim Bailey, "Parker Doesn't See Red over Coaching Ups, Downs," *Ark. Democrat-Gaz.*, 16 July 1994, at C5. \(Because the writer refers to a nonverbal evasion, *euphemism* wouldn't be a good replacement. See double bobbles.\)

 - "Like many old Southerners, Rucker prefers gentlemanly *euphuisms* \[read *euphemisms*\] to mask life's brutal confrontations." Jim Schlosser, "Raising a Rail Ruckus," *News & Record* \(Greensboro\), 14 Apr. 1996, at E1.

 - "Most cases \[of Covid infections among those aged 20–39\] are attributed to ‘community contact'—a *euphuism* \[read *euphemism*\] for cases that can't necessarily be tracked." Jonathan Duncan, "Residents Asked to Take Public Health Covid-19 Survey," *Guelph Mercury Trib.* \(Can.\), 1 Dec. 2020, News §.

A close synonym is *gongorism* /**gong**-gә-riz-әm/, after the style of the Spanish poet Luis de Góngora y Argote \(1561–1627\). This word is only for those who dabble in sesquipedality.

Unlike its corresponding noun, the adjective *euphuistic* is rarely misused. Here it appears correctly:

 - "The virtuoso style mimics the sexual display, becoming a *euphuistic* romp through all the elaborate rhetorical tropes available to 16th-century prose—from alliteration, antithesis and anaphora to zeugma and chiasmus." Maureen Quilligan, "Glistening Torsos," *N.Y. Times*, 25 May 1986, § 7, at 16.

 - "Here we have a novel that could've been a modern Australian classic, but instead coherent and accessible storytelling has been eschewed for baroque and *euphuistic* self-seriousness." Sam Cooney, "Beware the Too-Wise Child," *Sydney Morning Herald* \(Aus.\), 3 July 2021, at 15.

For a similar error, see **euphemistic**.
