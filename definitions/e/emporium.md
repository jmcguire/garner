# emporium

emporium (= a large shop) predominantly made the plural *emporiums* in all varieties of English from about 1830. Around 1940, BrE predominantly adopted the Latinate *emporia*, and within a decade World Englishes followed suit. But by 1960, the homegrown plural *emporiums* had once again firmly established itself in AmE. So today it's *emporiums* in AmE and *emporia* in much of the rest of the world. See plurals (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*emporia* vs. *emporiums* in World Englishes): 1.4:1
