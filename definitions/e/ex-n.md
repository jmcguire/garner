# ex, n

ex, n., is a casualism in the sense "a former spouse or lover." The plural of *ex* is *exes*, and the possessive is *ex's*—but many readers find these forms odd-looking. George Strait's 1987 hit song had the unfortunate title "All My *Ex's* \[read *Exes*\] Live in Texas." A few websites reporting on the song tacitly correct the error.
