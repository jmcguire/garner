# emanate

emanate \(= to flow forth, issue, originate from\) has diverse uses. Sometimes the word refers to something physical—e.g.: "Inside, the hardwood floors gleamed and delicious aromas *emanated* from the kitchen." Jamie Marshall, "Charm on the Farm," *Town & Country*, Sept. 1996, at 49. At other times, though, the movement is figurative—e.g.: "All the more ironic was the fact that this fatuous rhetoric *emanated* from Chicago, of all places." "Educators or Lobbyists?," *Wash. Times*, 1 Sept. 1996, at B2.
