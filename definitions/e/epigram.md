# epigram

epigram; **epigraph**. Because these similar-sounding terms are similar in meaning, they are therefore often subject to word-swapping. *Epigram* = (1) a short, witty poem; or (2) a concise, pointed, and usu. clever saying. (Cf. **apothegm**.) *Epigraph* = (1) an inscription, esp. on a building or statue; or (2) a thematic quotation at the beginning of a book, chapter, etc. So although an *epigram* can constitute an *epigraph*, the reverse does not hold true.
