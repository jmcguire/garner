# emulable

emulable, not *\*emulatable*, is the traditional form of the adjective meaning "capable of being emulated." E.g.: "All I'm asking is that we take a look at the examples of behavior exhibited by Columbus and ask ourselves if they are justifiable and *emulatable* \[read *emulable*\]." Scott Lafee, "Take That Columbus\!," *San Diego Union-Trib.*, 6 Oct. 1991, at D1 \(misusing the word as if it meant "worthy of emulation"\). The form *\*emulatable* has been on the rise since about 1960. See -atable.

Language-Change Index

*\*emulatable* for *emulable*: Stage 4   
Current ratio in print \(*emulable* vs. *\*emulatable*\): 2.6:1
