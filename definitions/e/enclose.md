# enclose

enclose; **\*inclose**. The former spelling is now preferred in all senses. E.g.: "While parents are off shopping, children ages 3 to 8 can play in the *glassed-inclosed* \[read *glass-enclosed* or *glassed, enclosed*\] child-care center." Gregory J. Gilligan, "Kids Quarters," *Richmond Times-Dispatch*, 26 July 1993, Bus. §, at 1. See en-.

 - Current ratio in print \(*enclose* vs. \**inclose*\): 10:1
