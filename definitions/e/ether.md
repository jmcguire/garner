# ether

ether \(= \[1\] the upper part of the sky; \[2\] the space through which radio waves or computer signals travel; or \[3\] a clear volatile liquid used primarily as a solvent or, formerly, as an anesthetic\), a French/Latin loanword dating in English from the 1300s, is predominantly so spelled. **Aether* is a variant in sense 1.

 - Current ratio in print \(*moving through the ether* vs. *moving through the \*aether*\): 4:1
