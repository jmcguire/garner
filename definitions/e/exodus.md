# exodus

exodus means "a mass departure or emigration." Hence the common phrase *\*mass exodus* \(common since the 1930s\) is a redundancy, perhaps a venial one influenced by *mass migration*—e.g.: "On the streets, buses lined the curb, waiting for the *mass exodus* \[read *exodus*\] of fans after the game." Kevin Mayhood, "Loyal Indians Fans Soak Up Playoff Atmosphere," *Columbus Dispatch*, 4 Oct. 1995, at B2. As a corollary to that point, one person's leaving does not make an *exodus*—e.g.: "Jones' *exodus* \[read *exit*\] has sparked a discussion among university staffers and the deaf community over what went wrong with the training program." Lynn O'Shaughnessy, "Student Concern Helped to Oust Leader at CSUN Center for Deaf," *L.A. Times*, 27 Oct. 1985, § 2, at 8.
