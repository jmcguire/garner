# -ed/-'d

-ed/-'d. Although R.W. Burchfield says that the BrE preference is for past-participial adjectives such as *cupola'd arch*, *mustachio'd*, *shanghai'd sailor*, and *subpoena'd witness* \(*FMEU3* at 237\), the strong preference in AmE is for *cupolaed arch*, *mustachioed*, *shanghaied sailor*, and *subpoenaed witness*—and even *stockinged feet* \(although people tend to say *stocking feet*\). The past-tense *OK'd* is an exception \(though the spelled-out *okayed* eliminates the issue—see **okay**\). The forms *cc'd* and *bcc'd* are universally preferred.
