# epaulette

epaulette; **epaulet**. In the 1780s, English borrowed this word from French, in which the word is spelled *epaulette*. Through 1880, that was the predominant spelling in English as well, but from 1900 or so the feminine suffix was frequently dropped: *epaulet* vied with *epaulette* for about a century, during which time most American dictionaries gave priority to *epaulet*. Today, though, *epaulette* remains the more frequent spelling in AmE and BrE alike. *Epaulette* is accented on the last syllable, *epaulet* on the first.

 - Current ratio in print (*epaulette* vs. *epaulet*): 1.2:1
