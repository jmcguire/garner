# excuse me

excuse me; **pardon me**. Traditionally, *excuse me* was used for minor offenses such as bumping into someone; *pardon me* was reserved for more serious situations requiring a more explicit apology. Today, the terms are interchangeable, *excuse me* being only slightly less formal. *Sorry* alone is more casual.

When the word *me* receives the stress, either *pardon me* or *excuse me* is used either as a mild challenge of someone else or as a polite waving off of someone else's apology. When the first word is stressed in either phrase and an interrogative inflection is given, the sense is typically "What did you say?" A polite equivalent \(if delivered in a civil tone\) is *I beg your pardon*.
