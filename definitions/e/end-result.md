# end result

end result, a redundancy for *result,* rose to prominence over the course of the 20th century. Safire called it "redundant, tautological and unnecessarily repetitive, not to mention prolix and wordy." William Safire, "Peace-ese," *N.Y. Times*, 17 Nov. 1991, § 6, at 22. The only exception occurs when the writer needs to refer to *intermediate results* as well as *end results*. Cf. **final outcome**.
