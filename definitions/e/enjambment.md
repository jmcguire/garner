# enjambment

enjambment (= in poetry, the continuation of a line, couplet, or verse from one to the next without a noticeable pause) is the standard spelling—e.g.: "Traditional forms are reanimated through syncopated rhythms and line *enjambment*." Rita Dove, "Poet's Choice," *Wash. Post*, 2 Sept. 2001, at T12. *\*Enjambement* is a variant spelling common in the late 1800s but now archaic.

 - Current ratio in print (*enjambment* vs. *\*enjambement*): 5:1
