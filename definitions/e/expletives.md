# Expletives, Essay

Expletives. In general usage, *expletives* are understood to be curse words or exclamations. This sense was fortified in AmE during the Watergate hearings in the early 1970s, when coarse language was replaced with *expletive deleted* in transcripts of the White House tapes. In the rarefied world of grammarians, however, an *expletive* is a word having no special meaning but standing \(usually at the beginning of a clause\) for a delayed subject. The two most common expletives are *it* and *there* at the beginnings of clauses or sentences. See **it** & **there is**. See also **epithet \(c\)**.
