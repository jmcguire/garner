# ethereal

ethereal \(= \[1\] heavenly; \[2\] having no material existence; or \[3\] extremely delicate and light in a way that seems unreal\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled since the 1600s. **Etherial*, \**aethereal*, and \**aetherial* are variant forms. Of the choice between *ethereal* and \**etherial*, the *OED* notes: "The uncertainty of the spelling began in Latin, the orig. *aetherius* from the Gr\[eek\] being often written -*eus* after the ordinary Latin adj. ending, as in *ciner-eus*, *lign-eus*, etc."

 - Current ratio in print \(*ethereal* vs. \**etherial* vs. \**aethereal* vs. \**aetherial*\): 102:1.8:1.5:1
