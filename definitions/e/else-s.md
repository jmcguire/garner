# else's

else's. Possessive constructions such as *anyone else's* and *everyone else's*, common since the mid-1800s, are preferred to the obsolete constructions *\*anyone's else* and *\*everyone's else*. Although *whose else* is technically correct, modern usage prefers *who else's* by analogy to the forms made with *anyone* and *everyone*. *Who else's* has predominated over *whose else* in AmE since the 1960s and in BrE since the 1990s. See possessives \(i\).

Current ratio in print \(*anyone else's* vs. *\*anyone's else*\): 202:1
Current ratio in print \(*who else's* vs. *whose else*\): 1.5:1
