# epidemic

epidemic; **endemic**. A disease is an *epidemic* when it breaks out and rages in a community, only to subside afterward. A disease is an *endemic* when it constantly exists within a certain population or region. The latter term, which also applies to plants, is comparatively rare.

The words are sometimes used as adjectives. *Epidemic* = extremely prevalent; widespread. *Endemic* = native to a particular region or group. Cf. **pandemic**.
