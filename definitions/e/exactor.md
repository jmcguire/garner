# exactor

exactor; **exacter**. These terms are best kept distinct. The first is a noun denoting either a tax collector or someone who makes stringent or even oppressive demands <the exactor of onerous tasks>. The second is a comparative adjective meaning "more exact" <the exacter calculation is as follows>. Concededly, *exacter* sometimes takes on the oppressor sense, but it is less common \(and perhaps less exacting\) than *exactor*. Also, concededly, the periphrastic comparative *more exact* is commoner than *exacter*.
