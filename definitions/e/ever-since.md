# ever since

ever since. This is the standard phrase. *\*Every since* is AmE dialect—e.g.: "Her mother . . . took her to Jazzercise classes in Los Alamos and she's been a devotee *every since* \[read *ever since*\]—except for her high-school years when taking Jazzercise ‘wasn't cool.' " John Knoll, "Popular Dance Class Returns to Pojoaque," *Santa Fe New Mexican*, 31 July 2002, Pojoaque §, at 4.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*every since* for *ever since*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*ever since* vs. *\*every since*): 671:1
