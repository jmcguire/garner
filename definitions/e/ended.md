# ended

ended, p.pl.; **ending**, pr.pl. In phrases such as *the year ending* *31 December 1997*, some fiscal writers have pedantically insisted that *ending* should be *ended* if the year has already passed. The present participle, it is argued, should be used for future periods \<the week ending 7 March 2026\>, but not past ones. In those circumstances, the argument runs, the past participle is more accurate \<the week ended 14 September 1996\>.

But the correlative term *beginning* is used in analogous senses \<the week beginning 12 January 1992\>, and there is no valid objection to *ending*, whether the reference is to a future or past period.
