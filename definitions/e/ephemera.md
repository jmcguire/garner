# ephemera

ephemera (= a small cheap thing that is used in everyday life and has no lasting significance), a Latin/Greek loanword dating in English from the mid-1600s, is most often thought of as a plural ("small cheap things . . ."). Although some dictionaries list the variant plurals **ephemerae* and \**ephemeras* (exceedingly rare), the plural sense of *ephemera* is far more usual.

 - Current ratio in print (*these ephemera* vs. \**these ephemerae*): 27:1
