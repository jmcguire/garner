# exegesis

exegesis; **epexegesis**; **eisegesis**. *Exegesis* /eks-ә-**jee**-sis/, the usual term, denotes an explanation or exposition of a word, sentence, or passage. The other two are rare. *Epexegesis* = the addition of one or more words to convey more clearly the meaning implied by a text. *Eisegesis* /ī-sә-**jee**-sis/ = the interpretation of a word or passage by imbuing it with one's own ideas or biases. But surely *reading into*—or some like phrase—is clearer than *eisegesis*.

Each of these terms forms the plural by changing the final syllable to *-es*; hence *exegeses*, *epexegeses*, and *eisegeses*.
