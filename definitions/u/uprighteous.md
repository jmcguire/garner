# uprighteous

uprighteous, a 19th-century portmanteau word, combines *upright* and *righteous*—e.g.: "You may recall the uproar over Atlanta pitcher John Rocker and his lowly opinions of New York City and its inhabitants. It was hardly an original view, but he said it to a magazine reporter, and the *uprighteous* sky fell on him." Blackie Sherrod, "Athletes Can Swallow Their Feet," *Dallas Morning News*, 3 May 2001, at A19. Few dictionaries recognize it, but *W2* records it as an adjective and *W3* as an adverb \(*uprighteously*\). It's fairly rare today.
