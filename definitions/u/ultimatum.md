# ultimatum

ultimatum \(= a final proposal whose rejection will purportedly have serious consequences\) makes the plural *ultimatums*. The native plural *-ums* has long been considered preferable to the Latinate *\*ultimata*, which has been comparatively uncommon in print sources since about 1900. E.g.: "The 49ers president delivered an ultimatum to a town that doesn't respond to *ultimata* \[read *ultimatums*\]." Ray Ratto, "This Ultimatum Goes Against Team Policy," *S.F. Examiner*, 12 Feb. 1997, at D1. See plurals \(b\).

Current ratio in print \(*ultimatums* vs. \**ultimata*\): 14:1
