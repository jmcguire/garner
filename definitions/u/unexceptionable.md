# unexceptionable

unexceptionable; **unexceptional**. *Unexceptionable* (= not objectionable) is historically the far more usual term. E.g.: "The imperial Chinese went so far as to dub their country ‘the Middle Kingdom' so as to reflect what to them seemed an *unexceptionable* truth." Paul Campos, "Self-Absorption American-Style," *Rocky Mountain News* (Denver), 11 Dec. 2001, at A33.

*Unexceptional* (= not unusual) didn't come into common use until the mid-1800s. It now occurs with almost equal frequency. E.g.: "Outfielder Matt Lawdon is fine but *unexceptional*." Rod Beaton, "Yankees Land Their Men Again," *USA Today*, 14 Dec. 2001, at C7. See **exceptional (a)**.
