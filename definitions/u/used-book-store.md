# used-book store

used-book store. Preferably so written, not *used bookstore*. E.g.: "We all have this vision of the perfect *used bookstore* \[read *used-book store*\]: it seems to come out of a Dickens story, or maybe the film version of *The Old Curiosity Shop*." Ian C. Ellis, *Book Finds: How to Find, Buy, and Sell Used and Rare Books* 84 \(2006\). But since about 1985, instances of *used bookstore* are more frequent in print sources than the more logical *used-book store*—by a 6:1 ratio.

An apologist for the illogical phrasing might argue that it involves hypallage: it's a bookstore specializing in used stock. Then again, think about the ambiguity in its antonym: *new bookstore*.

In the U.K., you're more likely to encounter *secondhand bookshop*.

## Language-Change Index

*used bookstore* for *used-book store*: Stage 2
