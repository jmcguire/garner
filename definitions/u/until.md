# until

until. In the phrase *\*up until*, the *up* is superfluous, though it's common in speech. Use either *until* or *up to*—e.g.:

• "*Up until* \[read *Until*\] about 30 years ago, Sisters of Mercy were the teachers; today, lay teachers dominate." Kym Soper, "St. James School Celebrating 75th Anniversary," *Hartford Courant*, 7 Aug. 1997, at 1.

• "*Up until* \[read *Until*\] quite recently, this was accepted as what's called Life." Henrietta Thompson, "Trends," *Bus. Life*, July–Aug. 2017, at 9.

See **till**.
