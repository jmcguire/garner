# unconscionably

unconscionably \(= unreasonably, unscrupulously, outrageously\) is sometimes misused for *unconsciously* or *unselfconsciously*—e.g.: "Educated speakers who *unconscionably* \[read *unconsciously* or *unselfconsciously*\] say ‘It is *me*' generally shy away from ‘It is *him*,' ‘It is *her*,' ‘It is *us*,' and the like." Norman Lewis, *Better English* 186 \(rev. ed. 1961\) \(in which the esteemed author argues that "It is me" is "established, acceptable English"\). See **conscionable**.

## Language-Change Index

*unconscionably* misused for *unconsciously*: Stage 1
