# ukase

ukase \(/yoo-**kays**/ or /**yoo**-kays/\), originally a Russian term, meant literally "a czar's decree or edict having the force of law." By extension it has come to mean "any proclamation or decree, esp. of a final or arbitrary nature." E.g.: "Robert Landauer, former editorial page editor of The Oregonian, says citizens see the planning as growing out of a participatory process and not from *ukases* issued by professional planners." E.J. Dionne, "Portland Has Cut Through Obstacles to Make Urban Planning Work," *News & Record* \(Greensboro\), 1 June 1997, at F4.
