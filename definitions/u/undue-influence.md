# undue influence

undue influence \(= \[1\] someone's improper use of power or trust so as to coerce another to do something that he or she wouldn't otherwise do; or \[2\] any disproportionate effect that one thing has on another\), a phrase dating from the early 1700s, is so spelled. The blunder \**undo influence* emerged in the 1950s and spread during the 1970s and 1980s. E.g., "This would be the *undo influence* \[read *undue influence*\] a great master's work would have when compared to cheap second-rate commercial work." Adrienne Rebecca Borisy, *The Points and Counterpoints of Art* 44 \(1977\). The error is more likely in AmE \(in which *undue* and *undo* are typically homophones\) than in BrE \(in which *undue* is normally pronounced /әn-**dyoo**/\).
