# quitclaim

quitclaim \(= the formal release of a potential right, esp. of any claim to ownership\) often appears in the legal phrase *quitclaim deed*. The word is occasionally mangled into *\*quick claim* \(a mondegreen that sprang up in the 1950s\)—e.g.:

 - "Adult children also abuse their elderly parents by gaining control over their money. The parents sign over their home via a *quick-claim* \[read *quitclaim*\] deed, the children put them in a nursing home and then leave the area with the proceeds from the sale of the home." Steve Heisler, "Women Reach Out to Those Left Alone," *Sarasota Herald-Trib.*, 10 Oct. 2007, at BM4.

 - "The board approved Caron to execute the land swap through a *quick claim* \[read *quitclaim*\] deed." "School Approves Land Swap," *Hartford Courant*, 5 Jan. 2008, at B2.

 - "Cascade County commissioners approved a resolution Tuesday allowing mortgages, *quick claim* \[read *quitclaim*\] deeds, power of attorney, land transfers, condominium bylaws, subdivision covenants and other documents to be posted on the county's Web site." Karl Puckett, "County Will Begin Posting Documents on Web Site," *Great Falls Trib.* \(Mont.\), 10 Sept. 2008, at M1.

See mondegreens.
