# quadriceps

quadriceps. While the correct term for these thigh muscles is *quadriceps* in both singular and plural forms, it is common when writing of a single muscle to drop the *s*—e.g.: "Brown had no update on the condition of kick returner Josh Davis, who suffered a *quadricep* \[read *quadriceps*\] injury against Missouri." Eric Olson, "NU's Ball-Control Keyed Win," *Omaha World-Herald*, 14 Oct. 2002, at C1. It's better to stick with the singular form *quadriceps*, by far the more commonly used form. But given the acceptance of *bicep* as a singular, *quadricep* probably can't be far behind. Cf. **bicep**, **pecs** & **triceps**.

 - Current ratio in print (*left quadriceps* vs. *\*left quadricep*): 9:1
