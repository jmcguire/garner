# quoth

quoth, an archaism, appears chiefly in historical contexts or jaunty prose. The word is always part of an inverted phrase. That is, *quoth* precedes its subject—e.g.: "*Quoth* the Ravens, ‘Charge ever more.' The average ticket price for the new \[Baltimore\] Ravens is $40.05, compared to $32.61 last year when the team was the Cleveland Browns." Jerry Stack, "Morning Briefing: Eye Openers," *St. Louis Post-Dispatch*, 12 Aug. 1996, at C2. \(That sentence is, of course, a play on Edgar Allan Poe's famous line, "Quoth the Raven, ‘Nevermore.' "\) See inversion.

The word should be used only in the past tense \(for "said"\). The present-tense verb *\*quethen* is obsolete.
