# median

median; **medium**. These words occasionally get confounded in two ways. First, *medium* \(= midrange\) sometimes displaces *median* \(= midpoint\) in set phrases such as *median income*—e.g.: "The gap between the *medium* \[read *median*\] incomes of white families versus black families is wide." Les Payne, "What Would King Say?," *Newsday* \(N.Y.\), 19 Jan. 2003, at A28. Second, *medium* is sometimes substituted for *median* in its physical manifestation as a roadway divider—e.g.: "Angela carries the greatest guilt, seeing this truck driver's face as he flew over the highway *medium* \[read *median*\], and the faces of the drivers in the oncoming cars. If he had not swerved to miss her, she would be the one in the obituary." Adair Lara, "Fellow Drivers Are Not Your Enemies," *S.F. Chron.*, 19 Mar. 1996, at E8. For more on *median*, see **mean**, n.
