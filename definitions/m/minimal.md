# minimal

minimal; **minimum**, adj. Both words are used adjectivally. *Minimal* is always an adjective, and *minimum* is used as an attributive noun in phrases such as *minimum wage*. The words mostly overlap, but in one sense there is a distinction: *minimal* may or may not be absolute, but *minimum* always is. *Minimum* = the least possible, practical, legal, etc. <the minimum age to serve as President is 35>. *Minimal* = (1) the least; or (2) few, little <win with minimal effort> <enact over minimal opposition>. Some authorities object to the use of *minimal* in a nonabsolute sense; presumably if they had their way a *minimalist* musical composition would consist of one note played one time on one instrument. Cf. **maximum**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *minimal* in reference to a small amount (e.g., *a minimal amount of salt*): Stage 5
