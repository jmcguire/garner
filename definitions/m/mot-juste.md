# mot juste

mot juste /moh **zhoost**)/ (= just the right word or phrase) raises three usage issues. First, the plural is *mots justes*, just as in French. (See plurals (b).) Second, the singular, as a naturalized gallicism, takes the English article *the*, not the French article *le* \<struggled to find the mot juste\>. Third, the phrase is preferably not italicized.

1948 ratio in print (*the mot juste* vs. *le mot juste*): 3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*the mot juste* vs. *le mot juste*): 1.1:1
 - Current ratio in print (*find the mot juste* vs. *find le mot juste*): 1.7:1
