# modernly

modernly (= in modern times), formerly common as a sentence adverb, is accurately described by the *OED* as being "now rare." Today (not to say modernly), it can usually be replaced by *today* or *now*—e.g.: "Brick sidewalks line the legendary Great Stage Road, *modernly* \[read *now*\] known as Main Street." Joanne Anderson, "Jonesborough, Tenn., Is the Town," *Roanoke Times*, 25 Sept. 1994, at F8. See adverbs (f).
