# medulla oblongata

medulla oblongata (= the broadened upper spinal cord that forms the lowest part of the brain and that controls involuntary vital functions such as breathing and circulation), a Latin loan dating in English from the 1600s, has always predominantly formed the Latinate plural *medullae oblongatae*. Anglicized plurals (such as *medulla oblongatas* or \**medullas oblongata*) find little if any evidence in print sources. See plurals (b); see also loanwords (a).
