# make do

make do \(= to manage with what happens to be available, however inadequate it may be\) is sometimes erroneously written *\*make due*—e.g.:

• "The Bulls will have to *make due* \[read *make do*\] until Rodman returns to the lineup Saturday night." Terry Armour, "Bulls Heed Call for All Rebounders," *Chicago Trib.*, 12 Dec. 1996, Sports §, at 6.

• "The state is trying to *make due* \[read *make do*\] by asking the State Patrol to pay particular attention in the area to driving that can cause accidents." Kery Murakami, "When a Single Driver Can Use HOV Lane," *Seattle Post-Intelligencer*, 13 Jan. 2003, at B1.

• "As much as I treasure Roberto and the kids, living free of the hierarchy, we're *making due* \[read *making do*\] with a loose network of other runaways, outcasts, antisocials." Cynthia Leitich Smith, *Tantalize* 45 \(2007\).

For more on this type of verb, see phrasal verbs.
