# mogul

mogul (= \[1\] any of the Mongolian conquerors of India or their descendants; or \[2\] someone with immense power and influence within a particular industry), a Persian word dating in English from the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled from the beginning. In sense 2, *mogul* is the exclusive spelling \<movie mogul\>. But in the ethnographic sense, the variant forms *mughal*, \**moghul*, and \**moghal* have sometimes occurred, especially in BrE. Since 1950, the trend has been to use *mughal* (usually capitalized) in the ethnographic sense and *mogul* in the extended sense 2.

The skiing term *mogul* (= a bump on a ski slope) is an unrelated germanicism.

 - Current ratio in print (*Indian Mughal* vs. \**Indian Moghul* vs. \**Indian Mogul*): 8:1.3:1
