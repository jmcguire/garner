# mental attitude

mental attitude is a common redundancy that arose in the late 1800s. The phrase might have made good sense when it was common to think of a person's *physical attitude* \(i.e., posture and carriage\), but those days are no more. E.g.: "Positive *mental attitude* \[read *attitude*\] will be the topic addressed by former University of Tennessee basketball coach Ray Mears." "State Mental Health Consumers' Group Meets Here Saturday," *Knoxville News-Sentinel*, 17 Apr. 1997, at B4.
