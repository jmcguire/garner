# metropolis

metropolis; **metropole**. *Metropolis* (= \[1\] a large city that is the most important in its geographic region; \[2\] any large, bustling city), a Greek/Latin loanword, dates from the late 1300s but wasn't widely used until the 1500s. *Metropole*, a mostly synonymous French borrowing, dates from about 1500 in overlapping senses. But in the early 1800s, it acquired the sense "a mother country"—which amounts to useful differentiation. It also occasionally bears the sense "a luxury hotel."

The plural of *metropolis* is *metropolises* /mә-**trop**-ә-lә-sәz/. The plural of *metropole* is *metropoles* /**me**-trә-pohlz/.
