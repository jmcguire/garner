# marquetry

marquetry \(= decorative woodwork in which colored pieces of wood, shell, or ivory are laid together in patterns and applied to a board that becomes part of a piece of furniture or other collectible\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been predominantly so written since the mid-1700s. The gallicism \**marqueterie* \(actually the etymon of the anglicized form\), though usual in French, is but a variant in English.

Current ratio in print \(*marquetry* vs. \**marqueterie*\): 7:1
