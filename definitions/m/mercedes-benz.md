# Mercedes-Benz

Mercedes-Benz. The plural is *Mercedes-Benzes*, but some writers wrongly leave off the *-es*—e.g.: "And last week that élite group of electors arrived at the Hong Kong Convention Center in a procession of Rolls-Royces and *Mercedes-Benz* \[read *Mercedes-Benzes*\] to cast their ballots for a winner anointed long before." Sandra Burton, "Beijing's Capitalist," *Time*, 23 Dec. 1996, at 47.

But what if the name is shortened, as it so frequently is, to *Mercedes*? The plural is then *Mercedeses*—e.g.: "He is so wild about his car, Young says, that sometimes when he stops other *Mercedeses* along the highway, ‘I check the interiors, the dash.' " Laura Blumenfeld, "Gentlemen, Start Your Imaginations," *Wash. Post*, 30 Dec. 1996, at D1. Although it is true that many people say *Mercedes* as a plural, this usage flouts the age-old rule for pluralizing names. See plurals \(f\).
