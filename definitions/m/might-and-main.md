# might and main

might and main (= all the power, intensity, and effort of which someone is capable), an alliterative phrase dating from about 950 in the Old English period, typically appears in the emphatic phrase *with all one's might and main*. Other than in this phrase, *main* is archaic to denote force or physical strength (the same sense in this context as *might*). Since the mid-20th century, the phrase has occasionally been misprinted \**might and mane*, as if it had something to do with horses or lions.

 - Current ratio in print (*might and main* vs. \**might and mane*): 1,535:1
