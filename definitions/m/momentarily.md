# momentarily

momentarily. Strictly speaking, this word means "for a moment," not "in a moment." But the latter sense is now widespread, and the word has therefore become occasionally ambiguous—e.g.: "I'll be able to talk with you *momentarily*." When the latter sense is meant, it is much clearer to say, "I'll be able to talk with you in a moment." Cf. **presently**.
