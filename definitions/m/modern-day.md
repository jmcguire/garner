# modern-day

modern-day, a phrase that spread dramatically after 1950, is invariably inferior to *modern*—e.g.:

 - "\[In\] Walt Disney's syndicated kid show ‘Gargoyles,' . . . animated 1,000-year-old stone creatures are transplanted to *modern-day* \[read *modern*\] Manhattan to ward off evil-doers." Cathy Hainer, "Good-Guy Gargoyles Ringing Up Big Sales," *USA Today*, 17 Mar. 1995, at D1.

 - "These *modern-day* \[read *modern*\] pioneers are the homeschool parents." Joe Rohner, "Free-Market Principles Should Apply to Education as Well," *Idaho Statesman*, 19 Apr. 1995, at A9.
