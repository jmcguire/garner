# marsala

marsala; **masala**. The term *marsala*, deriving from the name of the town in Sicily, denotes a fortified Sicilian wine often used in cooking \<chicken marsala\>. In English, it has been used in this meaning since the early 1800s. The term *masala*, surprisingly enough even older in English (dating from the late 1700s), denotes any of various blends of spices used in Indian cookery \<chicken tikka masala\>. Though rarely confounded, the terms have indeed been known to induce word-swapping.

 - Current ratio in print (*garam masala* \[Indian\] vs. \**garam marsala*): 99:1
