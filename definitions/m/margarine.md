# margarine

margarine \(the butter-like substance used in or with various foods\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1830s, has always been predominantly so spelled. **Margarin* is a variant. For pronouncing the word, H.W. Fowler wanted a hard /g/: /**mah**-gә-rin/. \(*FMEU1* at 343.\) But no well-adjusted American says it that way, and few British speakers do today. It's /**mahr**-jә-rin/ in AmE and /mah-jә-**reen**/ in BrE.

Current ratio in print \(*margarine* vs. \**margarin*\): 309:1
