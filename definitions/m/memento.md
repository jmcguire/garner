# memento

memento. So spelled—not *\*momento*. The misspelling, which has lurked in the language since the 1600s, was never widely used until the late 1900s. The etymology has to do with the Latin verb *meminisse* "to remember." It has nothing to do with *moment*. Yet *memento* outranges *\*momento* in AmE by only a 2-to-1 margin and in BrE by only a 3-to-1 margin. Pl. *mementos*. \(See plurals \(d\).) The word is pronounced /mә-**men**-toh/—not /moh-**men**-toh/.
