# mumblety-peg

mumblety-peg; **\*mumbly-peg**; **\*mumble-peg**; **\*mumble-the-peg**; **mumbledy-peg**. These terms denote an old game in which the players try to flip or throw a knife so that the blade sticks into the ground. In early versions, dating back to the 1600s, the loser had to use his teeth to pull out a peg that had been driven into the ground. The phrase \**mumble-peg* predominated for most of the 1800s; \**mumble-the-peg* then became the predominant form from about 1875 to 1920, after which time *mumblety-peg*, with its reduced internal syllable, established itself as the standard spelling.
