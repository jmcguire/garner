# mischievous

mischievous /**mis**-chә-vәs/ is so spelled and pronounced. *\*Mischievious* is an impishly common misspelling and mispronunciation /mis-**chee**-vee-әs/—e.g.:

 - "I could not imagine them driving, getting mouthy, moody or *mischievious* \[read *mischievous*\], let alone going to drinking parties at the homes of friends whose parents were out of town." Eleanor Mallet, "The Tranquility of School Age," *Plain Dealer* \(Cleveland\), 25 Feb. 1995, at E1.

 - "Mayan Indians considered this place hell's fun house, inhabited by *mischievious* \[read *mischievous*\] gods who had to be soothed with heaping food bowls and the occasional human sacrifice." Judith Wynn, "Lodge Guests Settle In Among Tropical Wildlife," *Boston Herald*, 26 Dec. 2002, at 38.

See pronunciation \(b\). Cf. **grievous**.
