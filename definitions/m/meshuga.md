# meshuga

meshuga /meh-**shoo**-gә/ (= crazy, mad, insane), a Yiddishism dating in English from the 1880s, has been predominantly so spelled since the early 1990s. Throughout the 1980s until 1993, \**meshugge* was the usual spelling. Other variants include \**meshuggah*, \**meshugah*, \**meshugga*, and \**meshuggeh*.

A crazy or foolish person is known as a *meshuggener* (dating from 1900), for which a variant spelling is \**meshugener*.

 - Current ratio in print (*meshuga* vs. \**meshugge* vs. \**meshuggah* vs. \**meshugga* vs. \**meshugah* vs. \**meshuggeh*): 13:8:6:3:3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*meshuggener* vs. \**meshugener*): 1.7:1
