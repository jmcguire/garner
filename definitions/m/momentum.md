# momentum

momentum. Pl. *momentums* or *momenta*. Though you might understandably consider the latter a pretension, in fact it has always predominated in technical writing—e.g.: "Heisenberg proposes that these ‘particles' are in reality a field of potential masses and *momenta*." Karen G. Evans, "Chaos as Opportunity," *Pub. Admin. Rev.*, Sept.–Oct. 1996, at 491. See plurals (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*momenta* vs. *momentums*): 22:1
