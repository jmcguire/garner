# maybe

maybe.

**A. And *perhaps*.** The adverb *maybe* (= possibly, perhaps) began life in Middle English as a shortened form of *it may be*. It wasn't a standard fixture in literary English until the 1800s. The somewhat newer *perhaps*, dating from Early Modern English, has long been the more frequent word in published prose—and, unlike *maybe*, wasn't considered dialect in the period from the 1500s through the 1700s.

 - Current ratio in print (*perhaps* vs. *maybe* for comparative purposes merely): 1.9:1

**B. And *mayhap*, *peradventure*, *perchance*.** Among these archaic synonyms of *maybe* and *perhaps*, the words *peradventure* (ca. 1300) and *perchance* (ca. 1350) are the oldest. But *mayhap* (ca. 1533) is the most frequent of these archaisms today. Even so, it occurs once for every 202 times *maybe* appears in print and once for every 467 times that *perhaps* appears.
