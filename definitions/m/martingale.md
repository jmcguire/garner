# martingale

martingale (= \[1\] a forked strap attached to a horse's bit or noseband for holding down the horse's head; \[2\] on a sailing vessel, the lower stay for a jib boom; or \[3\] in gambling, a method for doubling or otherwise increasing one's wager after every loss), a French loanword dating in English from the late 1500s, has always been so spelled in AmE and BrE alike. **Martingal* is a variant that was in use especially from 1790 to 1880.

 - Current ratio in print (*martingale* vs. \**martingal*): 1,297:1
