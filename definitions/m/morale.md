# morale

morale; **moral**, n. H.W. Fowler spent a page and a half discussing the difficulties with these words \(*FMEU1* at 361–62\), but today problems are rarely encountered. *Morale* = the mental or emotional condition of a person or group, esp. as exhibited by confidence, discipline, or zeal; spirit. *Moral* = \(1\) the lesson of a fable, story, etc.; or \(2\) \(pl.\) principles of right and wrong. For more on *morals*, see **ethics \(b\)**.

Current ratio in print \(*the moral of the story* vs. *\*the morale of the story*\): 148:1
