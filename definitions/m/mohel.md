# mohel

mohel /**moh**-\[h\]әl/ \(= a person who performs the ritual of Jewish circumcisions\) is a Hebrew word with the alternative plurals *mohels* \(anglicized\), \**mohelim*, and \**mohalim*. Although the word came to be used in English as early as the mid-1600s, the contest among the plurals didn't arise until the mid-20th century. And it was a close contest from the 1930s through the mid-1990s, but since then *mohels* has established itself as the predominant plural. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*mohels* vs. \**mohalim* vs. \**mohelim*\): 2.4:1.2:1
