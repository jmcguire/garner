# moneyed

moneyed; **\*monied**. The first has been standard in all varieties of English since the mid-1800s—e.g.:

 - "There is a spot of trouble here in Cheever country \[Greenwich, Conn.\], the suburban promised land where *moneyed* executives ride the train to Manhattan." Blaine Harden, "Nonresident Sues Over Park Access Litigation," *L.A. Times*, 7 Sept. 1997, at A12.

 - "Bailey came from a *moneyed* family." Jeffrey Toobin, "Candide," *New Yorker*, 16 Dec. 2002, at 42, 44.

Cf. **honeyed**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*moneyed* vs. \**monied*\): 3:1
