# merchandise

merchandise; \***merchandize**. The first is standard. The second is a misbegotten verb and noun. *\*Merchandize* emerged as a variant in both the noun and verb senses during the 1600s and flourished until the mid-1800s. Today, it's an anomaly as either part of speech—e.g.: "The goal should be to make it harder for criminals to pay or receive money for stolen *merchandize* \[read *merchandise*\], he said." Jaikumar Vijayan, "The Identity Underworld," *Christian Science Monitor*, 6 May 2015. See -ize.

 - Current ratio in print (*merchandise* vs. *\*merchandize*): 52:1
 - Current ratio in print (*merchandisers* vs. *\*merchandizers*): 35:1
