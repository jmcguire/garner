# \*muchly

\*muchly is nowadays considered a jocular nonword—though several centuries ago it was not so stigmatized. *Much* is the preferred form in all adverbial contexts. But *\*muchly* occasionally finds its way into print—e.g.:

 - "More of this would have been *muchly* \[read *much*\] appreciated." Jennifer Barrs, "Roy Clark Still Strong on Strings," *Tampa Trib.*, 1 Mar. 1996, Florida/Metro §, at 4.

 - "*Thanks muchly* \[read *Thank you*\] to Rafael Palmeiro, whom the Cubs traded because he showed little power potential." Bob Verdi, "Ah, a Ballpark with Warmth—and People," *Chicago Trib.*, 11 Sept. 1996, Sports §, at 1. \(For more on *thanks much*, see **thank you**.)

 - "Merl is survived by his father and one brother, both named Sam. He's already *muchly* \[read *deeply* or *sorely*\] missed." Vic Fleming, "Man of His Words," *Ark. Democrat-Gaz.*, 28 Aug. 2015, at 17.

For other adverbs with a superfluous *-ly*, see adverbs \(d\).

## Language-Change Index

*\*muchly* for *much*: Stage 1
