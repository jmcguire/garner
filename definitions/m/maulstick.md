# maulstick

maulstick (= a long, light stick that painters hold in their nondominant hand to rest and steady the brush hand while at work), a Dutch loanword dating in English from the mid-1600s, has been variously spelled since the word first appeared. Since 1800 the two competing forms have been *maulstick* (predominant during the 1800s) and *mahlstick* (common in the 1900s, but usually in close competition). Neither one can be said to be etymologically superior, since both amount to a phonetic approximation of the Dutch word *maalstok*. Today, *maulstick* predominates in AmE, *mahlstick* in BrE.

 - Current ratio in print (*maulstick* vs. *mahlstick* in AmE): 1.5:1
 - Current ratio in print (*mahlstick* vs. *maulstick* in BrE): 1.1:1
