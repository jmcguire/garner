# mad

mad. Although this word has various nuances, the two primary senses are \(1\) "demented, insane," and \(2\) "angry." Unfortunately, sense 2—though predating Shakespeare and actually used by him—became stigmatized during the early 20th century as somehow substandard. The stigma should never have attached. The word is less formal than *angry*, *ireful*, *wrathful*, or *\*wroth*, but it's perfectly acceptable and has been for centuries. Even so, it is much more common in AmE than in BrE.
