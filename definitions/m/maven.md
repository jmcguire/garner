# maven

maven \(= an expert of wide experience and profound knowledge, esp. one who proclaims that fact\), a Yiddish loanword dating in English from about 1940, derives from the Yiddish word *meyvn*. From 1940 to 1980, the two spellings *maven* and \**mavin* competed closely for dominance. But by 1980 the sharp separation in frequency of occurrence had begun, and today the variant \**mavin* is rarely encountered.

 - Current ratio in print \(*maven* vs. \**mavin*\): 149:1
