# manifold

manifold; **manyfold**. *Manifold* /**man**-ә-fohld/ means "many and varied" <the reasons are manifold>, or \(as a noun\) a multichannel air chamber such as the one in an automotive engine <exhaust manifold>. *Manyfold* /**men**-ee-fohld/ applies to something multiplied many times <a manyfold increase>.

*Manifold* is occasionally misspelled with a *y*—e.g.: "Concept cars, or dream cars, as they were once known, are wondrous and magical creations. The reason for their existence is *manyfold* \[read *manifold*\]." Arv Voss, "Chrysler's Concept Cars Depict Real Intent," *Wash. Times*, 19 Feb. 1999, at E4. \(Given the automotive context, however, even the correct spelling would look odd there.\)
