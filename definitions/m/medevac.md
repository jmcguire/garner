# medevac

medevac /**med**-i-vak/ \(= the evacuation of people to provide them with medical treatment, esp. by helicopter and usu. during a war, natural disaster, or other emergency\), an American portmanteau word coined in the 1960s, consists of the beginnings of two words: *medical* and *evacuation*. The less-pleasing variant \**medivac*, never as common, takes more from *medical* and only the second syllable of *evacuation*.

The term can also function as a verb: to *medevac* someone is to transport the person by medevac helicopter. \(As there, the word can function as an adjective as well.\) On the analogy of *havocking* and *politicking*—not to mention *frolicking*, *mimicking*, *panicking*, and *trafficking*—one would expect the verb to be inflected as *medevacked* and *medevacking*. But since 2000, those forms haven't been as common as the anomalous spellings *medevaced* and *medevacing*. Snoots might be justified in sticking to *medevacked* and *medevacking* \(the latter form not even registering in Google's ngrams\). But given their predominance today, *medevaced* and *medevacing* cannot justly be called errors.

Current ratio in print \(*medevac* vs. \**medivac*\): 7:1   
Current ratio in print \(*medevaced* vs. *medevacked*\): 3:1
