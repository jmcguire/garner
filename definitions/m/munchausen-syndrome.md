# Munchausen syndrome

Munchausen syndrome \(= a psychological disorder involving the feigning of illness or injury in order to receive medical attention or treatment\) is so spelled even though the eponym was Karl Friedrich Hieronymus von Münchhausen \(1720–1797\), with the -*hh*- in his name. He was legendary for his hyperbolic accounts of his exploits. The variant form with a possessive, \**Munchausen's syndrome*, is much less common.

A related condition, in which the disorder expresses itself vicariously, is *Munchausen syndrome by proxy*, denoting a form of child abuse. *Black's Law Dictionary* defines it as "a psychological disorder that induces a caregiver, usu. a parent, to fabricate or cause a child's medical problem and to seek medical treatment for the child on the basis of the fabrications or problem" \(*Black's Law Dictionary* 1222 \[11th ed. 2019\]\).

Current ratio in print \(*Munchausen syndrome* vs. \**Munchhausen syndrome*\): 37:1
Current ratio in print \(*Munchausen syndrome* vs. \**Munchausen's syndrome*\): 4:1
