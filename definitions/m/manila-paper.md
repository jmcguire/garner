# manila paper

manila paper \(= a strong brown paper used for making envelopes\), a phrase dating from the mid-1800s, has been predominantly so written since the late 1890s. Originally made from Manila hemp, or abaca \(a strong fiber obtained from a banana tree's leafstalk\), the phrase has sometimes been capitalized—but the standard form is lowercase. \**Manilla* is a variant spelling.

 - Current ratio in print \(*manila envelope* vs. *Manila envelope* vs. \**manilla envelope*\): 54:1.1:1
