# minable

minable (= capable of being mined), an adjective dating from the late 1500s, was predominantly so spelled in all varieties of English until the 1970s, when *mineable* surged into prominence in BrE. It remains the usual form there. In AmE, *minable* has long predominated despite close competition from *mineable* since 1990. The recent data are inconclusive. Cf. **finable**.

 - Current ratio in print (*minable* vs. *mineable* in AmE): 1:1.03
 - Current ratio in print (*mineable* vs. *minable* in BrE): 1.2:1
