# meatus

meatus /mee-**ay**-tәs/ (= \[1\] a ductlike passage naturally existing in the body; or \[2\] the external opening for such a passage), a medical latinism dating from the 1500s, has two plurals: the anglicized *meatuses* and the Latinate *meatus* (as a fourth-declension Latin noun that doesn't change its form). Since the 1840s, the anglicized form has predominated. Even in medical journals, one can imagine that it's less confusing to refer to *two meatuses* than to \**two meatus*.

 - Current ratio in print (*two meatuses* vs. \**two meatus* vs. *\*two meati*): 10:8:1
 - Current ratio in print (*three meatuses* vs. \**three meatus*): 5:1
