# mow \> mowed \> mowed

mow \> mowed \> mowed. So inflected. The past-participial *mown* is best reserved for adjective uses—e.g.: "You can practically smell the *mown* outfield grass and taste the dirty-water hot dogs." Don Mayhew, "Opening Day Hits," *Fresno Bee*, 1 Apr. 1997, at E1. See irregular verbs (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*has mowed* vs. *\*has mown*): 2.3:1
