# maestro

maestro (= \[1\] an eminent composer or conductor; or \[2\] one who excels in the technique of an art), an Italian loanword dating in English from the 1720s, predominantly makes the anglicized plural *maestros*. The Italianate plural \**maestri* was the usual form until 1915, but today it is merely a pretentious variant.

Current ratio (*maestros* vs. \**maestri*): 3:1
