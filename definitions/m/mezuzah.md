# mezuzah

mezuzah /mә-**zoo**-zә/ \(= a small scroll on which the Shema \[a biblical passage from Deuteronomy\] has been printed, rolled up, encased, and attached to the doorpost of a Jewish home to remind the family of their faith\), a Hebrew word dating in English from the mid-1600s, has been predominantly so spelled since the mid-1800s. \**Mezuza* is a variant. Although the anglicized plural \(*mezuzahs* or \**mezuzas*\) predominated for a time in the 19th century, the Hebrew plural *mezuzot* has been consistently predominant in English-language print sources since the 1930s. See plurals \(b\).

Current ratio in print \(*mezuzah* vs. \**mezuza*\): 26:1
Current ratio in print \(*mezuzot* vs. *mezuzahs* vs. \**mezuzas*\): 25:15:1
