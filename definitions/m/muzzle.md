# muzzle

muzzle; **nozzle**. *Muzzle* = \(1\) the nose and mouth of an animal such as a dog or horse; \(2\) a strapped cover fitted over a dog's mouth to prevent biting or eating; \(3\) something that deters or prevents free speech or full discussion; or \(4\) the front end of a gun where bullets are discharged. *Nozzle* = \(1\) a spout or other device at the end of a hose, pipe, bellows, etc. to direct and control the stream of liquid or gas emitted; or \(2\) \(slang\) nose.

Given the closeness of sense 1 of *muzzle* and sense 2 of *nozzle*—and the similarity between sense 4 of *muzzle* and sense 1 of *nozzle*—it's hardly surprising that the two terms are subject to word-swapping. Since about 1905, writers have occasionally referred to the \**nozzle of the gun* in place of the *muzzle of the gun*. In the senses relating to discharges, *muzzles* shoot out bullets and *nozzles* shoot out liquids.

Current ratio in print \(*muzzle of the gun* vs. \**nozzle of the gun*\): 31:1
