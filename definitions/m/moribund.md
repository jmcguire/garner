# moribund

moribund \(= dying\) does not mean "dead." Yet many writers misuse the word in this way—e.g.: "If Brian O'Neill's controversial proposal to end the fight over the Boundary Waters Canoe Wilderness isn't *totally moribund* \[read *a dead letter* or *quite dead*\], it's clearly in deep trouble." Dean Rebuffoni, "Embattled BWCA Plan Has Led to More Proposals," *Star Trib.* \(Minneapolis\), 4 Dec. 1996, at B1.

Language-Change Index

*moribund* used to mean "dead" instead of "dying": Stage 2
