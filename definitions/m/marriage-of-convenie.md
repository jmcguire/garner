# marriage of convenience

marriage of convenience; **\**mariage de convenance***. Since the 1700s, the anglicized version has always predominated over the gallicism. But it should be understood rightly: *marriage of convenience* is not "an ill-considered marriage that happens to be convenient to the parties involved," but instead "a marriage contracted for social or financial advantages rather than out of mutual love."

 - Current ratio in print \(*marriage of convenience* vs. \**mariage de convenance*\): 37:1
