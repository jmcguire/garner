# macaroni

macaroni.

**A. Plural.** *Macaroni* \(= \[1\] a tube-shaped pasta; \[2\] a foppish man or dandy; or \[3\] a subantarctic penguin with an orange, yellow, and black crest and a black chin\), an Italian loanword dating in sense 1 from the 1500s, has predominantly made the plural *macaronis* since about 1853. Before that, \**macaronies* was the usual form. In sense 1, avoid overboiling.

 - Current ratio in print \(*macaronis* vs. \**macaronies*\): 4:1

**B. Spelling: *macaroni* vs. *\*maccaroni*.** The first has been the preferred spelling since the 1700s.

 - Current ratio in print \(*macaroni* vs. \**maccaroni*\): 43:1
