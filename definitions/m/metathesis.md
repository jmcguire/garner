# Metathesis

Metathesis. This term refers to the transposition of letters or sounds in a word or phrase. Historical examples abound (e.g., the modern words *bird* and *third* from Old English *bridd* and *thridda*). But modern examples—such as *ax* for *ask* (typical of modern dialect, but with historical antecedents), *\*irrevelant* for *irrelevant*, *\*progidy* for *prodigy*, and the like—are to be avoided in standard english. For entries discussing various modern examples, see **anemone**, **calvary**, **chaise longue**, **diminution**, **irrelevant**, **jodhpur**, **medley**, **nuclear** & **relevant**. See also **quick (b)**.
