# Manichaeism

Manichaeism \(= a dualistic religious system developed in Persia by Mani \[AD 216–274\], who saw good and evil as locked in an eternal struggle and believed that salvation was possible only through education and asceticism\), a term dating from the 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled since the 1890s. Before that, *Manicheism* predominated. Other variants are \**Manicheanism*, \**Manichaeanism*, and \**Manicheeism*.

A follower of this essentially extinct religious belief is traditionally termed a *Manichaean*, though the variant *Manichee* competes closely with it. \**Manichean* and \**Manicheean* are variant nouns.

For the corresponding adjective used normally to describe a binary moral code or world view \(regardless of its religious basis\), *Manichaean* and *Manichean* are locked in what seems to be an eternal struggle.
