# memorandums

memorandums; **memoranda**. *Memorandum* is always the singular noun. Either *memorandums* or *memoranda* is correct as a plural. Shakespeare used *memorandums* \(see *Henry IV, Part 1* 3.3.158\). One nice thing about *memorandums* is that it will help curb the tendency to misuse *memoranda* as a singular, as in the erroneous phrasing *\*This memoranda is late*. But the plural *memoranda* has been far more common than *memorandums* in print sources since the early 1800s.

Current ratio in print \(*these memoranda* vs. *these memorandums*\): 7:1   
Current ratio in print \(*one memorandum* vs. *\*one memoranda*\): 139:1
