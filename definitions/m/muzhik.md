# muzhik

muzhik /moo-**zheek**/ (= a Russian peasant), a Russian word imported into English in the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1925. From 1840 to 1925, the predominant spelling—especially in BrE—was \**moujik*. Other variants are \**mujik*, \**muzjik*, \**mougik*, \**mooshik*, and \**mouzhik*.
