# mobocracy

mobocracy; **ochlocracy**. Both mean "mob rule" and denote the rabble as a ruling class. *Ochlocracy*, a word of erudition matching a Greek root with a Greek suffix \(*ochlos* "multitude" \+ *kratos* "strength"\), dates from the late 1500s. *Mobocracy*, a much more plebeian word built on what was originally a slang shortening \(*mob*, a shortened form of the Latin *mobile vulgus* "vacillating crowd"\), dates from the mid-1700s and became almost equally common in the mid-1800s. Although *ochlocracy* appears about as often as *mobocracy*, neither term appears as often as the phrase *mob rule*.

Current ratio in print \(*mob rule* vs. *mobocracy* vs. *ochlocracy*\): 6:1.2:1
