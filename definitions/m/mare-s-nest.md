# mare's nest

mare's nest \(= \[1\] something erroneously thought to be a miraculous discovery but proving to be a delusion or hoax; or \[2\] a thoroughly messy or confused situation\), a phrase dating from the 1500s, has always been predominantly so written. Some dictionaries inexplicably hyphenate the phrase \(\**mare's-nest*\) despite the paltry historical evidence for this form.

The plural is of some interest. The predominant form has pretty consistently been *mares' nests*. The variant forms are \**mare's nests*, \**mares'-nests*, and \**mare's-nests*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*mare's nest* vs. \**mare's-nest*\): 8:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*mares' nests* vs. \**mare's nests* vs. \**mares'-nests* vs. \**mare's-nests*\): 6:4:1.6:1
