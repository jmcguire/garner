# mean, n

mean, n.; **median**. Please distinguish between these two words. The *mean* (also called the *arithmetic* \[/ar-ith-**met**-ik/\] *mean*) is what is usually understood by the term *average*: the sum of a set of values divided by the number of elements in the set. The *median* is the point in a series of numbers above which is half the series and below which is the other half. Selectively using one or the other is a common way to make statistics lie. The *median* income among an unemployed person, a burger-flipper making minimum wage, and Bill Gates may be $25,000, but the *mean* salary would make some less-critical readers think they were each earning vast sums of money.
