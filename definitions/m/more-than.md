# more . . . than

more . . . than.

**A. Parallel Constructions.** To maintain parallel phrasing with this construction, it's often important to repeat the preposition. Here, for example, the second *by* is necessary to clarity: "What saddens Ms. Showalter is her conviction that the Allbucks and thousands like them would stand a better chance of obtaining cures if they could accept that their symptoms would be treated *more effectively by* psychotherapy *than by* endless inconclusive medical tests which encourage paranoia while obfuscating science." John Carlin, "It's All in the Mind, Bud," *Independent*, 27 Apr. 1997, at 18. See parallelism.
