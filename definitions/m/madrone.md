# madrone

madrone; **\*madrona**; **\*madrono**. What's the name of the evergreen tree that grows along the Pacific coast of North America? Dictionaries vary in their main listings. But here are the facts. When the name was first needed in English, in the 1840s, the Spanish term *madroño* was simply borrowed. Within two decades, many speakers had changed the ending to \**madrona*, and from 1860 to 1930 those two spellings—\**madrono* and \**madrona*—vied for predominance. But other speakers had begun dropping the final vowel sound altogether, resulting in *madrone*, and by 1930 that form of the word had become the firmly predominant spelling—as it remains today. The others are now but variants. Nevertheless, some dictionaries continue to list the headword as *madroño*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*madrone* vs. \**madrona* vs. \**madrono*\): 4:1.1:1
