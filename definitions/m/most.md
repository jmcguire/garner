# most

most —in the sense "quite, very"—is an established casualism. Today it is standard english, though less formal than *quite*—e.g.:

 - "Cerulli's findings are *most* inauspicious for the banking industry." "Can US Bank Funds Survive?," *Retail Banker Int'l*, 7 Mar. 1995, at 4.

 - "The shredded lamb, encased in a flaky crust, was seasoned deliciously and was *most* enjoyable." Pat Bruno, "A Field Day," *Chicago Sun-Times*, 21 Nov. 1997, at 32.

See **very \(a\)**. See also comparatives and superlatives & **all \(c\)**.
