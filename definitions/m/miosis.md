# miosis

miosis /mī-**oh**-sis/ (= contraction of the pupil of the eye), a Latin loanword dating in English from the early 1800s, has been predominantly so spelled since the late 1940s. Before that, the variant **myosis* was about equally common. The standard plural is *mioses*.

The ocular term *miosis* is quite distinct from the etymologically related homophone *meiosis*, which as a rhetorical term denotes underemphasis or understatement purposefully used, or as a biological term denotes the early division of germ cells or spores. For more on this biological sense, see **mitosis**.
