# figment

figment \(= something contrived by the mind\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1400s, appears almost exclusively in the well-worn phrase *figment of the imagination*. At times writers pun on the phrase by referring to a given color as either \(if unreal\) *a pigment of the imagination* or \(if real\) *not a mere pigment of the imagination*. Actual misuses of *pigment* for *figment* are rare: they amount to malapropisms.
