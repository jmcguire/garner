# faerie

faerie = \(1\) fairyland; or \(2\) a fairy. Sense 1 is archaic, sense 2 unnecessary except as a fancy spelling. Because the word isn't generally needed, the variant spelling *\*faery* \(to be avoided\) merits only the barest mention. Edmund Spenser's renowned poem "The Faerie Queene" \(completed in 1596\) accounts for the persistence of the word.

 - Current ratio in print \(*faerie* vs. *\*faery*\): 1.7:1
