# foolhardy

foolhardy (= unwisely bold or adventurous), a French loanword (OF *fol hardi*) dating in English from the 1200s, has sometimes been erroneously spelled \**fool-hearty* or \**foolhearty*—especially since the mid-1900s. These are mondegreens. Cf. **hardy**.

 - Current ratio in print (*foolhardy* vs. \**fool-hearty* vs. \**foolhearty*): 4,427:3:1
