# fetish

fetish (= \[1\] a material object of blind devotion or extreme affection; or \[2\] anything about which someone is obsessed, often implying a connection to sexual gratification) was borrowed from the French *fetiche* in the early 1600s. (The French got it from the Portuguese.) By the end of that century, the spelling had been anglicized to *fetish*. The competing form in English is \**fetich*, which has never been more than a variant about which a few authors have been oddly obsessed.

 - Current ratio in print (*fetish* vs. \**fetich*): 21:1
