# far-flung

far-flung; **far-fetched**. These adjectives are, literally speaking, etymological opposites—*far-flung* literally meaning "flung (i.e., cast) a long distance" and *far-fetched* meaning "fetched (i.e., retrieved) from afar." Of course, both terms are now used almost exclusively in their figurative senses. *Far-flung* means "widespread" or "remote"; *far-fetched* means "improbable" or "strained."

Some dictionaries make *far-fetched* a single word, without the hyphen. Although that may signal the future of this term, it isn't now the predominant form in any variety of literary English.
