# ferment

ferment, n. & vb. To *ferment* /fәr-**ment**/ something is to cause it to undergo a chemical change in composition through enzymatic effervescence, especially as sugar is converted into alcohol (*fermentation*). As a noun, *ferment* /**fәr**-mәnt/ denotes (1) a yeast or enzyme that causes fermentation, or, by analogy, (2) a situation of great unrest, trouble, or excitement in a country or region, esp. caused by political change.

For the misuse of *foment* for *ferment*, see **foment**.
