# frenetic

frenetic; \***phrenetic**; **frenzy**; **\*phrenzy**; **\*phrensy**. Although *\*phrenetic* and *\*phrenzy* are closer to the Greek etymon \(*phrenetikos* "having brain inflammation"\)—and predominated until about 1880—the spellings with *f* have long been standard in both AmE and BrE. Today, *\*phrenetic*, *\*phrenzy*, and variant spellings such as *\*phrensy* are all oddball archaisms—e.g.:

 - "Anyway, you can still make the best of a *phrenetic* \[read *frenetic*\] December." Peter C. Hotton, "Deck the Halls, Safely and Sanely," *Boston Globe*, 5 Dec. 1993, Home & Garden §, at A94.

 - "And he understood . . . that the Senate was uniquely insulated against the *phrensy* \[read *frenzy*\] of public opinion." Robert A. Caro, *The Years of Lyndon Johnson: Master of the Senate* 373 \(2002\).
