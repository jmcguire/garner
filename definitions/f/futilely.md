# futilely

futilely, adv., is sometimes misspelled *\*futiley*—e.g.:

• "As she *futiley* \[read *futilely*\] tries to emotionally latch on to someone . . . , she becomes more unhinged." Paul Sherman, "Video View," *Boston Herald*, 2 July 1995, at 10.

• "But the bottom line is Starks got the calls and the Nuggets were left *futiley* \[read *futilely*\] pleading their case to referees." Dave Krieger, "Nuggets End East Trip 0–4," *Rocky Mountain News* \(Denver\), 15 Dec. 1996, at C12.

• "\[Mr.\] Whales traces his spiritual quest to write his life's magnum opus as he *futiley* \[read *futilely*\] rages against his condition." Charlotte Cripps, "Brainwaving but Drowning," *Independent*, 2 June 2003, at 18.

Cf. **agilely** & **solely**. See adverbs \(c\).
