# fare-thee-well

fare-thee-well, n.; **fare-you-well**, n. If you research something *to a fare-thee-well*, you research it with great thoroughness—to the utmost degree. When the expression first arose in the 1800s, the usual form was *to a* *fare-you-well*, which remained standard until the 1940s. By 1910, the phrase *to a* *fare-thee-well* had sprung up, sporting the archaism *thee*, and by 1945 it had become the predominant form.

 - Current ratio in print \(*fare-thee-well* vs. *fare-you-well*\): 2.4:1
