# foolscap

foolscap; **fool's cap**. *Foolscap* is a size of paper (around 13½ by 17 inches), so called because 17th-century papermakers used a *fool's cap* as their watermark. A *fool's cap* is either (1) the hat once worn by court jesters, with three peaks, each tipped with a bell; or (2) the conical hat formerly put on dull pupils or, notably, on political dissidents in Maoist China. In sense 2, it's also called a *dunce cap* or *dunce's cap*.
