# fantastic

fantastic; **fantastical**. These words entered the language in the 1300s as mere by-forms. Since the mid-1700s, *fantastic* has always predominated in use—and for a long time *fantastical* seemed like a needless variant. But as *fantastic*, during the 20th century, became an especially popular colloquial adjective for anything excellent or superb, the term *fantastical* rose in prominence in the sense "imaginary, unreal, or whimsically outlandish." Today, readers and listeners are likely to think that *fantastic ideas* are especially good while *fantastical ideas* are especially bad and unrealistic. The differentiation seems well established. Hence *fantastical* answers more closely to *fantasy* than *fantastic* does today. See estranged siblings.
