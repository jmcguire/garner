# factor

factor, n., traditionally means "an agent or cause that contributes to a particular result." By slipshod extension, it has taken on the sense "a thing to be considered; an event or occurrence." It's often a symptom of verbosity—e.g.: "The index rates suppliers *according to factors including* \[read *by their*\] flexibility and their importance to the business." John Riley, "Top Suppliers Score Low in TIF User Poll," *Computer Weekly*, 20 May 1999.
