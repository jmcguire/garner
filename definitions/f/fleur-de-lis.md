# fleur-de-lis

fleur-de-lis (= \[1\] the flower known as an iris; \[2\] the French royal coat of arms; or \[3\] any representation of the archetypal heraldic lily), a phrase dating in English from the 1300s, has been predominantly so written since the mid-1800s. (The Modern French is unhyphenated: *fleur de lis*.) The English plural is *fleurs-de-lis*. (See plurals (b).) Whether singular or plural, the pronunciation is /flʊr-dә-**lee**/.

The phrase has two needless variants in English: *\*fleur-de-lys* and *\*fleur-de-luce*, both of which are archaic. The *OED* records more than 20 historical spellings.

 - Current ratio in print (*fleur-de-lis* vs. *\*fleur-de-lys* vs. *\*fleur-de-luce*): 327:115:1
