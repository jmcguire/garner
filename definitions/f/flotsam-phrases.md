# Flotsam Phrases

Flotsam Phrases take up space without adding to the meaning of a sentence. So there is usually no reason, when it is clear whose opinion is being expressed, to write *In my opinion* or *It seems to me that.* Other examples are *in terms of*, *on a* . . . *basis*, *my sense is that*, *in the first instance*, *the fact of the matter*, and *the fact that.* \(Admittedly, some of these phrases may be useful signals in speech.\) We have enough written words without these space-fillers. See **basis \(a\)** & **fact**, n. **\(a\), \(b\)**.
