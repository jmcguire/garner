# furrowed brow

furrowed brow \(= a wrinkled forehead commonly thought to show concern, worry, or concentration\), a phrase dating from the late 1700s, is sometimes mangled into the malapropism \**furled brow*. To *furl* a flag or a newspaper is to fold it or roll it neatly—not really something to imagine doing with one's brow. The mistaken phrasing began spreading about 1967.

 - Current ratio in print \(*furrowed brow* vs. \**furled brow*\): 161:1
