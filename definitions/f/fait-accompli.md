# fait accompli

fait accompli (= something irrevocably done and not now changeable), from French, makes the plural *faits accomplis*. E.g.: "But in the view of the palace, by presenting her demands as *faits accomplis*, Diana was limiting Charles's room to bargain." Jerry Adler & Daniel Pedersen, "Diana's Battle Royal," *Newsweek*, 11 Mar. 1996, at 20. See plurals (b).

The singular and plural forms are pronounced identically: /**fayt** ә-kom-**plee**/ or /**fet** ә-kom-**plee**/.

By the way, since the mid-1960s the misspelling \**fate accompli* has occasionally appeared—a sort of visual malapropism.

 - Current ratio in print (*fait accompli* vs. \**fate accompli*): 876:1
