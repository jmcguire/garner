# feign

feign; **feint**. These words, though deriving from the same French verb (*feindre* "to touch or shape"), have undergone differentiation in English. To *feign* is either to make up or fabricate \<she feigned an excuse\> or to make a false show of \<he feigned illness\>. To *feint* is to deliver a pretended blow or attack designed to confuse an opponent momentarily. *Feint* is also, in its older (but still current) sense, used as a noun meaning either a sham or a pretended blow or attack (i.e., the act of *feinting*).
