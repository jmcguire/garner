# flatulence

flatulence; **\*flatulency**. These words—meaning either \(1\) "the state of having and emitting excessive intestinal gas," or \(2\) "pomposity and pretentiousness"—came into common use in the early 1700s. The older term, *\*flatulency*, gave way to *flatulence* by 1760 and is now a needless variant. Except for a brief period from 1790 to 1810, *flatulence* has been predominant throughout the English-speaking world. Cf. **borborygmus**.

Current ratio in print \(*flatulence* vs. \**flatulency*\): 64:1
