# forever

forever. One word. The *OED* has now abandoned its old distinction—the solid *forever* meaning "incessantly" <she's forever twirling her hair> and the two-word *\*for ever* meaning "for all future time" <we'll be together for ever>. The solidified version has become standard in both AmE and BrE, and the two-word version is best described as archaic.

Current ratio in print \(*forever* vs. *for ever*\): 4:1
