# fisticuffs

fisticuffs \(= a fistfight\), though singular in sense \(a single fistfight being *fisticuffs*\), is plural in form. Even so, one refers to *fisticuffs*, not *\*a fisticuffs*—e.g.: "The city's famously combative District Attorney, Terence Hallinan, who got into *a fisticuffs* \[delete *a*\] with a trade-union leader at Mr. Davis's last birthday celebration, said the party-givers' biggest error might have been to invite a pair of political columnists from The San Francisco Chronicle." Tim Golden, "Political ‘Party' Goes So Far, Even San Francisco Is Aghast," *N.Y. Times*, 10 May 1997, at 1, 15. Often found in the idiom *to trade fisticuffs*, the word carries a quaint tone.
