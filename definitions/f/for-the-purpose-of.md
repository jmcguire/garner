# for the purpose of

for the purpose of \[\+ vb. \+ -ing\]. This construction signals wordiness. A simple infinitive is often a superior alternative—e.g.:

• "Elections Board lawyer George Dunst said the board could agree with the group's argument that because it was created solely *for the purpose of boosting* \[read *to boost*\] voter turnout, what it does and who gives to it can remain secret." Steven Walters, "Court Race Defends Its Anonymity," *Milwaukee J. Sentinel*, 13 July 1997, at 5.

• "And if you're doing it only *for the purpose of selling* \[read *to sell*\] your house more quickly, yes, you should avoid overimproving." Ruth Hepner, "Pick Improvements to Make Home Nicer for You," *Wash. Times*, 1 Aug. 1997, at F2.
