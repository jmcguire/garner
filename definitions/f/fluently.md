# fluently

fluently; **fluidly**. Both adverbs derive from the same Latin root, *fluere* \(= to flow\), and both connote grace and elegance. When referring to one's mastery of a foreign language, *fluently* appears far more often than *fluidly*. The latter word might usefully imply a higher level of mastery than the former—e.g.: "My ability to speak Khmer *fluently* but not *fluidly* exposed my real identity." Putsata Reang, "A Search for Home," *Seattle Times*, 27 July 1999, at E1.
