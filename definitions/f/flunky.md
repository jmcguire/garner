# flunky

flunky (= someone who does small jobs for a prominent person, esp. for sycophantic reasons), a Scotticism dating from the late 1700s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since the 1940s; before that, *flunkey* predominated. In BrE, the predominant spelling was *flunkey* from the 1840s to the first decade of the 2000s. \**Flunkie* is a variant spelling.

 - Current ratio in print (*flunky* vs. *flunkey* vs. \**flunkie* in AmE): 37:8:1
 - Current ratio in print (*flunky* vs. *flunkey* vs. \**flunkie* in BrE): 65:49:1
