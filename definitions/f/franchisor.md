# franchisor

franchisor; \***franchiser**. *\*Franchiser* was long the preferred form for the word denoting the holder of a franchise. It predates *franchisor* by about 400 years—the *-or* spelling dating only from the 1930s. But today the *-er* form is ambiguous: it may denote the granter of a franchise or the holder of one, as here: "Regulators also emphasize that before potential investors write any checks, they need to read and be sure they understand the disclosure documents—called Uniform Franchise Offering Circulars—that *franchisers* are required to file in certain states." Richard Gibson, "Have I Got a Franchise Opportunity for You\!," *Chicago Trib.*, 22 May 2007, Small Bus. §, at 6.

Perhaps because of that ambiguity, *franchisor* is about ten times as common as *\*franchiser* in law, and about 16 times as common in modern print sources. It has won the fight in our fast-food nation—and has won the franchise as the newly preferred spelling.
