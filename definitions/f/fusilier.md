# fusilier

fusilier \(= an infantry soldier armed with a flintlock musket \[called a *fusil*\]\), a French loanword dating in English from the late 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled since the late 1840s. The spelling **fusileers*, prevalent during the 1700s until the 1840s, is now but a variant. See gallicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*fusiliers* vs. \**fusileers*\): 13:1
