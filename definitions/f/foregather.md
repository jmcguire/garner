# foregather

foregather; **forgather**. The latter \(meaning "to meet in a group; assemble"\) has long been thought to be the standard spelling—as the listings in the *OED* and *W11* attest. In fact, though, in modern print sources, both AmE and BrE, the spelling *foregather* has enjoyed a significant preponderance. \(See for-.) In any event, though, *gather* usually suffices—e.g.: "On the Greek isle of Skagathos, a gaggle of New Agers *forgather* \[read *gather*\], booked by Skagathos Holidays to enroll in such courses as Orgasmic Consciousness and Therapy of Fiction Writing." Nicholas Delbanco, "From D.M. Thomas, a Fine, Funny Novel of the Writing Life," *Chicago Trib.*, 9 June 1996, Books §, at 9. \(On second thought, the quirky word might be appropriate in that particular context.\)

 - Current ratio in print \(*foregather* vs. *forgather*\): 3:1
