# feasible

feasible = (1) capable of being accomplished; (2) capable of being used or handled to good effect; or (3) reasonable, likely. Sense 3 is a classic example of slipshod extension—and ambiguous to boot. Does someone who says that a cure for cancer is *feasible* mean that a cure can definitely be found (if we work hard enough to find it) or that a cure might one day be found (but not necessarily)?

To avoid this problem, it's best to reserve *feasible* for senses 1 and 2. When sense 3 is the intended meaning, *possible* or *probable* is the more effective choice. Cf. **viable**.

The word is sometimes misspelled *\*feasable*. See -able (a).
