# fiscal

fiscal /**fis**-kәl/ (= of, relating to, or involving money, debts, taxes, etc. owned and managed by a government or business), a French loanword dating in English from the mid-1500s, has been in common use since the mid-1700s. Perhaps because it is commonly mispronounced as if it had three syllables—with an epenthetic schwa before the /k/—it has sometimes, especially since the early 1900s, been confounded with *physical*.

 - Current ratio in print (*fiscal year* vs. \**physical year*): 3,872:1
