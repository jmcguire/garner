# frequently

frequently. This adverb can be ambiguous when used with a plural subject and verb. Do individuals do something frequently \(i.e., often\), or is the characteristic true of a group that may do something only once \(i.e., commonly\)? Note the miscue here: "A study last year by Jack Hadley of the Georgetown University School of Medicine showed that uninsured patients arrived at the hospital sicker than those with health insurance, and died in the hospital more *frequently*." Jane Bryant Quinn, "Woe the Reformers," *Newsweek*, 19 Oct. 1992, at 55. If the final clause is changed to *and were more likely to die in the hospital*, the miscue disappears.
