# flagrant

flagrant \(= glaring\) is occasionally confused with *fragrant* \(= nice-smelling\)—e.g.:

 - "The Inspector General examined the 45 businesses that were assessed penalties by OSHA in 1989 and 1990 for *fragrantly* \[read *flagrantly*\] violating safety laws." L.M. Sixel, "Big OSHA Fines Often Cut Down to a Pittance," *Houston Chron.*, 2 Apr. 1992, at A1.

 - "Ryan Volenberg, a conservation warden with the DNR, said the salmon run is very popular with sportsmen and women, but is also known for *fragrant* \[read *flagrant*\] violations." Neil Rhines, "Salmon Run: Poor So Far," *Herald Times Rptr.* \(Manitowoc, Wis.\), 4 Oct. 2004, at A1.

See malapropisms.

For more on the use of *flagrant*, see **blatant**.
