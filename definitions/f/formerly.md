# formerly

formerly; **formally**. *Formerly* \(= once, at a time in the past\) is sometimes confounded with *formally* \(= \[1\] by authorized sanction; or \[2\] based on form rather than substance\). Doubtless the blunder results from the similar pronunciations—e.g.:

 - "Roger Westwell, *formally* \[read *formerly*\] chief accountant for Nadin Contracting, has now been promoted to financial controller of the company." "Mining People," *Mining Mag.*, Dec. 1989, at 543.

 - "The Sheriff's Office *formally* \[read *formerly*\] had used for its rolling station a converted bread truck and a county hand-me-down bookmobile." "Playback," *Herald-Sun* \(Durham, N.C.\), 8 Sept. 2002, at C3.

Language-Change Index

*formally* misused for *formerly*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*was formerly the case* vs. *\*was formally the case*\): 54:1
