# fuchsia

fuchsia is so spelled—after the German botanist Leonhard Fuchs \(1501–1566\). The term denotes either \(1\) a decorative shrub with drooping reddish-purple or purplish-pink flowers, or \(2\) the color associated with those flowers. Although the botanist's name was pronounced /fʊks/ or /fooks/, the derivative word is /**fyoo**-shә/. Doubtless because of its standard pronunciation in English, the term is often misspelled *\*fuschia*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*fuchsia* vs. \**fuschia*\): 28:1
