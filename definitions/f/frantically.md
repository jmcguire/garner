# frantically

frantically —so spelled—is fairly often wrongly made *\*franticly*, probably because the *-al-* is not pronounced. E.g.:

• "U.S. Park Police said two officers spotted a man waving his arms *franticly* \[read *frantically*\] and pulled over, but the baby was born before they made it to the truck." "Baby Arrives on Parkway," *Wash. Post*, 13 May 1997, at B8.

• " ‘This is so much fun, and it's all about tradition and getting everyone out here together,' Leverton said moments before he began *franticly* \[read *frantically*\] dancing circles with a group of four children." Geoff Grammar, "Lighting the Menorah," *Santa Fe New Mexican*, 30 Nov. 2002, at B1.

See -ic/-ical.
