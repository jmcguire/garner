# flotation

flotation; **\*floatation**. *Flotation*, the standard spelling, first appeared in the mid-1800s. According to *The Oxford Dictionary of English Etymology* \(1966\), "the sp. with *flot-* has been adopted to make the word conform to \[*flotilla* and *flotsam*\], and *rotation*" \(p. 364\). The *OED* calls *flotation* "not etymologically justifiable." A more recent work gives a different etymology: "The current spelling appeared . . . probably by influence of French *flottaison*, which was used in technical terms translated into English, such as *ligne de flottaison* line of flotation." *The Barnhart Dictionary of Etymology* 393 \(Robert K. Barnhart ed., 1988\). *\*Floatation* is a variant sporadically recorded since 1806.

Current ratio in print \(*flotation* vs. \**floatation*\): 10:1
