# jailer

jailer \(= a prison guard\), a respelled French loanword dating in English from the 1200s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since the 1820s and in BrE since about 1990. The variants \**gaoler* \(long predominant in BrE\), \**jailor*, and \**gaolor* are variants. See -er \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*jailer* vs. \**gaoler* vs. \**jailor* vs. \**gaolor*\): 1,391:466:257:1
