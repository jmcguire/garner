# jasmine

jasmine (the plant with sweet-smelling white or yellow flowers), a French loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1861. The older spelling, \**jessamine*, together with \**jasmin*, is a variant form.

 - Current ratio in print (*jasmine* vs. \**jessamine* vs. \**jasmin*): 72:5:1
