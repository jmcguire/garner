# just exactly

just exactly. Although this redundancy is common in speech, it shouldn't appear in careful writing (apart from dialogue)—e.g.: "*Just exactly* \[read *Just*\] how good are the big, fast and skillful Flyers?" Stephen Harris, "Hockey: B's Can't Cool Red-Hot Flyers," *Boston Herald*, 8 Jan. 1997, Sports §, at 8. The phrase might also be replaced by *precisely*.

For a similar phrase involving *exact*, see **exact same**.
