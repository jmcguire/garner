# juror

juror should be distinguished from *potential juror* or *veniremember*—e.g.: "When the court was cleared of unchosen *jurors* \[read *veniremembers*\], the spectators waiting in the corridor were allowed inside." John Bryson, *Evil Angels* 346 \(1985\). The difference is that a *potential juror* or *veniremember* hasn't yet been selected to sit on the jury, but is merely in the pool of people who might be selected; a *juror* is someone who has been empaneled on a jury. For a surprising misuse, see **jurist \(b\)**.

*Juror* is preferably pronounced /**jʊr**-әr/, though it is frequently overpronounced in AmE /**jʊr**-ohr/ to distinguish the syllables—especially by lawyers and judges. In BrE, it is simply /**jʊr**-ә/.
