# judging from

judging from; **judging by**. Meaning "if we are to judge by," each of these phrases is an acceptable dangler or disguised conjunction <judging from your smile, the interview must have gone well>. \(See danglers \(e\).\) The phrases became especially common in print sources beginning about 1800. Since that time, *judging from* was predominant in AmE until 2007, when *judging by* became more common; since the 1930s, *judging by* has been somewhat more common in BrE.

 - Current ratio in print \(*judging by* vs. *judging from* in World Englishes\): 1.5:1
