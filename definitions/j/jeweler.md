# jeweler

jeweler \(= someone who buys, sells, makes, or repairs jewelry\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since the first decade of the 1900s. Before that, *jeweller*—which has always been the predominant BrE spelling—was the usual AmE spelling. Across World Englishes, the two spellings have vied closely for predominance since the 1910s.

 - Current ratio in print \(*jeweler* vs. *jeweller* in AmE\): 4:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*jeweller* vs. *jeweler* in BrE\): 3:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*jeweler* vs. *jeweller* in World Englishes\): 1.04:1
