# junta

junta; **junto**. Of Spanish origin, *junta* \(= a political or military group that controls a government, esp. after a coup d'état\) is pronounced either /**hoon**-tә/ \(more usual in AmE\) or /**jәn**-tә/. During the Falklands War of 1982, BrE saw a dramatic spike in /**hoon**-tә/, but BrE pronunciation gradually reverted to /**jәn**-tә/—the latter being rarely heard in AmE.

*Junta* is much more common in AmE than its altered form, *junto* /**jәn**-toh/, which has undergone slight differentiation to mean "a self-appointed committee having political aims." Ernest Gowers wrote that *junto* "is an erroneous form" \(*FMEU2* at 319\), but it appears frequently in BrE where an American would write *junta*—e.g.: "Even so, a compliant civilian government may not be easy for the deeply unpopular *junto* to achieve." "Myanmar: Deja Vu," *Economist* \(Am. ed.\), 16 Jan. 1993, at 34. \(See **government \(b\)**.) The plural of *junto* is *juntos*, not *\*juntoes*. See plurals \(d\).
