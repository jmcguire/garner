# jeweled

jeweled \(= adorned with or as if with jewels\), a past-participial adjective dating from the late 1500s, has been the predominant AmE spelling since about 1910. Before that, *jewelled* predominated in AmE and BrE alike—as it still does in BrE. A similar point: although the synonymous term *bejewelled* has always been predominantly so spelled in BrE, *bejeweled* has predominated in AmE since the 1930s. See spelling \(c\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*jeweled* vs. *jewelled* in AmE\): 5:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*jewelled* vs. *jeweled* in BrE\): 2.2:1
