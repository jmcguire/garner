# jurist

jurist.

**A. Generally.** In BrE, this word is reserved for someone who has made outstanding contributions to legal thought and legal literature. In AmE, it is loosely applied to every judge of whatever level, and sometimes even to nonscholarly practitioners who are well respected.

**B. For *juror*.** This is a surprising error. On the front page of the *Oakland Tribune*, a deck line states: "Group may recommend better pay for *jurists*." The meaning of that statement isn't clear until you read the body of the story: "The panel also is expected to recommend *jurors*—who are increasingly reluctant to serve on trials—be paid more money." "Jury Changes Would Drop Requirement for Unanimity," *Oakland Trib.*, 30 Apr. 1996, at A1. Nowhere does the story mention judges' pay. See **juror**.
