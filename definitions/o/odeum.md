# odeum

odeum; **odeon**. During the 18th and 19th centuries, the indisputably standard term between these two rarities, both of which denote a theater or concert hall \(especially an ancient one\), was *odeum*. But since 1900, the spelling *odeon*—closer to the Greek etymon *ōideion*—has vied closely for predominance. Today, *odeon* predominates slightly in AmE and BrE alike—avoiding the unfortunate homophone in *odeum*/*odium*. Either way, the plural is *odea*. \(See plurals \(b\).) But if you asked most English speakers whether they'd ever been to odea, they'd be at a loss to say.

Current ratio in print \(*odeum* vs. *odeon* in World Englishes\): 1:1.8   
Current ratio in print \(*Roman* *odeon* vs. *Roman* *odeum* in World Englishes\): 5:1
