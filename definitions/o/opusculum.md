# opusculum

opusculum; **opuscule**; **\*opuscle**. Although the Latinate *opusculum* \(= a minor literary or musical work\) was long ago anglicized to *opuscule*, the latter has never occurred as frequently in English-language print sources as the former. And although we might expect *opuscules* to be more convenient to readers of English than the plural *opuscula*, in fact the more bookish term has predominated in all varieties of English. *\*Opuscle* is a variant. For nonscholars, of course, all these terms are hopelessly pedantic.

 - Current ratio in print \(*opuscula* vs. *opuscules* vs. *\*opuscles*\): 131:56:1
