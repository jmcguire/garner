# omicron

omicron. When the Omicron variant of Covid-19 arose in the fall of 2021, people were instantly worried about how to say the word—and tried out various pronunciations. On December 7, the *Wall Street Journal* printed a front-page article on the subject. The authors seemed to suggest that the "correct" pronunciation would be what the ancient Greeks said. See Anna-Maria Andriotis & Joanna Sugden, "Oh My Krone? Omni Kron? It's All Wrong," *Wall Street J*., 7 Dec. 2021, at A1, A14. Yet the word has long appeared in English dictionaries.

The standard pronunciations of the term in English are /**om**-i-kron/ (listed first in *W2*) and /**oh**-mi-kron/. My Twitter poll on 7 December 2021, with 935 votes tallied, yielded these results: /**oh**-mi-kron/ (56%), /**om**-i-kron/ (35%), /oh-**mee**-kron/ (6%), /oh-**mī**-kron/ (3%).
