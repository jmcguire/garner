# ouzel

ouzel /**oo**-zәl/ \(= a thrush-like bird, such as a blackbird\) is an old word dating in English from before the 1100s. In Modern English the settled spelling has been *ouzel* since the late 1700s. The variant \**ousel* has vied for predominance since the early 1800s but has never surpassed the traditional spelling.

Current ratio in print \(*ring ouzel* vs. *ring \*ousel*\): 4:1   
Current ratio in print \(*water ouzel* vs. *water \*ousel*\): 10:1
