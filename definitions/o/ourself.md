# ourself

ourself; **ourselves**; \***theirself**. *Ourself* is technically ill formed, since *our* is plural and *self* is singular. In the preferred form, *ourselves*, both parts are plural. *Ourself* is established, though, in the editorial or royal style—e.g.: "You'd be surprised how hard it is to write a column from the shower. *We've* been stuck in here, hosing *ourself* down, since watching Michael Jackson creep the hell out of the entire country." Tim Goodman, "Jackson Show Is Over, but He's Still Creeping Us Out," *S.F. Chron.*, 12 Feb. 2003, at D1. But *\*theirself* for *themselves*, increasingly common since 1900, is indefensible (see sexism (b)).
