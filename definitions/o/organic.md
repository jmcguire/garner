# organic

organic. This adjective traditionally means \(to a chemist\) "carbon-based" or \(to a physician\) "of, relating to, or involving a body organ." Purists may object to its odd extensions in reference to plant foods that are grown without chemicals, pesticides, or genetic manipulation, but the phrase is here to stay—it's even codified in federal law. The phrase *\*inorganic food* is, strictly speaking, an oxymoron, since all foods contain carbon. Use *nonorganic food* instead.
