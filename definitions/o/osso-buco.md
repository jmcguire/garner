# osso buco

osso buco /oh-soh **boo**-koh/ or /os-oh/, an Italian loanword literally meaning "marrowbone" and dating in English from 1908, is the standard spelling to denote the dish consisting of veal shanks stewed in white wine with tomatoes, garlic, and minced vegetables. \**Osso bucco* is a variant spelling. See loanwords \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*osso buco* vs. \**osso bucco*\): 2.1:1
