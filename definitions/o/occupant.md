# occupant

occupant; **occupier**. Although both refer to a person who occupies a particular place, AmE greatly prefers *occupant* for most purposes, while BrE distinguishes between the words based on time. In BrE, *occupant* connotes a short-term stay, as in a taxi, a theater seat, or an overnight hotel room; *occupier* connotes a long-term resident or tenant <my uncle has been the sole occupier since the house was built> or an occupying military force <the occupier of France during World War II>. E.g.: "While at first these unions were formed with the blessing of the *occupier*, they could be transformed into spaces of resistance against that *occupier* . . . ." *Japan as the Occupier and the Occupied* 79 \(Christine de Matos & Mark E. Caprio eds., 2015\).
