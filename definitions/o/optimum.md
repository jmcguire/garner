# optimum

optimum. *Optimum* is the noun, but *optimal* is—optimally speaking—the better adjective. Hence the phrase should be *optimal advantage*, not *\*optimum advantage*. The reason is that there's no need for *optimal* if we use *optimum* both as a noun and as an adjective. It serves the principle of differentiation to distinguish between the two forms. But the adjectival *optimum* seems to be edging out *optimal* in practice, and the latter may one day be just a needless variant.

 - Current ratio in print (*optimal solution* vs. \**optimum solution*): 9:1
