# old wives' tale

old wives' tale \(= a false belief based on folk superstitions\), a phrase dating from the early 1500s, has always been so spelled—with a plural possessive. The variants \**old wives tale* \(which is ungrammatical\), \**old wife's tale*, \**old wive's tale* \(an ungrammatical mixing of a plural form with a singular possessive\), and \**old wise tale* \(a mondegreen\) appeared sporadically during the 1900s, but they've never come close to being standard. Some consider the phrase objectionable on grounds of sexism.

 - Current ratio in print \(*old wives' tale* vs. \**old wives tale* vs. \**old wife's tale* vs. \**old wive's tale* vs. \**old wise tale*\): 162:17:16:2.3:1
