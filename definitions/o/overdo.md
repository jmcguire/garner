# overdo

overdo; **overdue**. These are homophones in AmE but not in BrE, which uses /oh-vәr-**dyoo**/ for *overdue*. The first is the verb \(= to do too much\), the second the adjective \(= past due, late\). But the two are sometimes confounded—e.g.:

 - " ‘If you have blue eyes, bring them out. But when you do, don't *overdue* \[read *overdo*\] it with too much makeup or jewelry.' " "By Design," *L.A. Times*, 16 June 1994, at E3 \(quoting Homer Prefontaine\).

 - "The Izod Center—eaten alive by the Xanadu mess at the Meadowlands—needs millions in long-*overdo* \[read *overdue*\] improvements for it to continue as a viable home." Steve Politi, "It's Time to Bring Nets to Newark," *Star-Ledger* \(Newark\), 2 May 2008, at 31.
