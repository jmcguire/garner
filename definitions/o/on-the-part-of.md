# on the part of

on the part of. This phrase is usually verbose—e.g.:

 - "Watterson is somewhat self-effacing about how he comes up with such metaphysical thoughts *on the part of* \[read *for*\] the kid, such cogent comments by the cat." "After a Highly Successful Decade, Cartoonist Is Pulling the Plug on ‘Calvin & Hobbes,' " *Asbury Park Press* (Neptune, N.J.), 3 Dec. 1995, at C3.

 - "Although the election rerun is the result of illegal financial actions *on the part of* \[read *by*\] the Carey campaign, Cherkasky's first job will be to investigate possible financial irregularities in the Hoffa campaign." Frank Swoboda, "Overseer Named for Rerun of Teamsters' Vote," *Wash. Post*, 3 Dec. 1997, at A19.

 - "It's more of a pause and reassessment *on the part of* \[read *by*\] the developer, rather than pulling the plug." Paul Grondahl & Eric Anderson, "Retail Project Near TU Stalls," *Times Union* (Albany), 2 Sept. 2015, at C10.
