# ochre

ochre \(= \[1\] earth or soil filled with iron ore ranging in color from yellow to deep orange to brown and frequently used in paints; or \[2\] a reddish-yellow color\) is predominantly so spelled in AmE and BrE alike. *Ocher* is an AmE variant that sprang to fairly widespread use in the late 1800s and has vied closely with *ochre* ever since. Although many American dictionaries list *ocher* as the primary spelling, it has never achieved sustained predominance. American writers have long used both spellings, and since the late 1700s they have always preferred *ochre*. It's now more than twice as common as *ocher*. *Ochre* is the only BrE spelling. See -er \(b\).

Current ratio in print \(*ochre yellow* vs. *ocher yellow* in World Englishes\): 4:1   
Current ratio in print \(*ochre yellow* vs. *ocher yellow* in AmE\): 2.3:1
