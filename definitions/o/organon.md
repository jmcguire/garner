# organon

organon; **organum**. These doublets—the first borrowed from Greek, the second from Latin—have undergone differentiation and ought to be kept distinct. *Organon* denotes a system of rules, principles, and standards by which knowledge or thought is to be developed or assessed. Examples are Aristotle's system of deductive logic and Francis Bacon's system of induction. *Organum*, by contrast, is normally a musical term denoting either an organ (please use *organ* instead) or a type of polyphonic medieval music involving harmonized singing.
