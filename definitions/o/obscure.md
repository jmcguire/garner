# obscure

obscure; **obscurantist**, adj.; \***obscurant**; **obscurantic**; **\*obscurantistic**. What is *obscure* is either not important or well known <an obscure poet> or difficult to understand <obscure reasoning>. What is *obscurantist* is either opposed to the free exchange of ideas and dissemination of information <obscurantist tyranny> or else willfully abstruse <obscurantist literature>. *Obscurantist* can also function as a noun denoting someone who opposes human enlightenment or whose speech and writing are deliberately vague and difficult to comprehend. \**Obscurant* \(predominant from the early 1700s until the 1860s\) is a needless variant of *obscurantist* both as an adjective and as a noun. \**Obscurantic* and \**obscurantistic* are needless variants of *obscurantist* as an adjective. That's about all the light this entry can shed.

 - Current ratio in print \(*obscurantist tyranny* vs. \**obscurant tyranny*\): 2:1
