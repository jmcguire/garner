# overfly \> overflew \> overflown

overfly \> overflew \> overflown. So inflected. Oddly, this verb dates from the 1300s. Though never common, it's become more so in the age of flight—e.g.: "It'll be the first time the shuttle crew has *overflown* the heartland since the 2003 Columbia tragedy." Mark Carreau, "Shuttle Begins Journey Home," *Houston Chron.*, 6 Nov. 2007, at A4.
