# overweening

overweening (= presumptuous, arrogant, or immoderate) is so spelled—but the misspelling *\*overweaning* is common. The word derives from the Old English verb *wenan*, meaning "to hope or believe." It has nothing to do with the verb *to wean* (see **wean**). E.g.:

 - "Bush has a certain self-confidence, as one Democrat put it, so that he doesn't exude an *overweaning* \[read *overweening*\] need to fill up every corner of every room with his magnificent presence, or be right all the time." Carolyn Lochhead, "The Name of the Game," *S.F. Chron.*, 12 Feb. 2001, at A25.

 - "The law of averages trumped Rivera's *overweaning* \[read *overweening*\] pride." Stephen Cannella, "Inside Baseball," *Sports Illustrated*, 23 Apr. 2001, at 78.

The word is sometimes misused for *overwhelming*. Jacques Barzun cites the example of a suggestion "greeted with *overweening* \[read *overwhelming*\] disapproval." *Simple and Direct* 30 (1975).

## Language-Change Index
