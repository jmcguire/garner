# oft-quoted

oft-quoted (= frequently repeated as an interesting, amusing, or authoritative phrase or sentence from a book, speech, etc.), a phrase dating from the late 1700s, has been predominantly so hyphenated since about 1830. The mistaken phrase \**off-quoted*, a mondegreen, has occurred sporadically in print since the late 1920s.

 - Current ratio in print (*oft-quoted* vs. \**off-quoted*): 857:1
