# obbligato

obbligato (= a musical accompaniment traditionally viewed as obligatory in performing a piece) has been predominantly so spelled since 1880—when *\*obligato* became just a variant spelling.

The word has two plurals—the Italianate *obbligati* (predominant from the early 1800s until the mid-1980s) and *obbligatos* (slightly predominant from the mid-1980s until 2015). See plurals (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*obbligato* vs. \**obligato*): 4:1
 - Current ratio in print (*obbligatos* vs. *obbligati*): 1:1.4
