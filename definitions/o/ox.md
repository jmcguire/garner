# ox

ox \(the bovine mammal\) is said to be the only word in the English language that forms its plural by adding -*en*. \(See Harry Bochner, *Simplicity in Generative Morphology* 53 \[1993\].\) But *brethren* and the dialectal *sistren* \(even *children*\) are formed on the same defunct principle: by the mid-1500s, English had discarded plurals formed with -*n* or -*en* in favor of the regular plurals formed with -*s* or -*es*. Today the regular plural \**oxes* is viewed as erroneous—as is the double plural \**oxens*. See plurals \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*oxen* vs. \**oxes*\): 388:1   
Current ratio in print \(*oxen* vs. \**oxens*\): 1,699:1
