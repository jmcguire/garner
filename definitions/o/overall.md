# overall

overall, a vogue term in the 1960s and 1970s, often functions as a sentence adverb \(especially since about 1950\). Many sentences would read better without it—e.g.:

 - "*Overall, Wada* \[read *Wada*\] says Toyota has doubled its engineering output over the past four years." Alex Taylor III, "How Toyota Defies Gravity," *Fortune*, 8 Dec. 1997, at 100.

 - "*Overall, Berea's statistics* \[read *Berea's statistics*\] speak worlds about the demand for affordable higher education; this year, the college accepted only 22 percent of its applicants." Tamar Lewin, "With No Frills or Tuition, a College Draws Attention," *N.Y. Times*, 21 July 2008, at A12.

See adverbs \(f\).
