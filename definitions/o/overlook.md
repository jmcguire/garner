# overlook

overlook; **oversee**; **look over**. To *overlook* is to neglect or disregard. To *oversee* is to supervise or superintend. To *look over* is to examine.

Although historically *overlook* bore the meanings of *oversee* and *look over* \(see *OED*\), the word has narrowed its sense in standard english. Today it's sloppy to use it interchangeably with *oversee*—e.g.: " ‘It will improve the quality of the water,' said Mark Norton, who *overlooks* \[read *oversees*\] projects for a joint powers agency." Vanessa Colon, "Underwater Propellers to Save Fish," *Press-Enterprise* \(Riverside, Cal.\), 31 Jan. 2003, at B1. See **oversight**.
