# violative

violative. The phrase *to be violative of* is verbose for *to violate.* E.g.: "This proposal *is too flagrantly violative of the First Amendment* \[read *violates the First Amendment too flagrantly*\] to merit anything but condemnation." Walter G. Markum, "Campaign Finance Reform? Don't Bet on It," *Virginian-Pilot* (Norfolk), 13 Oct. 1997, at B10. See be-verbs (b).

The *OED* records *violative* from 1856 at the earliest, but the word appeared more than half a century before in a famous Supreme Court case, *Marbury v. Madison* (1803): "To withhold the commission, therefore, is an act deemed by the court not warranted by law, but *violative* of a vested legal right."
