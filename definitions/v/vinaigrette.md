# vinaigrette

vinaigrette (= an oil-and-vinegar salad dressing), a French loanword dating from the late 1600s, is so spelled. In French, the word simply means "vinegar sauce." But since the mid-1800s it has infrequently been misspelled on the model of the anglicized *vinegar*—e.g.: "Whisk together the red wine vinegar, olive oil, salt and pepper, and remaining mixture of fennel, rosemary, and orange zest to make a *vinegarette* \[read *vinaigrette*\]." John Edward Young, "Up to Your Gills in Canned Tuna? Try Fresh," *Christian Science Monitor*, 26 Jan. 2000, at 17.

Similarly, the word is often mispronounced. It should be /vin-ә-**gret**/, not /vin-ә-gә-**ret**/. See pronunciation.
