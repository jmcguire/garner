# venerable

venerable = \(of a person\) worthy of being venerated, revered, or highly respected and esteemed, on account of character or position; commanding respect by reason of age combined with high personal character and dignity of appearance; \(of a thing\) worthy of veneration or deep respect. The word is overblown when used merely for *old*. E.g.: "*More venerable* \[read *Older* or, perhaps, *Senior*\] citizens may recall the days when the electric chair was trucked around the state so that executions could be carried out at parish halls." James Gill, "Taking Executions on the Road," *Times-Picayune* \(New Orleans\), 20 June 1997, at B7.
