# volitional

volitional; **\*volitive**. *Volitional* \(= of or belonging to volition \[i.e., an act of willing or resolving\]; pertaining to the action of willing\) has been the standard term since the late 1700s. E.g.: "Mary Ann Sandoval . . .testified that she believed Stuart's memory loss and behavioral problems were self-serving and *volitional*." Ginny McKibben, "Suspect Cleared for Slay Trial," *Denver Post*, 29 Oct. 1994, at B3. *\*Volitive* has long been a needless variant.

Current ratio in print \(*volitional* vs. \**volitive*\): 34:1
