# velvet

velvet; **velveteen**. *Velvet*, a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1300s, denotes a soft fabric with a smooth, dense pile and a plain backing—or something else resembling this fabric. The most typical fibers are silk, mohair, linen, and wool, often combined with rayon or some other synthetic fiber. *Velveteen* (also known as \**velveret*), dating from the late 1700s, is a less luxurious cotton fabric resembling velvet, sometimes combining cotton with other fibers. Cf. **satin**.

 - Current ratio in print (*velveteen* vs. *\*velveret*): 662:1
