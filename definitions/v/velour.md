# velour

velour (= a heavy cloth similar to velvet but less expensive), a French loanword dating in English from the late 1700s, was originally spelled *velours*—after the French etymon, with the *s* silent. The spelling *velour* became predominant as a singular noun about 1910, and today *velours* as a singular is but a variant. The spelling with *s* is reserved in standard english for plural uses. See gallicisms.

 - Current ratio in print (*velour hat* vs. \**velours hat*): 5:1
