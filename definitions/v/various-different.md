# \*various different

\*various different has been a fairly common redundancy since the 1700s. E.g.: "Also available is a map that lets you take a self-guided native-trail walk through *various different* \[read *various* or *different*\] parts of the gardens." Karen C. Wilson, "Native-Plant Demonstration Gardens," *San Diego Union-Trib.*, 28 Sept. 1997, at H13. If *\*various different occasions* means "a number of different occasions," then the better wording is *several different*.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*various different* for *several different*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*several different* vs. *\*various different*\): 10:1
