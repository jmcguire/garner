# vapid

vapid \(= flat, dull, and intellectually barren\) makes *vapidity*, preferably not *\*vapidness*. E.g.: "Whatever was once unique and involving about its music has been usurped by synth-pop *vapidness* \[read *vapidity*\]." Greg Kot, "Faithful Followers," *Chicago Trib.*, 23 Apr. 1995, at C7. The adjective is pronounced /**vap**-id/, the noun /va-**pid**-i-tee/—preferably not with a long /ay/ in the first syllable.

 - Current ratio in print \(*vapidity* vs. *\*vapidness*\): 8:1
