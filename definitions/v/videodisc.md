# videodisc

videodisc. The Associated Press and *The New York Times* stylebooks both make this and most other *video-* compounds one word <videotape> <videoconference>. But when preceded by *digital*, the two-word form is far more common in print—no doubt because of the ubiquity of dvds, popularly known as *digital video discs* \(but see **dvd**\). When the phrase appears without *digital*, the compound is usually made one word: *videodisc*. The variant spelling *\*videodisk* is not recommended. In the age of downloads, these terms are doubtless moribund, and this entry may read like horse-and-buggy stuff.

Current ratio in print \(*videodisc* vs. *\*videodisk*\): 8:1
