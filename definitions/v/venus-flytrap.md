# Venus flytrap

Venus flytrap; **\*Venus' flytrap**; **\*Venus's flytrap**. All three were in frequent use from the mid-1800s. Not until the 1960s, in AmE and BrE alike, did the form without an apostrophe establish itself as standard. The others are variants to be avoided in much the same way as flies should avoid the plant itself. See possessives \(a\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*Venus flytrap* vs. \**Venus' flytrap* vs. \**Venus's flytrap*\): 81:1.4:1
