# vice-, prefix

 vice-  is a prefix denoting someone who is the next in rank below an authority having the same designation without the prefix, and who can act in the place of that authority if necessary. Traditionally the prefix takes a hyphen, as in *vice-admiral*, *vice-chamberlain*, *vice-chancellor*, *vice-consul*, and *vice-regent*. But with the terms most commonly used, there is a tendency especially in AmE to make the prefix a separate word, as in *vice chair* (predominant over *vice-chair* in AmE since 1977), *vice chairman* (predominant over *vice-chairman* in AmE since 1940), and *vice president* (predominant over *vice-president* in AmE since 1946). BrE tends to retain the hyphen in most terms, an exception being *vice president*, which has predominated over *vice-president* in that variety of English since 2008.
