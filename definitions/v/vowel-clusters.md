# Vowel Clusters 

 Vowel Clusters  are not indigenous to the English language, although one finds them in our imported vocabulary, in words such as *giaour* \(= one outside the Muslim faith\), *maieutic* \(= Socratic\), *moueing* \(= making a pouting face\), *onomatopoeia* \(= the use of imitative or echoic words, such as *click*, *fizz*, *plop*, and *splash*\), and *queuing* \(AmE\) or *queueing* \(BrE\). In forming neologisms, especially by agglutination, one should be wary of clumping vowels together in a way that would strike readers as un-English. Even three consecutive vowels may have this effect, as in *\*antiaircraft*, which is better hyphenated: *anti-aircraft*.
