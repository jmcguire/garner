# violoncello

violoncello, not *\*violincello*, is the correct spelling for the bass member of the violin family. Not surprisingly, the word has often been misspelled through association with *violin*—e.g.:

 - "He used a *violincello* \[read *violoncello*\] for the body of the female figure." Robert L. Pincus, "Surrealist Max Ernst Just Having Fun with Sculpture," *San Diego Union-Trib.*, 19 July 1992, at E2.

 - "Edward Brewer is the keyboard artist, appearing with his wife, oboist Virginia Brewer, and Loretta O'Sullivan, playing Baroque *violincello* \[read *violoncello*\]." Robert Sherman, "Pulvermann Foundation in Fifth Concert Year," *N.Y. Times*, 19 May 1996, § 13, at 19.

 - "Selections will include Beethoven's Ninth Symphony, Mozart's ‘Marriage of Figaro' and Haydn's ‘Concerto in C Major for *Violincello* \[read *Violoncello*\] and Orchestra.' " "Music Briefs," *Knoxville News-Sentinel*, 12 May 2002, at G4.

Of course, the safest approach is to use the simpler and much more recognizable *cello* instead.

## Language-Change Index

 - *violoncello* misspelled *\*violincello*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*violoncello* vs. \**violincello*): 33:1
