# in excess of

in excess of, which grew in popularity after 1850, is verbose for *more than*, *exceeding*, or some other word—e.g.: "Within hours a storm with maximum wind velocities *in excess of* \[read *exceeding*\] a hundred miles an hour swept across southern England and Wales." Thomas Levenson, "At the Speed Limit," *Atlantic Monthly*, Mar. 1990, at 40.
