# individualize

individualize; **individuate**. These words, which have basically the same sense ("to make individual in character, to give individuality to"), have undergone differentiation. *Individualize* is the ordinary term—e.g.: "While the strategies for just how to prepay your mortgage can be *individualized* to fit your own cash flow, there are some other general rules to follow." Don Hunt, "Full Speed Ahead," *Chicago Trib.*, 8 Aug. 1997, at 1.

*Individuate* is often used in scientific contexts and in Jungian psychology in highly technical senses, and it should generally be confined to these uses. E.g.: "Not that the Bob Joneses and Jane Smiths of the world aren't *individuated*, as the Jungians say." Bill Tammeus, "Newt Should Have Known Enough to Be Wary of Guys with Goofy Names," *Milwaukee J. Sentinel*, 3 Aug. 1997, at 6.
