# intuit

intuit, a mid-19th-century back-formation from the noun *intuition*, is often useful—e.g.:

 - "Perhaps Scott McNealy—the avowed anti-intellectual who once told an interviewer that his favorite book of all time was *How to Putt Like the Pros*—simply *intuited* that the day would come when Sun and Microsoft would directly face off against one another." Randall E. Stross, "Sun's Secret Weapon," *U.S. News & World Rep.*, 26 Feb. 2001, at 49.

 - "Waking from a dream of a snake swallowing its own tail, he *intuited* the structure of the benzene ring, the central mystery of his scientific quest." Rob Brezsny, "Free Will Astrology," *Village Voice*, 10 Apr. 2001, at 22.

 - "Abakanowicz makes visible what is usually only *intuited*." Mary Thomas, "Sculptor Treats Art as a Sacred Creation," *Pitt. Post-Gaz.*, 6 June 2001, at E1.
