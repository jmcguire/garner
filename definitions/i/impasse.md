# impasse

impasse (= \[1\] a deadlock; or \[2\] a blind alley), a French loanword, is sometimes misspelled *\*impass*—e.g.: "Finally Brearley broke the *impass* \[read *impasse*\], taking the puck around the back of the Renegades' net and scoring on a wraparound shot at 19:01." Katrina Waugh, "Roanoke Fights Back in 2nd," *Roanoke Times*, 6 Dec. 1997, at C1. The pronunciation has long been anglicized: /**im**-pas/. In fact, the word is so well established that we hardly think of it as a gallicism.

## Language-Change Index

 - *impasse* misspelled *\*impass*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*an impasse* vs. *\*an impass*): 1,059:1
