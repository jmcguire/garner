# in prison

in prison. This idiom does not ordinarily take an indefinite article: *he is in prison*, not *\*he is in a prison*. But if an adjective modifies *prison* and the term therefore becomes more concrete, the article should be inserted—e.g.: "Mr. Bararella, now *in* \[read *in a*\] high-security prison, wears \[his wife's\] wedding band \[on a chain\] around his neck, traditionally a sign of mourning." Celestine Bohlen, "As Omerta Crumbles, the Mafia Changes the Rules," *N.Y. Times*, 11 Oct. 1995, at A3.
