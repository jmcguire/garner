# illusion

illusion; **delusion**. These words are used differently despite their similar meanings. An *illusion* exists in one's fancy or imagination. A *delusion* is an idea or thing that deceives or misleads a person. *Delusions* are dangerously wrong apprehensions; *illusions* are also wrong perceptions, but the connotation is far less dire. Cf. **hallucination**.

Sometimes *delusion* is misused as hyperbole for *illusion*—e.g.: "Houston Coach Kim Helton has no *delusions* \[read *illusions*\] about why the Cougars ended an 11-game losing streak by beating Southern Methodist 38–15." Mark Rosner, "Longhorns Had *Alternate* \[read *Alternative*\] Plan Ready," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 23 Oct. 1995, at C3.

For a misuse of *illusion* for *allusion*, see **allusion**.
