# inflection

inflection \(= \[1\] the way a person's voice varies in pitch; \[2\] the way a word changes its form to show a difference in use or meaning; or \[3\] one of two or more word forms reflecting such a difference in use or meaning\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled since 1790 in AmE and since 1958 in BrE. \**Inflexion* is a variant.

Current ratio in print \(*inflection* vs. \**inflexion* in World Englishes\): 16:1
