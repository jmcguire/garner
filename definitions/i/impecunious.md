# impecunious

impecunious \(= poor; penniless\) is sometimes misused in either of two ways. Some writers seem to think it means "unthrifty," as in *she was utterly impecunious with her earnings*. Other writers mistake the word as meaning "hapless, unfortunate," as in *the impecunious junior left to work on the assignment through the night*. For the antonym *pecunious*, see **pecuniary**.
