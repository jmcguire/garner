# impolitic

impolitic, adj. \(= not politic; injudicious; imprudent\), preferably forms the unusual adverb *impoliticly*. Some writers impoliticly make it *\*impolitically*—e.g.: "All these solicitations are protected speech and . . . the government may not discriminate among them—even when some of them are *impolitically* \[read *impoliticly*\] and counterproductively ‘surly, bilious, \[and\] argumentative.' " Stuart Comstock-Gay, "The Panhandler's Message," *Baltimore Sun*, 22 Sept. 1994, at A13.

*Impoliticly* predominates in BrE only, not in AmE. Yet the longer form is certain to invite miscues. See -ic/-ical. Cf. **politically**.
