# immediately

immediately, used as a conjunction in the sense "as soon as, promptly when," is obsolete in AmE but still occurs in BrE—e.g.: "He succeeded in persuading MPs to vote to restore the ban on benefits for all those who fail to claim asylum *immediately* they arrive in Britain." Paul Eastham, "MPs Slam Door on Bogus Refugees," *Daily Mail* (U.K.), 16 July 1996, at 11. In AmE phrasing, the word *when* or *after* would be inserted after *immediately*.
