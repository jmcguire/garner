# ill

ill. The comparative form of this adjective is *worse*, the superlative *worst.* The adverb is *ill*, *\*illy* being an illiterate nonword that is acceptable neither in formal writing nor in nondialectal informal writing. *Ill* itself acts as an adverb—e.g.: "There he had knocked a hole in the roof and poured in gasoline, a primitive technique *that illy fit* \[read *ill-fitting* or *that ill fitted*\] the Waldbaum evidence." Murry Kempton, "Standing by Convictions, Wrongly," *Newsday* \(N.Y.\), 19 Aug. 1994, at A8. For other adverbs with a superfluous *-ly*, see adverbs \(d\).
