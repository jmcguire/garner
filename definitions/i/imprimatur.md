# imprimatur

imprimatur; **\*imprimatura**; **\*imprimature**. The normal form for ordinary purposes is *imprimatur* \(pronounced /im-pri-**mah**-tәr/ or /im-**prim**-ә-tәr/\), meaning literally "let it be printed," from the formula used in the Roman Catholic Church by an official licenser, approving a work to be printed. This term \(now meaning "commendatory license or sanction; sponsorship"\) takes the preposition *on*—e.g.: "The announcement . . . puts Pena's *imprimatur* *on* a draft proposal that was announced two months ago." "Rocky Flats Cleanup May Speed Up," *Salt Lake Trib.*, 8 Aug. 1997, at A14. *\*Imprimatura* is a needless variant.

*\*Imprimature* \(= a print, impression\) is an obsolete term sometimes wrongly used for *imprimatur*—e.g.: "He produced the special under the *imprimature* \[read *imprimatur*\] of his Daddy's Krazee production company." Allan Johnson, "Standup Guy," *Chicago Trib.*, 26 Dec. 1991, at C15.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*imprimature* for *imprimatur*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*imprimatur* vs. \**imprimatura* vs. \**imprimature*\): 597:9:1
