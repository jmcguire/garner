# in future

in future. This phrase is formal BrE, perhaps through direct translation of the Latin phrase *in futuro* or perhaps because of the BrE pattern of usage without the definite article \<she's in hospital\>. AmE uses the definite article: *in the future*. Today, it predominates also in BrE. For the phrase *in the near future*, see **future**.

 - Current ratio in print (*in the future you* \[as an admonition\] vs. *in future you* in AmE): 7:1 1950 ratio in print (*in the future you* vs. *in future you* in BrE): 1:2.2
 - Current ratio in print (*in the future you* vs. *in future you* in BrE): 2.2:1
