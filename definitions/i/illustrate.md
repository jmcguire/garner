# illustrate

illustrate, in modern usage, means "to provide a good example of (something); to exemplify." In the following sentence it is used ambiguously: "Jennings's analysis *illustrates* the fallacy of accepting the theory too literally." The writer here wasn't claiming—as the sentence seems to do—that Jennings's analysis is itself a good example of "the fallacy of accepting the theory too literally." Rather, the sentence was intended to hold up Jennings's analysis as one that elucidates well the nature of this fallacy.

*Illustrate* is accented on the first syllable: /**il**-ә-strayt/.
