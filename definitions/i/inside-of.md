# inside (of)

inside (of). When used as a preposition or adverb, the word *inside* shouldn't typically be followed by *of* \<the ball was inside the 40-yard line\>. (See **of (b)**.) But as a noun, *inside* normally takes *of* \<the inside of the ball\>.

 - Current ratio in print (*inside the* vs. *\*inside of the*): 10:1
