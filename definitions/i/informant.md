# informant

informant; **informer**. Both terms are used in reference to those who confidentially supply police with information about crimes. *Informant* is now more than four times as common in AmE and BrE alike. The Evanses wrote that *informant* is neutral, whereas *informer*, which acquired strong connotations of detestation in the 1600s and 1700s, remains a connotatively charged term (*DCAU* at 245). Although that statement doesn't hold true for legal writing, it does in most other contexts. See inelegant variation.
