# \*irregardless

\*irregardless, a portmanteau word from *irrespective* and *regardless*, originated in early-20th-century AmE. Often decried and mocked as a semiliterate nonword, it is most common in speech. But it also sometimes finds its way into straight-faced print—e.g.:

 - "*Irregardless* \[read *Regardless*\] of the Big Ten outcome, Knight said he is gratified with IU's improvement over last season." Stan Sutton, "Hoosiers, with Help from Iowa, Could Share Big Ten Crown," *Courier-J.* \(Louisville\), 10 Mar. 1991, at C1.

 - "Pensions were awarded by the states in which veterans lived after the war, *irregardless* \[read *regardless*\] of which state the veteran served in." Laura C. Edwards, *Three Centuries in America* 205 \(1999\).

 - "He pledged to represent the voices of all Kansans, *irregardless* \[read *regardless*\] of who they voted for." Andrew Bahl, "Marshall Defeats Bollier to Win Senate Seat," *Topeka Capital-J.*, 5 Nov. 2020, at A10.

Given its spread in AmE, perhaps it was inevitable that the term would invade BrE—e.g.: "The venue already stood to be closed for a period this year *irregardless* \[read *regardless*\] of the pandemic." "Arts Centre Funding," *Norwich Evening News* \(U.K.\), 15 Dec. 2020, at 16.

Surprisingly, the barbarism occurs four times on a single page of a linguistics text \(here's one example\): "Compounds that were presented without a space received shorter gaze durations \(*irregardless* \[read *regardless*\] of how they were supposed to be spaced\)." Barbara J. Juhasz, Albrecht W. Inhoff & Keith Rayner, "The Role of Interword Spaces in the Processing of English Compound Words," in *Current Issues in Morphological Processing* 291, 305 \(Pam Frost et al. eds., 2005\).

Senator Josh Hawley, who seemed to encourage the insurrectionist attack on the U.S. Capitol building on January 6, 2021, gave a speech that day in which he excoriated the Pennsylvania officials for allowing mail-in balloting "*irregardless* of what the Pennsylvania Constitution says." Shortly after, A.B. Stoddard, the political commentator, remarked on national television: "Josh Hawley will be forever shamed on this day, not only for his objection to the certification of the Electoral College count, but also for his use of *irregardless* despite his Ivy pedigree."

Since 1975 Raleigh, North Carolina, has been home to an award-winning vegetarian restaurant named the Irregardless Café. Rather than imagining its customers to be more regardful of cuisine than of English usage, we might surmise that the name gets many people talking about the place.

The widely scorned term seems unlikely to move into standard english. Careful speakers and writers avoid it.
