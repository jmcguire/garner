# importune

importune is a verb meaning "to beg or beseech; entreat." It is also a needless variant of the adjective *importunate* (= troublesomely urgent) and an obsolete variant of *inopportune*. The intended meaning in the following sentence is not clear, but perhaps *inopportune* would have been the right word: "It is a minor comfort to learn that Soviet audiences in the 1950's and 60's coughed as *importunely* \[read *inopportunely*\] during concerts as their free-world counterparts." Bernard Holland, "Classical View: Horsepower in Place of History," *N.Y. Times*, 18 Feb. 1996, § 2, at 33.

## Language-Change Index

 - *importune* misused for *inopportune*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*inopportune time* vs. *\*importune time*): 171:1
