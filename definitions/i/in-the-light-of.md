# in the light of

in the light of; **in light of**. Both are clichés. *In the light of* is the more traditional phrasing dating back to the 1500s. Increasingly, in figurative contexts other than the set phrase *in the light of day*, the shorter form has come to be the standard form <in light of how much our bags weigh, it's understandable that we'd have to pay extra>.
