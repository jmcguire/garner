# impliedly

impliedly; **implicitly**. Though neither form is strictly incorrect, *impliedly* (answering to *expressly*) is awkward and characteristic of legalese. H.W. Fowler wrote merely that "*impliedly* is a bad form" (*FMEU1* at 260). Though unknown to most people, it is a favorite of lawyers. *Impliedly* is old, dating in the *OED* from the mid-1400s. Still, *implicitly*—long the usual term—is normally an improvement. See **implicit**.

 - Current ratio in print (*implicitly* vs. *impliedly*): 34:1
