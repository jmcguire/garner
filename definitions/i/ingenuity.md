# ingenuity

ingenuity once corresponded to *ingenuous*, and *\*ingeniosity* to *ingenious.* Through a curious historical reversal of the role of *ingenuity*, it came to mean "ingeniousness." *Ingenuousness* was the only term left to do the work of the noun corresponding to the adjective *ingenuous*. So although *ingenuity* appears to be the correlative of *ingenuous*, it no longer is.

## Language-Change Index

*ingenuity* as the noun corresponding to *ingenious*: Stage 5
