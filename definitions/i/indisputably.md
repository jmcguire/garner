# indisputably

indisputably \(= unquestionably\) is the usual word, not *\*indisputedly*, which is an odd hybrid between *indisputably* and *undisputedly* \(= without challenge\). E.g.:

 - "She is the mother of three sons, which *indisputedly* \[read *indisputably*\] makes her the only justice to have experienced pregnancy." "All Eyes on Justice O'Connor," *Newsweek*, 1 May 1989, at 34.

 - "The third is ‘Shadow of a Doubt' \(1943\), in which \[Teresa\] Wright starred—‘his favorite film,' Dr. \[Ruth\] Prigozy said, and *indisputedly* \[read *indisputably* or *undisputedly*\] first-class." Alvin Klein, "Scholarly Regard for a Master of Suspense and the Cinema," *N.Y. Times*, 17 Oct. 1999, § 14, at 15.

 - "As the composer of one *indisputedly* \[read *indisputably*\] great musical \(‘A Chorus Line'\) . . . , Hamlisch is still reaching for the sustained depth found in the mentors he often cites: Cole Porter, Richard Rodgers, Jerome Kern and so on." Andrew Adler, "Hamlisch Still Seeking the Depth of His Mentors," *Courier-J.* \(Louisville\), 25 Nov. 2001, at D3.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*indisputedly* for *indisputably* or *undisputedly*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*indisputably* vs. *\*indisputedly*\): 548:1
