# in

in; **into**. These prepositions aren't ordinarily interchangeable, and care must be taken in choosing between them: *in* denotes position or location, and *into* denotes movement. So a person who swims *in* the ocean is already there, while a person who swims *into* the ocean is moving from, say, the mouth of a river. There are many exceptions, however, especially with popular idioms <go jump in the lake>.
