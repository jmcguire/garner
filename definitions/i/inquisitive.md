# inquisitive

inquisitive; **inquisitorial**; \***inquisitional**. The two main terms are distinct. *Inquisitive* = (1) curious; given to questioning \<an inquisitive mind\>; or (2) unduly or inappropriately curious; prying \<inquisitive neighbors\>. *Inquisitorial* = (1) of, relating to, or involving someone engaged in an inquisition—that is, an official investigation; (2) of, relating to, or involving a system of decision-making in which the roles of prosecutor and judge are merged; or (3) of, relating to, or involving a secret criminal prosecution that is both intrusive and harsh. (To contrast *inquisitorial* \[in sense 2\] with *accusatorial*, see **accusatory**.) \**Inquisitional* is a needless variant of *inquisitorial*.

 - Current ratio in print (*inquisitorial* vs. \**inquisitional*): 16:1
