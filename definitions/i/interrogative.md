# interrogative

interrogative; \***interrogatory**, adj.; **\*interrogational**. *Interrogative* (= of, relating to, or resembling a question) is the standard term. E.g.: "Wimsey smiled at Harriet, an odd, *interrogative* smile." Dorothy L. Sayers, *Gaudy Night* 364 (1936; repr. 1995). The others are needless variants. In legal contexts, *interrogatory* is a noun meaning "a written question (usu. in a set of questions) submitted to an opposing party in a lawsuit as part of discovery" (*Black's Law Dictionary* 982 \[11th ed. 2019\]).

 - Current ratio in print (*interrogative* vs. *\*interrogatory* vs. *\*interrogational*): 46:15:1
