# indoctrinate

indoctrinate is sometimes displaced by *\*indoctrine*, an obsolete variant that has rarely appeared in Modern English. E.g.:

 - "If one generation of parents—that's all it takes, just one—could be *indoctrined* \[read *indoctrinated*\] with valid child-rearing concepts, those children *will* \[read *would*\] grow up to pass on those universal truisms to their children." Lendon H. Smith, "Where Have They Been?," *Health News & Rev.*, 22 Sept. 1993, at 7.

 - "African American women often are *indoctrined* \[read *indoctrinated*\] with the belief that there's something wrong, unattractive, about their curvy bodies, broad noses, full lips and kinky hair, the women said." Norma Martin, "Austinites Experience ‘Exhale,' " *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 24 Dec. 1995, at E1, E5.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*indoctrine* for *indoctrinate*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*indoctrinated* vs. *\*indoctrined*): 878:1
