# in the final analysis

in the final analysis; **in the last analysis**. Both clichés are likely to detract from your prose. Try to state the proposition without this tepid lead-in. The first of these phrases to gain popularity was *in the last analysis*, dating from the late 1800s. By the 1950s, speakers and writers had started using *final* instead of *last*, perhaps because of the cadence. No further analysis is needed here.
