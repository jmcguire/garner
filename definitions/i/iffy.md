# iffy

iffy (= uncertain) is a casualism dating from the mid-20th century—e.g.:

 - "Hair growth? It's *iffy*. Some say yes; others no. You would think that female hormones in the birth-control pills would reduce facial hair growth." Paul Donohue, "Immune System's Been in the News a Lot Lately," *St. Louis Post-Dispatch*, 15 Nov. 1996, Everyday Mag. §, at E2.

 - "We also believe Michael Jordan can wheeze, struggle, brick, shoot air balls, be frozen by Allen Iverson and depend on *iffy* officiating calls to win games he normally devours like a chew toy." Jay Mariotti, "Next Heroics for MJ Involve Taking a Seat," *Chicago Sun-Times*, 20 Mar. 1997, at 118.

 - "Indoors stuff is *iffy*. Outdoors stuff less so." Mark Lane, "Schools Under Fire for Trying to Tame COVID," *Daytona Beach News-J.*, 20 Aug. 2021, at D1.
