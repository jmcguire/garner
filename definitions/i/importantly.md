# importantly

importantly. This word is enjoying an odd vogue. Here, Meg Greenfield uses it puzzlingly: "But almost without exception, it seems, the thing \[a politician's prospective embarrassment\] comes back and *importantly* it looks worse and gets worse the second time around." Meg Greenfield, "We're Wallowing Again," *Newsweek*, 25 Apr. 1994, at 72. See **more important\(ly\)** & adverbs \(f\).
