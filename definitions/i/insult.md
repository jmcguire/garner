# insult

insult, in medical and scientific jargon, often means "something that disturbs normal functions; a trauma"—e.g.:

 - "American experience . . . could be useful in reminding \[Russians\] that government still has a vital role in repairing the cruel injuries inflicted by chemical and nuclear *insults* to the environment." "Russia's Ravaged Environment," *Boston Globe*, 5 Aug. 1995, at 8.

 - "The evidence of increased danger from teenage smoking is also important, the researchers said, because it's consistent with the idea ‘that environmental *insults* are most deleterious during breast development.' " Robert Cooke, "Study Links Smoking, Breast Cancer," *Newsday* (N.Y.), 13 Nov. 1996, at A4.

 - "Cognitive reserve \[is\] the capacity of the brain to withstand aging-related changes and other *insults* to the brain." Padmaja Genesh, "Seniors: Women and Dementia," *Red Deer Advocate* (Can.), 7 June 2019, Community §.

See **trauma**.
