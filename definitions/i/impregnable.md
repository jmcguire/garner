# impregnable

impregnable; **impregnatable**. Etymologically, these two words are unrelated—the *g* in *impregnable* having been added by Renaissance philologists who misunderstood the history of the Middle English *imprenable* \(= not able to be taken\). In any event, the meanings today are also quite distinct. *Impregnable* = not capable of subjugation by force; not subject to being taken by assault. *Impregnatable* = \[1\] capable of being made pregnant; or \[2\] capable of being permeated or saturated.
