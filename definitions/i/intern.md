# intern

intern; **extern**. An *intern* is an advanced student or recent graduate who temporarily works in a particular job to gain experience, usually for pay. (In the U.S., under federal law, interns must almost always be paid.) An *extern* is a college student who works in such a situation, usually without pay but for course credits. While *interns* are often full-time workers, *externs* are usually part-time. Before the 1930s, the spellings \**interne* and \**externe* were common.

 - Current ratio in print (*intern* vs. \**interne*): 12:1
 - Current ratio in print (*extern* vs. \**externe*): 3:1
