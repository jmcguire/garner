# inanity

inanity; **inanition**. As the noun corresponding to *inane*, *inanity* means "intellectual or spiritual emptiness or shallowness; vapidity." *Inanition*, by contrast, means "emptiness from lack of nourishment." *Inanition* sometimes gets misused for *inanity*—e.g.: "Elvis didn't have to appear in just ‘Elvis movies,' those formulaic monuments to cinematic *inanition* \[read *inanity*\], full of females, fistfights, forgettable songs—and little else." Mark Feeney, "Elvis Movies," *Am. Scholar*, Winter 2001, at 53, 54.
