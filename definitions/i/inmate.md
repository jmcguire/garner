# inmate

inmate. Strictly speaking, anyone who lives in a dwelling with others, whether involuntarily or not, is an *inmate* of that dwelling. The usual reference today is to a prisoner or to a patient in an asylum, other uses of the term \(such as in a boardinghouse or dormitory\) having become archaic. Even when used in contexts not involving institutionalization, the word still usually carries unpleasant connotations—e.g.: "The houses are large, drafty places where fires are slow to draw, meals are protracted, and the *inmates* often feel physically uncomfortable." Lesley Chamberlain, "Torments of Pretense," *L.A. Times*, 10 June 2001, at 14.
