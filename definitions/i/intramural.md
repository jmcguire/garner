# intramural

intramural = conducted within the limits of an organization or body, esp. of an educational institution. The term is misused when the sense extends beyond one college or university—that is, it is impossible to have a competition remain intramural if there are competitors from elsewhere: "They are also trying to determine if there has been any recent contact between Harvard and Dartmouth, like *intramural* sports." "Link Sought Between Sicknesses at Two Colleges," *N.Y. Times*, 6 Dec. 1994, at A12.

There is no counterpart *intermural* meaning "between colleges, etc." Instead, *intermural* is an exceedingly rare "dictionary word" \(attested in the *OED* only in early lexicons\) meaning "between walls."
