# illegal

illegal; **illicit**; **unlawful**. These terms are fundamentally synonymous, although *illicit* carries moral overtones <illicit love affairs> in addition to the basic sense of all three: "not in accordance with or sanctioned by law." Since 1800 or so, *illegal* has been the most frequently used, followed by *unlawful* and then *illicit*. During most of the 1700s, *unlawful* appeared in print somewhat more frequently than *illegal*.

*Illegal* is not synonymous with *criminal*, though some writers mistakenly assume that it is. Anything against the law—even civil statutes—is, technically speaking, *illegal*, but only violations of criminal law are *criminal*. See **legal**.

For two malapropisms involving *illicit*, see **illicit**.
