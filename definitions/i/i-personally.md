# I personally

I personally, a phrase that grew enormously in popularity during the 20th century, is usually prolix for a simple *I.* Occasionally, though, it legitimately contrasts one's personal opinion with a contrary stance or action <I personally don't care if you smoke, but the city ordinance says you can't do it here, and I can get fined if I let you>. See first person. For *I myself*, see **myself** & pronouns \(e\).
