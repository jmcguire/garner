# iris

iris \(= \[1\] a tall plant having long, thin leaves and large flowers that are usu. white, yellow, or purple; or \[2\] the round colored part of the eye surrounding the black pupil\), a Greek loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has predominantly had the anglicized plural *irises* from the 1880s in AmE and from about 1900 in BrE. Before that, the classical plural *irides* /**i-**ri-deez/ was predominant. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*irises* vs. *irides*\): 33:1
