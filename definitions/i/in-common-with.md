# in common with

in common with occasionally displaces *like* from its rightful place—e.g.: "Houdini, however, wanted to be special. *In common with* \[read *Like*\] many other entertainers of the time \(including Al Jolson and Irving Berlin\), he was the son of a rabbi." Andrew Rosenheim, "Escapes of an Egotist," *TLS*, 5 Nov. 1993, at 24. See **like \(c\)**.
