# icon

icon \(= \[1\] an image, likeness, or portrait; \[2\] a famous person who is thought to represent an idea or movement; or \[3\] a small symbol on a computer screen used to start a particular operation\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s (sense 1), has been predominantly so spelled for most of its life. For a time in the early 1900s, the spelling **ikon* surpassed *icon*, but not after the mid-1930s. \(*Ikon* remains the Greek and Russian Orthodox style.\) \**Eikon* has been another rare variant since the 1700s.

 - Current ratio in print \(*icon* vs. \**ikon* vs. \**eikon*\): 236:4:1
