# inculpatory

inculpatory; **\*inculpative**. The standard form is *inculpatory* \(= tending to incriminate\)—e.g.: "When the room was finally checked, police found no *inculpatory* evidence." Kristen Delguzzi, "Jurors See Scene of Hotel Slaying," *Cincinnati Enquirer*, 15 Nov. 1996, at C1. *\*Inculpative* is a needless variant. Cf. **culpable** & **exculpatory**.

Current ratio in print \(*inculpatory* vs. \**inculpative*\): 337:1
