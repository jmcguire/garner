# rabbinic

rabbinic (= of, relating to, or involving the writings or teachings of rabbis), a French/Latin loanword dating in English from the early 1600s, has been the predominant form of the adjective since about 1954 in BrE and 1964 in AmE. Before that, the variant \**rabbinical* was usual.

 - Current ratio in print (*rabbinic* vs. \**rabbinical*): 4:1
