# refugee

refugee; **evacuee**. *Refugee* \(= someone who flees home to seek safety\) originally denoted French Huguenots who fled to England in the 1600s and 1700s to escape religious persecution. The word has another \(rare\) sense, denoting a fugitive on the run. *Refugee* had lost most of its connotations of foreignness or truancy when Hurricane Katrina forced thousands of New Orleans residents out of the city in August 2005. But many of those who fled objected to being referred to as *refugees* in news accounts and political discourse. Almost overnight the word was displaced by *evacuee* \(= someone who withdraws from a place, esp. under an order from some authority\). See -ee.

The words are pronounced /ref-yoo-**jee**/ or /**ref**-yoo-jee/ and /ee-vak-yoo-**ee**/.
