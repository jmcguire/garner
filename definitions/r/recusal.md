# recusal

recusal; \***recusation**; **\*recusement**; **recusancy**; **\*recusance**. The preferred noun corresponding to the verb *recuse* (= to remove \[oneself\] as a judge considering a case) is *recusal*, though its earliest known use is only as recent as 1911. *\*Recusation* reigned supreme as standard english from the 1700s to the early 1970s, but mysteriously it is now almost unknown; it has become a needless variant. *\*Recusement* (not listed in the *OED*), though once common, is also now a needless variant.

*Recusancy* is a different word, meaning "obstinate refusal to comply." *\*Recusance* is a needless variant of *recusancy*.

 - Current ratio in print (*recusal* vs. *\*recusation*): 26:1
