# rabble-rouser

rabble-rouser (= someone who deliberately turns a crowd into a mob by inciting anger and violence), a compound dating from the mid-1800s, is so spelled and hyphenated. *Rabble*, of course, is a snarl-word that denotes the lowest classes of people. Since the mid-1960s, the phrase has sometimes been mistakenly written \**rebel rouser* or \**rebel-rouser*—granted, occasionally as a pun but usually just as a mondegreen.

 - Current ratio in print (*rabble-rouser* vs. \**rebel rouser* vs. \**rebel-rouser*): 194:3:1
