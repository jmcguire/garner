# regulatory

regulatory; **regulative**. Although both adjectives were once common, *regulatory* achieved vast predominance in AmE beginning in the 1920s and in BrE beginning in the 1960s. It's accented in AmE on the first syllable \(/**reg**-yә-lә-tor-ee/\), in BrE often on the third \(/reg-yә-**lay**-tә-ree/\). *Regulative* might generally be regarded as a needless variant, but in theology *regulative principle* is a set phrase.

 - Current ratio in print \(*regulatory* vs. *regulative*\): 37:1
