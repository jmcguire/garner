# raven

raven; **ravin**. For the verb meaning "to plunder, devour greedily," *raven* is the standard spelling <they ravened their meal>. *Ravin* is a variant spelling.

But for the related noun meaning either (1) "a violent plundering" <the utter ravin of the town>, or (2) "something preyed upon" <the town was their ravin>, *ravin* is standard and *raven* the variant. Perfectly logical and intuitive, no?
