# respect

respect. The phrases *with respect to* and *in respect of* are usually best replaced by single prepositions. E.g.:

 - "Clinton . . . has continued to enjoy stronger support from women than men even *with respect to* \[read *in*\] the Paula Jones case." Susan Estrich, "Will Clinton Stoop to Conquer?," *Denver Post*, 5 June 1997, at B11.

 - "Notices stating the action taken *in respect of* \[read *against* or *on*\] each licensee have been placed on the Consumer Credit Public Register." "Mortgage Lenders Warned by OFT," *Fin. Times*, 29 June 1995, at 8.

 - "Dublin's Philip McMahon and Mayo's Cillian O'Connor will not face any further action *in* *respect of* \[read *for*\] other incidents." Sean Moran, "Proposed Ban Likely to Rule Connolly Out," *Irish Times*, 1 Sept. 2015, Sport §, at 14.

Cf. **regard \(a\)**.
