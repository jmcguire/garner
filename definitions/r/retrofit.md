# retrofit

retrofit, n. & v.t. The noun *retrofit*—dating from the early 1950s—is a hybrid meaning "a modification of equipment or a building to include developments not available at the time of original manufacture or construction." The verb uses are known to be somewhat older—traceable to the 1940s. The past tense is *retrofitted*, not *retrofit*—e.g.: "The Tickells, 21, were driving the Veggie Van, a 3-ton Winnebago splashed with yellow daisies and *retrofitted* to run on used cooking oil." Connie Koenenn, "Sunday Brunch," *L.A. Times*, 21 Sept. 1997, at E3.
