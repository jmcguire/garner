# raja

raja \(= the ruler or chief of an Indian, Malay, or Javanese state or province\), a Hindi/Marathi loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled in BrE since about 1900 and in AmE since 1958. The older spelling, *rajah*, which strongly predominated in the 1700s and 1800s, went into retreat across World Englishes during the 1900s. Oddly, however, *rajah* has made a comeback in AmE since 2000.

Current ratio in print \(*raja* vs. *rajah* in World Englishes\): 1.2:1
Current ratio in print \(*raja* vs. *rajah* in AmE\): 2.2:1
