# reciprocity

reciprocity; **reciprocation**. *Reciprocity* = (1) the state of being reciprocal <over the years they achieved a satisfactory reciprocity>; or (2) the mutual concession of advantages or privileges for purposes of commercial or diplomatic relations <their reciprocity in according favors worked to each one's advantage>. *Reciprocation* = the action of doing something in return <a concession made without hope of reciprocation>.

Coupling either word with *mutual* creates a redundancy—e.g.: "We also witness a touching emotional resolution of the situation and learn of the long-standing *mutual reciprocation* \[read *reciprocity*\] between the two." Joan Hinkemeyer, "Ferroll Sams Authors 3 Hopeful Looks at the Human Condition," *Rocky Mountain News* (Denver), 28 May 1995, at A80. See **mutual**.
