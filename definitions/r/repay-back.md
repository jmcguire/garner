# \*repay back

\*repay back. Although this usage dates back to the 1700s, it is considered bad style because of the redundancy. Use *repay* or *pay back*—e.g.: "Students who obtain Stafford loans borrow at a reduced interest rate and wait until after graduation to *repay them back* \[read *repay them* or *pay them back*\]." Mike Bucsko, "Grove City to Stop Federal College Aid," *Pitt. Post-Gaz.*, 11 Oct. 1996, at A1.

Language-Change Index

*\*repay back* for *repay* or *pay back*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*pay back* vs. *\*repay back*\): 375:1
