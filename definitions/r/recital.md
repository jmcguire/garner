# recital

recital; **recitation**. These words overlap but are distinguishable. Aside from a (usually) solo musical or dance performance, *recital* may mean "a rehearsal, account, or description of some thing, fact, or incident" \<a recital of all the incidents would be tedious\>. *Recitation* usually connotes an oral delivery before an audience, whether in the classroom or on stage. Yet it is more often the general noun meaning "the act of reciting"—e.g.: "This continuing tension is realized in a series of formal tropes: angry monologues, *recitations* of dreams, discussions of cartographic representation of Greenland and what they call their ‘truth game.' " Ben Brantley, "Whimsy as a Tool to Deal with AIDS," *N.Y. Times*, 29 June 1995, at C11.
