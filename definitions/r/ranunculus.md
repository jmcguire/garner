# ranunculus

ranunculus /rә-**nәng**-kyә-lәs/ \(= a small shiny yellow wildflower known also as a "buttercup"\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, etymologically means "little frog." During the 1700s and 1800s, the anglicized plural *ranunculuses* predominated. During the 20th century, *ranunculi* /rә-**nәng**-kyә-lī/ became predominant in AmE and BrE. Since 2004, *ranunculuses* has come back to the fore in AmE alone. But of course, *buttercups* is far more commonly used.
