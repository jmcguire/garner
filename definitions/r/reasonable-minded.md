# \*reasonable-minded

\*reasonable-minded is a redundancy that sprang up in the late 1800s, first in BrE in the late 1860s and then in AmE in the late 1870s. It persists—e.g.:

 - "It's the kind of hard work that any *reasonable-minded* \[read *reasonable*\] person would go to extreme lengths to avoid." Arthur Hoppe, "The Secret Key to Fitness," *S.F. Chron.*, 3 July 1994, at 1.

 - "Operation Rescue turns off *reasonable-minded* \[read *reasonable*\] people when it harasses women at abortion clinics." Joseph Perkins, "Animal Rights Extremists Are Hurting Own Cause," *Rocky Mountain News* (Denver), 22 May 1997, at A68.
