# replying to your letter of

replying to your letter of; **referring to your letter of**. Commonly found in 19th-century and early-20th-century commercialese, these participial openers are widely condemned as weak and stilted. Also, they typically result in danglers. E.g.: "*Replying to your letter of March 7,* the report you inquired about is soon to be acted on by the standing committee." \(A possible revision: *The standing committee will soon act on the report that you asked about in your letter of March 7*.\)
