# resolution

resolution; **motion**. In parliamentary law, a *motion* is a proposal made in a meeting, in a form suitable for its consideration and action, that the meeting (or the organization for which the meeting is acting) take a certain action or view. A motion may relate to a procedural matter (such as a motion to extend debate or establish the method of voting); or it may be substantive (such as a motion to amend a pending proposal). A *resolution* is a type of parliamentary motion—technically, a kind of original main motion—that formally expresses the sense, will, or action of a deliberative assembly. A resolution is highly formal, often containing a preamble and one or more resolving clauses in the form, "*Resolved*, That . . . ."
