# rare

rare; **scarce**. In the best usage, *rare* refers to a consistent infrequency, usually of things of superior quality \<diamonds having more than three carats remain quite rare\>. *Scarce* refers to anything that is not plentiful, even ordinary things that are temporarily hard to find \<job opportunities are scarce this year\>. Writers sometimes misuse *rare* for *scarce*—e.g.: "Flowers are frequent, usually in the warmer months, but fruit is *rare* \[read *scarce*\] in the Tampa Bay area." Bette Smith, "Plant Profile," *St. Petersburg Times* (Fla.), 17 May 1997, at D3.
