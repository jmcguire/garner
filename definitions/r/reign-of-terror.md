# reign of terror

reign of terror (= a period when a ruler or government cruelly tyrannizes the population, esp. by killing political opponents), originally referring to the First Revolution in France (1793–1794), quickly came to have more generalized uses by the mid-1800s. The phrase is sometimes used in puns relating to floods <rain of terror> or harsh equestrians <rein of terror>—but these spellings also often amount to mere unwitting blunders.

 - Current ratio in print (*reign of terror* vs. *rain of terror* vs. *rein of terror*): 858:2.1:1
