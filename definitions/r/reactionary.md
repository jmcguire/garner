# reactionary

reactionary, adj.; **\*reactionist**; **\*reactionarist**; **reactive**. *Reactionary* \(= ultraconservative\) is the standard form. *\*Reactionist* and *\*reactionarist* are needless variants.

*Reactive* = \(1\) of, relating to, or characterized by reactions <Jones's reflexes made him an extraordinarily reactive athlete>; or \(2\) occurring in response to a stimulus <reactive depression>. Occasionally *reactionary* is misused for *reactive*—e.g.:

 - "A strong chairman should give Mr. Barley the push he needs to make the MPO a pro-active—rather than *reactionary* \[read *reactive*\]—player on transportation matters." "Get Moving in 1997," *Orlando Sentinel*, 5 Jan. 1997, at G2.

 - "Dan Footman, an outstanding athlete who tends to underachieve and is not a great *reactionary* \[read *reactive*\] player, will take \[an injured player's\] place." Vic Carucci, "Bills Better Be Wary of Cornered Colts," *Buffalo News*, 19 Oct. 1997, at B9.

Cf. **proactive**.
