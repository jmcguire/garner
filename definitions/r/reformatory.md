# reformatory

reformatory (= tending or intended to improve as a result of purposeful changes), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been the standard adjective since the mid-1800s. **Reformative*, an equally old word, has become a needless variant.

 - Current ratio in print (*reformatory efforts* vs. \**reformative efforts*): 1.3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*reformatory zeal* vs. \**reformative zeal*): 4:1
 - Current ratio in print (*reformatory influences* vs. \**reformative influences*): 1.1:1
