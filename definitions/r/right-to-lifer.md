# right-to-lifer

right-to-lifer (= an opponent of abortion rights) is journalists' jargon—and is often used as a pejorative. E.g.: "The cast of characters includes . . . Attorney General Dick Thornburgh, a strident *right-to-lifer* who took the questionable step of asking the court to reconsider *Roe*." "The Battle over Abortion," *Newsweek*, 1 May 1989, at 28. The term dates from 1970.
