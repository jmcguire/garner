# repetitive

repetitive; **repetitious**; \***repetitional**; **\*repetitionary**. The first two terms are undergoing differentiation. *Repetitive* generally means "repeating; containing repetition" <repetitive cadences>. It is a largely colorless term. *Repetitious*, which has taken on pejorative connotations, means "full of tedious repetitions" <a highly repetitious essay in need of pruning>. Neither word was in frequent use until the early 20th century; since 1915 or so, *repetitive* has occurred with much greater frequency than *repetitious*.

*\*Repetitional* and *\*repetitionary* are needless variants of *repetitive*.
