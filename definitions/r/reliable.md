# reliable

reliable, despite some earlier objections, is perfectly sound English. The word dates back to the 1500s but wasn't widely used until the 1800s, during the middle of which a furor arose over it. Critics thought it badly formed because the *-able* suffix, they said, works only with transitive verbs; that is, *eatable* means "able to be eaten," but *reliable* doesn't mean "able to be relied." It was also incorrectly denounced as an Americanism—this though it was born more than 200 years before Americanisms were ever talked about. Some writers therefore suggested that it should be *rely-on-able* or *rely-upon-able* \(analogous to *come-at-able*\). The critics' voices have long since been silenced, and nobody today pauses over the word. See -able \(c\).
