# reasonable

reasonable; **rational**. Generally, *reasonable* means "according to reason; sensible." *Rational* means "having reason." Yet *reasonable* is often used in reference to people in the sense "having the faculty of reason" <reasonable person>. When applied to things, the two words are perhaps more clearly differentiated: "In application to things *reasonable* and *rational* both signify according to *reason*; but the former is used in reference to the business of life, as a *reasonable* proposal, wish, etc.; *rational* to abstract matters, as *rational* motives, grounds, questions, etc." George Crabb, *Crabb's English Synonymes* 589 \(John H. Finley ed., 2d ed. 1917\).
