# replicable

replicable, not *\*replicatable*, has always been the standard form since the word emerged in the 1940s—e.g.: "Theoretically still required to shed all programming *replicatable* \[read *replicable*\] by a commercial channel, Alan Yentob's BBC1 thumbed its nose at Birtist teachings, notably via the Lottery." John Dugdale, "The Woof and the Smooth," *Guardian*, 2 Jan. 1995, at T14. See -able \(d\) & -atable.

Language-Change Index

*\*replicatable* for *replicable*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*replicable* vs. *\*replicatable*\): 240:1
