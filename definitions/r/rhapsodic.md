# rhapsodic

rhapsodic (= \[1\] of, relating to, or involving the recitation of an epic poem; or \[2\] written or spoken rapturously, with excited extravagance or overenthusiasm) has vastly predominated in print sources over its rival *\*rhapsodical*—in AmE since 1920 and in BrE since the late 1930s. The longer form is now a needless variant. See -ic/-ical.

 - Current ratio in print (*rhapsodic* vs. \**rhapsodical*): 6:1
