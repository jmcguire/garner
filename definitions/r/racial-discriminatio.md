# racial discrimination

racial discrimination; **race discrimination**. The first phrase predominates in print sources and is slightly better because, other things being equal, an adjective is preferable if it has the form as well as the function of an adjective \(hence *racial*\). But predictably, idiomatic English is not consistent: we speak of *racial equality* but *race relations*, and of *racial tension* but *race riots*. Cf. **sex**, adj.

Current ratio in print \(*racial discrimination* vs. *race discrimination*\): 10:1
