# rumbustious

rumbustious (= boisterous, rambunctious)—a term that sprang to life as an 18th-century Briticism—is sometimes misspelled *\*rumbustuous*. E.g.: "Harle makes a marvellous case for the lyrically reflective, eventually *rumbustuous* \[read *rumbustious*\] and jazzy three-movement saxophone concerto, launched at the 1984 Proms." Paul Driver, "Classical," *Sunday Times* (London), 15 June 1997, Culture §, at 26. Cf. **rambunctious**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *rumbustious* misspelled *\*rumbustuous*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*rumbustious* vs. \**rumbustuous*): 38:1
