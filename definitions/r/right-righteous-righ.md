# right; righteous; rightful

right, adj.; **righteous**; **rightful**. These terms are sometimes confused. *Right* = correct, proper, just. *Righteous* (= morally upright, virtuous, or law-abiding) has strong religious connotations, often of unctuousness. *Rightful* = (1) (of an action) equitable, fair \<a rightful solution\>; (2) (of a person) legitimately entitled to a position \<the rightful heir\>; or (3) (of an office or piece of property) that one is entitled to \<his rightful inheritance\>. Cf. **purposely**.
