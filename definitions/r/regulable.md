# regulable

regulable /**reg**-yә-lә-bәl/, meaning "able to be regulated; susceptible to regulation," has long been standard in AmE. It was in BrE, too, until the 1970s, when the longer *regulatable* became predominant in BrE by a significant margin. Even so, in AmE some regard the longer word as a blemish—e.g.: "Where the highway leads is cloudy, but the traffic keeps growing and does not seem to be readily *regulatable* \[read *regulable*\]." Walter Goodman, "At Age 9, Light-Years Ahead," *N.Y. Times*, 13 Apr. 1995, at C16. (The sentence would sound better if *readily* were changed to *easily*. See sound of prose.) See -able (d) & -atable.
