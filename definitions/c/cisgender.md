# cisgender

cisgender /sis-**jen**-dәr/ (= of, relating to, or involving a person whose gender corresponds to his or her sex at birth), dating from the 1990s, was invented as an antonym for *transgender*. The Latin element *cis-*, meaning "on this side of," normally contrasts either with *trans-* "across; on the farther side of" or with *ultra-* "beyond." Hence we have these antonyms:

 - *transalpine*/*cisalpine* (denoting which side of the Alps);

 - *transborder*/*cisborder* (denoting which side of a border);

 - *transatlantic*/*cisatlantic* (denoting which side of the Atlantic Ocean);

 - *ultramarine*/*cismarine* (denoting which side of a sea);

 - *ultramontane*/*cismontane* (denoting on which side of a mountain); etc.

Essentially, *cisgender* is a retronym used predominantly by advocates for transgender rights. See **gender** & **transgender**.
