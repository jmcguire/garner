# caveman

caveman (= \[1\] a hominid who lived in a cave thousands of years ago; or \[2\] insultingly, someone thought to be crude, rude, barbarous, violent, etc.) was originally two words in the 1860s, then predominantly hyphenated (about 1868 to 1895), then predominantly two words again (about 1895 to 1963), and then predominantly solid (about 1964 to present). Hence: one word.

 - Current ratio in print (*caveman* vs. \**cave man* vs. \**cave-man*): 17:1.5:1
