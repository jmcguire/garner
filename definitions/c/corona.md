# corona

corona \(= \[1\] a crown or crown-like object; \[2\] the shining circle of light seen around the sun, esp. when the moon passes in front of it during an eclipse; or \[3\] the top projection of wood, plaster, fiberglass, etc. that runs decoratively along the top edge of a wall, shelf, column, or other architectural composition\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has predominantly (though barely) made the anglicized plural *coronas* since the 1850s in AmE, and since the mid-1940s in BrE. The older plural, still often seen in scientific contexts, is the Latinate *coronae*. See plurals \(b\).

Current ratio in print \(*coronas* vs. *coronae*\): 1.8:1
