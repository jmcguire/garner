# commander-in-chief

commander-in-chief (= a country's high-ranking official in charge of all the military operations), a phrase dating from the mid-1600s, has been predominantly so hyphenated since about 1826. *Commander in chief*, predominant before that time, is a variant. \**Commander and chief* is a malapropism.

 - Current ratio in print (*commander-in-chief* vs. *commander in chief* vs. \**commander and chief* vs. \**commander-and-chief *): 13,557:11,182:135:1
