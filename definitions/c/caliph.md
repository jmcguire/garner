# caliph

caliph /**kal**-if/ or /**kay**-lif/ \(= the supreme ruler of Islam\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, derives ultimately from the Arabic word *khalifa* "successor," meaning one of Mohammed's successors as a secular and religious head of Islam. The word has been predominantly spelled *caliph* since the mid-1700s; \**calif* is a variant.

The cognate word *caliphate* denotes either the rank or reign of a caliph or the land over which he reigns. It is pronounced /**kal**-i-fayt/, /**kay**-li-fayt/, or /**kal**-i-fәt/. Through metathesis, the term is sometimes mispronounced by news announcers as if it were /**kaf**-i-layt/. \**Califate* is a variant.

Current ratio in print \(*caliph* vs. \**calif *\): 95:1

Current ratio in print \(*caliphate* vs. \**califate*\): 930:1
