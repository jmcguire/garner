# cost > cost > cost

cost > cost > cost. This is the normal inflection. But when the sense is "to determine the cost of," the past tense and past participle both become *costed*—e.g.: "Its plan to rebuild the Royal Shakespeare Theatre at Stratford, originally *costed* at £80 million, is also one of three Lottery projects still earmarked for Arts Council support." Penny Layden, "£50m Lifeline for Arts Gives New Hope to ‘Ailing' RSC," *Evening Standard*, 5 Jan. 1999, at 9. This usage is especially common in BrE; in AmE the common choice is *estimated*.

The past-participial adjective *costed* likewise appears frequently—e.g.: "Birmingham's National Exhibition Centre put in a full *costed* bid for a green-field site on the edge of the city." Chris Gray, "Charles Falconer," *Birmingham Post*, 8 Jan. 1999, at 13. See irregular verbs.
