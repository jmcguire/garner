# cat's-paw

cat's-paw (= someone used as the tool of another to achieve some discreditable end), a phrase dating in the sense indicated from the mid-1700s, is predominantly so written. *Catspaw* and *cat's paw* (which should be reserved for literal uses) are variant spellings. During the second half of the 1900s, *catspaw* looked to have achieved predominance, but not after the turn of the 21st century. The metaphor derives from the fable of the monkey that used the cat's paw to draw chestnuts from the fire.

 - Current ratio in print (*mere cat's-paw* vs. *mere catspaw* vs. *mere cat's paw*): 4:2.3:1
