# cure

cure; **heal**. Both words are commonly used in medical senses. To *cure* is to successfully treat or remove (an illness, disease, or disorder), especially with medicine <an antibiotic will cure that infection>. It is often used as a passive-voice verb meaning "to get well again" <I'm cured\!>. *Cure* is invariably transitive.

*Heal* may be either transitive or intransitive. As a transitive verb, it is essentially synonymous with *cure* but is used less frequently. As an intransitive verb, *heal* generally denotes recovery from wounds or injuries <that cut needs time to heal> <broken bones heal slowly>. It is the word more often used in figurative senses <time heals all wounds>, but at law one *cures* a title defect, delivery of nonconforming goods, and other legal problems.
