# -cast, Essay

-cast. On the analogy of *broadcast*, many 20th-century neologisms arose, such as *cablecast* (1975), *podcast* (2004), *radiocast* (1931), *simulcast* (1948), and *telecast* (1937). They are irregular verbs (like *cast* itself) that don't change in the past tense. Adding *-ed*, though fairly common, is incorrect. For individual treatments, see **broadcast**, **cablecast**, **radiocast** & **telecast**.
