# came near

came near \[\+ **present participle**\], in the sense "almost," is a casualism that can usually be replaced by *almost* \[\+ past-tense verb\]—e.g.: "Armed with might and mystique, the Cowboys *came near losing* \[read *almost lost*\] to the NFL's Tinker Toy team Sunday in Cincinnati." Frank Luksa, "Looking into the Whys of Cowboys 23, Bengals 20," *Dallas Morning News*, 1 Nov. 1994, at B2. The phrasing was especially common from about 1850 to about 1950.
