# causality

causality; **causation**. These words have a fine distinction. *Causality* = the principle of causal relationship; the relation of cause and effect <causality is a very large subject in philosophy>. *Causation* = \(1\) the causing or producing of an effect <multiple causation complicates the analysis>; or \(2\) the relation of cause and effect <the principles of causation weren't even considered>. True, sense 2 of *causation* overlaps with the meaning of *causality*. But generally that sense is best left to *causality*.

*Causation* should not be used for *cause*—e.g.: "Similarly, if affirmative action was the *causation* \[read *cause*\] of White male labor displacement, the unemployment statistics would reflect such displacement." Byron A. Ellis, "The Displacement Myth," *Baltimore Afro-Am.*, 5 Aug. 1995, at A5.
