# coxswain

coxswain; \***cockswain**. Both spellings are pronounced /**kok**-sәn/. In maritime use, the word denotes a crew member who is responsible for the ship's boat or one of the ship's boats and directs the work of its crew. In the sport of rowing, the word denotes the one member of the crew who does not row but is responsible for steering the boat, encouraging its crew, and selecting and modifying race tactics. Most dictionaries, American and British, correctly list *coxswain* as the main headword. It has greatly predominated since the 1700s. Cf. **boatswain**.

 - Current ratio in print (*coxswain* vs. \**cockswain*): 5:1
