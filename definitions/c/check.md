# check

check.

**A. And *cheque*.** Both denote a written order directing a bank to pay money to a specified person. The first, much older spelling is standard AmE. The second has been used by the British since the late 1700s. Still, the second spelling turns up occasionally in AmE.

**B. *Check in the amount of*.** Instead of this wordy phrasing <enclosed is a check in the amount of $75>, use *check for* <enclosed is a check for $75>. The long phrasing began to spread during the first decade of the 20th century.
