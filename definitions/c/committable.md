# committable

committable; \***commissible; \*committible**. The first has been the established form since the early 1700s. E.g.: "That leaves Mata, who has had many psychological evaluations since being extradited to Minnesota, in limbo. He is neither competent nor *committable*." Margaret Zack, "Man Is Incompetent to Stand Trial, but Also Uncommittable," *Star Trib.* (Minneapolis), 22 July 1997, at B1. The other forms are needless variants. See -able (a).

 - Current ratio in print (*committable* vs. *\*commissible*): 19:1
