# collaborator

collaborator. The phrase *\*fellow collaborator*, which sprang up in the 1950s, is a redundancy—e.g.: "According to longtime set designer and *fellow collaborator* \[read *collaborator*\] Catherine Martin, Luhrmann succeeds in leaving his mark on a film whose title insists only on the mark that ‘William Shakespeare' has left on the Romeo and Juliet legend." Courtney Lehmann, *Shakespeare Remains* 145 \(2002\).
