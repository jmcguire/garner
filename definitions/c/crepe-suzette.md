# crêpe suzette

crêpe suzette \(= a thin French-style pancake rolled or folded in a hot orange sauce, usu. served flaming in brandy\), a term dating from the 1920s, is ordinarily so written—with a circumflex over the first *e*. The English plural \(the usual name of the dish\) has predominantly been *crêpes suzette* since about 1940. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*crêpes suzette* vs. *crepes suzette* vs. \**crepe suzettes* vs. \**crepes suzettes*\): 8:3:1.5:1
