# confessor

confessor (= \[1\] someone who confesses; or \[2\] a priest who takes confessions), a term dating in English from the 1100s, has always been predominantly so spelled during the Modern English period. \**Confesser* is an infrequent variant. See -er (a).

In the name of Edward the Confessor (1003–1066) the word *confessor* has an unusual historical sense: someone who has been recognized by the church as having avowed his or her religion in the face of great danger, and adhered to it while in extremis, yet not having suffered martyrdom. By the way, Edward the Confessor wasn't a name he bore in his own age; it was applied first by medieval Latin historians before appearing in English in the 1600s.
