# \*couth

\*couth, a back-formation from *uncouth*, has long been disapproved by usage authorities. The *OED* records the word as a Scotticism meaning "kind, affable, agreeable, pleasant," from the 1300s. But its new incarnation \(in the sense "cultured, civilized"\) first appeared in the late 1800s as a direct antonym of *uncouth*. In short, some people consider *\*couth* an uncouth word. Its popularity ebbed over the course of the 1900s. Cf. **unkempt**.
