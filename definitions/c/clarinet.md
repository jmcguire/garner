# clarinet

clarinet \(the reeded wind instrument resembling a long black tube—sometimes referred to by the dysphemism *licorice stick*\), a gallicism dating in English from the early 1700s, was originally so spelled. For most of the 1800s, the spelling \**clarionet* \(a Latinate respelling\) was predominant. But toward the end of the century, *clarinet* once again established itself as the standard spelling, first in BrE \(from about 1885\) and later in AmE \(from about 1895\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*clarinet* vs. \**clarionet*\): 23:1
