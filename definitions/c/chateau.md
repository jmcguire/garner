# chateau

chateau (= a castle or large country house in France), a gallicism dating in English from the early 1700s, predominantly makes the plural *chateaux*—not the anglicized \**chateaus*. (See plurals (b).) Most often in AmE, the word does not take a circumflex over the first *a*—so it's *chateau*, not *château*. But in recent years, the diacritical mark has had a resurgence across World Englishes.

 - Current ratio in print (*chateaux* vs. \**chateaus*): 6:1

 - Current ratio in print (*château* vs. *chateau* in World Englishes): 1.1:1
