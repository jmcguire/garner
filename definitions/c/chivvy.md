# chivvy

chivvy /**shiv**-ee/ \(= \[1\] to cajole or nag to speed up, esp. in a pesky way; or \[2\] to pester or harass \[an adversary\] with frequent minor attacks\), a verb of uncertain origin dating in English from the late 1800s, has been predominantly so spelled in both AmE and BrE since about 1930. The older spelling, \**chivy*, is now a variant.

Current ratio in print \(*chivvied* vs. \**chivied*\): 3:1
