# conjurer

conjurer; \***conjuror; \*conjurator**. *Conjurer*—not *\*conjuror*—is the preferred spelling for the word meaning "magician; juggler." E.g.: "Though once considered the domain of alchemists and other soul-bargaining *conjurers* . . . , glasshouses, or greenhouses, soon became proof of a homeowner's modernity and wealth." Mitchell Owens, "At Home with Books," *N.Y. Times*, 5 Dec. 1996, at C1. *\*Conjurator* is an obsolete word meaning "one joined with others by an oath; a co-conspirator."

Current ratio in print \(*conjurer* vs. \**conjuror* vs. \**conjurator*\): 818:505:1
