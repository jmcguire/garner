# collect

collect is a verb sometimes loosely used in the press. As any lawyer will confirm, being awarded damages is quite a different thing from collecting them: "Under Thursday's ruling, plaintiffs could *collect* \[read *seek* or *receive*\] damages from local governments only if they proved that discrimination resulted not from the act of an individual but from an official policy." William Choyke, "High Court Backs DISD in Rights Suit," *Dallas Morning News*, 23 June 1989, at A1. Perhaps most local governments would be good for most judgments, but to use *collect* in this way is sloppy thinking about the law. *Receive*, which sounds akin to *collect*, is actually quite different because it connotes a giver (the jury).
