# conflict of interest

conflict of interest. Today the phrase "ranges from being a euphemism for the result of outright bribery to describing a situation in which one subject to a duty takes a position inconsistent with that duty." John T. Noonan Jr., *Bribes* 446 (1984).

Although the predominant phrase was the more logical *conflict of interests* until the mid-1930s, a shift had occurred by the 1960s: the set phrase is now *conflict of interest*.
