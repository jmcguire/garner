# construction

construction is the noun corresponding to both *construct* and *construe*. In law, *construction* usually corresponds to *construe*. You might think that the *construction of statutes* is the business of legislatures, since lawmakers *construct* \(i.e., build\) statutes. But *construction* in that phrase means "the process of construing," which is the business of the courts. For an extended discussion of confusion wrought by the fact that *construction* answers to both *construe* and *construct*, see Antonin Scalia & Bryan A. Garner, *Reading Law: The Interpretation of Legal Texts* 13–15 \(2012\).
