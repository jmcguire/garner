# capable

capable. The phrase *is capable of* \[verb \+ -ing\] can be put more simply—typically by writing either *can* \[\+ verb\] or *is able to* \[\+ verb\]. This bit of verbosity is especially common in sportswriting—e.g.:

 - "Talk to him for a while and you come to the conclusion that either he *is capable of being* \[read *can be*\] very calm in difficult circumstances or he's a very good actor." Kent Youngblood, "Two Men, One Opportunity," *Star Trib.* (Minneapolis), 27 Sept. 2001, at C10.

 - "Duhon said he thinks that Dunleavy *is capable of being* \[read *can be*\] an outstanding rebounder." Bill Cole, "Two Goals, Williams, Duke," *Winston-Salem J.*, 15 Nov. 2001, at F1.

 - "Quarterback Quincy Carter needs to continue to improve and prove he *is capable of being* \[read *can be*\] a starter in the NFL." Richard Justice, "Restoring Glory," *Houston Chron.*, 16 Dec. 2001, Sports §, at 1.
