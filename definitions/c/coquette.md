# coquette

coquette /koh-**ket**/ or /ko-**ket**/ \(= a flirtatious girl or woman\) is the standard spelling of the noun. *Coquet*, a variant spelling in this sense, is uncommon—no doubt because, in English, this term has historically been sex-specific. \(See sexism \(d\).) In fact, though, *coquet* was formerly common in the sense "a male flirt; a lady-killer."

But *coquet* survives \(barely\) as a verb meaning either \(1\) "to behave as a coquette," or \(2\) "to consider \(something\) jestingly but not as a serious possibility." Sense 2 isn't sex-specific, but sense 1 usually denotes women—e.g.: "The band is tight. Sharon's rich, sensuous voice *coquets* above the piano, the drums, the bass." Arthur Allen, "When It Comes to Twins, Sometimes It's Hard to Tell the Two Apart," *Wash. Post*, 11 Jan. 1998, Mag. §, at W6. The verb is inflected *coquetted* and *coquetting*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*a coquette* vs. *a coquet*\): 56:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*to coquet* vs. *\*to coquette*\): 3:1
