# carabiner

carabiner /ka-rә-**bee**-nәr/ (= a metal ring that mountain climbers use to connect and hold a freely running rope), a German loanword dating in English from the 1920s, was originally spelled **karabiner*—after the German etymon. In the early 1970s, *carabiner* became the predominant spelling. It is associated with *caribineer*, since a *carabiner* is also known as a "caribineer's hook." See germanicisms.

 - Current ratio in print (*carabiner* vs. \**karabiner*): 11:1
