# cookie

cookie (= \[1\] a small, flat, sweet cake; \[2\] someone both clever and successful; or \[3\] information that a website leaves in a computer), a term dating in sense 1 from the early 1700s, didn't come into frequent use until the 1900s. Before that time, the word was variously spelled *cookie*, \**cookey*, or \**cooky*. The spelling *cookie* wasn't firmly established until about 1940. In BrE, the crisp, crumbly type is typically called a *biscuit*, whereas the soft, large, gooey type imported from the U.S. typically bears the AmE name *cookie*.

 - Current ratio in print (*cookie* vs. \**cooky* vs. \**cookey*): 2,673:14:1
