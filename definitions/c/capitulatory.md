# capitulatory

capitulatory; \***capitulative**. The standard adjective corresponding to *capitulation* is *capitulatory* /kә-**pich**-ә-lә-tor-ee/. The form *\*capitulative*, which is much less common, is a needless variant—e.g.: "Solidarity activists and many independent economists maintain that authorities save their most effective weapon of the price campaign for last: *capitulative* \[read *capitulatory*\] wage hikes above the level of prices for any factory considered a potential trouble spot." Jackson Diehl, "Poles Await Yearly Rise in Prices," *Wash. Post*, 25 Jan. 1988, at A15.

 - Current ratio in print (*capitulatory* vs. \**capitulative*): 21:1
