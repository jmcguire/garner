# carrel

carrel (= a library cubicle) originated as a monastic term in the late 1500s, referring to a small study within a cloister. (This term, in the sense specified, was also spelled *carol*.) Not until the early 1900s did the library sense take hold. \**Carrell* has long been a variant spelling; it was a close contender for predominance until about 1960.

 - Current ratio in print (*carrels* vs. \**carrells*): 30:1
