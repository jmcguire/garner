# chartered plane

chartered plane, not *\*charter plane*, is the traditional form. One specially hires an airplane, ship, bus, or the like by a contract known as a *charter*. Once the contract is in place, the means of conveyance has been *chartered*. During the 20th century, speakers and writers increasingly dropped the *-ed*, but this is neither the best nor the most frequent usage—e.g.: "Scott McInnis, a Colorado representative whose home district is larger than Florida, rides tiny *charter* \[read *chartered*\] planes between several far-flung stops." "Sallying Fourth," *N.Y. Times*, 29 June 1997, § 6, at 13.
