# cutlass

cutlass (= a short sword with a curved blade—associated esp. with pirates), a French loanword dating in English from the late 1500s, has gone through several metamorphoses. The *OED* says that "the original *coutle-as*, *coutel-ace*, has undergone many perversions in English under the influence of popular etymology \[see etymology (d)\], which has transformed the first part into *cuttle*, *curtal*, *curtle*, *curt*, *cut*, and the second into *ax*, *axe*. A later change has made *cutlass* into *cut-lash*." Since about 1740, however, the standard spelling has been *cutlass*. The only variant left standing is \**cutlas*, which should be given a merciful swipe.

 - Current ratio in print (*cutlass* vs. \**cutlas*): 134:1
