# coffee klatch

coffee klatch. *Kaffeeklatsch,* a German loanword borrowed in the late 1800s, means "a coffee-drinking group that engages in leisurely conversation." It has been anglicized to *coffee klatch*, which since the 1960s has been standard in AmE. (The term is little known in tea-drinking Britain.) In English-speaking contexts, *\*kaffeeklatsch* (despite its being the etymon) is a mere variant—as is *\*coffee klatsch*. See germanicisms.
