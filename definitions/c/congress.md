# Congress

Congress does not require an article, except in references to a specific session <the 104th Congress>. Although some congressional insiders use the phrase, *\*the Congress* is a quirk to be avoided in polished prose. The possessive form is preferably *Congress's*—e.g.: "*Congress's* enumerated powers include the power to ‘establish Post Offices and post Roads.' " Mehmet K. Konar-Steenberg, "Can Government Do That?," *St. Paul Pioneer Press*, 3 Oct. 2021, at A17. See possessives \(a\). Cf. **Parliament**.
