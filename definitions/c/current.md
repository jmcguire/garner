# current

current, n.; **currant**. A *current* is (1) a steady flow of a fluid element, such as water, air, or electricity, in a definite direction \<wind currents\>, or (2) metaphorically, the movement of something intangible \<the current of time\> \<the current of conversation\>. A *currant* is a fruit, either (1) a very small raisin produced from a Mediterranean variety of seedless grapes, or (2) the dried, edible berry of a Eurasian shrub. As homophones, the words are occasionally confounded—e.g.:

 - "Some \[fruits\] to consider include apples, apricots, banana chips, blueberries, cranberries, *currents* \[read *currants*\], figs, ginger, mango, pears, peaches, plums, raisins, and strawberries." Maurie Markman et al., *Cancer Nutrition and Recipes for Dummies* 248 (2013).

 - "They had nothing to do but stare at ocean *currants* \[read *currents*\]." D.L. Faughn & S.D. Schakel, *Below Gravity III: The Resurrection* 56 (2015).
