# choosy

choosy (= highly selective in considering what is acceptable), an Americanism dating from the mid-1800s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1904 in AmE—and since about 1930 in BrE (which was late to adopt the term at all). Earlier, \**choosey* had been just as common as *choosy* in both major varieties of English. But then picky users of the language started avoiding it.
