# croque monsieur

croque monsieur; **croque madame**. *Croque monsieur* /krohk mә-**syә**/ (= the French-style toasted or fried sandwich with ham and cheese), a French loan dating from 1915, denotes a dish reputedly first served in Paris in 1910. About 45 years later, the correlative term *croque madame* /krohk mә-**dam**/ (same with a poached or fried egg on top) was devised—appearing, curiously enough, in English before French, as far as anyone knows.
