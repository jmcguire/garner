# capacity

capacity; **capability**. These words overlap, but each has its nuances. *Capacity* = the power or ability to receive, hold, or contain <the jar was filled to capacity>. Figuratively, it refers to mental faculties in the sense "the power to take in knowledge" <mental capacity>. In law, it often means "legal competency or qualification" <capacity to enter into a contract>. *Capability* = \(1\) power or ability in general, whether physical or mental, esp. of a challenging nature <he has the capability to play first-rate golf>; or \(2\) the quality of being able to use or be used in a specified way <nuclear capabilities>. See **ability** & **incapacitation**.
