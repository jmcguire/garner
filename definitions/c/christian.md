# Christian

Christian, n., appears unfortunately in contexts in which it seems to be used synonymously with *fundamentalist right-winger*. A letter to the editor in *The New York Times* quite rightly objects: "I was disappointed to read a headline that began ‘As Christians Pull the G.O.P. to the Right' \(news article, June 27\). Such broad-brush characterizations \[are misleading\]. . . . Liberals proud to be Christian include Eugene McCarthy, George McGovern, the Browns of California, Jimmy Carter, Mario Cuomo and the Rev. Jesse Jackson. Of course, it is also true that certain other Christians have heard their religious beliefs calling them to more conservative causes. But treating this one point on the spectrum as if it stood for a Christian monolith is as offensive as it is misleading." Letter of A.G. Fortunato, "Don't Use ‘Christian' as a Synonym for the Right-Wing Fringe," *N.Y. Times*, 10 July 1994, at E18.
