# coordinate

coordinate, adj., n. & vb., a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1600s, was originally solid but went through a 200-year period of being almost uniformly hyphenated (**co-ordinate*\). The term was then solidified again, first in AmE by 1900 and then in BrE by 1970. For a time in the 1900s, some American stylesheets specified that a diaeresis should be used over the second *o* \(\**coördinate*\), but this convention never really took hold except in the pages of *The New Yorker*. Today it looks quaint.

The same points hold true for the nouns *coordination* and *coordinator*.

Current ratio in print \(*coordinate* vs. \**co-ordinate* vs. \**coördinate*\): 729:32:1
