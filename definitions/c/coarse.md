# coarse

coarse, adj. The word *coarse* (= \[1\] inferior in quality; or \[2\] unrefined, rude, and vulgar) is sometimes misspelled *course* (through confusion with the noun, of course). It's a vulgar, unrefined instance of word-swapping—e.g.:

 - "Allow to cool, peel skin off and grate potato with *course* \[read *coarse*\] grater." "Chef's Choice," *Newcastle Herald*, 14 Apr. 1999, at 32.

 - "Since it was developed last spring, ‘Action' has raised eyebrows among insiders for its *course* \[read *coarse*\] language and references to sex." Richard Huff, " ‘Action' Up for Tough Rating," *Daily News* (N.Y.), 22 July 1999, at 104.

 - "I wrapped a bit of *course* \[read *coarse*\] sandpaper around a pencil and rubbed it smooth as glass." Bryan Hendricks, "New Life for a Classic," *Ark. Democrat-Gaz.*, 2 Sept. 2018, at 26.

Originally, it is true, *coarse* and *course* were the same word. But the difference in spelling and in meaning emerged in the 1700s, and the words have long since gone their separate ways.
