# ceiling

ceiling, used in the sense of "maximum," is in itself unobjectionable but can sometimes lead to unfortunate mangled metaphors. E.g.: "The task force recommended *increasing* the *ceilings.*" One *raises* a ceiling rather than *increases* it.

An English writer on usage quotes a preposterous example about "a ceiling price on carpets." In using words figuratively, of course, one must keep in mind their literal meanings. See metaphors.
