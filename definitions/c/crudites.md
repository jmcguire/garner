# crudités

crudités. The raw vegetables often served with a dip are called *crudités*. Calling them *crudities* \(= things that are rough and unrefined\) is scandalously unrefined, and the chef might not serve you. E.g.:

 - "The Blythswood Square menu offers an excellent range of afternoon teas, including . . . the Tea Tox Afternoon Tea, consisting of delicious healthy options such as vegetable *crudities* \[read *crudités*\] and a detox salad." Sophie Hughes, "Britain's Best Teas," *Daily Telegraph*, 13 Aug. 2014, Lifestyle §.

 - "I also liked this hybrid between hummus and guacamole for a new dip to serve with fresh vegetable *crudities* \[read *crudités*\], crackers, or tortilla chips." Nancy Lindahl, "'Tis Now the Season of Pointy Green Things," *Marin Independent J.* \(Cal.\), 28 Apr. 2015, Features §.
