# connoisseur

connoisseur (= an authority, esp. on matters of taste) is best pronounced in three syllables (/kon-ә-**s**ʊ**r**/) to rhyme with *sir.* As pronunciation connoisseur Charles Harrington Elster puts it, "there is no *sewer* in *connoisseur*." *BBBM* at 105. Although traditionally a noun \<a wine connoisseur\> \<a connoisseur of Flemish art\>, it is often used attributively (that is, as an adjective)—e.g.: "By the time a marathon of bidding ended early Saturday morning, a record $755,185 had been paid for *connoisseur* \[or *fine*\] wines, trips to exotic destinations, banquet meals to be cooked by star chefs and a broad selection of what were grouped as ‘luxury items.' " William Rice, "A Rich Toast to Lyric," *Chicago Trib.*, 26 Feb. 2003, Good Eating §, at 2.
