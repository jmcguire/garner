# conceptualize

conceptualize, a 19th-century coinage, is often a grandiose equivalent of *conceive*, *envision*, *see*, *think*, *understand*, or *visualize*—e.g.:

• "Like Veblen, Innis *conceptualizes* \[read *conceives of* or, better, *sees*\] the price system as an active social force." Fletcher Baragar, "The Influence of Thorstein Veblen on the Economics of Harold Innis," 30 *J. Econ. Issues* 667 \(1996\).

• "Class 29 is seeking an artist or landscape architect to *conceptualize* \[read *design*\] and fabricate the monument." Amanda Purser, "Local Briefs," *Pensacola News J.*, 14 Aug. 2015, at A4.

• "No one on either side \[of the Civil War\] could possibly have imagined what lay in store for them over the next 24 hours or *conceptualize the implications* \[read *envision the aftermath* or, perhaps, *understand the repercussions*\]." Edward M. Gilbreth, "Prelude to Civil War Battle," *Post & Courier* \(Charleston, S.C.\), 23 Sept. 2021, at A3. \(The sentence also has a tense problem: \**could have imagined or conceptualize*. \[See tenses.] The best cure might be to use present tense: *could imagine or . . . .*\)
