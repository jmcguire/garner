# Court of St. James

Court of St. James = the British royal court. Although one sometimes encounters *Court of St. James's*, the more usual form is *Court of St. James*. E.g.: "In 1938, Mrs. Kennedy accompanied her husband to the Court of St. James and was formally welcomed by King George VI." Robert D. McFadden, "Rose Kennedy, Political Matriarch, Dies at 104," *N.Y. Times*, 23 Jan. 1995, at A1, B7. Despite appearing to be a redundant double possessive, *Court of St. James's* is arguably accurate because the former seat of the British court in London is St. James's Palace. So in *Court of St. James's*, the term *Palace* has historically been an understood word at the end. \(Cf. possessives \(d\).) But that form has not predominated since the 1700s.
