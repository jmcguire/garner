# chest of drawers

chest of drawers, an old-fashioned equivalent of *dresser* or *bureau*, is sometimes mistakenly morphed into *\*chester drawers*—e.g.: "\[The following items were stolen:\] snow blower, lawn mower, weed eater, table, *chester* \[read *chest of*\] drawers, three racks, love seat, couch, bike, value over $3,800, reported Monday." "The Record," *Omaha World-Herald*, 28 May 1993, at 12. This error has been characterized as "orthographically represent\[ing\] a common pronunciation of *chest of drawers*." Allison Burkette, "The Story of Chester Drawers," 76 *Am. Speech* 139, 141 \(2001\).

In a similar vein, *\*Chip and Dale furniture* can sometimes be found in classified ads.

*\*chester drawers* for *chest of drawers*: Stage 1
