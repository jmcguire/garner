# confit

confit; **confiture**. Both derive from the French verb *confire*, meaning "to prepare." A *confit* /ko*n*-**fee**/ is a preparation of meat or poultry stewed in its own fat with seasonings and then congealed. The retained French pronunciation reflects the fact that the word *confit* did not enter the English language until the mid-1900s. A *confiture* /**kon**-fi-chʊr/ is a confection of candied fruit—a preserve or jam. The pronunciation was anglicized shortly after the word entered English in the early 1800s.
