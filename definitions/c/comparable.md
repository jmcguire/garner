# comparable

comparable; **comparative**. *Comparable* is stressed on the first syllable (/**kom**-pә-rә-bәl/); *comparative* is stressed on the second syllable (/kәm-**pa**-rә-tiv/ or /kәm-**pair**-ә-tiv/). *Comparable* = capable of being compared; worthy of comparison \<comparable salaries\>. *Comparative* = (1) of, relating to, or involving comparison \<a comparative discourse on economics\>; (2) involving comparison \<the field of comparative religion\>; or (3) estimated by comparison \<comparative distances\>.

Occasionally *comparative* is used where *comparable* is called for—e.g.: "This industry, from the capital standpoint, is quite *comparative with* \[read *comparable to*\] the banking industry." John M. Boyle, "The Role of Capital in the Banking Industry," *Am. Banker*, 2 Dec. 1980, at 4. Though the *OED* documents this use of *comparative* with four examples ranging from the early 1600s to the early 1800s, it labels the usage obsolete.

For a common mispronunciation of *comparable*, see pronunciation (b).
