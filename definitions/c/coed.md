# coed

coed. This characteristically AmE word \(which has been traced back to 1875\) is quite acceptable as an adjective <coed dormitory>, but less so as a noun <a 23-year-old coed>. Why? Two reasons: \(1\) the noun use almost invariably denotes a female student in a way that strikes many as sexist \(men are just called *students*\), and \(2\) the usage dates back to the time when female college students were relatively rare. See sexism \(e\).

In any event, the word has long been solid—not hyphenated \(as it predominantly was before 1950\).
