# chancellery

chancellery /**chan**-sә-lә-ree/ or /**chan**-sәl-ree/ or, in BrE, /**chahn**-/ \(= \[1\] the building that houses a chancellor's office; \[2\] collectively, the officials who work in a chancellor's office; or \[3\] the office or staff of an embassy or consulate\), a term dating from the 1300s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1840. Before that, \**chancellory* and \**chancellary* \(both of which still sometimes appear\) occurred with some frequency.

 - Current ratio in print \(*chancellery* vs. \**chancellory* vs. \**chancellary*\): 121:5:1
