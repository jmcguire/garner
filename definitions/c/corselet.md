# corselet

corselet /**kor**-slәt/ \(= \[1\] archaically, a piece of armor covering one's trunk; or \[2\] a woman's undergarment comprising both girdle and brassiere\), a term dating in sense 1 from the 1500s, has been predominantly so spelled since 1880. \**Corslet* is a variant spelling for sense 1, \**corselette* for sense 2 \(since about 1920, when it was popularized as a brand name\). Of course, the Americanism \**corselette* puts the accent on the final syllable. See diminutives \(f\).

Current ratio in print \(*corselet* vs. \**corslet* vs. \**corselette*\): 49:32:1
