# consulate

consulate; **consulship**. *Consulate* = the office, term of office, jurisdiction, or residence of a consul. *Consulship* = the office or term of office of a consul. *Consulate* is the more common and \(therefore\) the broader term. *Consulship* may be useful in conveying one's meaning precisely.

Because *consulate* most often means "the office or official premises of a consul," the phrase *\*office of the consulate* is a redundancy—e.g.: "Starting Tuesday, people wishing to pay their respects may sign the book in the *offices of the consulate* \[read *consulate* or *offices of the consul*\], on the 13th floor of the south tower of the building, at 400 N. Michigan." Brenda Warner Rotzoll, "Chicago Bids Farewell," *Chicago Sun-Times*, 1 Sept. 1997, at 3.
