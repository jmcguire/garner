# carte blanche

carte blanche; \****carta blanca***. The French form, *carte blanche* \(= free permission\), has been usual since the early 1700s—not the Italian *\*carta blanca*, which in English is a needless variant. The phrase, meaning literally "a blank sheet or white card," does not take an article—e.g.: "The team's owner gave the coach *carte blanche* to trade or waive players." It is pronounced /kahrt **blahnsh**/ or /**blahnch**/. The plural is *cartes blanches* \(/kahrt\(s\) **blahnsh**/ or /**blahnch**/\).

Current ratio in print \(*carte blanche* vs. \**carta blanca*\): 399:1
