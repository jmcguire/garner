# catharsis

catharsis /kә-**thahr**-sis/ (= the process of purging strong or violent emotions by expressing them through writing, talking, acting, art, etc.), borrowed into English in the late 1700s, is a Latinized spelling of the Greek term *katharsis*. In English, the overwhelming preference is for the spelling *catharsis*, but some writers—perhaps to emphasize the technical, psychological, or poetic use of the term, as opposed to the watered-down popular sense (of "getting it out of your system")—use the Greek spelling.

 - Current ratio in print (*catharsis* vs. *katharsis*): 22:1
