# carabineer

carabineer /ka-rә-bi-**neer**/ \(= a cavalry soldier armed with a carbine—that is, with a short light rifle\), a French loanword dating in English from the late 1600s, has almost always been predominantly so spelled. The French spelling **carabinier* has been used as well, but it has always been just a variant in English—as has \**carbineer*.

The Italian word *carabiniere*, first used in English in the mid-1800s, denotes a member of the Italian national police force.
