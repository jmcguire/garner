# channel

channel, vb.; \***channelize**. Because no real differentiation has developed between these terms, *\*channelize* ought to be branded a needless variant. *Channel*, the usual term, means \(1\) "to form channels in, to groove"; \(2\) "to guide"; or \(3\) "to act as a medium through whom the dead \(or absent\) supposedly speak." It makes *channeled* and *channeling* in AmE, *channelled* and *channelling* in BrE. See spelling \(c\).

Current ratio in print \(*channeled* vs. *\*channelized*\): 14:1
