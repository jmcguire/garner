# \*copartner

\*copartner need not exist alongside *partner*. The joint relationship is implicit in *partner* itself: one partner entails the existence of one or more others. Because *\*copartner* adds nothing to the language, it should be avoided as a needless variant. E.g.: "In her firm, which she shares with Amy Newman, Rakinic feels it's a plus having a woman as a *copartner* \[read *partner*\]." Leslie M. Clouden, "Suburbs Are a Welcome Home for Women Out on Their Own," *Legal Intelligencer*, 10 July 1996, at S1. Cf. **coequal**.

Language-Change Index

*\*copartner* for *partner*: Stage 1
