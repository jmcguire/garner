# colossus

colossus \(= \[1\] a gigantic statue; or \[2\] someone or something of great importance, power, or size\), a Latin/Greek loanword dating in English from the 1300s, has predominantly formed the classical plural *colossi*, not \**colossuses*, since the late 1700s. But during the mid-1700s, \**colossuses* was the more frequent form. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*colossi* vs. \**colossuses*\): 12:1
