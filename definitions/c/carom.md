# carom

carom /**ka**-rәm/ \(= to rebound after a glancing blow\) makes *caromed* and *caroming* in AmE and BrE alike. The variant spelling \**carrom* has never been predominant.

As a noun \(dating from the late 1700s\), *carom* denotes any of various billiard shots involving a rebound—depending on the game being played. By extension, the noun can denote any type of rebound after an impact.

For the possible influence of *carom* on the verb *careen*, see the final paragraph of **careen**.

Current ratio in print \(*carom* vs. \**carrom*\): 4:1

Current ratio in print \(*caromed* vs. \**carommed*\): 1,179:1
