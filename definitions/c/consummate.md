# consummate

consummate as an adjective is pronounced /kәn-**sәm**-it/, and as a verb /**kon**-s\[y\]ә-mayt/. To pronounce the word /**kon**-s\[y\]ә-mәt/ as an adjective is acceptable, but that pronunciation has long been considered an inferior one.

*Consummate* is sometimes misspelled *\*consumate*—e.g.: "Widely regarded as a *consumate* \[read *consummate*\] professional and bridge builder with a reputation for mediating potentially explosive disputes, Scott, 59, said yesterday he is leaving to spend more time with his family." William K. Rashbaum, "No. 2 City Cop Plans to Retire in December," *Newsday* \(N.Y.\), 27 Oct. 1994, at A62.

## Language-Change Index

 - *consummate* misspelled *\*consumate*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*consummate* vs. \**consumate*\): 250:1
