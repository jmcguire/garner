# caravanserai

caravanserai \(= in eastern countries, an inn with a large open central area where groups of travelers with animals can stay\) has been predominantly so spelled since the 1700s. The most common variants are *\*caravansary* \(predominant in AmE in the late 1800s\) and *\*caravansery*. *Caravanserai* is pronounced /kar-ә-**van\[t\]**-sә-rī/. Unsurprisingly, the word is etymologically related to the modern *caravan*.

Current ratio in print \(*caravanserai* vs. *\*caravansary* vs. *\*caravansery*\): 75:23:1
