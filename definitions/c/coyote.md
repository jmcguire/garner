# coyote

coyote. In standard American speech, this word is pronounced /kī-**yoh**-tee/. But in the western U.S., the pronunciation /**kī**-oht/ is often heard.

According to Charles Harrington Elster, both pronunciations have been acceptable since the word was first recorded in dictionaries in the late 1800s. The two-syllable version dominated until the 1960s, when "the weight of authority . . . shifted" to the three-syllable version, with the shorter version "heard chiefly in the western United States." *BBBM* at 113. Unsurprisingly, the mascot of the University of South Dakota is pronounced /**kī**-oht/.

Given that there are no coyotes in the United Kingdom, the British are largely unconcerned about the word.
