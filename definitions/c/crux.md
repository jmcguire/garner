# crux

crux \(= \[1\] a puzzle, conundrum, or difficult problem; or \[2\] the central point or essential feature\), a loanword dating in English from the early 1700s, has two plurals: *cruxes* and *cruces* /**kroo**-seez/. From the Latin word for "cross," *crux* has predominantly made the English plural *cruxes* since the mid-1900s. Before 1925, *cruces* was greatly predominant, but no longer. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*the cruxes* vs. *the cruces*\): 3:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*two cruxes* vs. *two cruces*\): 3:1
