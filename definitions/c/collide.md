# collide

collide is construed with *with* or *against*. Although *with* is much more common today, the *OED* provides historical evidence of *against*, and that usage still sometimes appears—e.g.: "In the eighth he collided *against* the outfield wall while chasing a drive by Missouri designated hitter Jake Epstein." Rick Cantu, "Aspito's 3-Run Homer Saves Longhorns," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 20 Feb. 1999, at C3.

 - Current ratio in print \(*collide with* vs. *collide against*\): 72:1
