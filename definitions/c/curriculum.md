# curriculum

curriculum. Pl. *curricula* or *curriculums*. E.g.:

 - "These *curricula* don't ‘radically change the way students learn how to add, subtract, multiply and divide.' " "Bethlehem's Math Program Gives Students an Edge," *Morning Call* (Allentown, Pa.), 24 Sept. 2002, at A12.

 - "Even as many other colleges and universities have abandoned their core *curriculums*, Columbia University has held fast." Karen W. Arenson, "Science May Soon Join Core Courses at Columbia," *N.Y. Times*, 26 Sept. 2002, at B3.

The Latinate plural remains much more common. See plurals (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*curricula* vs. *curriculums*): 18:1
