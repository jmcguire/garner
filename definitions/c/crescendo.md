# crescendo

crescendo, n., refers to a gradual increase in the volume of sound, not to a blast or a peak. To say that something *\*reaches a crescendo* is woolly-minded from a musical point of view, but increasingly common—e.g.: "The tension reached a *crescendo* \[read *peak* or *climax*\] last January, when, in a move to restore peace, Nicastro did not reappoint one of the new board members, Denise Murphy, and replaced her with the Rev. William Barnes." Loretta Waldman, "Library Board Member Announces Resignation," *Hartford Courant*, 11 Jan. 2001, at B1.

The plural is *crescendos*—preferably not the Italianate *\*crescendi*. See plurals \(d\).
