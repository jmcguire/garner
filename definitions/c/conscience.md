# conscience'

conscience' **sake**; **conscience's sake**. The traditional and overwhelmingly predominant form is *conscience' sake*, which is parallel to *goodness' sake*. Many writers, though, have made it *conscience's sake*, which is hard to speak—e.g.: "She knows it won't make a difference, but she has to go through the motions for *conscience's sake* \[read *conscience' sake*\]." Nelson Pressley, "Emotional Jackpot Eludes ‘Boomtown,' " *Wash. Times*, 10 Nov. 1994, at C16. See possessives \(n\).

Current ratio in print \(*conscience' sake* vs. *conscience's sake*\): 6:1
