# curare

curare /kyʊ-**rahr**-ee/ (the poison used on arrow tips by certain South American Indians—paralyzing and then killing by suffocation), a corrupted loanword from the Carib dialect of Macusi dating in English from the mid-1700s, has always been predominantly so spelled. **Curari* is an infrequent variant.

 - Current ratio in print (*curare* vs. \**curari*): 23:1
