# caulk

caulk; **calk**. *Caulk* = \(1\) vb., to fill \(cracks or seams\) in order to make airtight or watertight; or \(2\) n., the pastelike material used for this purpose \(also known as *caulking*\). *Calk* = \(1\) n., a piece fitted to a shoe \(esp. a horseshoe\) to prevent slipping; \(2\) vb., to fit with calks; or \(3\) vb., to injure with a calk. Both words are pronounced /kawk/ or, in AmE, /kok/.

Though *calk* is sometimes used for *caulk*, the words have undergone differentiation, so that the spellings should be distinguished according to the definitions above.

Current ratio in print \(*caulking the seams* vs. \**calking the seams*\): 1:1.2
