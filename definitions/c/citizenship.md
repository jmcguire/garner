# citizenship

citizenship; **residence; domicile**. The distinctions between these related terms are important. *Citizenship* = the status of being a citizen, with its attendant rights and privileges. *Residence* = (1) the act or fact of living in a particular place for a time \<a one-year residence requirement for in-state tuition\>; (2) the locale in which one actually or officially lives \<he has recently made Nevada his residence\>; or (3) a house or home \<a two-story residence\>. (*Residency* is a needless variant of *residence* in senses 1 and 2.) In some legal contexts, the more specific word *domicile* often bears the broad sense 2 of *residence*. Generally, though, your *domicile* is your fixed, permanent, and principal home for legal purposes.
