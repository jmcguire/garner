# clap

clap (= \[1\] to strike one's hands against each other; \[2\] to put one's hand or hands on \[something\] quickly and firmly; or \[3\] to put into chains or into prison with sudden force) forms the regular past tense *clapped*. \**Clapt* is an archaic variant.

 - Current ratio in print (*we clapped our hands* vs. \**we clapt our hands*): 33:1

 - Current ratio in print (*clapped into irons* vs. \**clapt into irons*): 11:1
