# calends

calends (= the first day of each month in the ancient Roman calendar), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1300s, vies closely with the spelling *kalends*. The term is plural in both form and construction <the calends of May were often cold>. The singular *calend* or *kalend* is rare.

 - Current ratio in print (*calends* vs. *kalends* in BrE): 1.6:1 Current ratio in print (*kalends* vs. *calends* in AmE): 1.2:1
