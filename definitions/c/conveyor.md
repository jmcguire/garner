# conveyor

conveyor (= \[1\] someone or something that carries or communicates; or \[2\] a long, continuous band of rubber or similar material, or a series of such bands, that moves things from one place to another—also known, in sense 2, as *conveyor belt*), a term dating from the early 1500s, was predominantly spelled \**conveyer* until the early 1900s. Today, *conveyor* is standard in all senses.

 - Current ratio in print (*conveyors of information* vs. \**conveyers of information*): 3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*conveyor belt* vs. \**conveyer belt*): 7:1
