# copyeditor

copyeditor; **copyediting**; **copyedit**, vb. The one-word form of the verb *copyedit* has been preferred over *copy edit* and *copy-edit* since the mid-1980s. But the noun presents a quandary: most commonly written as *copy editor* ever since the term's birth at the end of the 1800s, it creates an inconsistency that gnaws at the *copyeditor*'s mind. The closed form is on the rise, though, perhaps on the solid analogy of *proofreader*. The *copy editor*/*copyeditor* ratio has fallen from 7:1 in 1986 to 2.3:1 in 2007 to 1.6:1 today. Given the choice, better to opt for consistency: *copyedit*, *copyeditor*.

 - " ‘Copyediting' is one of those made-up words that somehow needed to be written that way. I—and many others before me—made the term one word, even though *Webster's* says that ‘copy editor' is two. With ‘proofreading' indisputably one word, it seems appropriate that ‘copyediting' should be also. And ‘copyeditor.' As we shall see, a copyeditor *copyedits* copy. Throughout this book, then, ‘copyeditor' and ‘copyediting' will be one word." Karen Judd, *Copyediting: A Practical Guide* 1 \(1982\).

 - Elsie M. Stainton, *The Fine Art of Copyediting* \(1991\).

 - "A *copyeditor* found the following ubiquitous error." Mary Newton Bruder, *The Grammar Lady* 126–27 \(2000\).

The usual BrE equivalent of *copyeditor* is *sub-editor* or *subeditor*.

Current ratio in print \(*copyedit* vs. *copy edit* vs. *copy-edit*\): 3:1.4:1   
Current ratio in print \(*copy editor* vs. *copyeditor* vs. *copy-editor*\): 5:3:1   
Current ratio in print \(*sub-editor* vs. *subeditor* in BrE\): 2.2:1
