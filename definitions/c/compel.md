# compel

compel; **impel**. *Compel* is the stronger word, connoting force or coercion, with little or no volition on the part of the one compelled. *Impel* connotes persuasive urging, with some degree of volition on the part of the one impelled—e.g.: "He moved like a sleepwalker, like one *impelled*." Ursula K. Le Guin, *The Lathe of Heaven* 88 (1971).
