# conversable

conversable; \***conversible**; **convertible**. *Conversable* = easy to talk with; enjoyable as a conversationalist. E.g.: "Friends say Ethel Kropf was as likable as they come—kind, thoughtful, *conversable*, quick with a compliment and full of stories." Alex Tizon, "Ethel Kropf, 97, Was Full of Stories," *Seattle Times*, 20 June 1995, at B6.

*\*Conversible* is a needless variant of *convertible* (= capable of being converted). E.g.: "As well as Comcast's deal, an issue for The Limited, the U.S. women's clothing chain, also had a success with a $50m *conversible* \[read *convertible issue*\] paying a 6¾ per cent coupon with a put option to give a 9.48 per cent yield after five years." Maggie Urry, "Low-Cost Borrowing Via a Convertible," *Fin. Times*, 2 Sept. 1985, § II, at 20. See -able (a).
