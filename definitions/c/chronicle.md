# chronicle

chronicle, n. & vb., is often misspelled *\*chronical*—e.g.:

 - "Frame your interests with photographs that *chronical* \[read *chronicle*\] your life and are cherished keepsakes." Frances Ingraham Heins, "Framed\!," *Times Union* \(Albany\), 23 Jan. 2000, at H1.

 - "Her book, ‘God, Dr. Buzzard and the Bolito Man,' is a detailed, readable *chronical* \[read *chronicle*\] of Saltwater Geechee heritage on the island." Steve Hendrix, "Sweet Georgia Coast," *Wash. Post*, 19 May 2002, at E1.

In its one near-legitimate use, *\*chronical* is a needless variant of the adjective *chronic*.

## Language-Change Index

 - *chronicle* misspelled *\*chronical*: Stage 1

 - Current ratio in print \(*chronicle* vs. \**chronical*\): 124:1
