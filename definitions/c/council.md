# council

council; **counsel**. The two words share a Latin root and "were, from the beginning, completely confused" (*OED*). But in the 1500s, they underwent useful differentiation. *Council* (= a deliberative assembly) is primarily a noun. *Counsel* (= to advise) is primarily a verb, but in legal writing it's commonly used as a noun meaning "a legal adviser or group of legal advisers" \<counsel for the corporation\>. See **counsel** & **consul**.
