# callipygian

callipygian /kal-i-**pij**-әn/ \(= having beautiful buttocks\), a Greek loanword dating in English from about 1800, didn't become popular until about 1910—and then only among intellectuals. The root *calli*- is the same as in *calligraphy* \("beautiful writing"\). The -*pyg*- is the same root as in *steatopygia* \(= excessive fat in the buttocks\). The film and theater critic John Simon called *callipygian* one of the most pleasant words in the English language. It can refer to people of either sex. The variant \**callipygous* /kal-i-**pī**-gәs/ predominated in print during the 1920s but has since taken the back seat.

Current ratio in print \(*callipygian* vs. \**callipygous*\): 2:1
