# conundrum

conundrum. Pl. *conundrums*. This mock-Latin word, which grew popular in the 1600s, has always been so pluralized. The mistaken form *\*conundra* sometimes appears—e.g.: "The rhythms of baseball, unlike those of more hectic games, often induce in the spectator long thoughts and provide ample opportunity to tackle *conundra* \[read *conundrums*\] that have long stumped philosophers for millennia." George W. Hunt, "What Are Hyenas Laughing at Anyway?," *America*, 13 Apr. 1996, at 2. \(If the author was trying to make *\*conundra* parallel to *millennia*, the echo hardly works.\) See plurals \(b\).

## Language-Change Index

*\*conundra* for *conundrums*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*conundrums* vs. *\*conundra*\): 48:1
