# highfalutin

highfalutin \(= pretentious, pompous\) has been the standard spelling since the mid-1800s, as opposed to *\*highfaluting*, *\*highfalutin'*, or *\*hifalutin.* But the variants persist—e.g.: "Chief among these are the geo people—those apostles of geopolitics, geostrategies and all the *hifalutin'* \[read *highfalutin*\] rest—who argue against the ‘sentimentality' of human rights and democratic concerns." Meg Greenfield, "No Hard Feelings?," *Newsweek*, 27 Sept. 1993, at 80. The *W11* spelling is *highfalutin*, without the apostrophe and with *high* spelled out. The *OED*, recording the word as an Americanism dating from the mid-1800s, plumps for *highfalutin* \(formerly also *\*highfalutin'*\). The *Funk & Wagnalls New Standard Dictionary* \(1942\) records *\*hifalutin* \(without the apostrophe\) as a variant spelling; the main entry is under *highfalutin*. \(Other variants listed there are *\*highfaluten* and *\*highfaluting*.\) The best course is to do two things: spell the word *highfalutin*, and avoid being what it denotes.

Current ratio in print \(*highfalutin* vs. \**highfalutin'* vs. \**hifalutin* vs. \**highfaluting* vs. \**highfaluten*\): 720:131:91:40:1
