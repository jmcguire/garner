# harass

harass; **harassment**. During the Senate's confirmation hearings on the appointment of Justice Clarence Thomas in October 1991, senators were divided over whether to say /**har**-is/ and /**har**-is-mәnt/ on the one hand, or /hә-**ras**/ and /hә-**ras**-mәnt/ on the other. \(They were divided on other issues as well.\) Because the proceedings were closely watched throughout the country, the correct pronunciation became a popular subject of discussion. Although in BrE first-syllable stress predominates—and many Americans \(therefore?\) consider it preferable—in AmE the second-syllable stress is standard.

The words are often misspelled *-rr-*. See spelling \(a\).
