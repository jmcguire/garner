# \*had used to be

\*had used to be is unidiomatic and awkward in place of *had been* or *had once been*—e.g.: "Still there came a time when I was anxious again. I was still in St. Louis but in another apartment, shared with the man who was later to be my husband. The house *had used to be* \[read *had once been*\] a butcher's shop." Abby Frucht, "Come in, Come in," *Seattle Times*, 21 May 1995, Pacific §, at 50. See **used to**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*had used to be* for *had once been*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*had once been* vs. *\*had used to be*\): 165:1
