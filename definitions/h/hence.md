# hence

hence. This adverb has several meanings, listed here in decreasing order of frequency: \(1\) "for this reason; therefore" <your premise is flawed; hence, your argument fails>; \(2\) "from this source" <she grew up in Colorado: hence her interest in mountain climbing>; \(3\) "from this time; from now" <our anniversary is just two weeks hence>; and \(4\) "from this place; away" <the park is three miles hence>.

*From hence* for *hence* \(in senses 3 and 4\) is an archaism. See **from hence**. Cf. **thence** & **whence**.
