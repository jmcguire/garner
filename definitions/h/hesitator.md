# hesitator

hesitator \(= someone who pauses nervously or unsurely\), an agent noun dating from the early 1800s, has always been predominantly so spelled. Yet most dictionaries, including those published by Oxford and Merriam-Webster, record \**hesitater* as the primary spelling. In fact, that has never been more than a variant. So don't pause for even a moment in using the -*or* spelling. See -er \(a\).

1913 ratio in print \(*hesitator* vs. \**hesitater*\): 6:1

 - Current ratio in print \(*hesitator* vs. \**hesitater*\): 1.7:1
