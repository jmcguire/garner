# heartsease

heartsease /**hahrt**-seez/ (= \[1\] peace of mind; \[2\] a pansy; or \[3\] the common wallflower), a compound dating from the 1400s, has been predominantly so spelled since the late 1850s. Among the variant spellings are \**heart's ease*, \**heart's-ease*, \**hearts-ease*, and \**hearts ease*.

 - Current ratio in print (*heartsease* vs. \**heart's-ease* vs. \**heart's ease* vs. \**hearts-ease* vs. \**hearts ease*): 22:9:9:1.1:1
