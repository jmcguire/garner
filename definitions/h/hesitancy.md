# hesitancy

hesitancy; \***hesitance**; **hesitation**. Strictly speaking, *hesitancy* is a quality ("the state of being hesitant; reluctance"), while *hesitation* is an act ("the act of hesitating"). So the better usage is to say that you have *no hesitancy* about doing something, not to say that you have *no hesitation*. But *hesitation* may well drive *hesitancy* out of the language. In word frequency within modern print sources, it vastly predominates over *hesitancy*. Meanwhile, *\*hesitance* is a needless variant of *hesitancy*.

 - Current ratio in print (*hesitation* vs. *hesitancy* vs. *\*hesitance*): 62:4:1
