# hocus-pocus

hocus-pocus, a sham-Latin phrase from the 1600s, is most commonly a noun denoting a sleight of hand or fakery or gibberish intended to obscure by trickery or deception. But the phrase can also be a verb meaning "to deceive or cheat," and in that use it is inflected *hocus-pocused* and *hocus-pocusing* in AmE but *hocus-pocussed* and *hocus-pocussing* in BrE. As to AmE, this fact seems anomalous, given the AmE preference for *hocussed* and *hocussing*. \(See **hocus**.) But *hocus* presents the peculiar inflection, while the verb *hocus-pocus* follows the normal AmE inflectional pattern. See spelling \(c\).

Current ratio in print \(*hocus-pocused* vs. *hocus-pocussed*\): 5:1
