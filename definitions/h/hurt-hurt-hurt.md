# hurt > hurt > hurt

hurt > hurt > hurt. So inflected. The past form *\*hurted*, which has been nonstandard from the 1700s, appears most frequently in quotations from dialectal speakers and children—e.g.: " ‘She *hurted* me to the point where I just walked up to her and shanked her probably like six, seven times,' said Bell, then 24, who was charged with murder for the July 2000 stabbing of his mother, Netta." Ken Armstrong, Steve Mills & Maurice Possley, "Coercive and Illegal Tactics Torpedo Scores of Cook County Murder Cases," *Chicago Trib.*, 16 Dec. 2001, at C1. See irregular verbs. For more on nonstandard tense forms, see dialect.

Current ratio in print \(*had hurt* vs. *\*had hurted*\): 6,149:1
