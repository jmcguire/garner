# honeyed

honeyed \(= \[1\] covered in honey; \[2\] tasting like honey; or \[3\] \[of a statement\] sounding soft and pleasant but perhaps insincere\), a past-participial adjective dating from the 1300s, has been predominantly so spelled since 1845 in AmE and 1860 in BrE. Before those times, \**honied* was the usual spelling. Now a variant form, it still sometimes appears—e.g.: "This is a mold that weakens the skins and allows the water to escape at the same time imparting an extraordinary *honied* \[read *honeyed*\] flavor to the concentrated juice." Frank J. Prial, "Wine Talk," *N.Y. Times*, 24 July 1996, at C4. Cf. **moneyed**.
