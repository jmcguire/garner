# hallelujah

hallelujah (the interjection used to express thanks, or joy, or praise to God), a Hebrew loanword dating in English from the 1200s, has been predominantly so spelled for most of the period of Modern English. Since 1930, the variant *alleluia* has been especially common to differentiate solemn religious utterances from popular exclamations in everyday contexts. Among the variant spellings are \**halleluia*, \**halleluiah*, \**alleluja*, \**alleluiah*, \**halleluja*, and \**allelujah*.
