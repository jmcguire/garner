# Hispanic

Hispanic, n.; **Latino**; **Latina**; **Chicano**; **Chicana**. To denote someone of Spanish descent, the term *Hispanic* (gender-neutral in all its applications) was the commonest of all these terms from 1980 to 2000. Since that time, it has vied closely with *Latino* /la-**tee**-noh/ for predominance—the feminine form being *Latina* /la-**tee**-nә/. The first similar words to gain widespread use in AmE were the much more specific terms *Chicano* /chi-**kah**-noh/ (= a U.S. resident of Mexican extraction) and its feminine equivalent *Chicana* /chi-**kah**-nә/, which arose in the late 1960s and spread in the 1970s. (Note that the first syllable isn't /shi-/—except in BrE.) But a hypernym having been needed for the broader group, *Hispanic* and *Latino* have eclipsed the more specialized terms (including *Mexican American*). Justice Sonia Sotomayor of the U.S. Supreme Court, of Puerto Rican descent, is more frequently identified in the press as a Latina than as Hispanic. See race-related terms (i).

If a definite article is needed before *Hispanic*, it should be *a*, not *an*. See **a (a)**.
