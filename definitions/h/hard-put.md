# hard put

hard put. G.H. Vallins once insisted that it's wrong to say *the reader is hard put to know what is happening*, insisting instead on adding *to it* to the sentence: *the reader is hard put to it to know what is happening*. Vallins, *Better English* 73 (4th ed. 1957). Today, however, idiom doesn't even allow the *to it*, and virtually no one is hard put to know why—because hardly anyone even thinks of it.

 - Current ratio in print (*hard put to* vs. *hard put to it to*): 3:1
