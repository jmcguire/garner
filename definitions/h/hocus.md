# hocus

hocus \(= \[1\] to deceive by trickery, jugglery, or subterfuge; \[2\] to drug \[someone\] for illicit purposes; or \[3\] to spike \[as a drink\] with drugs for the purpose of stupefying\) is inflected *hocussed* and *hocussing* in AmE and BrE alike. That makes it an exceptional form in AmE, in which the seemingly analogous terms *focused* and *focusing* are so spelled. See spelling \(c\).

Current ratio in print \(*hocussed* vs. \**hocused* in World Englishes\): 8:1   
Current ratio in print \(*hocussing* vs. \**hocusing* in World Englishes\): 23:1
