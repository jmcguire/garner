# haply

haply (= by chance, perhaps) is an archaism much in decline and best avoided—e.g.: "One may rush out into the middle of Broadway in moving traffic observed by a large group of police officers *haply* \[delete *haply*\] grazing on doughnuts and not merit even a menacing belch." Scott Patterson, "The Truth About Jaywalking," *Virginian-Pilot* (Norfolk), 23 Dec. 1995, at A10. Many readers of that sentence would haplessly misread *haply* as a misprint for *happily*; they are to be sympathized with, not scoffed at.
