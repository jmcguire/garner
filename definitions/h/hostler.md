# hostler

hostler; **ostler**. *Hostler* (= \[1\] someone who takes care of horses or mules, esp. at an inn or stable; or \[2\] someone who moves a railroad engine or a truck at the end of a trip) is so spelled in AmE—and has been, predominantly, since about 1850. The BrE spelling has always been *ostler*—representing the mute *h* that has typified the word's pronunciation in BrE beginning in Middle English. Sense 2 does not apply in BrE.

 - Current ratio in print (*a hostler* vs. *an hostler* in World Englishes): 3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*an ostler* vs. *a hostler* in World Englishes): 1.6:1
