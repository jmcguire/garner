# handicraft

handicraft \(= \[1\] an art or avocation requiring manual skill; or \[2\] an article made by manual skill\) is the standard noun. *Handcraft*, n., is a needless variant.

But the verb *handcraft* \(= to fashion by hand\) and the adjective *handcrafted* are perfectly good. In fact, sometimes *handicraft* is wrongly asked to do their work—e.g.: "Dress up a wooden container with a selection of *handicrafted* \[read *handcrafted*\] tiles." "Tile Looks Terrific Out in the Garden," *S.F. Chron.*, 25 June 1997, Home §, at 5 \(photo caption\).

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*handicrafted* for *handcrafted*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*handcrafted* vs. *\*handicrafted*\): 1,105:1
