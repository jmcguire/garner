# hemoglobin

hemoglobin; **haemoglobin**. *Hemoglobin* (= the red protein that transports oxygen in the blood of vertebrates) has been the standard AmE spelling since about 1900. *Haemoglobin* was the established BrE spelling from the late 1800s, but the levels of *hemoglobin* in BrE print sources surged in the 1970s, and the two spellings have vied for supremacy ever since. So far, *haemoglobin* holds a slight lead in BrE —especially in medical writing.

In AmE and BrE alike, *a* (not *an*) precedes the word. See **a (a)**.

 - Current ratio in print (*hemoglobin* vs. *haemoglobin* in World Englishes): 4:1
