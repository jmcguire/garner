# hypothecate

hypothecate is not, as some believe, a synonym of *hypothesize* \(= to make a hypothesis, i.e., a proposition put forward for discussion\). *Hypothecate* is a legal term meaning "to pledge \(property\) without delivery of title or possession."

President George H.W. Bush occasionally misused *hypothecate*—e.g.:

 - " ‘So I'm not going to speculate or *hypothecate* \[read *hypothesize*\] beyond that. I want to see them out of there . . . .' " "Confrontation in the Gulf," *N.Y. Times*, 9 Aug. 1990, at A15 \(quoting President George H.W. Bush\).

 - "Our sitting president, George Bush, . . . said, ‘Now is no time to speculate or *hypothecate* \[read *hypothesize*\], but rather a time for action . . . .' " Don Addis, "Wrapping Up Homelessness," *St. Petersburg Times* \(Fla.\), 22 Mar. 1992, at D5.

 - "That, of course, means less time for pointless *hypothecating* \[read *hypothesizing*\] about each twist and turn of constitutional bickering." Brian Wilson, "Permanent Cacophony of Grievance from SNP," *Scotsman*, 31 Mar. 2017, Opinion §.
