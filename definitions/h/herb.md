# herb

herb, n.; **herbal**, adj. Although in AmE *herb* is pronounced /әrb/, *herbal* has traditionally been pronounced /**hәr**-bәl/. In BrE, *a herbal* has predominated in usage since 1890. In AmE, *a* and *an* vied closely until the early 1970s, when *an* decisively won. Today, /**әr**-bәl/ predominates in AmE. It therefore seems more natural to most American readers to sip *an herbal tea*, not *a herbal tea*. But *herbicide*, with an aspirated *h-*, should be preceded by *a*, not *an*. See **a \(a\)**. Cf. **homage** & **humble**, adj.

In BrE, the aspirated *h* in *herb* has been restored since the early 1800s. It's a telltale distinction in pronunciation between AmE and BrE.
