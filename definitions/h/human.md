# human

human, n. Purists long objected to *human* as a shortened form of *human being*, but today it's so pervasive—even in formal writing—that it should be accepted as standard. References to *humans* as opposed to *human beings* became predominant in AmE about 1978 and in BrE about 1985.

Language-Change Index

*human* as a noun equivalent to *human being*: Stage 5
Current ratio in print \(*humans* vs. *human beings*\): 3:1
