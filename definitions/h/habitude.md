# \*habitude

\*habitude. As generally used today, this gallicism is a needless variant of *habit*, one that supplies a hint of archaism—e.g.: "Tocqueville spent nearly a year examining American *habitudes* \[read *habits*\] and mores." Richard Wolin, "Liberalism as a Vocation," *New Republic*, 2 Sept. 1996, at 34. \(On the use of *Tocqueville*—without the particle *de*—in that sentence, see names \(d\).)
