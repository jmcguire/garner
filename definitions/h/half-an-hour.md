# half an hour

half an hour; **a half hour**. Which is the more usual, more idiomatic way of referring to a period of 30 minutes? The answer is *half an hour* <we met for half an hour>. But if a phrasal adjective is required, *half-hour* is the standard form <a half-hour meeting>.

 - Current ratio in print (*half an hour* vs. *a half hour* vs. *one-half hour*): 230:35:1
