# hoof

hoof. This word, pronounced either /hʊf/ or /hoof/, has undergone a curious change in its plural. From 1700 to about 1975, the predominant form in English-language print sources was *hoofs*, analogous to *roofs*. But in the early 20th century, *hooves* emerged as a competing plural and became predominant first in BrE \(1960\) and later in AmE \(1975\). In modern journalistic sources, *hooves* is now more than nine times as common, but in book publishing, the two forms vie more closely. Traditionalists will stick to *hoofs*, just as they do to *roofs*. See **roof** & plurals \(c\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*their hooves* vs. *their hoofs*\): 1.9:1
