# has to do with

has to do with; **is to do with**. The first predominates in AmE and BrE alike; the second is a chiefly BrE variant. E.g.: "The other measure *is to do with* \[or, in AmE, *has to do with*\] one's colleagues." Lucy Kellaway, "Yes, We Have No Free Bagels," *Fin. Times*, 15 Jan. 2001, at 10.

Current ratio in print \(*has to do with* vs. *is to do with*\): 24:1
