# amicable

amicable; **amiable**. The first came directly from Latin, the second from French, but the two forms are at base the same word. Yet they have undergone differentiation. *Amiable* applies to people <an amiable chap>, *amicable* to relations between people <an amicable resolution>.

*Amicable* is pronounced /**am**-i-kә-bәl/—not /ә-**mik**-ә-bәl/. *Amiable* is pronounced /**ay**-mee-ә-bәl/.
