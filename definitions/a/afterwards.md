# afterwards

afterwards; **afterword**. *Afterwards* (= later) is often changed to *afterward* by American editors, though in popular usage the two forms are used interchangeably. Across World Englishes, *afterwards* has predominated since the 1600s. *Afterword* is a noun meaning "epilogue." Cf. **foreword**.

For more on *afterward(s)*, see directional words (a).

 - Current ratio in print (*afterwards* vs. *afterward*): 2.2:1
