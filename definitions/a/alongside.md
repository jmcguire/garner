# alongside

alongside, prep., = at the side of. Hence one car is parked *alongside* another and logs are stacked *alongside* one another. It is unnecessary—and bad style—to write *\*alongside of*. See **of \(a\)**.

## Language-Change Index

*\*alongside of* for *alongside*: Stage 3
Current ratio in print \(*alongside the* vs. *\*alongside of the*\): 45:1
