# animalculum

animalculum \(lit., "little animal"\) forms the plural *animalcula*, not *\*animalculae*—e.g.: "John Crawford, a reputable Baltimore physician and an early promoter of contagion theory in America, lost both his reputation and his practice for maintaining, in 1806/7, that disease was spread by microscopic insects or *animalculae* \[read *animalcula*\]." Ronald Rees, "Under the Weather: Climate and Disease, 1700–1900," 46 *History Today* 35 \(1996\). But the more common term is *animalcule* \(pl. *animalcules*\). See diminutives \(b\).

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*animalculae* for *animalcula*: Stage 3
