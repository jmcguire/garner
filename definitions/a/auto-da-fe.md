# auto-da-fé

auto-da-fé /aw-toh-dә-**fay**/, a Portuguese loanword dating in English from the early 1700s, literally means "act of the faith." Its more concrete meaning, from the days of the Spanish and Portuguese holy inquisitions, is "the public burying of a heretic." That included punishments of torture and even burning at the stake. The usual plural is *autos-da-fé*.

Sometimes the Spanish spelling appears in English: *auto-de-fé*. But the Portuguese *da* is standard. Either way, the acute accent is normally retained over the final *e*.

 - Current ratio in print (*auto-da-fé* vs. *auto-de-fé*): 17:1

 - Current ratio in print (*autos-da-fé* vs. *auto-da-fés*): 5:1
