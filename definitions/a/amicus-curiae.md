# amicus curiae

amicus curiae; friend of the court. These phrases refer to "someone who is not a party to a lawsuit but who petitions the court or is requested by the court to file a brief in the action because that person has a strong interest in the subject matter." *Black's Law Dictionary* 106 (11th ed. 2019). Lawyers write *amicus curiae*; journalists often write *friend of the court*. See legalese.

The Latin phrase is variously pronounced. The singular is /ә-**mee**-kәs **kyoor**-ee-ī/ or /ә-**mī**-kәs **kyoor**-i-ee/ and the plural (*amici curiae*) is /ә-**mee**-kee **kyoor**-ee-ī/ or /ә-**mee**-see/ or /ә-**mī**-kee/ or /ә-**mī**-see/. Another acceptable pronunciation of the first word—a common pronunciation in AmE—is /**am**-ә-kәs/.

 - Current ratio in print (*amicus curiae* vs. *friend of the court*): 7:1
