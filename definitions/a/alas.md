# alas

alas; alack. *Alas*, a mild exclamation, expresses woe caused by a lamentable state of affairs. E.g.: "The creatures keep Susan alive \(inexplicable unless she is meant to be mated with the king bug\), and they stop evolving into humans \(so we never, *alas*, see the final stage of a really uggy bug-man\)." Richard Corliss, "Really Bugged: In *Mimic*, Giant Roaches Invade New York City," *Time*, 25 Aug. 1997, at 70.

*Alack*, a synonymous exclamation, is archaic. *Alas and alack* is an old cliché. Because the words are so old-fashioned, virtually all uses have a touch of humorous irony. See archaisms.
