# agenda

agenda is \(1\) the plural form of *agendum*, which means "something to be done" \(another, less proper plural of *agendum* being *agendums*\); and, more commonly, \(2\) a singular noun meaning "a list of things to be done" or "a program." The plural of *agenda* in sense 2 is *agendas* \(certainly not *\*agendae*\). Decrying *agendas* as a double plural is bootless.

In fact, sense 1 of *agenda* is archaic today and sounds pedantic—e.g.: "Place your notes, thoughts, quotations, queries, and *lists of agenda* \[read *agenda items*\], divided according to topics, in envelopes." Lester S. King, *Why Not Say It Clearly* 74 \(1978\).
