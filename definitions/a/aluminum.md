# aluminum

aluminum; **aluminium**. *Aluminum* is the standard spelling in AmE; *aluminium* is standard in BrE. In 1812, the metallic element was named *aluminum* by the English chemist Sir Humphry Davy. The same year, *aluminium* was offered as being more "classical" in sound, since the *-ium* suffix harmonizes better with the names of other elements such as sodium, potassium, and magnesium. In his 1828 unabridged American dictionary, Noah Webster recorded the word as *aluminum*; his British counterparts, who admitted the word somewhat later, recorded it as *aluminium*. The AmE–BrE difference has existed ever since. It became most strongly pronounced beginning about 1900.

*Aluminum* /ә-**loo**-mi-nәm/ is sometimes, in AmE, mispronounced /ә-**lim**-i-nәm/. The BrE word *aluminium* is pronounced /al-yoo-**min**-ee-әm/ or /a-loo-**min**-ee-әm/.
