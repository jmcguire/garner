# appear

appear. The phrase *it would appear* is invariably inferior to *it appears* or *it seems*—e.g.: "*It would appear* \[read *It appears*\] that more than a few of us are desperate for an easy dinner." Ruth Fantasia, "Desperate Times Require Desperate Measures," *Virginian-Pilot & Ledger Star* \(Norfolk\), 7 Jan. 1998, at F1. See **would** & subjunctives.

On the sequence of tenses in phrases such as *appeared to enjoy* \(as opposed to *appeared to have enjoyed*\), see tenses \(b\).
