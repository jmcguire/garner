# as is

as is; **as was**. "He bought the company ‘as is.' " Although a martinet of logic might insist on *as was* in the preceding sentence, that phrase is jarringly unidiomatic. *As is*, in the context of that sentence, is really an elliptical form of *as it is* or *on an "as is" basis* and is infinitely better than those paraphrases. The purpose of the phrase *as is*, of course, is for a seller to disclaim warranties and representations.

This use of *as is* sprang up in the mid-20th century and spread rapidly through English-speaking countries.
