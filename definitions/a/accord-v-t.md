# accord, v.t

accord, v.t.

**A. And *afford*.** These words share the meaning "to furnish or grant" <accorded \(or afforded\) all the respect due him>. Yet some differentiation is possible: *accord* has the nuance of granting something because it is suitable or proper <some 269 charter schools in 25 states have been accorded the freedom to do what is in their students' best interests>. *Afford*, in contrast, is the more general term meaning "to furnish \(something\) out of kindness, goodwill, or competitive strategy" <the airline afforded free upgrades to its most frequent fliers>.

**B. Construing with Prepositions.** When used intransitively, *accord* takes the preposition *in*, *to*, or *with*, depending on the context <we accord in our opinions> <we accord to plaintiff his due> <this accords with the prevailing view>.
