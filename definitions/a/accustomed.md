# accustomed

accustomed. Formerly, the idiom was *accustomed to do*, *accustomed to think*, etc. But in the mid-20th century the phrasing shifted to *accustomed to doing*, *accustomed to thinking*. Today the older usage sounds strange to many ears, but some traditionalists stick to it, especially in BrE—e.g.: "Both stem from the age profile of a profession in which nearly two thirds of teachers are over 40 and *accustomed to think* of early retirement as the norm." John Carvel, "Questioning Professionalism of Teachers Can Be Harmful," *Guardian*, 14 Jan. 1997, at 2.

 - Current ratio in print \(*accustomed to being* vs. *accustomed to be*\): 2.5:1
