# academicism

academicism; \***academism**. Dating back to the early 1600s, *academicism* /ak-ә-**dem**-i-siz-әm/ \(originally often capitalized\) at first denoted one or more tenets of Plato's Academy, especially as regarding epistemological skepticism. Since the 1880s, the term has predominantly denoted the state or quality of involving either \(1\) college or university education or \(2\) abstract theorizing. \**Academism*, a variant dating from the 1700s, has never been common, even among the erudite.

 - Current ratio in print \(*academicism* vs. *\*academism* \[case-insensitive search\]\): 4:1
