# azygos

azygos \(= not being one of a pair; not having a corresponding part\), a Greek loanword meaning "unyoked," dates in English from the 1600s. Anatomists speak of azygos arteries, lobes, and veins. The spelling **azygous*—the only form listed in *W11*—is a variant.

*Azygos* can also function as a noun—that is, the unmated anatomical part may be called an *azygos*.

In a way, the adjective *azygos* is itself azygos, since words with the same root form the adjective with an -*ous* suffix \(e.g., *heterozygous*, *homozygous*\).

Current ratio in print \(*azygos* vs. *\*azygous*\): 3:1

Current ratio in print \(*azygos vein* vs. *\*azygous vein*\): 4:1
