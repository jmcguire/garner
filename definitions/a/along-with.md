# along with

along with. Like *together with*, this connective phrase does not affect the grammatical number of the sentence. E.g.: "He admitted that he, *along with* other board members, *are* \[read *is*\] no longer sure about anything concerning the \[controversy\]." Elizabeth W. Crowley, "Salem Dispute Drags On," *Patriot Ledger* \(Quincy, Mass.\), 7 Aug. 2002, at 1. See subject–verb agreement \(e\).

When the sense is necessarily plural, use *and* instead of *along with*—e.g.: "He *along with* \[read *and*\] his wife, Edith, *were* the owners of the Snug Club until they sold it in 1997." "Thomas E. McDonald Sr." \(obit.\), *Daily Oklahoman*, 8 Aug. 2002, at C8.
