# accent à

à la mode \(= \[1\] fashionable; or \[2\] served with ice cream\) has been predominantly so written since it was introduced into English in the 1600s. \**Alamode* was fashionable for a time—even predominant for a while in the late 1700s. The form *à la mode*, retaining the grave accent, remains more common than the spelling without this diacritical mark.

 - Current ratio in print \(*à la mode* vs. *a la mode* vs.\**alamode*\): 19:6:1
