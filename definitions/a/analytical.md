# analytical

analytical; **analytic**. No differentiation has surfaced between the two. The long form is twice as common in print as the short, perhaps because it is perceived as being generally more euphonious. This being so, *analytic* could justifiably be labeled a needless variant—except in the few set phrases denoting disciplines or schools of thought, such as *analytic geometry* and *analytic philosophy*. See -ic/-ical.

Current ratio in print \(*analytical* vs. *analytic*\): 2:1
