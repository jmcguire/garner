# archaic

archaic; **obsolete; obsolescent**. *Archaic* = old-fashioned; antiquated; characteristic of an earlier time and rarely used today. *Obsolete* = no longer in general use; out of date. *Obsolescent* = passing out of use; becoming obsolete. The phrase *\*totally obsolescent* or *\*completely obsolescent* is an oxymoron—e.g.: "Widespread acceptance of that work would render his own much more conventional, delicately Impressionist poetry *completely obsolescent* \[read *obsolete*\]." Christopher Hampton, "The Long Brief Encounter," *Sunday Telegraph*, 6 Apr. 1997, at 9.
