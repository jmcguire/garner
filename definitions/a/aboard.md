# aboard

aboard. Usually restricted to ships or planes in BrE, this word is applied broadly in AmE to any public conveyance—e.g.: "The bus had about 35 pupils *aboard* from Varina and Mehfoud Elementary schools." Mark Bowes, "It Was Close to a ‘Catastrophe,' " *Richmond Times-Dispatch*, 18 Jan. 1997, at B1. More recently, it has come to be applied to organizations as well—e.g.: "Longtime in-house attorney Thomas ‘Tad' Decker was brought *aboard* in 2000 to become managing partner." Jeff Blumenthal, "New Year Brings Firm Leadership," *Legal Intelligencer*, 2 Jan. 2002, at 1.
