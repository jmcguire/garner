# all the time

all the time. Margaret Nicholson criticized this expression when used in a context that doesn't indicate a definite period \(*DAEU* at 17\). Hence she labeled the following usage "slang": "Actors act while they are on stage, but he acts *all the time*."

This may have been one of Nicholson's pet peeves, since no other usage commentator has objected to the phrase. Though slightly informal, *all the time* in the nonliteral sense \(= habitually\) is acceptable English.

*All the time* is more polished phrasing than the unidiomatic *\*all of the time*. See **all \(a\)**.

Current ratio in print \(*all the time* vs. *\*all of the time*\): 33:1
