# adulterous

adulterous; \***adulterate**, adj.; **adulterant**, adj.; **adulterated; adulterine**. *Adulterous* and *\*adulterate* both mean "of, characterized by, or pertaining to adultery," the former term being the more common. E.g.: "There are revelations about *adulterous* wrinkles in his generally happy fourth marriage to Lauren Bacall—an affair with a makeup artist for him and an affair with Frank Sinatra for her." L.S. Klepp, "Play It Again, Sam, and Again," *Entertainment Weekly*, 11 Apr. 1997, at 78. *\*Adulterate*, adj., more common in Shakespeare's day than in ours, has been relegated to the status of a needless variant.

*Adulterant* = tending to adulterate <adulterant chemicals in the mixture>. *Adulterated* = \(1\) corrupted or debased <an adulterated culture>; or \(2\) corrupted by an impure addition; made spurious <the vintage wine was thoroughly adulterated once the water was added>. *Adulterine* = \(1\) spurious; \(2\) illegal; or \(3\) born of adultery <adulterine bastard>.
