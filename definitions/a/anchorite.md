# anchorite

anchorite /**angk**-ә-rīt/ (= someone who lives in seclusion usu. for religious reasons; a pious hermit), a French/Latin loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has been predominantly so spelled since the 1600s. **Anchoret* is a variant. The feminine forms *anchoress* and \**ancress* are largely unnecessary given that *anchorite* can be applied to someone of either sex.

 - Current ratio in print (*anchorite* vs. *anchoress* vs. \**anchoret* vs. \**ancress*): 414:112:21:1
