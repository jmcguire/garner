# \*anyplace

\*anyplace for *anywhere* has appreciably crept into print only since the early 1960s. It remains nonstandard—e.g.: "The old \[athletic director\] hasn't gone *anyplace* \[read *anywhere*\], though." Ron Bush, "Green Replaces Smith at CSTCC," *Chattanooga Free Press*, 31 Jan. 1997, at H5. When the meaning is "any location," *any place* should always be two words \<at any place\>. E.g.: "Readers out there are looking for . . . *anyplace* \[read *any place* or *a place*\] that serves a good Italian beef sub, like the ones Cousins used to sell." Jeremy Iggers, "Celebrate Asian New Year in French," *Star Trib.* (Minneapolis), 7 Feb. 1997, at E15. Cf. \***noplace** & **someplace**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*anyplace* for *anywhere*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*anywhere* vs. *\*anyplace*): 102:1
