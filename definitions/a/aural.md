# aural

aural; **oral**. *Aural* = of, relating to, or involving the ears or hearing. *Oral* = of, relating to, or involving the mouth or speaking. The words present little problem in their written form; they're most confusing when given orally and received aurally.

In the early 1960s, when "the pill" was introduced as the first oral contraceptive, some women reportedly mistook *oral* for *aural* and stuffed pills into their ears. See Jacki Wullschlager, "When Family Planning Met the Joy of Sex," *Fin. Times*, 2–3 June 2001, FT Weekend §, at v.
