# administer

administer, v.t.; **minister**, v.i. *Administer*, which suffices in most contexts, has four meanings: \(1\) "to give" <administer treatment> <administer the oath of office>; \(2\) "to dispense" <administer frontier justice>; \(3\) "to manage" <administer the state health department>; and \(4\) "to manage and dispose of" <administer the movie mogul's estate>. The verb *minister*, now exclusively intransitive, shares all but the second sense, though only rarely. *Minister* is most commonly used in the sense of attending to others' needs or, in religious contexts, of administering sacraments. And people in need are *ministered to*. Cf. **\*administrate**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*minister to the sick* vs. \**administer to the sick*\): 4:1
