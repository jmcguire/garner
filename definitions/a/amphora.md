# amphora

amphora \(= a two-handled clay jar or vase, esp. one used for oil or wine in the ancient world\), a Latin/Greek loanword dating in English from the 1300s, has two plurals: the classical *amphorae* /**am**-fә-ree/ and the homegrown *amphoras* /**am**-fә-rәz/. Since the early 1800s, *amphorae* has strongly predominated.

Current ratio in print \(*amphorae* vs. *amphoras*\): 4:1
