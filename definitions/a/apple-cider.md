# apple cider

apple cider. Strictly speaking, this phrase is a redundancy because *cider* has traditionally referred to a drink made from apple juice. But for many decades now, beverage manufacturers have marketed other types of "ciders," from the juice of peaches, pears, raspberries, and the like. So if *apple cider* is redundant, it is also sometimes necessary for clarity. In any event, the phrase *apple cider* is old: it dates from the 1800s. Cf. **tuna fish**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *apple cider* for *cider*: Stage 5
