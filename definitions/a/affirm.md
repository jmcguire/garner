# affirm

affirm (= \[1\] to declare emphatically; or \[2\] \[of an appellate court\] to uphold a lower court's judgment) is sometimes misused for *vindicate* (= to justify by outcome): "The results tonight *affirmed* \[read *vindicated*\] Mr. McCain's decision to skip the Iowa caucuses." Richard L. Berke, "McCain Romps in First Primary," *N.Y. Times*, 2 Feb. 2000, at A1. Although one definition of *affirm* is "to validate or confirm," here *vindicated* or even *justified* would have been a better choice.
