# automaton

automaton (= \[1\] a humanoid robot; \[2\] someone who seems robotically emotionless and unreflective; or \[3\] an abstract computing machine) has predominantly formed the plural *automata* since the early 1700s. Although AmE seriously flirted with *automatons* from about 1915 to the early 1950s, that plural has been much in decline ever since.

 - Current ratio in print (*automata* vs. *automatons* in World Englishes): 5:1
