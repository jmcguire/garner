# apparatchik

apparatchik /ap-ә-**rat**-chik/ \(= a government or corporate official who, through blind allegiance, follows orders unthinkingly\), a Russian loanword dating in English from 1941, has two plurals: the homegrown *apparatchiks* and the Russian \**apparatchiki*. Beginning in the late 1970s, the word was considered fully anglicized, and the anglicized plural has predominated since then.

 - Current ratio in print \(*apparatchiks* vs. \**apparatchiki*\): 6:1
