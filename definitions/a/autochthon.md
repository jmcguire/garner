# autochthon

autochthon /aw-**tok**-thәn/, a Greek loanword from the late 1500s, means "a person or organism indigenous to a region." It's an academic word with two plurals: the homegrown *autochthons* and the Greek plural *autochthones* /aw-**tok**-thә-neez/. Since about 1980, the anglicized *autochthons* \(much less pretentious to say\) has predominated.

Current ratio in print \(*autochthons* vs. *autochthones*\): 1.4:1
