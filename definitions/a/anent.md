# anent

anent. Theodore M. Bernstein writes, "Except in legal usage, *anent* \[= about\] is archaic and semiprecious." *More Language That Needs Watching* 24 (1962). He could have omitted *except in legal usage* and *semi*. Perhaps the best statement is that *anent* "is a pompous word and nearly always entirely useless." Percy Marks, *The Craft of Writing* 47 (1932). Common in the 1600s, it has been in retreat ever since. See archaisms.
