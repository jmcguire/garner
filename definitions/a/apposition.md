# apposition

apposition \(= the grammatical relation between two words or phrases that stand for the same idea\) is sometimes misused for *opposition*—an odd malapropism. E.g.: "Their accounts were in stark *apposition* \[read *opposition*\] at several other points, too." Maureen Dowd, "Testimony Conflicts at Military Hearing on Abuse by Fliers," *N.Y. Times*, 18 Aug. 1993, at A1.

Language-Change Index
