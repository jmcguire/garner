# anytime

anytime, adv., = at any time; whenever. E.g.: "*Anytime* a seller rents back from a buyer, an interim occupancy agreement should be completed." Dian Hymer, "Seller Rent-Back Can Benefit Both Sides," *S.F. Examiner*, 25 Oct. 1992, at F1. Some writers consider this term a casualism, but it is highly convenient and has—for whatever reason—gained more widespread acceptance than *anymore* \(in positive contexts\) and *\*anyplace*. Cf. **anymore** & **\*anyplace**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *anytime* for *whenever*: Stage 5
