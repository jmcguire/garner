# anomalous

anomalous; **anomalistic**. Something that is an anomaly is *anomalous*. That is, *anomalous* is the general adjective corresponding to the noun *anomaly*. But for astronomical anomalies, the adjective is *anomalistic*. Sometimes, though, this much narrower adjective erroneously displaces the broader one—e.g.:

• "Whether the conflicting findings between the levels of analysis are *anomalistic* \[read *anomalous*\] is unclear." David W. Romero, "Requiem for the Lightweight," *Presidential Studies Q.*, 1 Sept. 2001, at 454.

• "It fails to mention the *anomalistic* \[read *anomalous*\] 1981–83 digression of Gov. Frank White." Michael Storey, "Paper Trails: Has Hillary Forgotten Us?," *Ark. Democrat-Gaz.*, 26 Jan. 2003, at 53.
