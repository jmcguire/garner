# adjutant general

adjutant general \(= a chief administrative officer in the military\), a phrase dating from the 1600s, consists of the noun *adjutant* \(= staff officer\) followed by the postpositive adjective *general*. Hence the traditional plural is *adjutants general*, not \**adjutant generals*. But the latter form has been in frequent use.

 - Current ratio in print \(*adjutants general* vs. \**adjutant generals*\): 4:1
