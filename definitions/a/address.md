# address

address, n. & vb. In several of its verb senses, this is a formal word: (1) "to speak to" \<Jones addressed the managers in a manner that they considered insubordinate\>; (2) "to direct (a statement, question, etc.) to" \<Walters addressed the question to Fawcett, not to Majors\>; (3) "to call attention to" \<the report addressed four issues\>; (4) "to deal with" \<it's time to address these problems\>.

Whether as a verb or as a noun, *address* is preferably accented on the second syllable: /ә-**dres**/. But for a residence or business, /**ad**-dres/ is fully acceptable in AmE.
