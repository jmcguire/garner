# artless

artless has opposing connotations. On the one hand, it is favorable when contrasted with the idea denoted by *artificial*—e.g.: "Generally, Ms. Benedis's works seem as *artless* as nature itself." Patricia Malarcher, "Show Features Artwork Formed from Cuttings and Branches," *N.Y. Times*, 9 Feb. 1997, at WC13. But it's often unfavorable when contrasted with *artful*—e.g.: "In an age of *artless* novels, here was an artful book." Richard Dyer, "Sibling Reveries," *Boston Globe*, 22 May 1994, at B15.
