# a la

a la; **à la**. This gallicism, meaning "in the manner or style of," was borrowed into English in the late 1500s. It appears in such well-known phrases as *à la carte* (= according to the menu's individual pricing for an item) and *à la mode* (= \[1\] fashionable, stylish; or \[2\] topped with ice cream). The phrase often serves a comparative function \<he's bound to be a record-breaking baseball player a la Hank Aaron\> \<a recipe for economic instability a la the 1930s\>. Generally, it is written without the diacritical mark and without italics \<he chipped in a la Tom Watson\>, unless it appears as part of a larger French phrase \<steak *à la grecque*\> \<pommes *à la duchesse*\>.
