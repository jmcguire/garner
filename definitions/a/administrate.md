# \*administrate

\*administrate, a back-formation from *administration*, is a needless variant of *administer*—e.g.:

 - "Inevitably, his unenlightened attempts to teach and *administrate* \[read *administer*\] were doomed to failure." Roger Braun, "Remember the Bad, Old Administrator-Less," *Wis. State J.*, 31 Dec. 1994, at A9.

 - "The only people who would have known just how \[much trouble\] Jeremy was in were the people involved in *administrating* \[read *administering*\] the wages deal." Christopher Brookmyre, *Quite Ugly One Morning* 108 \(1996\).

 - "This musical comedy features adults playing prepubescent teens and the grownups who *administrate* \[read *administer*\] the contest." "Is It the Weekend Yet?," *Hartford Courant*, 17 Aug. 2015, at B8.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*administrate* for *administer*: Stage 1 Current ratio in print \(*administering* vs. *\*administrating*\): 31:1
