# acre

acre measures area, not length. To put it differently, *acre* is a square, not a linear, measure. But the word is sometimes misused as if it referred to length—e.g.: "There were about 1,500 people buried at the Hardin Cemetery, once a pristine landscape *nine acres across* \[read *of nine acres*\] and now a muddy lake where minnows and snapping turtles live alongside broken headstones and toppled graves." Isabel Wilkerson, "Cruel Flood: It Tore at Graves, and at Hearts," *N.Y. Times*, 26 Aug. 1993, at A1.
