# Arapaho

Arapaho, the name of American Indian people of the Great Plains region, dates in English from the early 1800s. It has been predominantly so spelled since about 1900. The older spelling *Arapahoe*, however, has been retained by the northern branch of the tribe to refer to specific corporate bodies. Nevertheless, the spelling *Arapaho* is the one most often seen in ethnographic literature.

 - Current ratio in print \(*Arapaho* vs. *Arapaho*e in AmE\): 4:1
