# agape

agape. There are two words so spelled, with unrelated senses, different pronunciations, and even different syllabification. As a noun, *agape* /ә-**gah**-pay/ comes from a Greek word for "brotherly love" and denotes (1) "spiritual love in contrast to earthly or sensual love," or (2) "a love feast." As an adverb, *agape* /ә-**gayp**/ means "with mouth wide open, esp. in awe, surprise, or fear."
