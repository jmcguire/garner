# afflatus

afflatus; **\*afflation; \*inflatus**. For the sense "inspiration" or "supernatural impulse," *afflatus* is the standard—though rare—term. E.g.: "Richard Brookhiser, the author and editor, reminded us that it is generally alien to Dole's temperament to act as the advocate, charged with disseminating his *afflatus*—he is more a technician." William F. Buckley, "Are the Conservatives Mutinous?," *Buffalo News*, 10 Apr. 1996, at B3. *\*Afflation* and *\*inflatus* are needless variants.

The plural of *afflatus* is *afflatuses*, not *\*afflati*. See hypercorrection \(a\) & plurals \(b\).
