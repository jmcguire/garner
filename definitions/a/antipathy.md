# antipathy

antipathy \(= strong aversion; intense dislike\) is sometimes misused for *antithesis* \(= opposite; contrast\)—e.g.: "Jiang can wear tri-cornered hats and tour Independence Hall, but his regime represents the *antipathy* \[read *antithesis*\] of America's democratic values." "Jiang's Smiles Are a Thin Mask," *Wis. State J.*, 30 Oct. 1997, at A13.

*Antipathy* usually takes the preposition *toward* or *against* <they feel strong antipathy toward each other> <antipathy against the lame-duck mayor is palpable>. Also, it sometimes takes *to* or *for* <in the late 1990s, antipathy to tobacco manufacturers reached an all-time high> <society has always had an antipathy for child abuse>.

The adjectival form is *antipathetic*.
