# accuse

accuse; **charge**. One is *accused of*, then *charged with*, a crime. Perhaps under the influence of *charged with*, the verb *accused* is sometimes unidiomatically paired with *with*—e.g.: "Ross and Vince Fera, Local 57's recording secretary and a member of its executive board, were *accused with* \[read *accused of*\] violating the union's code of ethics." Jim McKay, "Monitor Accuses Union of Crime Ties," *Pitt. Post-Gaz.*, 25 Nov. 1997, at F1. (The headline writer got it right.) See **charge (a)**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*accused with* for *accused of*: Stage 1 Current ratio in print (*accused of* vs. \**accused with*): 136:1
