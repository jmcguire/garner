# ad-lib

ad-lib \(= to deliver \[speech or music\] extemporaneously or spontaneously\) is so hyphenated as a verb <she ad-libbed her lines>. It is two words as an adverb <she delivered the lines ad lib>. But what about the noun? Dictionaries differ, and usage has swung from one practice to another. For most of the 20th century, the unhyphenated form predominated in noun uses <several ad libs proved especially successful>, but since the early 1990s, the hyphenated form has predominated <her ad-libs drew uproarious laughter>. Today the unhyphenated form predominates only with adverbial uses.

 - Current ratio in print \(*the ad-libs* vs*. the ad libs*\): 1.2:1
