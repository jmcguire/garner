# allegedly

allegedly /ә-**lej**-id-lee/ does not mean "in an alleged manner," as it would if the adverb had been formed as English adverbs generally are. Wilson Follett considered adverbs like this one ugly and unjustified—especially *reportedly* \(*MAU* at 279\). Yet *allegedly* is a convenient space- and time-saver for *it is alleged that* or *according to the allegations*. Though not logically formed, *allegedly* is well established and, if used in moderation, unobjectionable. See -edly. Cf. **shamefacedly**.
