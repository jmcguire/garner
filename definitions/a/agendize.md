# \*agendize

\*agendize, an ugly bureaucratic neologism meaning "to put on an agenda," originated in the late 1980s and has spread—e.g.:

 - " ‘Mr. Eliot did not make a decision on his own,' he \[Robert Bacon\] said. ‘We made an error and did not *agendize* that item.' " Craig Quintana, "West Covina Renewal Deals to Get Closer Look," *L.A. Times*, 28 Apr. 1988, San Gabriel Valley §, at 1.

 - "William R. Ferris . . . thought that starting a tradition of presidential lecturers would raise the visibility of the annual Jefferson series and set up a nice chance for presidents to speak away from their usual *agendized* forums." Tom Peepen, "Presidency Becomes a Casualty," *Times Union* \(Albany\), 29 Sept. 1999, at A9.

 - "Twice the Tustin school board has *agendized*, then tabled, the matter." George Stewart, "Red Tape Slows After-School Program Series," *Orange County Register*, 27 Sept. 2001, Tustin §, at 1.

The word remains jargon and should be voted down.
