# accoutrement

accoutrement \(= a supplementary item of dress or equipment; accessory\) is predominantly so spelled in AmE and BrE alike, and always has been. Though given preference in several American dictionaries, *accouterment* has never prevailed in print. Likewise, the prevailing verb is *accoutre* in both AmE \(by a slight margin\) and BrE \(by a 5:1 ratio\).

Having been fully naturalized in the 1500s, the word is pronounced /ә-**koo**-tәr-mәnt/ in AmE. The British are more likely to use the Frenchified pronunciation /ә-koo-trә-**mah*n***/.

Current ratio in print \(*accoutrement* vs. *accouterment* in World Englishes\): 7:1 Current ratio in print \(*accoutrement* vs. *accouterment* in AmE\): 5:1 Current ratio in print \(*accoutred* vs. *accoutered* in World Englishes\): 4:1 Current ratio in print \(*accoutred* vs. *accoutered* in AmE\): 1.4:1
