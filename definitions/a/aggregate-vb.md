# aggregate, vb

aggregate, vb.

**A. Sense.** *Aggregate* /**ag**-rә-gayt/ = to bring together a mass of discrete things or individuals into a whole. The verb is sometimes misused for *total* in reference to sums—e.g.: "Trade between China and Taiwan has grown steadily in the past decade, *aggregating* \[read *totaling*\] almost $21 billion." V.H. Krulak, "China's Weapon Against Taiwan," *San Diego Union-Trib.*, 9 Mar. 1996, at B8.

**B. *Aggregate together*.** This redundancy dates from the late 1700s. Although it has declined since 1900, it still appears in print today—e.g.: "Terrestrial dust is mostly tiny fragments abraded from larger things; some of it may be even smaller things *aggregating together* \[read *aggregating* or *clumping together*\] to form motes of dust." C. Claiborne Ray, "Q&A," *N.Y. Times*, 13 Feb. 1996, at C5.
