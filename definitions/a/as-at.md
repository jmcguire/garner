# as at

as at (= as of) originated in the late 1800s and remains characteristic today chiefly of BrE financial jargon—e.g.:

 - "This book reflects the law *as at* August 1986." Stanley Berwin, *The Economist Pocket Lawyer* i (1986).

 - "The restructuring was needed, the group said, after a cumulative loss of GBP 75.4m *as at* December 31, 1996." "Seafield Plans Restructuring," *Fin. Times*, 27 Nov. 1996, at 22.

Cf. the cautionary note at **as of**.
