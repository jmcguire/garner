# astigmatism

astigmatism \(= an uneven curvature of the cornea; a defect in a lens\) is often incorrectly written \(and pronounced\) *stigmatism*—which, according to the *OED*, is actually a rare antonym of *astigmatism*. Sometimes the word is mistaken for a singular count noun \(*\*a stigmatism* instead of *astigmatism*\), as in the second and third examples below—e.g.:

 - "Singh, the 1983 and 1995 Buick winner, had laser eye surgery last week to correct slight *stigmatism* \[read *astigmatism*\] and nearsightedness." John Nicholson, "Major Preparation Makes Tiger Woods Pass on Buick Open," *Nat'l Post*, 8 June 2000, at B15.

 - "He has tried contacts, too, but that didn't work out, primarily due to *a stigmatism* \[read *astigmatism*\] on his right eye." Tim Buckley, "Polynice Eyes Better Shooting," *Deseret News* \(Salt Lake City\), 5 Oct. 2000, at D1.

 - "One son was in special education classes, the mom was suffering from *a stigmatism* \[read *astigmatism*\], another son was juggling college and work to help support the family." Piper Nichole, *The For Sale by Owner Handbook* 223 \(2005\).

For further misuse of *stigmatism*, see **stigma \(b\)**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*a stigmatism* for *astigmatism*: Stage 1
