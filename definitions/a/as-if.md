# as if

as if; as though. Attempts to distinguish between these idioms have proved futile. Euphony should govern the choice of phrase. As a matter of word frequency, *as if* has always been the more common phrase in Modern English. Eric Partridge suggested that *as if* is usually preceded by a comma and that *as though* rarely is (*U&A* at 47).

A better distinction is that *as if* often suggests the more hypothetical proposition when cast in the subjunctive <as if he were a god>. E.g.: "As he came closer to it he felt a great strength flow into him from the west, *as if* Silence had taken him by the hand after all." Ursula K. Le Guin, *Tales from Earthsea* 161 (2001). By contrast, *as though* suggests a more plausible suggestion <it looks as though it might rain>.
