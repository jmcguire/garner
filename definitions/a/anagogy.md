# anagogy

anagogy /**an**-ә-goh-jee/ \(= mystical interpretation of a text, as opposed to literal or even figurative interpretation\), a Greek loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has been predominantly so spelled—on the analogy of *analogy*—since the late 1970s. Before that, *anagoge* was the predominant form.

The corresponding adjective is predominantly *anagogical*, the form *anagogic* being a variant. See -ic/-ical.

Current ratio in print \(*anagogy* vs. *anagoge*\): 5:1

Current ratio in print \(*anagogical* vs. *anagogic*\): 3:1
