# awestruck

awestruck \(= greatly impressed by the importance, difficulty, or majesty of someone or something\), first recorded in the early 1600s in the writing of John Milton, was mostly hyphenated \(\**awe-struck*\) throughout the 1800s, but by 1907 it had become predominantly solidified in AmE and BrE alike. Oddly, the less frequently used past-participial adjective has remained hyphenated \(\**awe-stricken*, as opposed to \**awestricken*\). But that term is really a needless variant of *awestruck*, since *struck* itself is the standard past participle of *strike*. See **strike** & **stricken**.

Current ratio in print \(*awestruck* vs. \**awe-struck*\): 5:1

Current ratio in print \(*awestruck voice* vs. \**awe-stricken voice*\): 1:1.2
