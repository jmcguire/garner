# allegro

allegro. Pl. *allegros*, preferably not the Italian *allegri*. But some writers use the pedantic foreign plural—e.g.: "His delicate touch made the andante movements glisten like an expanse of water, his 3rd movement *allegri* \[read *allegros*\] skip deliriously." Rick Jones, "Rhythm, Religion and Gowns of Green for Television," *Evening Standard*, 29 July 1996, at 7. See plurals \(d\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*allegros* vs. *allegri*\): 2.2:1
