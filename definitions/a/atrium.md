# atrium

atrium \(= \[1\] a high open space within a building of more than one story; or \[2\] either of two spaces at the top of the heart for pushing blood into the ventricles\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the late 1500s, has two plurals: the Latinate *atria* and the homegrown *atriums*. *Atria* has always been strongly predominant. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*two atria* vs. *two atriums*\): 20:1

 - Current ratio in print \(*large atria* vs. *large atriums*\): 3:1
