# anemone

anemone /ә-**nem**-ә-nee/ (= \[1\] a flower of the buttercup family; or \[2\] a flowerlike sea polyp) is so spelled and pronounced—not *\*anenome*. But the misspelling (like the mispronunciation /ә-**nen**-ә-mee/) is common—e.g.:

 - "On the right rear tail brilliantly colored clown fish have made their home in a sea *anenome* \[read *anemone*\], waving in the warm water like a happy baseball crowd." Bob Howarth, "Guadalcanal's Ironbottom Sound: A Terrible Beauty," *Indianapolis Star*, 6 Aug. 1995, at K1.

 - "Two further points, illustrated by the *anenome* \[read *anemone*\] fish, are worth highlighting." Michael Begon, Colin R. Townsend & John L. Harper, *Ecology: From Individuals to Ecosystems* 555 (2006) (word misspelled five times on page).

The phrase *any money* can be a helpful mnemonic device for spelling, *an M on E* for pronunciation. See metathesis.

## Language-Change Index

 - *anemone* misspelled *\*anenome*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*anemone* vs. \**anenome*): 303:1
