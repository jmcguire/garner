# apodictic

apodictic /ap-ә-**dik**-tik/ \(= clearly proved by incontestable evidence\), a Latin/Greek loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has been the standard form of the word since 1940. Three variants that formerly vied with it are \**apodeictic*, \**apodictical*, and \**apodeictical*. See -ic/ical.

 - Current ratio in print \(*apodictic* vs. \**apodeictic* vs. \**apodictical* vs. \**apodeictical*\): 92:20:1.1:1
