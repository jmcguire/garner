# acerbic

acerbic, in AmE, is sometimes said to be inferior to *acerb* because it is a syllable longer. But *acerb* is so rare and *acerbic* so common—much more common than its sibling in print today—that the criticism is misplaced. *Acerbic* is also standard in BrE, in which *acerb* is now virtually unknown. \(Predominant in the 1700s, *acerb* rapidly declined in the 1800s.\) The corresponding noun is *acerbity*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*acerbic* vs. *acerb*\): 54:1
