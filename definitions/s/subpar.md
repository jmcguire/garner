# subpar

subpar. This vogue term has a curious double meaning. In ordinary contexts, of course, it means "below average, not measuring up to normal standards" \<a child's subpar report card\>. But in golf, the term means "below par for a hole, round, or match"—par being the standard number of strokes for a hole or course \<three subpar rounds in his last four tournaments\>. Oddly enough, then, it's desirable to be subpar in golf but not in other aspects of life. Both senses date from the 1920s.
