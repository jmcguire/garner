# sauté

sauté (= to cook \[some type of food\] in a small amount of hot oil or fat), a French loanword dating in English from the early 1800s, predominantly retains its acute accent. (See diacritical marks.) As a verb, it is inflected *sautéed* and *sautéing*. (The past-tense form \**sautéd* is a variant.) The once-common noun \<a sauté of flounder\> now appears considerably less frequently than inflected forms of the verb \<sautéed flounder\>. The adjective \<sauté pan\> is also more common than the noun. See gallicisms.

 - Current ratio in print (*sautéed* vs. \**sautéd*): 66:1
 - Current ratio in print (*sautéing* vs. \**sautéeing*): 44:1
 - Current ratio in print (*sautéed potatoes* vs. \**sauté potatoes*): 1.9:1
