# sanction

sanction = \(1\) to approve; or \(2\) to penalize. The word is generally understood as bearing sense 1. Hence lawyers, who use it primarily in sense 2, are likely to be misunderstood. See contronyms.

As a noun, *sanction* is burdened by the same ambiguity, meaning either \(1\) "approval" <governmental sanction to sell the goods>, or \(2\) "penalty" <the statute provides sanctions for violations of the act>. In phrases such as *give sanction to*, the word means "approval"—while *issue sanctions against* shows disapproval.
