# savable

savable; **salvable**; **salvageable**. *Savable* \(so spelled—not *\*saveable*\) means "capable of being saved." Originally this word was used in theological senses, and it still carries religious connotations. *Salvable*, too, has a theological sense \("admitting of salvation"\), as well as the sense \(used of ships\) "that can be saved or salvaged." *Salvageable*, dating from the 1910s in AmE, has become common in the sense "that can be salvaged"—e.g.: "The NTSB will study the engine and other *salvageable* parts to try to determine if there was a mechanical failure." Tom Wells, "Skydive Survivor May Quit the Sport," *Chattanooga Times*, 27 May 1997, at A6.

Current ratio in print \(*savable* vs. *\*saveable*\): 2:1
