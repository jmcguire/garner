# ski

ski \(= either of a pair of long, thin, narrow strips of wood, plastic, or metal used for gliding over snow or water\), a Norwegian loanword dating in English from the mid-1700s, once made the plural without a change in form (what linguists call a "zero plural"), but that usage is obsolete. The standard plural has been *skis* since 1900 at the latest. \**Skiis* is another variant plural. Cf. **skied**.

Current ratio in print \(*skis* vs. \**skiis*\): 520:1
