# stained glass

stained glass, not *\*stain glass*, is the standard form—e.g.: "One of the most prominent features will be a round *stain glass* \[read *stained-glass*\] window above the altar." Elizabeth Crooker, "It Took Seven Years, but They Finally Have Church," *Union Leader* \(Manchester, N.H.\), 21 Mar. 1997, at A5. \(On the reason for the hyphen in that correction, see phrasal adjectives.) Cf. **fine-toothed comb**, **iced tea** & **skim milk**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*stain glass* for *stained glass*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print \(*stained glass* vs. *\*stain glass*\): 101:1
