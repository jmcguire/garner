# seigneur

seigneur; **\*seignior**. *Seigneur* /say-**nyʊr**/, a 16th-century borrowing from Middle French, refers to a man of rank or authority, especially a feudal lord—or else to a Canadian member of the landed gentry. \(The semifeudal system of landownership in Quebec ended in 1854.\) *\*Seignior* is a variant spelling.

 - Current ratio in print \(*seigneur* vs. \**seignior*\): 12:1
