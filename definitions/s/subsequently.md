# subsequently

subsequently.

**A. For *later*.** Using the four-syllable word in place of the two-syllable word is rarely, if ever, a good stylistic choice.

**B. And *consequently*.** Though both words contain the sense "following" or "occurring later," *consequently* has primarily a causal nuance: "occurring because of." Frequently *subsequently* (which has no connotation of causation) is misused for *consequently*—e.g.: "My taste tends to be masculine to begin with. Chintz is not on my radar screen. *Subsequently* \[read *Consequently*\] I don't attract frou frou clients." Peter Marino, in "Peter Marino, Architect," *Celebrated Living*, Fall 2008, at 88, 89 (interview). See **consequent**.

## Language-Change Index

 - *subsequently* misused for *consequently*: Stage 1
