# suffrage

suffrage \(= \[1\] the right to vote; or \[2\] a short prayer\) is occasionally misspelled *\*sufferage*—e.g.:

 - "\[C\]lass formation in the cultural and political sense—as well as in the sense of economic identification—entails, among other things, the determined struggle of nascent working-class factions for *sufferage* \[read *suffrage*\]." Stanley Aronowitz, *The Politics of Identity* 132 \(1992\).

 - "Issue of Voting Rights for Blacks Roiled Early *Sufferage* \[read *Suffrage*\] Movement." Headline, *Telegram & Gaz.* \(Worcester\), 28 Feb. 2008, at A11.

 - "To better oppose the increasingly oppressive apartheid laws, \[Helen\] Suzman and 10 others broke away to form the Progressive Party—though she initially did not support universal, one-man-one-vote *sufferage* \[read *suffrage*\]." Denis Goldberg, "Appraisal: Suzman's Courage Failed Her," *The Forward*, 16 Jan. 2009, at 4.

Current ratio in print \(*suffrage* vs. \**sufferage*\): 2,400:1
