# subsidence

subsidence (= the act or process of settling or sinking) is pronounced /sәb-**sīd**-әns/ or /**sәb**-si-dens/. The word is sometimes misspelled *\*subsidance*—e.g.: "Saltwater intrusion is a by-product, of sorts, of conversion and *subsidance* \[read *subsidence*\] in combination with tidal surges." Doug Pike, "Saving the Wetlands," *Houston Chron.*, 8 May 1997, at 13.

 - Current ratio in print (*subsidence* vs. \**subsidance*): 5,992:1
