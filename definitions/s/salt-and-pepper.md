# salt-and-pepper

salt-and-pepper \(= dotted or speckled with black and white\), dating as a metaphorical phrasal adjective from 1915, was preceded by *pepper-and-salt*, dating from the late 1700s. In fact, not until the late 1970s did *salt-and-pepper* become the predominant phrase. You could argue that *salt-and-pepper* describes something mostly white with black speckling, and that *pepper-and-salt* describes something mostly black with white speckling—and the ultrafastidious may wish to observe that distinction. But in practice the *salt–pepper* order of the phrase is strongly predominant regardless of such details.

Current ratio in print \(*salt-and-pepper beard* vs. *pepper-and-salt beard*\): 36:1
