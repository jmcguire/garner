# staid

staid /stayd/ (= serious, old-fashioned, and muted in temperament), a past-participial adjective dating from the 1500s, is actually an obsolete past-tense inflection of *stay*, which of course today is inflected *stay > stayed > stayed*. Until the late 1830s, \**staid behind* or \**staid there* was more common than *stayed behind* or *stayed there*. In any event, *staid* underwent differentiation from *stayed* as a past-participial adjective. Today it is frequently coupled with a similar adjective, as in *staid* *and sober*, *staid and respectable*, *staid and solemn*, or else *old and staid*, *grave and staid*, or *prudent and staid*.
