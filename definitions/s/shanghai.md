# shanghai

shanghai, v.t., = (1) to drug or otherwise make insensible and then abduct for service on a ship needing crew members; or (2) to influence by fraud or compulsion. The inflected forms are *shanghaied* and *shanghaiing*.

The verb has its origin in the slang of 19th-century San Francisco. When gold was discovered in California, many sailors deserted their ships in the San Francisco Bay to seek their fortunes in the goldfields. So ship captains constantly needed new crew members, willing or not. More than a few people in San Francisco passed out from drugs or alcohol and woke up to find themselves on extended voyages, especially to Shanghai, a major Chinese seaport and trading center. Hence to be *shanghaied* soon meant to be kidnapped and pressed into service.

Sense 2 became common by the end of World War I.

Like any other disparaging term having geographic associations, the term will give offense to some. (Cf. **welsher**.) It seems destined to drift into disuse. See prejudiced and prejudicial terms.
