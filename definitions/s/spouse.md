# spouse

spouse. Whenever you know that you're referring to a *husband* or *wife*, use one of those terms. *Spouse*, which carries a legalistic flavor, has traditionally appeared only when the context is not sex-specific. *Married spouse* is an infrequent redundancy.

With the advent of same-sex marriages in the 21st century, *spouse* experienced an upsurge. *What's your spouse's name?* is a safer, more considerate question than one that makes assumptions about a new acquaintance's sexual orientation. Some people prefer *partner* over *spouse*. If you know the person's preference, that's typically your best choice.
