# spick and span

spick and span (= spotlessly clean and tidy), a phrase dating from the 1600s, has always been predominantly so spelled. \**Spic and span* is a variant that gained prominence with the 1933 introduction of the trademarked cleaner *Spic and Span* (or, in the American market, *Spic 'n' Span*).

The tendency is to use the unhyphenated form when the adjective is used predicatively \<the floors were spick and span\> but to hyphenate when used attributively \<a spick-and-span apartment\>.

Historically there was a variant phrase—\**speck and span*—but it is now obsolete.
