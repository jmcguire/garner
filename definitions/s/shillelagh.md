# shillelagh

shillelagh /shi-**lay**-lә/ or /-lee/ \(= a wooden cudgel or club associated with Irish folklore—after *Shillelagh*, a small Irish town\), dating as a common noun from the late 1700s, has been predominantly so written since about 1935. The variant spellings \**shillelah*, \**shillalah*, and \**shillala* are now uncommon.

Current ratio in print \(*shillelagh* vs. \**shillelah* vs. \**shillalah* vs. \**shillala*\): 570:75:75:1
