# stockinette

stockinette (= \[1\] a soft, stretchable cotton material used esp. in bandages and undergarments; or \[2\] a knitting stitch made with alternating rows of purling and knitting), a noun dating from the late 1700s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1950. Before that, the old variant \**stockinet* was common—even predominant until 1920. See diminutives (f).

In any event, the word is old-fashioned. Today in sense 1, the more usual terms are *spandex* and *elastane*, both of which denote materials containing artificial fibers.

 - Current ratio in print (*stockinette* vs. \**stockinet*): 9:1
