# swan song

swan song \(= \[1\] the final piece of work produced by an artist or writer; or \[2\] the final appearance, act, or tour given by a performer\), a loan translation from the German *schwanengesang*, dates from the early 1800s. It is traditionally so written as two words—in AmE since the 1880s. In BrE, however, the solidified *swansong* became predominant about 1985. Both *swansong* and \**swan's song* are variants in AmE.

Current ratio in print \(*swan song* vs. *swansong* vs. \**swan's song* in World Englishes\): 29:14:1
