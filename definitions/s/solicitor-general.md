# solicitor general

solicitor general \(= a legal officer who works with the attorney general, usu. by handling the government's appellate work\), a title dating from the mid-1600s, contains a postpositive adjective \(*general*\). Hence the plural is *solicitors general*. But this plural form has been firmly established only since about 1920 in AmE. Before that, \**solicitor generals* was fairly widespread, just as it is today in BrE. But *solicitors general* predominates even there today. See **general**. Cf. **attorney general**.

Current ratio in print \(*solicitors general* vs. \**solicitor generals* in AmE\): 4:1
Current ratio in print \(*solicitors general* vs. \**solicitor-generals* vs. \**solicitor generals* in BrE\): 20:3:1
