# syllabus

syllabus. Pl. *syllabuses* or *syllabi*. American teachers have always been fond of the Latin plural. Ernest Gowers wrote that "the plural *-buses* is now more used than *-bi*" (*FMEU2* at 610). He was right about BrE in the 1960s, where *syllabuses* edged out *-bi* by a 12:1 ratio. But today *syllabi* has taken the lead everywhere. See plurals (b).

 - Current ratio in print (*syllabi* vs. *syllabuses* in World Englishes): 2.3:1
