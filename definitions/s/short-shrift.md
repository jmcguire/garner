# short shrift

short shrift \(= little or no attention; perfunctory consideration\), a Shakespearean phrase that made its debut in *Richard III* \(1597\), originally referred to inadequate time for confession before execution of a death sentence. The phrase is most commonly preceded by the verb *get*, *give*, *make*, or *receive*. Because *shrift* is a relatively uncommon word, some have transmuted the phrase *short shrift* into the mondegreen *short shift* \(which misleadingly suggests something associated with a cushy job\).

Current ratio in print \(*given short shrift* vs. \**given short shift*\): 238:1
