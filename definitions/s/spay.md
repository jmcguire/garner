# spay

spay \(= to neuter by removing the ovaries from \[a female animal\]\) is used so often in the past-tense and past-participial form \(*spayed*\) that it's sometimes confused with *spade*—e.g.:

 - "Task force will probe way to curb county's animal population. Suggestions: mandatory *spade and neuter* \[read *spaying-and-neutering*\] laws." "Arizona," *USA Today*, 22 Aug. 1991, at A8.

 - "The Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals found that the animals are fed well and are *spade* \[read *spayed*\] and neutered." Krista Paul, "In Sea Cliff, 1 House, 70 Pets and a 911 Call," *Newsday* \(N.Y.\), 8 July 1993, at 22.

 - "Event sponsors hope to raise $50,000 for the Humane Society's *spade and neuter* \[read *spay-and-neuter*\] clinic." "Yogarama 2000 to Raise Funds for Animal Support," *Atlanta J.-Const.*, 22 Apr. 2000, at E6.

See word-swapping.
