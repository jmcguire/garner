# sergeant major

sergeant major \(= a high-ranking noncommissioned officer\), dating from the late 1700s, consists of a noun \(*sergeant*\) coupled with a postpositive adjective \(*major*\). The phrase mostly lost its hyphen in AmE in the 1910s and in BrE in the 2000s. You might expect the plural to be *sergeants major*, and that is the form given by most modern dictionaries. But anomalously, *sergeant majors* has predominated since about 2000. This plural stands in sharp contrast to the analogous *attorneys general*. See postpositive adjectives.

 - Current ratio in print \(*sergeant majors* vs. *sergeants major*\): 1.6:1
