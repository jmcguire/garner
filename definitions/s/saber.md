# saber

saber \(= \[1\] an old-fashioned heavy sword with a curved blade; or \[2\] a light pointed sword used in fencing\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since 1935. BrE has always followed the spelling of the French etymon: *sabre*. See -er \(b\).

With the solid *lightsaber* \(= \[1\] the swordlike *Star Wars* weapon emitting a beam of destructive light; or \[2\] a toy modeled after this fictional weapon\), the AmE/BrE pattern is broken: perhaps because the films are American, the -*er* spelling predominates across World Englishes. In these *Star Wars*–related senses, the term is always solid.
