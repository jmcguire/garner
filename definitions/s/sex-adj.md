# sex, adj

sex, adj.; **sexual**. Both *sex discrimination* and *sexual discrimination* are widely used. The former predominates, perhaps because *sexual* has come to refer more to sexual intercourse and related acts of a physical nature. Hence *sexual* is becoming rare in contexts not involving intercourse or the drive to engage in it. Today, *sexual education* seems to suggest something rather different from *sex education*—e.g.: "Family planning officials at MexFam said they hope that this legislation will improve the quality of *sexual education* \[read *sex education*\] and promote the use of condoms." Abigail Davis, "Value of Safe Sex Stressed in Mexico," *Dallas Morning News*, 30 July 1993, at A22. See **gender**. Cf. **racial discrimination**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*sex discrimination* vs. *sexual discrimination*\): 6:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*sex characteristics* vs. *sexual characteristics*\): 1.1:1
