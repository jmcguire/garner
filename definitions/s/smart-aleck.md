# smart aleck

smart aleck \(= a bumptious, annoying person who often makes wisecracks\), an Americanism dating from the mid-1800s, derives from *Aleck*, a nickname for *Alexander*. Since about 1900, the two-word spelling has predominated in English-language print sources. Among the variant spellings are \**smart alec*, \**smart-aleck*, \**smart-alec*, and \**smartaleck*.

The corresponding adjective is hyphenated *smart-alecky*—not \**smartalecky* or \**smart* *alecky*.
