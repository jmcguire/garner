# somebody

somebody; **someone**. The words are equally good; euphony should govern the choice. *Someone* is often better by that standard. Although both of these indefinite pronouns date from the 1300s in English, *somebody* predominated through the 1700s and 1800s; only in the 1920s did *someone* surpass *somebody* in frequency of occurrence in print sources. That trend has continued ever since. Each has traditionally been treated as a singular noun that, for purposes of concord, is the antecedent of a singular pronoun. But this has changed with the increasing acceptance of the singular *they*. (See **they (a).)** *\*Some body* and *\*some one* as two words are obsolete spellings.

On treating these terms as singular or plural, see pronouns (d). Cf. **anyone (b)**.
