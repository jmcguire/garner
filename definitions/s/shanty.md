# shanty

shanty /**shan**-tee/ (= a song that sailors sing in rhythm with their heaving or hauling), a corrupted French loanword dating in English from the mid-1800s, has traditionally been so written in BrE—but *chantey* in AmE (\**chanty* being a variant spelling). Since about 2004, however, *shanty* has come to predominate even in AmE.

Of course, the homonym *shanty* is an Americanism denoting a crudely built dwelling. Hence the differentiation in AmE spelling may be desirable, and the recent data may be skewed.

 - Current ratio in print (*sea shanty* vs. *sea chantey* vs. \**sea chanty* in World Englishes): 11:2.4:1
