# sync

sync; \***synch**. This word, a truncated form of *synchronization* \(or, as a verb, *synchronize*\), is spelled *sync* in print nine times as often as it's spelled *\*synch*. It occurs most frequently in the phrases *in sync* \(= correctly timed, compatible, in agreement\), *out of sync* \(meaning the opposite\), and *lip-sync*. The inflected forms are *synced* /sinkt/ and *syncing* /**sink**-ing/, both of which unfortunately suggest a sibilant *c* \(that is, an /s/ sound\). But the alternatives, *\*synched* and *\*synching*, are hardly better, since they might suggest a /ch/ sound instead of the proper /k/ sound. Most dictionaries are in \(ahem\) tune with the prevalent usage.

Interestingly, lexicographers have traced both the verb and the noun back to 1929. It seems likely that earlier occurrences of the noun will be found to predate 1929. See **lip-sync**.
