# symmetrical

symmetrical; **symmetric**. From the late 1700s to about 1987, *symmetrical* was the usual adjective preceding nouns such as *arrangement*, *components*, *distribution*, and *pattern*. *Symmetric* was little used until the mid-1900s; by 1987 it had surpassed its sibling in frequency of use, especially preceding such nouns as *encryption*, *functions*, *group*, *matrices*, and *tensor*—that is, usually in more technical contexts.

This shift toward *symmetric* probably reflected a yearning for symmetry, since *asymmetric* had become predominant over *asymmetrical* by the late 1970s—and during the early 1980s it probably made little sense for the usual terms to be *symmetrical* but *asymmetric*. Beginning in the mid-1980s, *asymmetric information* became something of a buzz phrase. The other most common nouns modified by *asymmetric* are *carbon*, *distribution*, *synthesis*, *top*, *unit*, and *warfare*.

The upshot is that *symmetric* and *asymmetric* are now the prevalent antonyms, but the *-al* forms more commonly occur in a few phrases such as *(a)symmetrical* *arrangement*, *(a)symmetrical relations*, and *(a)symmetrical power*. Euphony and prevalent usage with a particular phrase should govern the choice. See -ic/-ical.
