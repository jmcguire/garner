# stock in trade

stock in trade (= \[1\] goods, tools, or materials kept, used, or sold in one's job; or \[2\] anything that typifies or characterizes a particular person or group), a phrase dating from the early 1700s, has always been predominantly so written in AmE. That form was once also predominant in BrE, until about 1870; since then, the hyphenated *stock-in-trade* has become the usual BrE form.

 - Current ratio in print (*stock in trade* vs. *stock-in-trade* in AmE): 1.1:1
 - Current ratio in print (*stock-in-trade* vs. *stock in trade* in BrE): 1.1:1
