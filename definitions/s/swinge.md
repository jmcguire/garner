# swinge

swinge \(= \[1\] to beat or whip; \[2\] to chastise; or \[3\] to singe or scorch\), an archaism dating in English from the 1100s, makes the present participle *swingeing*. The *e* is retained to disambiguate the present participle from that of *swing*. In BrE, *swingeing cuts* are severe reductions in spending by a government or a company. The term is much less common in AmE. Cf. **twinge**.
