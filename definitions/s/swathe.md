# swathe

swathe, n. & vb.; **swath**, n. Let's first consider the nouns. In AmE, *swathe* generally denotes a wrapping, bandage, or other material in which something is enveloped. *Swath*, meanwhile, denotes a space cut by a scythe or mower; the grass or other low plants left within such a space; or a strip, belt, or lengthwise extent of something \(especially land\). To *cut a wide swath* is to make an ostentatious display or to attract attention.

In BrE, things are different: as a noun, *swathe* is taking over all senses—or has taken over. *Swath* is very much in decline even as a noun.

But in both AmE and BrE, the verb *swathe* means \(1\) to wrap up closely or fully, \(2\) to bandage or otherwise bind, or \(3\) in a figurative sense, to enwrap or enfold; to envelop as with a wrapping.

*Swathe* is pronounced /swayth/ or /swahth/ as both noun and verb. *Swath* is pronounced /swahth/.

 - Current ratio in print \(*a wide swath* vs. *\*a wide swathe* in World Englishes\): 6:1
