# scaloppine

scaloppine /skal-ә-**pee**-nee/ \(= a dish consisting of thin slices of meat, usu. veal, either sautéed or coated with flour and fried\), an Italian loanword dating in English from the early 20th century, has always been predominantly so spelled. **Scaloppini*, \**scallopini*, and \**scalloppine* are variants. *WNWCD* also lists \**scallopeni*, but that spelling occurs too infrequently to register at all in Google's ngrams.

 - Current ratio in print \(*scaloppine* vs. \**scaloppini* vs. \**scallopini* vs. \**scalloppine*\): 120:118:60:1
