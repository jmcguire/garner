# sibylline

sibylline (= prophetic; of, relating to, or involving fortune-telling) is often misspelled *\*sybilline*—and has been so since the early 1800s, doubtless through the influence of the etymologically distinct word *sybarite*. E.g.: "There were Joan's often *sybilline* \[read *sibylline*\] remarks—‘Of course, we always do Tibet from the north.' " Nicholas Haslam, "Joan Lady Camrose: Family Fortunes," *Guardian*, 29 May 1997, at 17. The word is pronounced /**sib**-ә-līn/ or /-leen/.

 - Current ratio in print (*sibylline* vs. \**sybilline*): 10:1
