# shudder

shudder; **shutter**. To *shudder* is to shake briefly from fear, the cold, or revulsion. If a vehicle or machine *shudders*, it shakes violently and usually rattles until it is turned off. The idiom *I shudder to think* expresses the idea that the speaker prefers not to consider something because it is so unpleasant. As a noun, *shudder* denotes a shaking movement.

A *shutter* is either \(1\) one of a pair of usu. wooden or metal window covers that, if functional, can be closed to keep light out or locked to deter illegal entry, or \(2\) the part of a traditional camera that opens briefly to expose the film to light. As a verb, to *shutter* is to close an office, a business, etc. either for a short time or permanently.

Although some people differentiate between the two words in pronunciation—/**shә**-dәr/ vs. /**shә**-tәr/—the words are homophones in many speech communities. Hence they get confounded in a variety of uses.
