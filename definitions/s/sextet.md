# sextet

sextet (= a group of six), a Latin loanword dating in English from the late 1700s, competed for a long time with the Italianate **sestet*, dating from 1811. (The Italian etymon is *sestetto*.) The spellings *sextet* and \**sestet* were close competitors until the mid-1970s, when the former became strongly predominant in English-language print sources. Two variants apart from \**sestet* sometimes appear: \**sextette* and \**sestette*. See diminutives (f).

 - Current ratio in print (*sextet* vs. \**sestet* vs. \**sextette* vs. \**sestette*): 219:163:17:1
