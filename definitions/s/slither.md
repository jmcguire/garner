# slither

slither, vb.; **sliver**, n. *Slither* = to slide over a surface with twisting or side-to-side motions, as a snake does \<we slithered down the muddy slope\>. *Sliver* = a thin piece or shard of something that has been cut or broken off \<a sliver of cheese\> \<not a sliver of evidence\>. The most common error with these words is to misuse *slither* for *sliver*—e.g.: "There's not a *slither* \[read *sliver*\] of difference between Rudd and Gillard on policy." Terry McCrann, "Labor in Crisis," *Advertiser* (Aus.), 23 Feb. 2012, at 6. The opposite error also occurs—e.g.: "For the connoisseur like Joe there's Boa, his constrictor, *slivering* \[read *slithering*\] down the banisters, her tongue darting expectantly for the kiss he always gives her." Sue Arnold, "Why You Should Never Give a Dog a Clone," *Independent*, 6 Aug. 2005, at 38.

 - Current ratio in print (*a sliver of* vs. \**a slither of *): 33:1
 - Current ratio in print (*slithered down* vs. \**slivered down*): 178:1
