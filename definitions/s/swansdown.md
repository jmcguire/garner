# swansdown

swansdown \(= \[1\] the soft underfeathers of a swan—used in trimming clothes, pillows, etc.; or \[2\] a heavy cotton flannel with a thick nap—made with a sateen weave\), dating from the early 1600s, is perhaps a loan translation of German *schwanendaune*, Swedish *svandun*, or Danish *svanedun*. The English word has been predominantly solid since about 1862 in BrE and 1906 in AmE. The older forms \**swan's-down* and \**swan's down* are variants today.

 - Current ratio in print \(*swansdown* vs. \**swan's-down* vs. \**swan's down*\): 3:1.3:1
