# shovel pass

shovel pass; \***shuffle pass**. The words *shuffle* and *shovel* cause confusion in a term from American football: *shovel pass*, in which the ball is scooped or flipped forward underhand as you might shovel dirt or snow. Some sportswriters have wrongly written *\*shuffle pass*—e.g.: " ‘But as I'm watching, they're doing something about our game with the Bills and they show Buffalo running a play on film—\(Jim\) Kelly throwing a *shuffle pass* \[read *shovel pass*\] to Thurman Thomas.' " "So They Say . . . ," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 13 Mar. 1994, at E2 \(quoting Jimmy Johnson, then coach of the Dallas Cowboys\).

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*shuffle pass* for *shovel pass*: Stage 1
