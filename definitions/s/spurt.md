# spurt

spurt; \***spirt**. Most AmE dictionaries list *\*spirt* merely as a variant of *spurt*. In 1926, H.W. Fowler suggested a differentiation: use *\*spirt* in the sense "gush, jet, flow" <a spirt of blood> <oil spirts up from the ground>, and reserve *spurt* for "sprint, burst, hustle" <work done in spurts> <Bailey spurted past>. But this distinction never took hold.

 - Current ratio in print (*spurted* vs. *\*spirted*): 42:1
