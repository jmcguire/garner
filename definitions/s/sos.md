# SOS

SOS. The signal for emergency help, especially at sea, is not an abbreviation (as folk etymology would have it) for "save our souls" or "save our ship." (See etymology (d).) It was adopted by the International Radio Telegraph Convention in 1908 as a universal code because it is easy to remember, easy to send, and easy to recognize by light or sound: three short pulses, three long pulses, three short pulses. Because it was never an abbreviation, there has never been a need to use periods. The plural form is SOSs.
