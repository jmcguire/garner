# sale

sale. Something *for sale* is simply being offered for a specified price. Something *on sale* is being offered at a discounted price. The two phrases are sometimes confounded—e.g.: "Theaters now have to choose for themselves whether to put their entire capacities *on sale* \[read *for sale*\], and some smaller venues are still operating with caution by spacing seats out." Matt Wolf, "At 82, Playing Hamlet to a London Full House," *N.Y. Times*, 9 Aug. 2021, at C2. (The piece is about selling fewer seats, not selling discounted tickets.) Cf. **hard sell**.
