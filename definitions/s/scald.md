# scald

scald \(= \[1\] to burn with hot liquid or steam; or \[2\] to heat \[milk or other liquid\] almost to the boiling point\), a verb dating from the 1200s, has also functioned as a noun since the early 1600s. In the noun senses, the term refers either to a burn on the skin caused by hot liquid or steam, or else to a brownish discoloration of fruits or other plants caused by the scorching sun or other conditions.

Occasionally *scald* gets confounded with *scold*, an etymologically unrelated word. Although someone who scolds is likely to be emotionally heated, it's wrong to write \**scolding hot* or similar phrases.

 - Current ratio in print \(*scalding hot* vs. \**scolding hot*\): 37:1
