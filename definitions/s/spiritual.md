# spiritual

spiritual; **spiritualistic**; **spirituous**; **\*spiritous**; **spirituel**; **spirituelle**; **spirited**. *Spiritual* is the broadest of these terms, meaning "of, relating to, or involving the spirit or soul; concerned with sacred or religious things" <spiritual rites>. *Spiritualistic* = of, relating to, or involving the belief that departed spirits communicate with and show themselves to the living, esp. through mediums <a spiritualistic proceeding known as a séance>. *Spirituous* = containing or consisting of alcohol <spirituous beverages>. E.g.: "The purveyors of *spirituous* liquors have lately taken to advertising on TV after years of voluntarily absenting themselves." Tom Teepen, "Purge the Vices, Ignore a Crisis," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 7 Apr. 1997, at A11. *\*Spiritous*, an archaism in the sense of "highly refined or dematerialized," is also a needless variant of *spirituous.* *Spirituel* \(masculine\) or *spirituelle* \(feminine\), a rare term, means "witty" or "having a refined and lively sense of humor." Finally, *spirited* means "full of spirit; lively; energetic" <a spirited debate> or "of a certain nature" <mean-spirited taunts>.
