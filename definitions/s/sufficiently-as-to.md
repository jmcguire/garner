# sufficiently . . . as to

sufficiently . . . as to can usually be simplified to *enough to*—e.g.:

 - "He sees little evidence of Asian economies being *sufficiently supple as to* \[read *supple enough to*\] be able to change direction quickly." Peter Aspden, "Life in the Old Tortoise Yet," *Fin. Times*, 30 Sept. 1997, Survey §, at 1.

 - "His recommendations, while satisfactory, are not *sufficiently notable as to* \[read *notable enough to* or *so notable as to*\] make a lasting impression." Vaughn A. Carney, "Where Does the Nation Go from Here?," *Chicago Trib.*, 30 Nov. 1997, at 19.
