# scalawag

scalawag; **scallywag**; \***scallawag**. For this Americanism meaning "a scruffy, disreputable person" dating from the mid-1800s, the first is the standard spelling. Though the word predates the American Civil War, it was popularized during Reconstruction to denote a white Southerner who cooperated with freedmen and carpetbaggers to control state and local governments. It still retains this limited sense in historical writing. In BrE, the variant spelling *scallywag* has predominated since about 1900. *\*Scallawag* is still another variant.

 - Current ratio in print \(*scalawag* vs. *scallywag* vs. \**scallawag*\): 7:6:1
