# sculpt

sculpt; **sculpture**, v.t.; **\*sculp**. Although the preferred verb was long thought to be *sculpture* <to sculpture a bust>, *sculpt* \(a back-formation from *sculptor*\) is now the predominant form and should be accepted as standard. *\*Sculp* is a needless variant.

The predominance of *to sculpt* over *to sculpture* is a more recent phenomenon than you might suspect: the linguistic coup occurred in AmE in the early 1990s and in BrE in the early 2000s.

For the agent noun, *sculptor* is preferred over *\*sculpturer*.

## Language-Change Index

*sculpt* as a verb: Stage 5
Current ratio in print \(*sculpted* vs. *sculptured* vs. \**sculped*\): 425:186:1
