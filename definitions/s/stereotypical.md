# stereotypical

stereotypical; **stereotypic**. The longer form is standard in figurative senses—e.g.: "Despite the wide variety of women who adhere to feminism, the *stereotypical* thinking that feminists are radicals lives on." Martha Ezzard, "South Can Use Fonda's Brand of Activism," *News & Record* \(Greensboro\), 30 Nov. 1997, at F2. *Stereotypic* is standard for the narrow sense "of or produced by stereotypy \(the process of printing from stereotype plates\)."

Current ratio in print \(*stereotypical roles* vs. \**stereotypic roles*\): 12:1
