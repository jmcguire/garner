# stoic

stoic, adj.; ***\**stoical**. H.W. Fowler argued in 1926 that *stoical* appears more often as a predicate adjective <his behavior was stoical>, while *stoic* is better used attributively <stoic indifference> \(*FMEU1* at 565\). No such distinction is maintained today, and the historical evidence is against Fowler. What can safely be said today is that *stoic* predominates over *\*stoical* in all uses in AmE and BrE alike. The longer form appears to be moribund. Unless specifically referring to the ancient Greek school of philosophy, *stoic* should not be capitalized. See -ic/-ical.

Current ratio in print \(*stoic* vs. *\*stoical*\): 5:1   
Current ratio in print \(*seemed stoic* vs. *seemed \*stoical*\): 6:1
