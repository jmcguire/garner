# sanctum

sanctum (= \[1\] a sacred place; or \[2\] a quiet, peaceful place secure from intrusions), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has predominantly made the plural *sanctums* since the early 1960s. The Latinate plural *sancta* appears mostly in Latin phrases (*sancta simplicitas*, *sancta Dei*, *sancta ecclesia*, etc.).

The emphatic phrase *sanctum sanctorum*, dating from the early 1800s and literally meaning "holy of holies," refers either to the most sacred chamber within a temple or, metaphorically, to an especially private and inviolable place where one meditates, studies, etc. (essentially, a more intense expression of sense 2 of *sanctum*). The plural is *sancta sanctorum*.

The set phrase *inner sanctum*, dating from the mid-1800s, is unobjectionable.
