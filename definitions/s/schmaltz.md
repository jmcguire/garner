# schmaltz

schmaltz /shmahlts/ \(= excessive or melodramatic sentimentality, esp. in music, literature, or art\), a Yiddish loanword dating in English from the early 20th century, has been predominantly so spelled since the 1930s. **Shmaltz* and \**schmalz* are variants.

Originally, the word was a culinary term denoting rendered animal fat \(usually from a chicken or goose\) or else melted butter. That use still has currency. By metaphorical extension, the idea of dripping with melted fat or butter was applied to entertainment and the arts.

Current ratio in print \(*schmaltz* vs. \**schmalz* vs. \**shmaltz*\): 44:1.6:1
