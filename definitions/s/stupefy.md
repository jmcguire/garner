# stupefy

stupefy. So spelled today, as it has been since the mid-1800s. The archaic form *\*stupify* is now regarded as a misspelling—e.g.:

 - "Insurance agents will *stupify* \[read *stupefy*\] their clients with \[obscure\] notations." James W. Johnson, *Logic and Rhetoric* 197 \(1962\).

 - "Drugs like heroin and cocaine typically *stupify* \[read *stupefy*\] and immobilize the user." Richard Morin, "New Facts and Hot Stats from the Social Sciences," *Wash. Post*, 27 Mar. 1994, at C5.

 - "It is, he said, that which *stupifies* \[read *stupefies*\]." "The Advocate and Introduction of Jagermeister," *Advocate* \(Baton Rouge\), 18 Apr. 2010, at F4.

See -fy. Cf. **liquefy** & **rarefy**.

Language-Change Index

*stupefy* misspelled *\*stupify*: Stage 2
Current ratio in print \(*stupefy* vs. \**stupify*\): 9:1
