# slingshoot \> slingshot \> slingshot

slingshoot \> slingshot \> slingshot. Although the word is most often a noun, the corresponding verb is so inflected. The erroneous past-tense and past-participial form *\*slingshooted* sometimes appears—e.g.: "It also *sling-shooted* \[read *slingshot*\] Schreyer into this week's U.S. Open." Jim Benson, "Hot Putter Fuels Big Comeback," *Pantagraph* (Bloomington, Ill.), 9 June 1997, at B1. See irregular verbs.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*slingshooted* for past-tense *slingshot*: Stage 1
