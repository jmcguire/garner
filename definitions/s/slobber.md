# slobber

slobber \(= \[1\] to have saliva, food, or drink dribble out of one's mouth; or \[2\] to express oneself with unrestrained sentimentality or effusive mawkishness\), a word of uncertain origin dating from about 1400, has been predominantly so spelled since the late 1820s. Before that, \**slabber* \(still a variant\) was often predominant. Among the less common but cognate verbs are *beslobber* \(dating from the 1300s\), *slaver* \(1300s\), and *beslaver* \(1500s\). Drool over that for a moment.

Current ratio in print \(*slobber* vs. \**slabber*\): 51:1
