# safe-deposit box

safe-deposit box. This is the original and predominant term, not *\*safety-deposit box*—e.g.: "He'll just go quietly, back to his day job at Home Depot, doing business with customers who don't know about that silver medal in his *safety-deposit box* \[read *safe-deposit box*\]." Mike Downey, "Bobsled Pioneer Prepares for a Slower Lifestyle," *Chicago Trib.*, 13 Feb. 2003, Sports §, at 1. The extra syllable in *safety* probably originated as an auditory error: people heard the phrase and associated the *de-* prefix on *deposit* with the *-ty* suffix on *safety*, and then duplicated the sound. In modern print sources, *safe-deposit box* is six times as common as *\*safety-deposit box*.

The phrasal adjective *safe-deposit* always requires a hyphen in this term.

## Language-Change Index

*\*safety-deposit box* for *safe-deposit box*: Stage 2
