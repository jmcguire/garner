# single out

single out (= to select or distinguish from others) is a phrasal verb that has been common since the 1600s. Although it's simplistically literal to insist that this idiom cannot refer to more than one thing, to couple it with a word such as *many* or *several* can be jarring. E.g.: "In both reviews, she *singled out* \[read *focused on*? *spotlighted*?\] several dancers for praise, including Yuan Yuan Tan in ‘Chi Lin' and Lorena Feijoo in Yuri Possokhov's ‘Damned.' " David Wiegand, "Making It Big in New York," *S.F. Chron.*, 16 Oct. 2002, at D3. (A possible rewrite: *In each review she praised individual dancers, including Yuan Yuan Tan in "Chi Lin" and Lorena Feijoo in Yuri Possokhov's "Damned."*)
