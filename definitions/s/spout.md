# spout

spout; **sprout**. As a noun, *spout* is either \(1\) a pipe, tube, or lip through which a liquid is discharged or poured <waterspout>; or \(2\) a discharge or stream of liquid from such a pipe, tube, or lip <the spout of seawater coming straight out of the whale>. As a verb, to *spout* is \(1\) to emit \(a liquid\) in a stream <the volcano spouted lava>; or \(2\) to speak or declaim rapidly, volubly, or unthinkingly <spouting gibberish>. The phrasal verb *spout off* means "to talk excessively about something in a boring or irksome way."

As a noun, *sprout* is \(1\) the shoot or tendril of a young plant <the sprouts are just emerging from the soil>; \(2\) the edible part of a vegetable that produces small cabbage-like projections such as bean sprouts, alfalfa sprouts, or Brussels sprouts <I'll have some mung-bean sprouts, please>; or \(3\) a very young person <he's just an inexperienced sprout>. As a verb, to *sprout* is \(1\) to begin producing shoots, buds, or leaves <the seedlings are starting to sprout leaves>; \(2\) to appear suddenly, esp. in great numbers <our neighborhood is sprouting for-sale signs>; or \(3\) to start growing <he's sprouting a beard\!>.

Unsurprisingly, these terms are susceptible to word-swapping. The confusion goes both ways.
