# supine

supine. Because the word means "lying on one's back," the phrase *\*supine on (one's) back* is a redundancy. It cropped up in the 1800s and still occasionally appears—e.g.: "Evans was to be laid *supine on his back* \[read *supine*\] as all four limbs were strapped with leather binds to a bed." Joe Domanick, "How California Failed Kevin Evans," *L.A. Times*, 26 Aug. 2001, Mag. §, at 10. See **prone**.
