# serendipity

serendipity (= luck in making happy accidental discoveries) was coined by the writer Horace Walpole in 1754. He derived it from the place name *Serendip*, formerly used for Sri Lanka. Walpole's neologism was rare until it gained wide currency in the 20th century. In the 1940s, the corresponding adjective *serendipitous* arose to describe either the people who make happy discoveries <the serendipitous adventurers> or the events they experience <a serendipitous meeting>.
