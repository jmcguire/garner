# smite > smote > smitten

smite > smote > smitten. So inflected in AmE and BrE alike. In the literal sense relating to hitting, this verb often appears in biblical allusions. When it appears in a secular setting, even figuratively, it can seem playful in tone—e.g.: "American and British readiness to *smite* Iraq has forced Mr. Hussein to listen." "Putting Steel in Democracy to Scare the Iraqis," *N.Y. Times*, 11 Feb. 1998, at A1, A6. See irregular verbs.

As a past-participial adjective, *smitten* means "deeply enamored" or "overwhelmed with affection." It occurs with much greater frequency than the other forms.
