# shred > shredded > shredded

shred > shredded > shredded. So inflected. Some people erroneously believe that this is an irregular verb that continues as *shred* in the past forms, perhaps from confusion with *shed* > *shed* > *shed*—e.g.: "Earlier this year, Mr. Lumbers attracted national attention after he *shred* \[read *shredded*\] 17,000 open edition prints holding a retail value of about $600,000." Kim Hanson, "Artists Urged to Change Approach to Business," *Nat'l Post*, 29 June 1999, at C8.
