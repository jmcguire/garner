# \*self-confessed

\*self-confessed is a redundancy that has come to be widespread. Yet it is not above editorial reproach—e.g.: "A court that frees a *self-confessed* \[read *confessed*\] murderer on a technicality would seem to bear responsibility for any harm that criminal may do in the future." Mario Pei, *Words in Sheep's Clothing* 86 \(1969\). Cf. **\*self-admitted**.

Language-Change Index

*\*self-confessed* for *confessed*: Stage 4   
Current ratio in print \(*a confessed spy* vs. *\*a self-confessed spy*\): 1:1.4
