# pincers

pincers \(= \[1\] a two-part gripping tool having two handles and two jaws; or \[2\] a grasping claw of a crustacean, insect, robot, etc.\), dating in English from about 1400, originally came into English as a French loanword (fr. OF *pinciers*\). The etymologically related verb *pinch*, also adapted from French, came into English about the same time—but with a -*ch*- from Anglo-French. Over the centuries, *pincers* and *pinch* retained their distinctive forms, and *pincher* became the agent noun meaning "someone who pinches." In Standard Written English, *pincers* \(in its distinctive senses\) has successfully staved off encroachments from *pinchers*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pair of pincers* vs. \**pair of pinchers*\): 25:1
