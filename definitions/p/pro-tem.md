# pro tem

pro tem is a now-anglicized term derived from the abbreviation for *pro tempore* (= for the time being). No longer should this fairly common latinism be italicized. It is used as a postpositive adjective in phrases such as *mayor pro tem*. Although it formerly required a period (i.e., *tem.*), today it is commonly and correctly used without one—e.g.:

 - "Led by co-chairs Lt. Governor Jack Riggs, Idaho, and Indiana Deputy Speaker *Pro Tem* Susan Crosby, committee members approved four resolutions." "Health Capacity Task Force Committee Meets," *State Government News*, 1 June 2002, at 32.

 - "Last Wednesday, police suspect a Superior Court *pro tem* judge got sauced, got into his Mercedes and proceeded to hit and kill an 18-year-old high school senior." Laurie Roberts, "Latest Road Outrage Doubly Outrageous," *Ariz. Republic*, 9 Apr. 2003, at B3.
