# pot liquor

pot liquor (= the liquid left in a pot after cooking), a term dating from the mid-1700s, is predominantly so written. Variant spellings are \**potlikker* (associated with Southern and Midland BrE), \**potlicker*, the solid form \**potliquor*, and the hyphenated \**pot-liquor*.

Since the 1960s, the phrase *pot liquor* has taken on a secondary sense in reference to a kind of tea infused with marijuana. Toke on that awhile: linguistically, it's reefer madness.
