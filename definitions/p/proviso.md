# proviso

proviso (= \[1\] a stipulation within a legal instrument, usu. introduced with the words *provided that*; or \[2\] a condition that someone insists on before agreeing to something), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has predominantly made the plural *provisos* since about 1885. Before that, \**provisoes* was a common spelling.

 - Current ratio in print (*provisos* vs. \**provisoes*): 36:1
