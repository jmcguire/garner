# petty larceny

petty larceny; **petit larceny**. These synonymous phrases denote larceny of property worth less than a statutorily prescribed amount, such as $100. Dating from the mid-1500s, *petit larceny* is the older spelling—preferably pronounced like *petty*, but sometimes /**pet**-it/. Since the latter half of the 1900s, the spelling *petty* has grown and in some places now predominates. But where *petit larceny* is the statutory phrase, it is often thought to avoid the suggestion that *petty* trivializes the criminal offense.

 - Current ratio in print \(*petty larceny* vs. *petit larceny*\): 3:1
