# \*potable drinking water

\*potable drinking water. This phrasing is redundant, since what is potable is drinkable—e.g.: "By hygiene I mean not only the use of toilets and the preservation of *potable drinking water* \[read *potable water*\] but a rational acceptance of birth control and an attitude neither phobic nor reckless toward sex." William A. Henry III, *In Defense of Elitism* 29–30 \(1994\). \(See redundancy.\) Its having appeared as early as 1900 is no recommendation of it. The phrase spread rapidly from 1960 to 2000.
