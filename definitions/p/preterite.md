# preterit\(e\)

preterit\(e\), n. & adj. This grammatical term refers to past tenses, but it fell into disuse during the 20th century because of the ambiguity contained within it. That is, some writers have used it to mean "the simple past tense," but others want it to include past participles. And in any event it smacks of sesquipedality, since *past* expresses the same idea. But if you must know how to spell it, *preterite* is standard in AmE and BrE alike. *\*Preterit* is a chiefly AmE variant.

 - Current ratio in print \(*preterite* vs. *\*preterit*\): 2.6:1
