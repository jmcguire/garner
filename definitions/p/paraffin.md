# paraffin

paraffin /**pa**-rә-fin/ (= \[1\] a soft white petroleum- or coal-based substance used for making candles; or \[2\] in BrE, kerosene), a German loanword dating in English from the early 1800s, was originally spelled **paraffine*—predominantly so until about 1862. Then the final *e* was dropped. Today \**paraffine* is but a variant spelling. The word is sometimes erroneously accented on the final syllable. See germanicisms.

 - Current ratio in print (*paraffin* vs. \**paraffine*): 49:1
