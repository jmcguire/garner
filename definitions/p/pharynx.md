# pharynx

pharynx /**fair**-ingks/ or /**fa**-ringks/ (= the muscular tube extending from the back of the throat to the esophagus), a Greek loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has historically formed the classical plural *pharynges* /fә-**rin**-jeez/. Since the mid-1900s, however, the anglicized plural *pharynxes* has begun to rival *pharynges*.

In AmE and BrE alike, the noun *pharynx* is liable to be misspelled and mispronounced \**pharnyx*. Cf. **larynx**.

 - Current ratio in print (*pharynges* vs. *pharynxes*): 2.5:1
 - Current ratio in print (*pharynx* vs. \**pharnyx*): 1,792:1
