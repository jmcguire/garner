# phosphor

phosphor \(= \[1\] any phosphorescent substance, as evidenced particularly by glowing after exposure to the sun or other light; or \[2\] when capitalized, the morning star, and esp. the planet Venus\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the early 1700s, has always been predominantly so spelled. **Phosphore* is a variant. What's the difference, then, between *phosphor* and the noun *phosphorus*? Essentially they're synonymous. *Phosphorus* is the more common noun, but *phosphor* is especially useful when a plural is called for <many different phosphors have this property>. Since the 1920s, *phosphors* has greatly predominated over *phosphori* in English-language print sources.

Current ratio in print \(*phosphorus* vs. *phosphor*\): 9:1
Current ratio in print \(*phosphor* vs. \**phosphore*\): 103:1
Current ratio in print \(*phosphors* vs. *phosphori*\): 185:1
