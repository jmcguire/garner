# \*proprietous

\*proprietous is a nonword that occasionally displaces *proper* or possibly *proprietary*—e.g.: "Besides regular guests, a *proprietous* \[read *proper*\] attitude infects bellmen, waiters, bartenders and the managing director of 32 years, Andrew Anspach." Diana Nelson Jones, "Revising the Round Table," *Pitt. Post-Gaz.*, 2 Apr. 1997, at B1. A back-formation from *propriety*, the word is highly improper. Cf. **\*improprietous**.

## Language-Change Index

*\*proprietous* for *proper*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*proper* vs. *\*proprietous*\): 96,302:1
