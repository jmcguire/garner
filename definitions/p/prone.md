# prone

prone; **prostrate**; **supine**. To lie *prone* or *prostrate* is to lie facedown. To lie *supine* is to lie face up. But in 1997, when a mass suicide occurred in San Diego, a local officer spread an incorrect usage to millions of listeners: " ‘They were lying *prone* on their backs,' the San Diego sheriff's spokesman told the TV camera." Richard K. Shull, "Fatal Dose of Copspeak," *Indianapolis News*, 1 Apr. 1997, at A11. Within seconds of that televised statement, "an earnest broadcast reporter, eager to set the record straight, declared, ‘They were *prostrate* on their backs.' " *Ibid.* The word that each speaker wanted, of course, was *supine*—or perhaps just *on their backs*.

For more on *prostrate*, see **prostate \(b\)**. See also **supine**.
