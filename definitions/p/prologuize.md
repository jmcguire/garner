# prologuize

prologuize; **prologize**. Although the term may seem like an ugly neologism in -ize, in fact it dates from Shakespeare's lifetime—about 1608. It means "to compose or deliver a prologue." The older form is *prologize* /**proh**-lah-gīz/, and that form has predominated for most of the word's history. But in the mid-1700s, the spelling *prologuize* was introduced and proved successful—for what is essentially a rare word. Although the two forms have been locked in an arcane rivalry ever since, *prologize* predominates.

 - Current ratio in print (*prologize* vs. *prologuize* in AmE): 10:1
