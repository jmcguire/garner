# precedent

precedent, adj., is inferior to *previous* or *prior*, except when used as a postpositive adjective in a phrase such as *condition precedent.* E.g.: "This rule in no respect impinges on the doctrine that one who makes only a loan on such paper, or takes it as collateral security for a *precedent* \[read *prior*\] debt, may be limited in his recovery to the amount advanced or secured." This adjective is best pronounced /pri-**seed**-әnt/, although /**pre**-si-dәnt/ is acceptable. For the noun form of *precedent*, see **precedence (b), (c)**.
