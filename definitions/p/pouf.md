# pouf

pouf /poof/ \(= \[1\] a soft roll of hair; \[2\] a fluffy part of a garment or accessory; or \[3\] a soft ottoman or cushion\), a French loanword dating in English from the late 1700s, has always been predominantly so written. **Pouff* and \**pouffe* are variants. In BrE, the term is confusable with the derogatory slang *poof*, referring to a gay man and widely considered objectionable. See gallicisms.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pouf* vs. \**pouffe* vs. \**pouff *\): 26:8:1
