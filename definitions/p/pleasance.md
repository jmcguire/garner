# pleasance

pleasance /**plez**-әn\[t\]s/ \(= \[1\] delight, agreeableness, or enjoyment; or \[2\] a pleasure ground or park attached to a mansion\), an archaism dating from the 1300s, is sometimes given the obsolete spelling **pleasaunce* or \**plesaunce*. This word is also sometimes written as a gallicism, \**plaisance* /play-**zahn\[t\]s**/, which was common in English until the mid-1800s.

Current ratio in print \(*pleasance* vs. \**pleasaunce* vs. \**plaisance* vs. \**plesaunce*\): 3:1.8:1.3:1
