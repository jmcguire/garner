# prohibitive

prohibitive; **prohibitory**. These terms have undergone a latent differentiation that deserves encouragement. *Prohibitive* may mean generally "having the quality of prohibiting," but more and more in modern prose it has the sense "tending to preclude consumption or purchase because of expense" <the costs are prohibitive>. *Prohibitory* has carved out a niche in the law in the sense "expressing a prohibition or restraint" <prohibitory injunction>.
