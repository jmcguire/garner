# papier-mâché

papier-mâché; **papier-mache**; **papier-maché**; **paper-mache**. During the 20th century, *papier-mâché* (the malleable paper-water-glue mixture that hardens after drying) mostly lost its diacritical marks. But it has predominantly reacquired them in the 21st century. The headwords in this entry appear in order of most to least frequent in English-language sources. In BrE, the term is most often given a French pronunciation /**pap**-yay ma-**shay**/ (or sometimes /**mash**-ay/); in AmE, the usual pronunciation is /**pay**-pәr mә-**shay**/.

 - Current ratio in print (*papier-mâché* vs. *papier-mache* vs. *papier-maché* vs. *paper-mache*): 22:1.7:1.5:1
