# pickaxe

pickaxe \(= a handheld tool consisting of a curved iron bar with a sharp point on each end, used for breaking up the ground\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1200s, has been predominantly so spelled in all varieties of English for most of its life. In the late 1980s, however, the spelling **pickax* became predominant for a time in AmE—just as \**poleax* overtook *poleaxe* in AmE for a period beginning in late 1970s. But since 2003, the traditional spelling, *pickaxe*, has again pulled ahead. The period from 1985 to 2003 was merely an anomaly in AmE.

Given the predominance of *axe* in both AmE and World Englishes, this compound should preferably be spelled *pickaxe*, not \**pickax*. Cf. **axe**, **battle-axe** & **poleaxe**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pickaxe* vs. \**pickax* in AmE\): 1.7:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*pickaxe* vs. \**pickax* in BrE\): 5:1
