# pincer movement

pincer movement (= \[1\] a military maneuver in which two or more coordinating forces close in on an enemy's position from different directions so as to attack it advantageously or to cut it off from support or supplies; or \[2\] by extension, any attack by allied competitors against a common opponent), a phrase dating from World War I (1917), has always been predominantly so spelled. \**Pincers movement* is a variant. \**Pincher movement* might be considered either a blunder or a welcome anglicization—more likely the former because *pincer* itself has been an English word since about 1400.

 - Current ratio in print (*pincer movement* vs. \**pincers movement* vs. \**pincher movement*): 89:6:1
