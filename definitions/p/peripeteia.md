# peripeteia

peripeteia /pe-ri-pә-**tee**-ә/ \(= a sudden or unforeseen reversal of fortune, esp. in a literary work\), a literary term dating from the late 1500s, has almost always been predominantly so spelled. The anglicized version of the word, *peripety*, which had a brief period of predominance from 1909 to 1915, never had a real turning point: it has generally remained but a variant.

 - Current ratio in print \(*peripeteia* vs. *peripety*\): 5:1
