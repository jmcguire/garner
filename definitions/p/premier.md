# premier

premier, adj. & n.; **premiere**, n. Three observations are in order. First, *premier*, the adjective meaning "first in importance or rank," is often pretentious in place of *first* or *foremost*. (As a noun, it means "prime minister.") Second, *premiere*, the noun meaning "a first performance," has come into standard use as a verb \<the new sitcom premieres in October\>. (See functional shift (d).) Third, the accent is no longer used in the noun—hence *premiere*, not *\*première*. See diacritical marks.

In AmE, the two words are homophones: both are preferably pronounced /pri-**meer**/. In BrE, *premier* is pronounced /**prem**-yә/, and *premiere* is pronounced /**prem**-ee-ә/.
