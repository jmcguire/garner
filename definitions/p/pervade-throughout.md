# \*pervade throughout

\*pervade throughout. This phrase \(which spread during the early 1800s\) is redundant, since *pervade* by itself means "to be throughout; to spread throughout"—e.g.: "The bold use of color *pervades throughout* \[read *pervades*\] the first floor rooms." Frances Jaques, "Decor Delight," *Capital* \(Annapolis\), 10 May 1997, at D1. See redundancy.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*pervade throughout* for *pervade*: Stage 1
