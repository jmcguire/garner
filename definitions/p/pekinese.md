# Pekinese

Pekinese /pee-kә-**neez**/ \(= of, relating to, or involving the small dog having a short, flat nose and long, silky hair\), a proper adjective dating from the 1890s, has almost always been predominantly so spelled in preference to the more literal *Pekingese*—somewhat more strongly in BrE than in AmE. And the capitalized form \(*Pekinese*\) has always predominated over the lowercase form \(*pekinese*\). Given the way *Peking* has receded as against *Beijing*, the nonetymological spelling that drops the *g* makes all the more sense. \**Beijingese dog* is all but unknown.

In reference to things other than dogs—natives of Beijing or the dialect of Beijing—*Pekingese* is the usual form, not *Pekinese*. And *Beijingese* hardly registers in frequency-of-occurrence searches. See **Beijing**.

Current ratio in print \(*Pekinese dog* vs. *Pekingese dog*\): 1.5:1
Current ratio in print \(*Pekingese* \(generally\) vs. *Pekinese* vs. *Beijingese*\): 38:27:1
