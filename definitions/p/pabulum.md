# pabulum

pabulum; **pablum**. *Pabulum* /**pab**-yә-lәm/ = \(1\) food, nourishment; \(2\) nourishment for the mind; or \(3\) a soft, bland cereal for babies. *Pablum*, with a capital *P*, is a trademark for a brand of cereal. Especially in AmE, the lowercase *pablum* \(formed through syncope\) has taken on the figurative sense "bland, trite, or simplistic writing or ideas"—e.g.: "Still, the raw charm of her pipes is strong enough to turn *pablum* such as ‘Just Missed the Train' and Ms. Warren's ‘Some Kind of Miracle' into pure ear candy." Thor Christensen, "A Voice Carries Only So Far," *Dallas Morning News*, 20 Apr. 2003, at G12. *Pabulum* sometimes carries this meaning also, but in modern print sources *pablum* outnumbers it by more than two to one in this sense—e.g.: "With ‘Butterfly,' Carey moves further away from the gorgeously sung pop *pablum* . . . and into her role as the Barbra Streisand of her generation." Michael Corcoran, "Mariah Carey Takes Flight on ‘Butterfly,' " *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 16 Sept. 1997, at E1. Other than that one use, however, the longer word has always appeared much more frequently in print.
