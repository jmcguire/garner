# pandemic

pandemic (= \[of a disease) prevalent over the whole of a country or continent, or over the whole world) was exclusively an adjective when introduced in the 1600s but then, in the 1800s, came into use as a noun denoting the widespread outbreak of a disease. Today the noun uses are most common—e.g.: "The 1918 flu *pandemic* killed 20 million." Shannon Brownlee, "The Disease Busters," *U.S. News & World Rep.*, 27 Mar. 1995, at 48. Beginning in March 2020, with the sudden spread of Covid-19, the noun use became significantly more frequent—ubiquitous, in fact. Cf. **epidemic**.
