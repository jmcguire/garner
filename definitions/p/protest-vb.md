# protest, vb

protest, vb., is transitive or intransitive in AmE but solely intransitive in most BrE writing. In BrE one writes, "They *protested against* discrimination," but not "They *protested* discrimination." Although he was writing on British usage only, Eric Partridge considered the latter, AmE usage incorrect and quoted an American writer as an offender against idiom (*U&A* at 248). The phrase *protest against* is common also in AmE \<they protested against the government's decision\>. In AmE, however, *against* is routinely omitted \<they protested the government's decision\>. The verb is preferably pronounced /prә-**test**/ in AmE and BrE alike. Yet the first-syllable stress (as with the noun) is spreading in all varieties of English: you might want to protest against it.
