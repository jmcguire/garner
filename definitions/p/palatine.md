# palatine

palatine, adj., functions as two very different words, one relating to palaces ("involving a palace or royal privileges") <palatine privileges> and one relating to the palate ("involving or lying near the palate") <the palatine nerve>.

Two nouns are spelled the same way. The first denotes (1) an officer of an imperial palace, (2) a medieval vassal invested with royal privileges, (3) a political subdivision ruled by a prince, or (4) a fur piece traditionally worn by women to cover the shoulders. The second denotes either of two irregularly shaped bones of the hard palate.
