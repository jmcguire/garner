# poised

poised, adj. To be *poised* is to behave in a calm and confident way with full control of feelings and reactions <poised job interviewees>. To be *poised* between one thing and another is to be positioned or situated between two things that seem equally likely <poised between raging success and abject failure>. To be *poised* for something is to be completely still but ready to move or pounce in an instant <an antelope poised for flight>. To be *poised* to do something (quite similar in sense) is to be entirely ready to act or to experience something <poised to become the number-one tennis player in the world>.

Occasionally, *posed* gets confounded with *poised*, especially in the idiom *poised to* do something.

 - Current ratio in print (*poised to become* vs. \**posed to become*): 134:1
 - Current ratio in print (*poised to attack* vs. \**posed to attack*): 76:1
