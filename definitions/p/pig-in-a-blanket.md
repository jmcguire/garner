# pig in a blanket

pig in a blanket \(= a small sausage wrapped and cooked in a dough pastry\), an Americanism dating from the 1920s, didn't come into widespread use until the late 1960s. The usual plural is *pigs in a blanket*—not *pigs in blankets*.

But in the 1880s and 1890s, a dish called *little pigs in blankets* was fairly popular in the U.S. It consisted of oysters wrapped in thin slices of fat bacon and fried.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pigs in a blanket* vs. *pigs in blankets*\): 1.4:1
