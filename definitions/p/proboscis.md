# proboscis

proboscis /prә-**bos**-is/ or /proh-**bos**-is/ \(= \[1\] the trunk of an elephant or snout of an animal; or \[2\] any similar tubular organ on an insect or other life-form\), a Latin/Greek loanword dating from the early 1600s, traditionally formed the classical plural *proboscides* in English-language contexts. But the anglicized plural *proboscises* became predominant in AmE in the late 1970s, and it has competed closely with the Greek plural in BrE since the mid-1980s. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*proboscises* vs. *proboscides* in World Englishes\): 3:1
