# pacificator

pacificator; **pacifier**. Both traceable to the fourth decade of the 1500s, these words have undergone differentiation. A *pacificator* is a person who pacifies, reconciles, or negotiates peace. Henry Clay \(1777–1852\) earned the moniker "the Great Pacificator" because of his pre–Civil War efforts to reconcile the interests of the North and the South. A *pacifier* is generally understood to be the nipple-shaped device designed for infants to suck on, or something else that calms people, and is best confined to those senses. In BrE, the nipple-shaped device for infants is called a *dummy*.

Current ratio in print \(*great pacificator* vs. *great pacifier* \[case-insensitive search\]\): 3:1
