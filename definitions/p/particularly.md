# particularly

particularly. The phrase *and particularly* is usually unnecessary for *particularly*—e.g.: "But the law, *and particularly* \[read *particularly*\] its quota system, has been sharply criticized by the Council of Europe and other Western organizations that Latvia hopes to join someday." Alessandra Stanley, "Divided Latvians Awaiting Clinton," *N.Y. Times*, 6 July 1994, at A1, A5. Once the *and* is deleted, the reader is comfortable with the phrase *particularly its quota system* as a mere appositive—and is comfortable with the singular verb.
