# pro and con

pro and con; ***pro et con***. The latter phrase is the Latin for "for and against." The English rendering—*pro and con*—is standard. The phrase may function as a noun <the pros and cons>, as an adverb <people argued pro and con>, or as an adjective <arguments pro and con>.

*Pro and con* has also been used as a verb phrase <to pro-and-con the issue>, and although today this use sounds somewhat odd, it has the sanction of long standing: the *OED* dates it from 1694. The *OED* also records the phrase as a preposition <arguments pro and con the proposal>, but *for and against* would be better.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pro and con* vs. *pro et con*\): 259:1
