# padrone

padrone /pә-**droh**-nee/ \(= \[1\] a patron, master, or boss; \[2\] an Italian innkeeper; or \[3\] someone who helps Italian immigrants get jobs\) has predominantly formed the Italianate plural *padroni* \(pronounced the same\) since the 1700s. The anglicized plural *padrones* has vied for predominance since the mid-1800s and is now almost as frequent. See plurals \(b\).

Current ratio in print \(*padroni* vs. *padrones*\): 3:1
