# pappus

pappus \(= a tuft of hairlike projections that grow from the fruit or ovary of a seed plant and that, through the action of the wind, blow off to disperse the seed\), a Latin loanword, dates in English from the early 1700s. The Latinate plural *pappi* is standard, the anglicized *pappuses* being significantly less common.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pappi* vs. *pappuses*\): 745:1
