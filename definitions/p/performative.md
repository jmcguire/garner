# performative

performative \(= of, relating to, or involving an expression or utterance that constitutes a speech act—as with *I apologize*, *I congratulate*, or *I promise*\) is a linguistic term dating from the 1920s. *Performatory*, an adjective dating from the 1940s, is often used as a needless variant. But if it bears a broader meaning—"of, relating to, or involving a performance"—then its use might be justified. Whether real differentiation is possible remains to be seen.

Since about 1990, *performative* has acquired a pejorative sense involving what someone does to improve reputation, image, or profile—with connotations of posturing or insincerity <performative hygiene> <performative politics>.

Current ratio in print \(*performative utterances* vs. *performatory utterances*\): 325:1
