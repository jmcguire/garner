# persona

persona is singular, not plural—*personas* (which is preferable) or *personae* being the plural. But writers sometimes also misuse *personae* as a singular—e.g.: "But instead of writing another original screenplay, Tarantino has staked his reputation on a different approach: he has acquired rights to a best-selling crime novel from the hot author of *Get Shorty* and adapted it around the retro-hip *personae* \[read *persona*\] of the ultimate 1970s blaxploitation babe, Pam Grier." Jeffrey Ressner, "Back in the Action," *Time*, 18 Aug. 1997, at 70.

 - Current ratio in print (*personas* vs. *personae*): 1.6:1
