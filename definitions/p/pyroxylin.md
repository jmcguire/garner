# pyroxylin

pyroxylin /pī-**rok**-si-lin/ (= a flammable mixture of nitric and sulfuric acids with cotton, wood, paper, etc.—used esp. in making plastics and coatings), a Greek loanword dating in English from the mid-1800s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1900. Before that, \**pyroxyline* was standard.

 - Current ratio in print (*pyroxylin* vs. \**pyroxyline*): 2.5:1
