# praying mantis

praying mantis is the correct spelling, not *\*preying mantis*. Even though this insect preys on others, its name comes from the way it holds its front legs raised, as if in prayer. But some writers misunderstand the etymology—e.g.: "On Sunday, the 6-10 *preying mantis* \[read *praying mantis*\] look-alike pitched seven innings, whiffed 14 and retired after throwing 130 pitches." Frank Luksa, "Series Reaches Striking Climax," *Dallas Morning News*, 14 July 1997, at B1.
