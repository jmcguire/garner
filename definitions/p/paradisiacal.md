# paradisiacal

paradisiacal /pa-rә-di-**zī**-ә-kәl/ or /-**sī**-ә-kәl/ \(= extremely pleasant, beautiful, and enjoyable, like a paradise\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the mid-1600s, has long been the standard term. In the 1700s, it ousted the older, more straightforward term *paradisal*, which has enjoyed a resurgence since about 1950. The two now vie in a close contest. The forms \**paradisiac*, \**paradisaical*, and \**paradisaic* are needless variants of *paradisiacal*—which \(it cannot be doubted\) is a heavenly word.

Current ratio in print \(*paradisiacal* vs. *paradisal* vs. \**paradisiac* vs. \**paradisaical* vs. \**paradisaic*\): 28:14:4:3:1
