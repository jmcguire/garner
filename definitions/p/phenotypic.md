# phenotypic

phenotypic /fee-noh-**tip**-ik/ \(= of, relating to, or involving an organism's observable characteristics that are produced by its genetic makeup in interaction with the environment\), a German/Greek loanword dating in English from the early 20th century, has always been the predominant adjective corresponding to the noun *phenotype*. The competing forms, \**phenotypical* and \**phenotypal*, are needless variants. See -ic/-ical; see also germanicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*phenotypic* vs. \**phenotypical* vs. \**phenotypal*\): 4,816:251:1
