# posthumous

posthumous, introduced into English in the early 1600s, acquired a nonetymological *h* early on as an English word. The Latin etymon *postumus* means "last" or "late-born." But the *h* was added in a 1608 book. *OED2* noted that the *h* was added "through erroneous attribution to *humus* the earth, or \(as explained by Servius\) *humāre* to bury." So *exhume* is not in fact cognate with *posthumous* at all. In any event, the well-established but erroneous *h* should have no bearing on the pronunciation, which is properly /**pos**-chʊ-mәs/—not /pohst-**hyoo**-mәs/ or /post-**hyoo**-mәs/.
