# petulance

petulance \(= unreasonable impatience and peevishness; insolent childishness\), a French loanword dating in English from the early 1600s, has been the predominant form since the mid-1700s. The slightly older by-form, **petulancy*, is now a needless variant.

 - Current ratio in print \(*petulance* vs. \**petulancy*\): 70:1
