# prior

prior; **previous**. The adjectives *prior* and *previous* for *earlier* are each within the stylist's license; \**prior to* and \**previous to* in place of *before* are not.

In fact, \**prior to*—one of the most easily detectable symptoms of bureaucratese, commercialese, and legalese—is terribly overworked. As Theodore Bernstein once pointed out, one should feel free to use \**prior to* instead of *before* only if one is accustomed to using \**posterior to* for *after.* See *The Careful Writer* 347 \(1979\). Cf. \***previous to** & **\*subsequent to**.
