# purposely

purposely; **purposefully**. *Purposely* = on purpose; intentionally \<purposely omitted any mention of her\>. *Purposefully* = with a specific purpose in mind; with the idea of accomplishing a certain result \<purposefully designed to achieve efficiency\>. Some writers fall into inelegant variation with these words—e.g.: "For someone to be guilty of witness tampering, they must have acted *purposely*. Accidentally interfering with a witness, for example, does not qualify. Because the indictments did not specify Montgomery acted *purposefully* \[read *purposely*\], Mohl ‘quashed,' or voided, the indictments." "Man, 22, Gets 5–12 Years for Rape," *Union Leader* (Manchester, N.H.), 23 Nov. 1996, at A7. Cf. **right**, adj.
