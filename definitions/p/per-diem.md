# per diem

per diem, a latinism, means "for or by the day" <per diem fee>. Sometimes it makes more sense to write *a day* <$50 a day> or *daily* <daily fee>. (See **a (b)**.) Although it has been defended when it is positioned before the noun it modifies <per diem allowance>, *daily* is usually an improvement. When used as an adjective, *per diem* is rarely the best available phrase.

As a noun (an Americanism), *per diem* has designated a daily stipend, especially for travel and living expenses, since the early 1800s. It is firmly established and well accepted. Cf. **per annum**.

 - Current ratio in print (*dollars a day* vs. *dollars per day* vs. *dollars per diem*): 67:16:1
