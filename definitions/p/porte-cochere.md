# porte cochere

porte cochere /port koh-**shair**/ = (1) a large gateway and passage that allows vehicles to pass into an inner courtyard; or (2) an overhanging structure, usu. projecting from a building's entrance, that protects vehicles and their occupants from the elements. Although Margaret Nicholson termed sense 2 "erroneous" (*DAEU* at 433), it has been standard AmE since the mid-1800s. In fact, it's the primary sense today.

Although the French phrase is written *porte cochère*, the grave accent is now omitted in AmE. See diacritical marks.
