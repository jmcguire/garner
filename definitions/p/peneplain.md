# peneplain

peneplain \(= a large flat land surface that stands in slight relief caused by long-term erosion\), a Latin/French hybrid dating from the 1880s, has always been predominantly so spelled. \**Peneplane* is a variant.

The geological process by which a peneplain is created is known as *peneplanation*—not \**peneplaination*.

Current ratio in print \(*peneplain* vs. \**peneplane*\): 23:1   
Current ratio in print \(*peneplanation* vs. \**peneplaination*\): 94:1
