# proactive

proactive = \(of a person, policy, etc.\) creating or controlling a situation by taking the initiative or anticipating events; ready to take initiative, tending to make things happen \(*SOED*\). Though, as a vogue term, *proactive* is widely viewed with suspicion, it's occasionally useful as an antonym of *reactive*. It seems to fill a gap in the language—one not adequately filled by *assertive* or any other common word. *Proactive* was introduced as a neologism in the early 1930s, spread moderately from 1950 to 1985, and then became wildly popular. It remains so. Cf. **reactionary**, n.
