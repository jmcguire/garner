# purl

purl is a knitting term denoting a type of stitch. For this sense, *pearl* is a variant to be avoided—e.g.:

 - "Key trends . . . include: cotton cable knee socks and cotton *pearl* \[read *purl*\] stitch knee socks, $12." Sylvi Capelaci, "Legs Long on Style," *Toronto Sun*, 23 Aug. 1994, Imagination §, at 38.

 - "With four hours of free time, a basic knowledge of the knit-and-*pearl* \[read *-purl*\] stitch and a creative mind, critter mittens were formed." Faith Mayer, "Critter Mittens Win Magazine Prize," *Telegram & Gaz.* (Worcester), 28 Feb. 1997, at B4.

But if you are referring to the "knotted pearl stitch"—an embroidery stitch that is designed to look like a string of pearls—then *pearl* is the correct spelling.
