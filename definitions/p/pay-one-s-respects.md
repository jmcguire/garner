# pay (one's) respects

pay (one's) respects (= to show polite respect for someone by appearing personally) is the set phrase, but some writers have begun to write the singular *respect* instead of *respects*: "McGovern and his wife tried to tour the building and view the displays, but it wasn't easy with well-wishers seeking to pay their *respect* \[read *respects*\]." John Knaggs, "McGovern Easy to Admire," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 12 Oct. 1995, at A15. That wording may be more logical than the accepted usage *to pay their respects*; the well-wishers each showed the same *respect* for McGovern. But logic must yield to the universally accepted idiom.

To *pay one's last respects* is to show respect for a dead person by attending a funeral or visiting a grave.
