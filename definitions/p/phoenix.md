# phoenix

phoenix (= \[1\] in legend, a magical bird born from a fire; or \[2\] someone or something that rises from the ashes of an earlier era), deriving ultimately from Greek and dating from the early Middle English period of the 1100s, has long been predominantly so spelled. For a time in the 1700s, the simplified spelling \**phenix* was tried, but since 1800 that spelling has never been popular. And since the incorporation of Phoenix, Arizona, in 1881, the spelling of even the common noun *phoenix* seems to have been firmly fixed by that proper noun.

 - Current ratio in print (*phoenix* vs. \**phenix*): 389:1
