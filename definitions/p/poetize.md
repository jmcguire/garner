# poetize

poetize; **poeticize**. The two have undergone differentiation. Although both originally meant "to compose poetry," only *poetize* (dating from the late 1500s) properly bears that sense today. *Poeticize* (dating from the early 1800s) means "to imbue with poetic qualities; to impart an aesthetic or emotional impact suggestive of poetry." *Poetize* has always been the more common word. Only after 1975 did *poeticize* rival it in frequency. The usual BrE spellings are *poetise* and *poeticize*.
