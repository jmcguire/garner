# pettifog

pettifog, n., is a needless variant of *pettifogger* \(= \[1\] a petty or disreputable lawyer; or \[2\] someone who quibbles over trivial matters\). It's a fairly unusual error—e.g.: "Yet though \[H.W. Fowler\] was a stickler, he was no pedant. Apart from newspaper writers, whose mangling of the language kept him supplied with horrible examples, nothing aroused his derision like the *pettifog* \[read *pettifogger*\]." William P. Cheshire, "The Man Who Kept the Light Burning in the Twilight," *Ariz. Republic*, 14 July 1991, at C4. But since the late 1600s, *pettifog* has been a verb meaning \(1\) "to engage in tricky conduct, esp. in legal business," or \(2\) "to bicker, cavil, or quibble" <he wastes most of his time pettifogging over trivialities>, a back-formation from the noun.

## Language-Change Index

*pettifog* misused for *pettifogger* as a noun: Stage 1
