# patzer

patzer /**paht**-zәr/ or, esp. in BrE, /**pat**-/ \(= an unskillful chess player\), a German loanword dating in English from the 1950s, is predominantly so spelled. From the early 1960s to the early 1980s, the variant spelling **potzer* was a close contender, but it fell off in use beginning in the 1980s. See germanicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*patzer* vs. \**potzer*\): 4:1
