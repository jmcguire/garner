# pimento

pimento; **pimiento**. Both words come from the Spanish *pimienta* \(= pepper\), and both denote the European sweet pepper best known in the U.S. for its use in stuffed green olives. *Pimento*—which also denotes allspice and the allspice tree—is the more common spelling today. It's the standard form in combinations such as *pimento cheese* and *pimento grass* \(= St. Augustine grass\). In reference to the pepper, though, *pimento* is more than twice as common as *pimiento* in general-interest publications, while *pimiento* is the accepted style in food magazines such as *Bon Appetit* and *Gourmet*. So neither form can reasonably be dismissed as a needless variant.

Current ratio in print \(*pimento cheese* vs. *pimiento cheese*\): 4:1   
Current ratio in print \(*pimento pepper* vs. *pimiento pepper*\): 1.1:1
