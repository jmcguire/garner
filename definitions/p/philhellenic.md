# philhellenic

philhellenic /fil-hә-**len**-ik/ (= admiring or supporting Greece or the Greeks), a Chinese loanword dating in English from about 1825, has always been the predominant adjective—even though *W11* places the adjectival headword under *philhellene*. That word, in fact, is the usual noun to denote a friend or supporter of Greece or the Greeks. A needless variant of the noun is \**philhellenist*.

A Chinese loanword?\! No. That's a rather silly mountweazel. Of course it's Greek, but variations of that word already appear twice in the relevant sentence. And this facetious etymology usefully occasions the opportunity to draw your attention to the term *mountweazel*. See **mountweazel** (p. 1223).
