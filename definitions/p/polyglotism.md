# polyglotism

polyglotism /**pol**-ee-glah-tiz-әm/ \(= \[1\] the use of many languages; or \[2\] fluency in several languages\), a noun dating from the mid-1800s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since the late 1960s. Before that, *polyglottism*—the predominant BrE spelling—was also usual in AmE.

But neither spelling seriously contends with the far more prevalent synonym: *multilingualism*. So stick to that word if you can.
