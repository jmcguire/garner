# pantalettes

pantalettes (= large ruffled leg coverings worn by women and children esp. during the 1800s) has always been predominantly so spelled in BrE, and almost always in AmE. Even so, many dictionaries record the entry under the variant spelling \**pantalets*. Big data will confirm, however, that *pantalettes* clearly predominated across World Englishes from 1915 to 1990. Since the latter date, the two have been locked in close competition. See diminutives (f).

 - Current ratio in print (*pantalettes* vs. \**pantalets* in World Englishes): 1.1:1
