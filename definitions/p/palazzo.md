# palazzo

palazzo /pә-**laht**-soh/, Italian for "palace," was introduced into English in the 1700s. Denoting an elaborately built museum-like building or mansion, the term has traditionally taken the Italianate plural *palazzi* /pә-**laht**-see/. Since the mid-1800s, the anglicized plural *palazzos* has been a variant. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*palazzi* vs. *palazzos*\): 3:1
