# pecking order

pecking order \(= a social system within which each member knows or soon learns who has a higher or lower rank within the system\), a phrase dating from the early 1920s and referring originally to poultry and other birds, has always been the predominant phrase. The variants \**peck order* and \**peck-order* were close contenders from the 1920s to the mid-1950s, when they fell out of favor.

 - Current ratio in print \(*pecking order* vs. \**peck order* vs. \**peck-order*\): 612:4:1
