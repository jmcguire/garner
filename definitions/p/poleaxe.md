# poleaxe

poleaxe \(= an old-fashioned weapon with a short handle and a hook or spike opposite the blade\), a compound dating from the late 1200s, has been predominantly so spelled in all varieties of English for most of its life. But from the late 1970s through about 2015, \**poleax* became equally common in AmE, just as \**pickax* overtook *pickaxe* in AmE about the same time. Given the worldwide predominance of *axe*, this compound should preferably be spelled *poleaxe*. Cf. **axe**, **battle-axe** & **pickaxe**.

Current ratio in print \(*poleaxe* vs. \**poleax* in World Englishes\): 3:1
Current ratio in print \(*poleaxe* vs. \**poleax* in AmE\): 1.8:1
