# pedal to the metal

pedal to the metal. To *put* \(or *push*\) *the pedal to the metal* is either to drive fast, by flooring the accelerator, or metaphorically to give one's utmost effort in a game, competition, etc. Some writers mistakenly replace *metal* \(from the literal sense of the phrase\) with *medal* \(perhaps from the association with prizes in the metaphorical uses—and also from the false association with the spelling of *pedal*\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*pedal to the metal* vs. \**pedal to the medal*\): 32:1
