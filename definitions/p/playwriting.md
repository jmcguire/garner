# playwriting

playwriting; **\*playwrighting**. The second, a corrupt form of the first, is lamentably common—e.g.:

 - "\[A\] professor in the English department at the University of Cincinnati counts *playwrighting* \[read *playwriting*\] among the courses she teaches." Jackie Demaline, "Playhouse Booking Good for Local Playwrights," *Cincinnati Enquirer*, 16 Mar. 2003, at E2.

 - "Kist said *playwrighting* \[read *playwriting*\] is a way for her to communicate her ideas." Melissa Hollander, "Festival Brings Playwright's Words to Life," *Daily Herald* \(Chicago\), 18 Apr. 2003, Neighbor §, at 1.

 - "She is a recent recipient of the prestigious National Endowment for the Arts/Theatre Communication Group *playwrighting* \[read *playwriting*\] residency." Holly Johnson, "The Sky's the Limit," *Oregonian* \(Portland\), 25 Apr. 2003, Arts & Living §, at 5–6.

See spelling \(a\). For a similar error, see **copyright**.
