# pictograph

pictograph \(= \[1\] an ancient drawing on rock, esp. within a cave; \[2\] a character or symbol in some type of pictorial code or writing system; or \[3\] a mathematical drawing that displays numbers, amounts, or statistics in pictorial form\), a compound dating from the mid-1800s, has always been the standard term in AmE. But in BrE the synonymous *pictogram*, dating from about 1910, has predominated since the late 1980s. Both words are composed of Latin elements.

Current ratio in print \(*pictograph* vs. *pictogram* in AmE\): 1.2:1   
Current ratio in print \(*pictogram* vs. *pictograph* in BrE\): 2:1
