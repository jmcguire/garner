# politically

politically; **politicly**. *Politically* /pә-**lit**-i-klee/ = in a political way; in a way that involves politics. *Politicly* /**pol**-i-tik-lee/ = in a politic (i.e., judicious or prudent) way. The latter, of course, is much less common—e.g.: " ‘Is that a duck or a cormorant?' Bruce Babbitt asked, resting his paddle on his chino-covered leg, looking at a distant water bird. ‘We have both,' raft guide Katy Strand told him *politicly*." Peter Bacque, "Babbitt Links Cleaner James to Federal Law," *Richmond Times-Dispatch*, 19 Oct. 1995, at B1. Cf. **impolitic**.
