# pat-a-cake

pat-a-cake \(the children's game in which two participants alternate clapping hands individually and with the other, usu. while reciting the accompanying nursery rhyme\), an English compound dating from the 1830s, has been traditionally so spelled in AmE and BrE alike—and still is in BrE. In 1998, however, the variant spelling *patty-cake* overtook *pat-a-cake* in AmE, and not just among bakers' men.

 - Current ratio in print \(*patty-cake* vs. *pat-a-cake* in AmE\): 1.7:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*pat-a-cake* vs. *patty-cake* in BrE\): 2:1
