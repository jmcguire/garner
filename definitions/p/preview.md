# preview

preview \(= \[1\] to get a preliminary view of; or \[2\] to give a preliminary survey of\), a verb dating from the early 1600s, has always been so spelled. By the late 1800s, the word had also come to be used as a noun, meaning either \(1\) "an occasion for seeing a film, play, exhibit, etc. before it is made public"; or \(2\) "a preliminary description of a film, play, exhibit, etc. before people see it." A variant of the noun is \**prevue*, a quasi-gallicism to be avoided.

The phrase *sneak preview*, by the way, originated in the American cinema of the 1930s.

 - Current ratio in print \(*the preview* vs. \**the prevue*\): 215:1
