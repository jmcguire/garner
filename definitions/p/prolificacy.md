# prolificacy

prolificacy; **\*prolificness**. The standard noun corresponding to *prolific* is *prolificacy*, not *\*prolificness*—e.g.:

 - "Alas, Ms. Oates—whose work I deeply admire—has paid a price for her prolificacy." Jay Parini, "On Being Prolific," in *Writers on Writing* 199, 204 \(Robert Pack & Jay Perini eds., 1991\).

 - "Allen's *prolificness* \[read *prolificacy*\] is legendary." John Beifuss, "So-So ‘Love You' Says: Woody Allen Needs a Hiatus," *Commercial Appeal* \(Memphis\), 17 Jan. 1997, at C1.

 - "The first problem that has to be confronted in relation to Ruiz's oeuvre is its sheer *prolificness* \[read *prolificacy*\]." Michael Goddard, *Impossible Cartographies* 1 \(2013\).

Cf. **genericness**.

Language-Change Index

*\*prolificness* for *prolificacy*: Stage 1
