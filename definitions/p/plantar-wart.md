# plantar wart

plantar wart (= a wart on the sole of the foot) is often wrongly transformed into *\*planter's wart*, probably on the false analogy of *planter's punch* and *Planters Peanuts*—e.g.:

 - "Hayes said June had complained of *planter's* \[read *plantar*\] warts on his feet." Winifred Yu, "Troy Housing Group Axes 1st Minority Administrator," *Times Union* (Albany), 8 July 1995, at B6.

 - "Clean your feet to avoid getting *planter's* \[read *plantar*\] warts." Chris Heidenrich, "Five Minutes with . . . ," *Daily Herald* (Chicago), 23 Dec. 1998, at 1.

 - "Our three kids' formerly kissable baby tootsies eventually . . . requir\[ed\] good personal hygiene to ward off potent foot odor, locker room fungus, *planter's* \[read *plantar*\] warts, and a most foul substance known as ‘toe jam.' " Lisa Smith Molinari, "The Agony of Da Feet," *Virginian-Pilot* (Norfolk), 13 Aug. 2020, at B2.

It doesn't help that *plantar* and *planter* are homophones: both are pronounced /**plan**-tәr/.

This example of folk etymology was first noted by Robert L. Chapman in 41 *Am. Speech* 238 (1966). For the real etymology of *plantar*, see **plantar fasciitis**. For more on folk etymology in general, see etymology (d).

## Language-Change Index

 - *plantar wart* mistakenly written *\*planter's wart*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*plantar wart* vs. \**planter's wart*): 33:1
