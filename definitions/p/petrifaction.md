# petrifaction

petrifaction; **petrification**. What is the usual noun corresponding to *petrify*? The traditional word is *petrifaction*, which can denote the process, state, or quality of being petrified—or sometimes the petrified thing itself. But from the early 1970s to about 2005, *petrification* was the predominant form, especially in references to the process as opposed to the state. Both *petrifaction* \(1400s\) and *petrification* \(1600s\) are old, and the two words appear with almost equal frequency. *Petrifaction* is analogous to *putrefaction* and *stupefaction*; *petrification* is analogous to *edification* and *mortification*. On balance, you're best advised to use *petrifaction*, but that advice hasn't turned to stone.

Current ratio in print \(*petrifaction* vs. *petrification*\): 1:1.4
