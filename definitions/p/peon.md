# peon

peon \(= \[1\] an indentured servant in Central or South America who works to pay back debts; or \[2\] someone who does menial work or physical labor for low pay\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has always predominantly formed the anglicized plural *peons*. The Spanish or Portuguese plural \**peones* /pay-**oh**-neez/ is a variant. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*peons* vs. \**peones*\): 10:1
