# phraseology

phraseology \(= the way in which words and phrases are used in a particular utterance, subject, or language\) is unusually formed, suggesting by the *-ology* suffix that it might denote a discipline or study of some type. Originally, in the 1500s, the term referred to a collection or handbook of idiomatic phrases—the coiner, according to the *OED*, having chosen an irregular form for his title. In any event, by the 1700s *phraseology* took on its modern sense: the choice and arrangement of words to express ideas. The word is pronounced /fray-zee-**ol**-ә-jee/, in five syllables—not /fray-**zol**-ә-jee/.
