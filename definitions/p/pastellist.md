# pastellist

pastellist /pa-**stel**-ist/ or, in BrE, /**pas**-tә-list/ (= an artist who draws with pastels), an agent noun dating from the late 1800s, has been predominantly so spelled for most of its existence in both AmE and BrE. For a time after 1980, AmE writers adopted \**pastelist*, which many American dictionaries list as the standard spelling. But given that the second syllable is accented, a doubling of the consonant is morphologically normal. (See spelling (c).) In fact, when *pastel* is made into a gerund in AmE, *pastelling* is the only known form. Concededly, the past-tense form \**pasteled* appears alongside *pastelled* in AmE, but this is an unfortunate anomaly wrought by those who don't recognize the distinction between *canceled* and \**pasteled*—having to do with which syllable is stressed. *Pastelled* is the only spelling known to BrE, in which the word is pronounced /**pas**-tәld/.

 - Current ratio in print (*pastellist* vs. \**pastelist* in World Englishes): 1.6:1
 - Current ratio in print (*pastelled* vs. \**pasteled* in World Englishes): 2.4:1
