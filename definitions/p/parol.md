# parol

parol \(= \[1\] spoken, oral; word-of-mouth; or \[2\] of, relating to, or involving evidence of what was said by parties to a contract before or as the contract was signed—but typically not memorialized in the contract itself\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1590s, is commonly said by dictionaries to be pronounced /**pa**-rәl/. But most American lawyers make it a homophone of *parole* /pә-**rohl**/, which of course denotes the conditional release of a prisoner whose full prison sentence hasn't yet been served. The correct phrase is *parol evidence*, not \**parole evidence*, even if the pronunciation of the two \(inconveniently enough\) is identical. See gallicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*parol lease* \[sense 1\] vs. \**parole lease*\): 73:1
Current ratio in print \(*parol evidence* \[sense 2\] vs. \**parole evidence*\): 17:1
