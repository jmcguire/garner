# glean

glean \(= to gather \[esp. information or, orig., crops left over after a harvest\]\) is occasionally confounded with *gleam* \(= to shine\)—e.g.: "A social worker visits new patients to *gleam* \[read *glean*\] information about their social and financial resources." Editorial, "The Graying of Texas," *Dallas Morning News*, 30 Dec. 1999, at A24.

## Language-Change Index

*gleam* misused for *glean*: Stage 1
