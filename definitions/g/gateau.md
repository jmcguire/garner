# gateau

gateau \(= a rich, elaborately decorated cake\), an 18th-century French loanword, predominantly retained its original circumflex (*gâteau*\) until the early 20th century. The standard plural is *gateaux* across World Englishes—not *\*gateaus*. Whether singular or plural, the word is pronounced /gah-**toh**/ in AmE and /ga-**toh**/ \(or even /**gat**-oh/\) in BrE.

Current ratio in print \(*gateaux* vs. *\*gateaus*\): 35:1
