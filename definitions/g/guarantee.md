# guarantee

guarantee, n. & vb.; **guaranty**, n. These homophones are related in meaning as well as in sound, but they are not interchangeable. The broader term, *guarantee*, is more common and is used as a noun and a verb. To *guarantee* something is to assure someone that a promise will be kept \<our company guarantees customer satisfaction\>. As a noun, a *guarantee* is the promise itself \<bring this written guarantee with your proof-of-purchase card if the television breaks down\>.

The narrower term, *guaranty*, is a noun denoting a promise to pay money or perform a contractual duty if someone else fails to do so \<a mortgage guaranty company\>. It is mostly used in banking and other financial contexts, and seldom appears outside legal writing.

A person who gives a *guaranty* is a *guarantor* \<the guarantor must sign a guaranty for the car loan in case the borrower doesn't make the payments\>.
