# gung-ho

gung-ho \(= extremely enthusiastic about exerting effort or undertaking some proposed course of action\), a Chinese loanword dating in English from the early 1940s, began during World War II as a motto shouted by U.S. Marines. It was their interpretation of a Mandarin phrase loosely translated as "work together." From the early 1950s, the term has been predominantly hyphenated—though some dictionaries continue to list the entry as *gung ho*. Other variants are \**gungho* and \**gun-ho* \(which mistakenly suggests a connection with guns\).

Current ratio in print \(*gung-ho* vs. *gung ho* vs. \**gungho* vs. \**gun-ho*\): 165:68:9:1
