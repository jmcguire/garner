# grade

grade; **grader**. These terms often take a hyphen \<a fourth-grade student\> \<a fifth-grader\>, but not when *grade* appears in a noun phrase \<the students are preparing for first grade\>. (See phrasal adjectives.) That's the traditional guidance—a rule abandoned in 2019 by the *AP Stylebook*, which now advises using the hyphen only when "needed to avoid confusion" (as in *He was the sixth fourth-grade student to win the prize*). But this new guidance ignores other kinds of miscues (as in *He was the fourth grader to be dismissed by the university*). Use *fourth-grader* for someone in fourth grade; use *fourth grader* when you've counted three other graders.
