# Genteelisms

Genteelisms are heightened, formal expressions considered (especially by the user) to be more refined or socially acceptable than the ordinary words for which they substitute. They are often wordy circumlocutions. Genteelisms can sometimes be jocular <Pardon me, but I believe someone might have liberated enteric airs>, sometimes just pretentious <If you'll be so kind as to excuse my absence for a moment while I perform ablutions prior to our repast>. Although the category can merge with euphemisms, the two are distinct. If a presenter says, "May I invite the committee's attention to the material which is presented on page 72," that's a genteelism for "Please turn to page 72"—but not a euphemism.
