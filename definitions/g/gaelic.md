# Gaelic

Gaelic; **Gallic**. *Gaelic* /**gay**-lik/ means "Scottish" or "Irish"; *Gallic* /**gal**-ik/ means "French." But there is a variant pronunciation in BrE: many Scots and academics prefer /**gal**-ik/ for *Gaelic*.

As a noun, *Gaelic* denotes the language spoken by the Celts of the Scottish Highlands—or, more broadly, by the Celts of Ireland and the Isle of Man as well. *Gallic*, though formerly denoting a person from France, is not used as a noun in modern English.
