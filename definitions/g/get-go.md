# get-go

get-go \(= the very beginning\), a compound dating from the 1960s, is a casualism that almost invariably appears in the phrase *from the get-go*. Some writers take the informality of this phrase even further by using *\*git*, the dialectal form of *get*, to write \**from the git-go*. Originally an Americanism, the term *get-go* is making inroads in BrE.

Current ratio in print \(*from the get-go* vs. \**from the git-go*\): 79:1
