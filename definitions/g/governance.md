# governance

governance. In 1926, H.W. Fowler pronounced *governance* an archaism for which either *government* or *control* suffices, allowing it only in "rhetorical or solemn contexts" (*FMEU1* at 220). The word was simply in a prolonged lull. In the 1960s, it was awakened in AmE and by the 1990s had become something of a vogue term throughout the English-speaking world. In legal contexts, it frequently refers to running or governing a corporation—e.g.: "Pierre-Henri Leroy \[is\] founder of Proxinvest, which advises companies on French corporate *governance* issues." "French Drug Stocks Soar as e.l.f. Proposes Alliance," *N.Y. Times*, 20 Dec. 1996, at D4.
