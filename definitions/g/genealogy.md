# genealogy

genealogy (= \[1\] the study of the history of a family; or \[2\] a description or depiction of how family members and their ancestors are related), a French loanword from the 1300s, doesn't end (like so many other words) in *-ology*—but instead in *-alogy*. How is it pronounced, then? Traditionally /jee-nee-**al**-ә-jee/, as opposed to /-**ahl**-ә-jee/. Cf. **mineralogy**.
