# gaudy

gaudy \(= \[1\] ostentatious, showy; or \[2\] dazzlingly brilliant or extravagant\) is the standard spelling. *\*Gawdy* is an archaic variant that still sometimes appears—e.g.: "Chris Warren carried nine times for 61 yards as Seattle averaged a *gawdy* \[read *gaudy*\] 6.1 yards per rush." Peter King, "Inside the NFL," *Sports Illustrated*, 22 Sept. 1997, at 56 \(photo caption\). In BrE, *gaudy* is also a noun denoting an annual festivity, especially at a college.

Current ratio in print \(*gaudy* vs. *\*gawdy*\): 147:1
