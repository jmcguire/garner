# generally accepted accounting principles

generally accepted accounting principles; **generally accepted accountancy principles**. The first is the usual phrase in AmE and BrE alike. The second is a variant. The phrase is often abbreviated *GAAP* /gap/—e.g.: "\[Deferred Acquisition Cost\] is the way insurers defer some of the costs of acquiring a customer and amortize them over a number of years under *generally accepted accounting principals* \[read *principles*\], or *GAAP*." Lee Ann Gjertsen, "Insurers' Annuity Moves Could Crimp Bank Profits," *Am. Banker*, 1 Nov. 2002, Ins./Inv. Prods. §, at 1. Because *GAAP* is an acronym, it should not have a period after each letter. See abbreviations \(a\) & **principal \(b\)**.
