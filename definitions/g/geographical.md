# geographical

geographical; **geographic**. Since the early 1700s, *geographical* has been the standard adjective throughout the English-speaking world. In the late 1800s, *geographic* emerged as a serious competing form—bolstered no doubt by the 1888 founding of *National Geographic Magazine* \(its original name\). Only in the late 1960s did *geographic* surpass *geographical* in AmE in frequency of use in print sources. The two remain locked in a tight race—but only in AmE. In BrE, *geographical* is more than twice as common in print sources. See -ic/-ical.

 - Current ratio in print \(*geographical area* vs. *geographic area*\): 1.1:1
