# griffin

griffin \(= a mythical animal having a lion's body and an eagle's wings and head\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, has been predominantly so written since about 1700. Among the variant spellings are \**gryphon* and \**griffon*.

 - Current ratio in print \(*winged griffin* vs. \**winged gryphon* vs. \**winged griffon*\): 24:1.9:1
