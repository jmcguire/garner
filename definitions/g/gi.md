# GI

GI \(= a member of the U.S. armed forces\) came into use about the time of World War II \(1939–1945\). The term originated in the 1930s as an abbreviation of *general* *issue* or *government issue*, and it was originally used attributively in reference to items <GI soap> <GI clothes>. By 1943, through metonymy, the abbreviation referred to the soldier with whom such articles were associated. \(See **metonymy** \[p. 1222\].\) For many years, the plurals \**GI's* and *GIs* vied for predominance, the former having the edge from 1950 to about 1970, but after 1970 *GIs* vanquished its rival. From the beginning, the abbreviation has been predominantly written without periods. See plurals \(h\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*American GIs* vs. *American GI's* vs. *American G.I.s* vs. *American G.I.'s*\): 48:3:3:1
