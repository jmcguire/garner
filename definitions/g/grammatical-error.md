# grammatical error

grammatical error. Because *grammatical* may mean either (1) "relating to grammar" <grammatical subject> or (2) "consistent with grammar" <a grammatical sentence>, there is nothing wrong with the age-old phrase *grammatical error* (sense 1). It's as acceptable as the phrases *criminal lawyer* and *logical fallacy*. The phrase *grammatical error* dates from the 1700s.

 - Current ratio in print (*grammatical error* vs. *error in grammar*): 19:1
