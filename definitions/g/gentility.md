# gentility

gentility; **gentleness**. Although these nouns ultimately have a shared etymology, *gentility* aligns more closely with such derivatives as *genteel*, *gentleman*, and *gentry*, while *gentleness* simply aligns with *gentle*.

*Gentility* = \(1\) good ancestry \(traditionally known as "gentle birth"\); \(2\) collectively, people of "good lineage"; or \(3\) social superiority as evidenced by cultivated taste and refined behavior—sometimes in a derogatory sense denoting exaggerated attempts at refinement or the pretense of high status. Since the 1800s, the word has often been used in mockery or disparagement. Cf. **genteel**.

*Gentleness* = the quality of being kind, mild, moderate, and amiable. The word's former associations with high social position have mostly disappeared. The phrase *Your Gentleness* was once used as a mode of address analogous to *Your Highness* or *Your Honor*, but no longer except with historical overtones or in derision \(*I entreat Your Gentleness to do me a favor*\).
