# goodbye

goodbye; **good-bye**; **good-by**; \***goodby**. Each of the first three is listed as the main headword in at least one major American dictionary. But *goodbye* is by far the most usual form, since the hyphen seems almost as archaic here as in *\*to-day* and *\*to-morrow*. And it has the advantage of resembling the related terms *bye* and *bye-bye*. The final headword—*\*goodby*—is merely a variant.

 - Current ratio in print \(*goodbye* vs. *good-bye* vs. *good-by* vs. \**goodby*\): 178:69:13:1
