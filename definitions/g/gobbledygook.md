# gobbledygook

gobbledygook; **gobbledegook**. Fontaine Maury Maverick \(1895–1954\), the American politician who coined the term, spelled it *gobbledygook*, and today that spelling is six times as common as its variant in AmE. In BrE, *gobbledegook* predominated from 1972 to about 2011, when *gobbledygook* took over. Most American and British dictionaries list *gobbledygook* first.

The term refers to the obscure language characteristic of jargonmongering bureaucrats. Thus *iterative naturalistic inquiry methodology* supposedly refers to a series of interviews. Much technical writing is open to criticism for using gobbledygook. And one purpose of this book is to wage a battle against it.
