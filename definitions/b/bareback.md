# bareback

bareback (= on a horse or similar quadruped without a saddle), a compound dating from the 1500s, is older in English than its rival \**barebacked*. In other words, the shorter form isn't actually a shortening. The two forms rivaled each other until the 1880s, when *bareback* became indisputably predominant. The hyphenated forms \**bare-backed* and \**bare-back* are variants.

 - Current ratio in print (*bareback* vs. \**barebacked* vs. \**bare-backed* vs. \**bare-back*): 31:2.1:1.8:1
 - Current ratio in print (*bareback horse* vs. \**barebacked horse*): 5:1
 - Current ratio in print (*bareback riding* vs. *bare-backed riding*): 661:1
