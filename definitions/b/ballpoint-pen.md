# ballpoint pen

ballpoint pen \(= a writing pen with a ball in the nib to roll ink onto the paper\) has been so spelled since 1970—as one word in *ballpoint*. From the mid-1940s until 1970, the usual spelling was *ball-point pen*. Never predominant was \**ball point pen*, an unhyphenated phrasal adjective. Typically today *ballpoint* alone is used.

In BrE, *biro* was once the usual term—from the proprietary name of a maker of ballpoint pens, after Lázló Biró, the Hungarian inventor—but this usage has been slipping against *ballpoint pen*.

Current ratio in print *\(ballpoint pen* vs. *ball-point pen* vs. \**ball point pen*\): 14:1.04:1
