# bitter cold

bitter cold. This is an age-old set phrase in which *bitter* functions adverbially. \(See adverbs.) It appears, for example, at the outset of Shakespeare's *Hamlet* \(1602\), where Francisco says: " 'Tis *bitter cold*, and I am sick at heart" \(1.1.6–7\). The phrase *bitterly cold* is an alternative wording that has coexisted with the more traditional phrasing since the late 1700s.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bitter cold* vs. *bitterly cold*\): 1.1:1
