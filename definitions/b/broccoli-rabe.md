# broccoli rabe

broccoli rabe; **rapini**; **\*broccoli raab**; **\*rappini**; **\*broccoli rape**. All these terms denote an Old World herb related to the turnip and having edible leafy branching stalks on which there are clusters of yellow florets. *Rapini*, first in English in the mid-20th century, is taken directly from the Italian etymon. In 1975 the English phrase *broccoli rabe* began to displace the earlier form \**broccoli rape* as a kind of euphemism, first in AmE and later in BrE. Today the usual forms in all varieties of English are *broccoli rabe* and *rapini*. The others are needless variants.
