# bedesman

bedesman \(= \[1\] someone retained to pray for another; \[2\] an inmate of a poorhouse; or \[3\] a beggar\), a compound dating in English from the 1200s, is an archaism often spelled **bedeman* or \**beadsman*. \(In Middle English, *bede* often meant "prayer."\) Although *beadsman* was standard from the mid-1700s to the early 2000s, it has yielded the linguistic right-of-way.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bedesman* vs. *beadsman* vs. \**bedeman*\): 16:14:1
