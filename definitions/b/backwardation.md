# backwardation

backwardation; **\*backadation**. This term—meaning "a fee paid by the seller of securities to allow for delivery after the delivery date originally agreed to" \(*Black's Law Dictionary* 171 \[11th ed. 2019\]\)—is preferably spelled *backwardation*. H.W. Fowler included the term in his "ill-favored list" of hybrid derivatives \(*FMEU1* at 241\), but it has become standard. \(See hybrids.\) It originated in BrE in the early 1800s.
