# burette

burette /byә-**ret**/ \(= a glass tube with measurements labeled on it, as for use in scientific experiments\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1400s, was often spelled **buret* in the first half of the 1800s. \(Evidence of the word between 1483 and 1836, however, is scant.\) The spelling *burette* has been predominant since about 1850. See gallicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*burette* vs. \**buret*\): 3:1
