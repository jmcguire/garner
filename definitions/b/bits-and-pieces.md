# bits and pieces

bits and pieces (= small things of various kinds), a phrase dating from the late 1800s, appeared infrequently until the 1920s, when it began to surge in popularity. Two chiefly BrE variants are the more alliterative *bits and bobs* and *bits and bats*—both little used in AmE and less frequent even in BrE than *bits and pieces*. Beginning in the mid-1940s, some writers began mistakenly rendering the phrase \**pits and pieces*.

 - Current ratio in print (*bits and pieces* vs. \**pits and pieces*): 5,727:1
