# ballpark

ballpark (= \[1\] a field, esp. one enclosed within walls or a fence, for playing games, esp. baseball; or \[2\] a range within which a cost, price, or estimate is considered acceptable) was originally two words—from the late 1800s till the early 1980s. But it has been predominantly solid across World Englishes since about 1980.

 - Current ratio in print (*ballpark* vs. \**ball park* vs. \**ball-park*): 105:9:1
