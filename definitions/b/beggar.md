# beggar

beggar (= someone who begs), a word of uncertain origin dating in English from the 1200s, is an oddly spelled agent noun. The *OED* notes that from the 1400s to the 1600s, \**begger* was "the usual form." In 1755, Samuel Johnson said the word "is more properly written *begger*; but the common orthography \[-*ar*\] is retained because the derivatives \[*beggarliness*, *beggarly*, *beggary*\] all preserve the *a*." The suffix might have been influenced by the BrE spelling *pedlar* (AmE *peddler*) or by such words as *bastard*, *coward*, and *drunkard*.

 - Current ratio in print (*beggar* vs. \**begger*): 200:1
