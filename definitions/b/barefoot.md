# barefoot

barefoot, adj.; \***barefooted**; **\*bare-footed**; **\*bare-foot**. For more than a thousand years, speakers of English have needed an adjective to describe someone wearing neither shoes nor socks. Since about 1890 in BrE and about 1910 in AmE, *barefoot* has been the standard term. Although the *OED* suggests that \**barefooted* or \**bare-footed* is more usual in reference to horses, in fact *barefoot* predominates even in equine contexts.

 - Current ratio in print (*barefoot* vs. \**barefooted* vs. \**bare-footed* vs. \**bare-foot*): 127:27:4:1
 - Current ratio in print (*barefoot horse* vs. \**barefooted horse*): 11:1
