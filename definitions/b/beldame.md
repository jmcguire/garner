# beldame

beldame /**bel**-dәm/ (= an old woman), an archaism dating from the 1400s, has been predominantly so spelled in all major varieties of English since the 1810s. The variant spelling *beldam*—though it appears as the first listed headword in the *OED*, *W11*, and *WNWCD*—appears in print only half as often. The predominance of *beldame* has led to the "spelling pronunciation" /**bel**-daym/. (See pronunciation (f).) The word has nothing to do with *bedlam* (= a place or situation of noisy confusion).

 - Current ratio in print (*beldame* vs. *beldam*): 1.8:1
