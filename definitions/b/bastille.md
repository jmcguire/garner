# bastille

bastille /ba-**steel**/ \(= \[1\] a small medieval fortress; or \[2\] a prison\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, antedates the Parisian prison/fortress destroyed during the French Revolution. But of course the term is now associated most strongly with Bastille Day (July 14), marking the anniversary of the 1789 storming and destruction of the Bastille. In any event, the common \(as opposed to the proper\) noun *bastille* has long had the variant spelling \**bastile*, which was the predominant spelling in English until the 1930s. That spelling came under siege and fell. See gallicisms.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bastille* vs. \**bastile*\): 6:1
