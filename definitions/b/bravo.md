# bravo

bravo. Two words are spelled this way. The older one, an Italian loanword dating in English from the late 1500s, denotes a villain or desperado—and more specifically a hired assassin; the newer one, also borrowed from Italian, dates from the mid-1700s and denotes a shout of approbation after a musical or theatrical performance. Either way, the standard plural is *bravos*—not \**bravoes*. See plurals \(d\). Cf. **bravado**.

Current ratio in print \(*bravos* vs. \**bravoes*\): 3:1   
Current ratio in print \(*hired bravos* vs. \**hired bravoes*\): 6:1
