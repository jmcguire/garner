# behoove

behoove \(= to be necessary or proper\) has been so spelled in AmE since about 1850 <it behooves you to bite your tongue in such circumstances>. In BrE, the spelling has always been *behove—*the predominant spelling in AmE through the mid-1800s. The word has an archaic flavor, and its noun form, *behoof*, is all but obsolete.

Historically, the verb in BrE was pronounced, as reflected in the AmE spelling, to rhyme with *move* and *prove*. In BrE today, it usually rhymes with *rove* and *grove* because those who use it "know it only in books" \(*OED*\). In AmE it is pronounced /bi-**hoov**/.
