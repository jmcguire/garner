# bus

bus, n. & v.t. The plural form of the noun (meaning "a large vehicle that holds many passengers") is *buses*. The verb (meaning "to transport by bus") is inflected *bus > bused > bused*; the present participle is *busing.* When the *s* is doubled, the sense is different: *bussed* means "kissed," and *bussing* means "kissing." Cf. **gases**. See spelling (c).

The verb *bus*, as a back-formation from *busboy*, has the additional meanings "to work as a busboy or busgirl" and "to clear dishes from (a table)." Here, too, the better-inflected forms are *bused* and *busing* <he helped pay his way through college by busing tables>.
