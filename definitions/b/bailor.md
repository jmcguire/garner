# bailor

bailor \(= someone who delivers personal property to another in trust\) is a legal term that has been predominantly so spelled since it entered the language in the 1600s. \(The correlative *bailee* \[= one to whom property is delivered in trust\] is actually somewhat older, an agent noun dating from the mid-1500s.\) \**Bailer* is a variant spelling that rarely appeared until about 1900. See -er \(a\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*bailor* vs. \**bailer*\): 3:1
