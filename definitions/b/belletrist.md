# belletrist

belletrist /bel-**let**-rist/ (= a writer of light and entertaining literature), a French loanword dating in English from the early 1800s, was originally *belle-lettrist* (or *belles-lettrist*). By 1920, the term had been firmly solidified in AmE and BrE alike. The corresponding adjective is *belletristic*, which has been standard since the 1870s. See gallicisms.

 - Current ratio in print (*belletrist* vs. \**belle-lettrist* vs. \**belles-lettrist*): 19:3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*belletristic* vs. *belle-lettristic* vs. *belles-lettristic*): 39:1.1:1
