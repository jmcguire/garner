# bravado

bravado \(= conduct deliberately intended to make people believe one is brave and self-confident\), a Spanish loanword dating in English from the 1500s, isn't a word that you might expect to have a plural—dealing as it does with conduct. But since the 1700s, the word has often appeared in a plural sense, and the plural has traditionally been spelled *bravadoes*—not \**bravados*. In recent years the two forms have competed pretty closely. See plurals \(d\). Cf. **bravo**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bravadoes* vs. \**bravados*\): 3:1
