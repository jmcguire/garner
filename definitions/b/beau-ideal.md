# beau ideal

beau ideal \(= \[1\] ideal beauty; or \[2\] the perfect example of a thing\) has, since the early 1800s, predominantly formed the English plural *beau ideals*—not *beaux ideals* \(the French plural\) or *\*beaux ideal*. Sense 2 is attributable to a misunderstanding of the French phrase. Although *beau* is the noun and *ideal* the adjective in French, many mistakenly think that *beau* is the adjective and *ideal* the noun. Sense 1 is the strictly correct translation.

 - Current ratio in print \(*beau ideals* vs. *\*beaux ideal* vs. *beaux ideals*\): 6:1.6:1
