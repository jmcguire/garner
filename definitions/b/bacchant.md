# bacchant

bacchant, n. & adj.; **bacchante**. Traditionally, the language has distinguished between men and women who worshipped Bacchus, the Greek god of wine and revelry. By extension, a drunken carouser who is male is known as a *bacchant* (/**bak**-әnt/ or /bә-**kant**/), whereas one who is female is known as a *bacchante* (/bә-**kant**/ or /bә-**kahnt**/ or /bә-**kant**-ee/). Curiously, the feminine *bacchante* has appeared in print about twice as often as *bacchant* from 1800 to the present day—in both singular and plural uses. See sexism (d).

The word *bacchant* is also an adjective and in that role may refer to both men and women <during those months, she led a bacchant life>.
