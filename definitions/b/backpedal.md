# backpedal

backpedal \(= \[1\] to pedal backward on a bicycle; \[2\] to change one's stated opinion by qualifying it; or \[3\] to renege on a promise by making a flimsy excuse\), a back-formation dating from the early 1890s, is a bicycling term that today is mostly metaphorical. Some writers misunderstand the basic idea and mistakenly make it **backpeddle*.

Why is it a back-formation? Simple: the gerund *backpedaling* \(BrE *backpedalling*\) is known to have antedated the straightforward verb by several years.

 - Current ratio in print \(*backpedaling* vs. \**backpeddling* in AmE\): 158:1
