# bruit

bruit /broot/ \(= to spread \[a rumor, report, etc.\]\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has traditionally been a transitive verb <it was bruited that he behaved in a scandalous manner>. By the mid-1900s, the preposition *about* was so commonly coupled with the verb that *bruit about* in effect became a phrasal verb <it was bruited about that her conduct was most unseemly>. \(Occasionally *abroad* is used in place of *about*.\) Since the early 1960s, the phrase *bruit about* has been susceptible to the malapropism \**brute about*. See gallicisms.
