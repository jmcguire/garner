# bonhomie

bonhomie /bon-ә-**mee**/ (= genial companionableness; affability), a French loanword dating in English from the 1700s, has long been the standard spelling. The archaic French spelling **bonhommie* had earlier been prevalent as a gallicism. The shorter spelling had become predominant in French by 1800 and in English by 1860.

 - Current ratio in print (*bonhomie* vs. \**bonhommie*): 22:1
