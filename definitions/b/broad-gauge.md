# broad gauge

broad gauge (= a width between railroad rails in excess of the standard width), a phrase dating from the mid-1800s, is two separate words as a noun phrase but hyphenated as a phrasal adjective \<broad-gauge rails\>. The one-word spelling, despite much to recommend it, hasn't caught on (perhaps because the antonym *narrow gauge* is necessarily two words).

By extension, the term has come to mean "broad-minded; tolerant of varied views" \<a broad-gauged diplomat\>. With this meaning, too, the adjectival form predominantly takes a past-tense form.

The misspelling \**broadgage* occasionally thrusts its way into print.
