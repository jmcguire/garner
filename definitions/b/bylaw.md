# bylaw

bylaw; **byelaw**. Both the spelling and the sense differ on the two sides of the Atlantic. In AmE, *bylaws* are most commonly a corporation's administrative provisions that are either attached to the articles of incorporation or kept privately. In BrE, *byelaws* are regulations made by a local authority or corporation, such as a town or a railway.

The spelling without the *e* is standard in AmE. Though etymologically inferior, *byelaw* \(sometimes hyphenated\) is common in BrE. Increasingly, though, the spelling *bylaw* is appearing in BrE just as commonly as the longer form.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bylaw* vs. *byelaw* in World Englishes\): 19:1
