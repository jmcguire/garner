# bestride

bestride \(= \[1\] to get on or sit on astride; to straddle; or \[2\] to dominate by being larger and more noticeable than anyone or anything else\), a verb dating from Old English \(ca. 1000\), is inflected *bestride* > *bestrode* > *bestridden*. This is most strange, given that *stride* is inflected *stride* > *strode* > *strode*—the word \**stridden* being both rare and quaint. Although *strode* is three times as common in print as \**had stridden*, the collocation *had bestridden* is three times as common as \**had bestrode*. Curious. And yet these terms cannot be said to *bestride* the English vocabulary: the inflected forms are themselves archaisms—and inconsistent ones at that. See **stride**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*had bestridden* vs. \**had bestrode*\): 3:1
