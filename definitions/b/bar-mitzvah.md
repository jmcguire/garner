# bar mitzvah

bar mitzvah; **bat mitzvah**; **bas mitzvah**. These terms are gender-specific. *Bar mitzvah* /bahr **mits**-vә/ = (1) the ceremony celebrating a Jewish boy's reaching religious adulthood at the age of 13; or (2) a Jewish boy who has turned 13 and thus attained religious adulthood. The variant spelling *bar mizvah* is nonstandard.

*Bat mitzvah* (or its variants *bas mitzvah* and *bat mizvah*) has the same meanings for a Jewish girl, though her age can vary from 12 to 13. The term is pronounced /baht **mits**-vә/.

While these terms were once compounded into single words, or joined by hyphens, today they are written as two-word phrases.

 - Current ratio in print (*bar mitzvah* vs. *bar mizvah*): 2,290:1
 - Current ratio in print (*bat mitzvah* vs. *bas mitzvah* vs. *bat mizvah*): 8,372:42:1
