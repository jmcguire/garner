# bibliolatry

bibliolatry /bib-lee-**ol**-ә-tree/ (= zealous devotion to the supposed inerrancy of the Bible), a Latin/Greek hybrid dating in English from the mid-1700s, could be read as meaning "zealous devotion to books generally." But using the word in this broad sense—applicable perhaps even to an atheist—ignores the inescapable history of the word's fundamentalist-Christian associations.

The corresponding agent noun is *bibliolater*; the adjective is *bibliolatrous*.
