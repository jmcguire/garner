# burn > burned > burned

burn > burned > burned. So inflected—increasingly even in BrE. As a verb, *burnt* is chiefly a BrE form, usually the past participle but sometimes the past tense. In AmE, *burnt* is almost exclusively an adjective <burnt orange> <burnt rubber>. But as a casualism, *burned out* (= physically or emotionally exhausted) is preferable to *\*burnt out*.

 - Current ratio in print (*burned rubber* vs. *burnt rubber*): 1.2:1
