# bougainvillea

bougainvillea /boo-gәn-**vil**-ee-ә/ \(the tropical wall-growing plant having red or purple flowers\) has been predominantly so spelled since the word was introduced in the mid-1800s. The plant was named after the French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville \(1729–1811\), whose botanist identified several varieties during the explorer's circumnavigation. The variant form \**bougainvillaea*, though somewhat common in the mid-20th century, has never predominated.

Current ratio in print \(*bougainvillea* vs. \**bougainvillaea*\): 12:1
