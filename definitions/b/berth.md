# berth

berth \(= a spot or position\) is occasionally misspelled *birth*, sometimes \(as in the first example below\) with unintended results—e.g.:

 - "The Academy finished second behind Tufts University in the four-division regatta. Two Navy women earned *births* \[read *berths*\] in the nationals last weekend." Jeff Nelson, "Race Wrapup," *Capital* \(Annapolis\), 19 Oct. 1995, at D6.

 - "The Washington Wizards . . . are beginning to think about a playoff *birth* \[read *berth*\]." Justin Brown, "NBA's New York Story," *Christian Science Monitor*, 21 Dec. 2001, at 10.

 - "The Tigers clinched an Empire 8 playoff *birth* \[read *berth*\], while improving their record 6–2, 6–1." "Andres, Vegy Help Spark MCC Shutout," *Rochester Democrat & Chron.*, 24 Sept. 2008, at D5.

Language-Change Index

*birth* misused for *berth*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*secure a berth* vs. *\*secure a birth*\): 15:1
