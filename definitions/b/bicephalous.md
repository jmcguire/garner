# bicephalous

bicephalous /bī-**sef**-ә-lәs/ \(= having two heads\), a Latin/Greek hybrid dating in English from 1803, has almost always been the predominant term in figurative senses <bicephalous executive>. *Bicephalic* /bī-sә-**fal**-ik/ is a variant that most commonly describes monsters, serpents, and other creatures. These bookish terms have never presented much competition to *two-headed*, *double-headed*, and *Janus-faced*.
