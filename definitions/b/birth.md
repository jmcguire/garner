# birth

birth was used with some frequency in the Middle Ages as a verb. It long fell into disuse but was revived in the 1970s—first in AmE and then in BrE <she birthed four babies>. It often appears as a participial adjective <birthing room>. Some dictionaries label it dialectal. But given its usefulness and its long standing in the language, it should be accepted as standard.

## Language-Change Index

 - *birth* as a verb: Stage 5
