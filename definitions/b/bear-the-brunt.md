# bear the brunt

bear the brunt \(= to have to deal with the most difficult, burdensome, or damaging part of something\), a phrasal verb dating from the 1400s, is susceptible to the malapropism \**bare the brunt* \(a mistake dating from the 1960s\). *Brunt*, of uncertain etymology, denotes the worst part of an attack, criticism, or bad situation.

Current ratio in print \(*bear the brunt* vs. \**bare the brunt*\): 513:1
