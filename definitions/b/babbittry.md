# Babbittry

Babbittry; **\*Babbitry**. In 1922, Sinclair Lewis wrote the popular novel *Babbitt*, about a vulgar antihero businessman \(George Follansbee Babbitt\) who smugly but unthinkingly adopts middle-class, materialistic values. Since then, a *Babbitt* is any person who follows that pattern, and *Babbittry* denotes the conduct of a philistine who follows it. The variant form \**Babbitry*, which drops a *t*, has never been as common.

Current ratio in print \(*Babbittry* vs. \**Babbitry*\): 3:1
