# blame

blame, v.t. In the best usage, one *blames* a person; one does not, properly, *blame* a thing *on* a person—e.g.: "I *blame* him *for* the fires." \(Not: *\*I blame the fires on him.*\) The collocation *blame it on* had no appreciable presence in the language till about 1900. Since then it has steadily become more common, especially in BrE. Blame it on Britons.

## Language-Change Index

 - *blame* \[the act or outcome\] *on* \[the actor or cause\] for *blame* \[the actor or cause\] *for* \[the act or outcome\]: Stage 3
 - Current ratio in print \(*blamed him for it* vs. *blamed it on him*\): 4:1
