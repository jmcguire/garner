# bougie

bougie. Since about 2008, this slang shortening of *bourgeois* (dropping the *r*)—pronounced /**boo**-zhee/—has described people who are label-conscious, status-conscious, and snobbish; their tastes tend toward the tinselly overelaborate and chichi. On the one hand, the adjective *bougie* can be a term of slight praise <oh, look at your bougie new purse\!>; on the other hand, it can be (and most often is) a term of disparagement <her tastes are predictably bougie>. In late 2016, the variant spelling \**boujee* was popularized in the hip-hop song "Bad and Boujee," by the group called Migos.

As a noun, *bougie* is a different word deriving from the city formerly known as *Bougie* (now Béjaïa), an Algerian seaport. It denotes a tapered, hand-dipped candle of the type once made plentifully in Béjaïa—and, by extension and shape-association, either a suppository or a tapered cylindrical instrument used by doctors to dilate a tubular anatomical structure or to insert something into such a structure.
