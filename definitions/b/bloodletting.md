# bloodletting

bloodletting; **phlebotomy**; **venesection**. *Bloodletting* \(now usu. solid, not hyphenated\) dates from the 1200s. Today it has three senses: \(1\) the practice or an act of bleeding a person for therapeutic reasons \(President John Adams almost died from it\); \(2\) bloodshed; and \(3\) in a figurative sense, a reduction in personnel or resources. For sense 1 in medical usage, the term *phlebotomy* /flә-**bot**-ә-mee/, a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, is much more usual today, especially in drawing blood for labwork. The blood-drawer is known as a *phlebotomist*. Until the 1770s, the near-synonym *venesection*, now rarely used even in surgical contexts, was more common than *phlebotomy*.
