# beggarticks

beggarticks; **beggar lice**; **beggar's lice**; **beggar's ticks**; **beggarlice**. Various American plants with prickly seeds or seed pods go by these names, which date from the early 1800s. From the 1880s to about 1930, *beggar ticks* was the most prevalent term. By 1940, the term had become predominantly solidified—hence *beggarticks*. Meanwhile, though, *beggar-lice* and *beggarlice* had grown in prominence. Today, *beggarticks* is the predominant form, with nine variants (*beggar lice*, *beggar's lice*, *beggar's-lice*, *beggar ticks*, *beggar-lice*, *beggars' lice*, *beggar's ticks*, *beggar's-ticks*, and *beggarlice*).
