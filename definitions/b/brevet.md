# brevet

brevet /bri-**vet**/ or, in BrE, /**brev**-it/ \(= an honorary military commission that promotes an officer to an honorary rank, sometimes with increased authority but never with increased pay\), a French loanword, dates in English from the late 1600s. As a verb, *brevet* has meant to convey such an honorary rank since the 1800s <the colonel was brevetted to the rank of brigadier general>. The usage question is how to inflect the verb: *brevetted* or *breveted*? The answer, oddly enough, is that *brevetted* and *brevetting* are usual in AmE, while *breveted* and *breveting* are somewhat more traditional in BrE. \(But it's *labeled* and *labeling* in AmE, *labelled* and *labelling* in BrE—see **label**.\) British usage seems to be shifting to *brevetted*/*brevetting*. See spelling \(c\); see also gallicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*brevetted* vs. *breveted* in World Englishes\): 2.5:1
