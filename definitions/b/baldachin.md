# baldachin

baldachin /**bahl**-dә-kәn/ \(= \[1\] a rich embroidered fabric used esp. in a cloth canopy that covers either a sacred object or a grandee; or \[2\] an ornamental canopy of any material, such as granite or marble\), a Romance-language loanword dating in English from the 1500s, has been the predominant form of the word since the 1840s. **Baldachino* \(close to the Italian etymon\) and \**baldaquin* \(as in French and Spanish\) are variants.

Current ratio in print \(*baldachin* vs. \**baldachino* vs. \**baldaquin*\): 6:1.1:1
