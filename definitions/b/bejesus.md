# bejesus

bejesus, a mild oath dating from the mid-1800s, has always been predominantly so spelled. (The term derives, of course, from *Jesus*.) The spellings \**bejeezus* and \**bejezus* are variants. The term most often appears in the colloquialism *scared the bejesus out of* (someone), but other verbs are sometimes used: *beat*, *kick*, *knock*, etc. In Irish English, the spelling *bejasus*, with /-**jay**-/ as the middle syllable, is common.

 - Current ratio in print (*bejesus* vs. \**bejeezus* vs. \**bejezus*): 141:41:1
