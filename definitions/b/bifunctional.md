# bifunctional

bifunctional \(= having two functions\), an adjective dating in English from the 1920s, appears most commonly in the context of chemistry <bifunctional compounds> <bifunctional monomers>. Since the mid-20th century, it has predominated over its synonym *difunctional*, which was introduced in the 1940s. Because the prefix *bi*- is more generally understood than *di*- \(both meaning "two" or "twice"\), the predominance is hardly surprising.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bifunctional* vs. *difunctional*\): 13:1
