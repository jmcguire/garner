# base

base, misused for *bass*, is a basic blunder—e.g.:

 - " ‘Three Days' was an exercise in carnality, propelled by the sinuous *base* \[read *bass*\] guitar of Flea." Michael Saunders, "Jane's Addiction: This Set Didn't Satisfy," *Boston Globe*, 8 Nov. 1997, at C3.

 - "Made up of four sopranos, four altos, three tenors and three *bases* \[read *basses*\], the student singers have been sharing their voices with audiences in Algonquin." "Neighbor," *Daily Herald* (Chicago), 16 Nov. 2000, at 1.

## Language-Change Index

 - *base* misused for *bass*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*bass guitar* vs. *\*base guitar*): 95:1
