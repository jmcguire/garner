# byword

byword (= \[1\] a proverb, saying, or characteristic slogan; or \[2\] someone or something representing a specific quality) is best spelled as one word and not hyphenated.

 - **Sense 1:** "Debussy's ambivalence can be attributed to his constant experimentation and renewal (*plus haut*—ever higher—was his *byword*)." Georgine Resick, *French Vocal Literature* 190 (2018).

 - **Sense 2:** "One of the major internal tasks facing the pope has been the reform of the Vatican Bank, which had become a *byword* for scandal and dysfunction." Paul Vallely, "The Bipartisan Pontiff," *N.Y. Mag.*, 9 Sept. 2015, at 1.
