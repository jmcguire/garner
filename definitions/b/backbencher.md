# backbencher

backbencher \(= a legislator, esp. a British one, who does not have an important leadership position and is therefore seated toward the rear of the legislative chamber\), a term popularized in the early 20th century, was predominantly hyphenated from 1910 until the early 1960s \(\**back-bencher*\). First in AmE and then in BrE, the word was solidified. The term *frontbencher* \(= a legislator who, as a party leader, sits in a prominent position within the legislative chamber\) followed a similar pattern.

Current ratio in print \(*backbencher* vs. \**back-bencher* in World Englishes\): 13:1
Current ratio in print \(*frontbencher* vs. \**front-bencher* in World Englishes\): 9:1
