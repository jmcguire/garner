# black, vb

black, vb.; **blacken**. Both verbs mean "to make or become black," but *black* is confined to the narrow, physical sense of using black polish <Frank blacked his boots>, whereas *blacken* is typically used in all other physical senses <the sky blackened> as well as in figurative senses <the candidate's mood blackened when the indictment was unsealed>. But any figurative use bearing negative connotations—those are the traditional figurative uses—are ill-advised on grounds that they perpetuate racist views equating blackness with badness. Avoid them. See prejudiced and prejudicial terms.

2017 ratio in print (*blacked his boots* vs. *blackened his boots*): 168:1
