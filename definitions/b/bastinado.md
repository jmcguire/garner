# bastinado

bastinado /bas-ti-**nah**-doh/ or /-**nay**-doh/ (= \[1\] a rod, stick, or cudgel; or \[2\] a beating or blow with such an instrument), a Spanish loanword dating in English from the 1500s—when corporal punishment was more widespread than it is today—has always been the predominant form of the word. The anglicized **bastinade* is a variant. The predominant plural has always been *bastinadoes*, not \**bastinados*. See plurals (d).

 - Current ratio in print (*bastinado* vs. \**bastinade*): 20:1
 - Current ratio in print (*bastinadoes* vs. \**bastinados*): 13:1
