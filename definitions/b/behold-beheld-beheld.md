# behold > beheld > beheld

behold > beheld > beheld. So inflected. The word *beholden* is an archaic past participle, now used only as an adjective meaning "indebted" and attached to a *to*-phrase—e.g.:

 - "Ryan implies that Eckels will be *beholden* to the contractors underwriting a large part of his campaign." Alan Bernstein, "County Judge Race Pits Opposing Styles," *Houston Chron.*, 9 Oct. 1994, at A1.

 - "The elder Mr. \[Duncan D.\] Hunter said each of the earmarked projects stands up to scrutiny and rejected the notion that accepting campaign funds from defense contractors made him *beholden* to them." Matthew Cella, "Hunter's Earmarks Benefit His Son," *Wash. Times*, 13 Oct. 2008, at A1.
