# bare

bare, v.t. \(= to uncover\), is sometimes confused with *bear* \(= \[1\] to carry; or \[2\] to endure\)—e.g.: "They've invited women to *bear* \[read *bare*\] their breasts on ‘Whip 'em out Wednesday.' " Larry Bonko, "Meet Charles DeFoore, Your Local Man in the Fight Against Vulgar TV," *Virginian-Pilot* \(Norfolk\), 1 Sept. 2002, at E2. See word-swapping.

The verb is inflected *bare* > *bared* > *bared*. For the opposite error, see **bear out**.

Language-Change Index

*bear* misused for the verb *bare*: Stage 1   
Current ratio in print \(*bare your soul* vs. *\*bear your soul*\): 18:1
