# bear out

bear out \(= to support or confirm as evidence\) is sometimes wrongly made *\*bare out*—e.g.:

• "These are just a few of the many numbers which *bare* \[read *bear*\] out the fact that Texas and Randall County are caught up in a wave of juvenile crimes." Bradley Harrington, "Living in Confinement," *Canyon News* \(Tex.\), 31 Mar. 1996, at 1.

• "A luncheon date with his offensive line next week *bares* \[read *bears*\] that out." Gary Voet, "Edmonds Scores High as SCC's Quarterback," *Sacramento Bee*, 22 Nov. 1996, at E1.

For the opposite error, see **bare**.

The past-participial form of *bear out* is *borne out*. For *borne* vs. *born*, see **born**.

Language-Change Index

*\*bare out* for *bear out*: Stage 1   
Current ratio in print \(*bear out* vs. *\*bare out*\): 121:1
