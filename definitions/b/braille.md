# Braille

Braille \(= a type of printing for blind people, with raised dots to signify characters\), named after its inventor, Louis Braille \(1809–1852\), is predominantly so capitalized. In AmE, the eponym was predominantly lowercase from about 1974 to 1985, but the uppercase form rebounded in print after that. BrE has always preferred the capitalized spelling.

Current ratio in print \(*Braille texts* vs. *braille texts* in World Englishes\): 1.6:1
