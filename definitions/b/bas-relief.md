# bas-relief

bas-relief /bah ri-**leef**/ or, esp. in BrE, /bahs-/ \(= a style of sculptural art in which stone, wood, or metal protrudes from a more or less flat surface\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1600s, is so written. The plural is *bas-reliefs*. The term is occasionally anglicized as *bass-relief* /bays ri-**leef**/. See gallicisms.

A competing Italian loanword, *basso-relievo* or *basso-rilievo* \(the latter being the proper Italian spelling\), has competed with *bas-relief* since the 1600s. Its plural is *bassi-relievi* or *bassi-rilievi*. Given the relative frequency of the French loanword in English, the Italian terms are largely unnecessary.

Current ratio in print \(*bas-reliefs* vs. *bassi-relievi* vs. *bassi-rilievi*\): 2,064:14:1
