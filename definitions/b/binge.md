# binge

binge (= to do too much of something in a short period), originally a dialectal word in the 1800s meaning "to soak," has predominantly formed the present participle *bingeing* since 1987 in BrE and since 1991 in AmE. Before that, \**binging* was about equally common. So the inflected form is analogous to *whingeing*—not to *cringing*, *fringing*, *hinging*, *lunging*, and *tinging*. (See the entries for those other words.)

 - Current ratio in print (*bingeing on food* vs. \**binging on food*): 1.9:1
