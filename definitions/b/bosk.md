# bosk

bosk \(= a thicket or copse\), dating originally from the 1200s, fell into disuse in Early Modern English \(1500–1699\) but was revived in the 1800s by Sir Walter Scott and Alfred, Lord Tennyson. Meanwhile, the synonym *boscage* /**bos**-kij/ \(with its variant spelling *boskage*\) has been in continual use from 1400 to the present day. It's all rather *bosky* \(= bushy; full of thickets\). Your choice may depend on the rhyme you're aiming for—since you wouldn't even be thinking of these words if you weren't something of a poet. Cf. **copse**.

 - Current ratio in print \(*bosk* vs. *boscage* vs. *boskage*\): 1.2:1.4:1
