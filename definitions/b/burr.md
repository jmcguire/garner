# burr

burr \(= \[1\] the prickly envelope of a plant seed; \[2\] something that sticks, as in the throat; \[3\] a rough spot in a piece of metal; \[4\] a regular sound, like a whir, caused usu. by something that turns continuously; or \[5\] a way of pronouncing English with trilled /r/ sounds\), traceable in some senses to the 1300s, has been predominantly so spelled in all of them. Actually, these senses represent several words with the same spelling \(see any unabridged dictionary\). For most senses, \**bur* is a variant spelling.

Current ratio in print \(*chestnut burr* vs. \**chestnut bur*\): 3:1   
Current ratio in print \(*Scottish burr* vs. \**Scottish bur*\): 192:1
