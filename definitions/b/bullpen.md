# bullpen

bullpen \(= \[1\] a fenced enclosure for bulls; \[2\] a large prison cell where prisoners are kept until brought into court; \[3\] a place near a baseball dugout where pitchers practice before they start pitching; or \[4\] collectively, the pitching staff on a baseball team\), a compound dating from the early 1800s in sense 1, was predominantly two words until the early 1960s. Since then, the solid form has been standard in AmE; the change didn't occur in BrE \(in which the term isn't common\) until the early 1990s.

Current ratio in print \(*bullpen* vs. *bull pen* vs. \**bull-pen*\): 64:8:1
