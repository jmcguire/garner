# within

within sometimes leads to illogic in announcing scores—e.g.: "The play set up Michael Reeder's fourth field goal to bring TCU to *within* 20–19." Suzanne Halliburton, "Welcome Back," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 19 Nov. 1995, at C8. TCU was not *within* 20–19, but right *at* it. *Within* \(not *to within*\) would work in that sentence if the preposition had a different object, e.g., *within a field goal*, or \(to avoid the repetition\) *within a single point*.
