# widow

widow, vb., can make a past-participial adjective *widowed*, which may apply to either sex. Although *widowed man* may seem unnatural, it is common and unobjectionable—e.g.: "Schmidt himself is no less persnickety; once *widowed*, he asks his daughter . . . to tend him, telling her precisely how to make his lunch as if instructing troops in the loading of a gun." Anthony Lane, "Looking Back," *New Yorker*, 16 Dec. 2002, at 106.
