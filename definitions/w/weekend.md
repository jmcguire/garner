# weekend

weekend (= the period between the close of business or school on Friday and the start of the business or school day on Monday), a compound dating from the 1600s, was predominantly two words until about 1870 and hyphenated from 1870 to about 1955. Since the 1950s in AmE and the mid-1960s in BrE, the term has been predominantly solid.

 - Current ratio in print (*weekend* vs. \**week-end* vs. \**week end*): 335:5:1
