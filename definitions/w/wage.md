# wage

wage; wages. The word can be indifferently singular <he earns a good wage> or plural <she earns good wages>, but the latter form has always predominated in all varieties of English. The plural, of course, is construed with a plural verb <your wages are now subject to a garnishment order>. The biblical phrasing *The* *wages of sin is death* \(Romans 6:23\) is regarded as a linguistic anomaly—and a set phrase.
