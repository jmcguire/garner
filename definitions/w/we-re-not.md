# we're not

we're not; **we aren't**. Although both forms are extremely common, *we're not* is somewhat more common in print than *we aren't*. And because the negative isn't contracted in *we're not*, the phrasing is more emphatic. E.g.: "*We're not* talking about a futuristic, Jetson-like electronic house where robots cook and clean. *We're not* even talking about so-called smart houses." Lew Sichelman, "Upgraded Wiring a Must in Homes," *San Diego Union-Trib.*, 31 Aug. 1997, at H1.
