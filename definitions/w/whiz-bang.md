# whiz-bang

whiz-bang; **whizz-bang**. Dating from the early 1880s and meaning "something resembling fireworks for its conspicuous flash, noise, and startling effect," this term has varied in its spelling over time. The predominant forms are *whiz-bang* in AmE and *whizz-bang* in BrE—so hyphenated. Although *W11* lists only the solid forms \**whizbang* and \**whizzbang*, these occur in print sources much less often.
