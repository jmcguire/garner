# wringer

wringer; ringer. A *wringer* can be \(1\) an oppressor, \(2\) a machine that squeezes or twists to drain and make dry, or \(3\) someone who wrings clothes that have been washed. A *ringer* can be \(1\) a bell-ringer, \(2\) something that rings, such as a telephone, \(3\) someone who fraudulently substitutes one thing for another, \(4\) a double—that is, a person or thing that looks very much like another, or \(5\) a phony or impostor.

In the phrase *put through the wringer*, an Americanism dating from the late 1800s, sense 2 of *wringer* is the applicable one. Since about 1980, the solecism \**put through the ringer* has been much on the rise. The solecism itself should be put through the wringer.

Language-Change Index

*put through the \*ringer* for *put through the wringer*: Stage 3   
Current ratio in print \(*put through the wringer* vs. \**put through the ringer*\): 3:1
