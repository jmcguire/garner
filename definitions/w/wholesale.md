# wholesale

wholesale (= \[1\] of, relating to, or involving the business of selling goods in large quantities at low prices to other businesses instead of to the general public; or \[2\] affecting almost everything or everyone, often without concern for specific detriments), a compound dating from the mid-1600s as an adjective, has always been so spelled. Beginning in the 1960s, some writers have mistakenly spelled it \**wholescale*, doubtless under the influence of *full-scale* (= as complete or thorough as possible).

 - Current ratio in print (*wholesale destruction* vs. \**wholescale destruction*): 131:1
