# worship

worship, vb. Although some American dictionaries give preference to the inflected forms *worshiped* and *worshiping*, these have never achieved predominance in AmE print sources. That is, *worshipped* and *worshipping* have steadily outranked the single-*p* forms in frequency of use despite the general rule about not doubling the final consonant in an inflected syllable that is unaccented. (See spelling (c).) The reason may have to do with the form of the second syllable (*nap* forms *napping*, and *ship* forms *shipping*) and the analogical associations that automatically arise in readers' minds. Even so, the single*-p* forms continue to vie with the double*-p* forms in AmE—doubtless because of the dictionaries' influence. In BrE, there has never been any real competition. On the evidence, though, one may deduce that *worshipped* and *worshipping* are standard AmE as well as BrE. Cf. **kidnapping (a)**.

Yet *worshiper* is surprisingly robust in AmE, perhaps because it is given priority in *WNWCD*, which tends to be followed by newspapers.

 - Current ratio in print (*worshipped* vs. *worshiped* in World Englishes): 2.5:1
 - Current ratio in print (*worshipper* vs. *worshiper* in BrE): 6:1
 - Current ratio in print (*worshipper* vs. *worshiper* in AmE): 1:1
