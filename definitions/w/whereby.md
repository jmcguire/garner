# whereby

whereby (= by means of which), though sometimes overworked, is more concise than alternatives such as *through which*. So it can be a useful word—e.g.: "Republican Congressman Ralph Regula of Ohio, chairman of the Appropriations subcommittee in charge of federal parks, is brokering a deal *whereby* Congress will appropriate $5 million and give it to California, which will in turn give it to the Reagans for the 100-year-old adobe house and grounds they paid $480,000 for in 1974." Margaret Carlson, "Lake Lucky, Here We Come\!," *Time*, 29 Sept. 1997, at 19.
