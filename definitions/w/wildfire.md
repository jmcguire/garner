# wildfire

wildfire \(= an uncontrolled conflagration that moves quickly, usu. with great destructiveness\), a compound dating back to Old English, commonly appears in the simile *to spread like wildfire*. Sometimes purposely but sometimes not, people since the 1970s have used the more innocuous mondegreen *to spread like wildflowers*. E.g., "The woman, being very disillusioned, began recounting her conversation with several others until the word *spread like wildflowers*." Donna McMenamin, *Popular Arts of Mexico: 1850–1950* 187 \(1996\). This floral simile suggests much less immediacy and destructiveness—and greater fragrance.

Current ratio in print \(*spread like wildfire* vs. *spread like wildflowers*\): 871:1
