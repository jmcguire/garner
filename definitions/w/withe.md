# withe

withe \(= a slender, flexible shoot, branch, or twig\), a Middle English term traceable to the 1100s, has long predominated in print sources over its variant *withy* /**with**-ee/. The plurals are *withes* /wīthz/ and *withies* /**with**-eez/. *Withy* has an additional sense as a synonym of *willow* or *osier*.
