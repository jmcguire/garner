# will-o'-the-wisp

will-o'-the-wisp \(= \[1\] a blue moving light caused by natural gases and visible over wet ground at night; \[2\] an unreachable goal or impossible hope; or \[3\] an unreliable person\), a phrase dating from the mid-1600s, has been predominantly so written since the late 1700s. Among the variants are \**will o' the wisp* \(omitting the hyphens\) and \**will of the wisp* \(restoring the elided *f *\). \**Willow-the-wisp* is a mondegreen.

 - Current ratio in print \(*will-o'-the-wisp* vs. \**will o' the wisp* vs. \**will of the wisp* vs. \**willow-the-wisp*\): 447:58:4:1
