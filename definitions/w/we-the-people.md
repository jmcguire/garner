# we the people

we the people. Many seem to consider these words a set phrase—even if the phrasing is *hiding information from we* \[read *us*\] *the people*. In the best English, *we the people* is always a subject or a predicate nominative, but never the object of a verb or preposition. If the syntax calls for *us the people*, don't be afraid to use it.

From the 1800s on, such phrases as *for us the people* and *with us the people* have been standard. Only since 2000 has there been a serious upsurge in the ungrammatical phrases *\*for we the people* and *\*with we the people*.
