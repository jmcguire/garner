# whale

whale, vb. \(= \[1\] to thrash or hit with force; or \[2\] to defeat decisively\), a colloquial verb dating from the late 1700s and having nothing to do with the sea animal, is thought by some etymologists to be an altered form of the verb *wale* \(= to mark \[the skin, a hide, etc.\] with welts\). Since the early 1900s in AmE, the verb *whale* has occurred perhaps most often in the collocation *whaling away at* <he went berserk, whaling away at anyone else he could see> or *whaling on* <in frustration, he whaled on the pillow until his fist hurt>. Since about 1969, the verb has sometimes been confounded with its near-homophone *wail* \(= to cry out in sadness or pain\).

The expression *whaling away* is virtually unknown in BrE.

Current ratio in print \(*whaling away at* vs. \**wailing away at*\): 1.8:1
