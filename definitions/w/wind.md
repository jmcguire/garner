# wind

wind, vb.; **wound**, vb.; **winded**. In most senses that are pronounced /wīnd/, the preferred past-tense and past-participial forms of *wind* are *wound* /wownd/ <the road wound through the countryside> <she hadn't wound the clock> <the luggage wound up in Boston>. In most senses pronounced /wind/ and related to breathing, airflow, and the sense of smell, the verb takes regular *-ed* endings <she became winded early in the race>. One uncommon sense defies this generalization, though: the preferred pronunciation of *wind* meaning "to blow a horn" is /wīnd/, and the preferred past-tense inflection is *wound*. See irregular verbs.
