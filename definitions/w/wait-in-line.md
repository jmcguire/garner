# wait in line

wait in line; wait on line. The former is the standard AmE expression. The latter is a regionalism in the Northeast, especially in New York. Although some might think that it's the product of the computer age (i.e., being *online*), in fact it dates back to the 1800s and became a common regionalism during the late 1960s. (Cf. **stand in line**.) The British prefer simply to *queue*.

 - Current ratio in print (*wait in line* vs. *wait on line*): 58:1
