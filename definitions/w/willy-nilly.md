# willy-nilly

willy-nilly, adv. & adj., = (1) by compulsion <he forced his brother to accompany him willy-nilly>; or (2) in a haphazard, unplanned way <so far, all our meetings have occurred willy-nilly>. The phrase is sometimes, as the *OED* remarks, erroneously used for "undecided, shilly-shally" <a willy-nilly disposition>.
