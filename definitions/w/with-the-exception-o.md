# with the exception of

with the exception of, a popular phrasing in the 1800s, is verbose for *except*, *except for*, *aside from*, or *apart from*—e.g.:

• "The best-actress field is generally hale, *with the exception of* \[read *aside from*\] the cancer victim played by Diane Keaton in ‘Marvin's Room.' " "A Sweep at Oscars May Be Hard to Get," *Tulsa Trib. & Tulsa World*, 24 Mar. 1997, at A10.

• "European Union Members, *with the exception of* \[read *except for*\] Greece, recalled their envoys to Iran for consultations over the court verdict." "Tehran Protesters Target German Embassy as Feud Deepens," *L.A. Times*, 12 Apr. 1997, at A4.
