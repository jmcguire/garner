# wither

wither (= to dry up or become weak) is misused for *whither* in the following title: John Darnton, "In Tory Vote, It's Not Just ‘Wither Major?' but ‘Wither Britain?' " *N.Y. Times*, 2 July 1995, at 6. Because Britain was, at the time the article appeared, withering under an unusual heat wave—the worst since 1976—this might have seemed to be a joke. But the misusage was in 24-point type. *Whither* is the formal interrogative adverb for asking what the future of something or someone will be. See **hither**.
