# wintry

wintry; \*wintery. The former is the older and the standard spelling for the adjective meaning "of or like winter." It is much more common than the latter in modern print sources—e.g.: "The crowd of about 50 . . . mill about, sipping coffee and commenting on the *wintry* weather." Gay Jervey, "Workaholics Anonymous," *Fortune*, 3 Mar. 2003, at 150.

Current ratio in print \(*wintry* vs. \**wintery*\): 9:1
