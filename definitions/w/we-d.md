# we'd

we'd = \(1\) we would; or \(2\) we had. Sense 2 has not held as much favor as sense 1, but it is common and typically doesn't cause any confusion because the past participle follows closely—e.g.: "*We'd* just arrived in Colorado . . . . *We'd* gone to sleep gliding through the farmlands of Missouri." Sue Wunder, "Perfect, Thanks to Mulligan," *Christian Science Monitor*, 21 Aug. 1997, at 16.
