# whinge

whinge \(= to complain peevishly or annoyingly\), originally a Scotticism dating from the Middle English period \(1100–1499\), is primarily a Briticism today. For the present participle, it predominantly makes *whingeing*, not *whinging*. \(The two vie closely.\) So the inflected form is more nearly analogous to *bingeing* than to *cringing*, *fringing*, *hinging*, *lunging*, and *tinging*. \(See the entries for those other words.\)

 - Current ratio in print \(*whingeing* vs. *whinging*\): 1.1:1
