# wobble

wobble \(= \[1\] to move unsteadily from side to side; or \[2\] to vacillate about whether to do something\), seemingly a Dutch loanword dating in English from the early 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled since the 1870s in BrE and the early 1900s in AmE. Before that, the variant spelling **wabble* was standard.

 - Current ratio in print \(*wobbling* vs. \**wabbling*\): 50:1
