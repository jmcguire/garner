# way(s)

way(s). In the sense "the length of a course or distance," *way* has long been considered the standard term \<a long way\>. In a singular sense, *ways* occurs frequently in AmE \<quite a ways to go\> but rarely in BrE. One might be tempted to call it dialectal, but the singular use in *a ways to go* actually occurs in AmE print sources about half as often as *a way to go*. E.g.: "This is premature, of course; Fox still has *a ways to go* \[read *some way to go*?\] before it's a full-fledged network." Larry Reibstein & Nancy Hass, "Rupert's Power Play," *Newsweek*, 6 June 1994, at 46. See dialect.
