# ketchup

ketchup; **catsup**; **catchup**. The first spelling greatly predominates in modern usage and has done so since about 1850 in BrE and about 1985 in AmE. It has the advantages of phonetically approximating and of most closely resembling the word's probable source, either the Cantonese *k'ē chap* or the Malay *kēhap*, both referring to a kind of fish sauce. The pronunciation is either /**kech**-әp/ or, in AmE, /**kach**-әp/; /**kat**-sәp/ is pretentious.

 - Current ratio in print \(*ketchup* vs. *catsup* vs. *catchup*\): 22:1.8:1
