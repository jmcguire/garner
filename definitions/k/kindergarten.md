# kindergarten

kindergarten. This German loanword for "children's garden" has been in use in English since at least the mid-1700s with its foreign spelling intact. It is sometimes misspelled as if it were anglicized—e.g.: "Lexington is the largest school in the state for the profoundly deaf and hard-of-hearing, and educates students from pre-*kindergarden* \[read *kindergarten*\] to age 21." Nicole Bode, "Deaf Get into the Act," *Daily News* (N.Y.), 6 June 2002, Suburban §, at 1. It is also sometimes misspelled \**kindegarten*.

The word may be pronounced /**kin**-dәr-gahrt'n/ or /-gahrd'n/.
