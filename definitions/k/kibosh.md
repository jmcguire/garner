# kibosh

kibosh \(= the stopping of a plan or idea from developing or progressing; veto\), a word of unknown origin dating in English from the early 1800s, refers to an action or decision that halts further progress on a plan, idea, or activity. It usually appears in the phrase *to put the kibosh on* \(something\). It was widely adopted in BrE in the 1830s and in AmE in the 1870s. The word is normally pronounced /**kī**-bosh/ in both AmE and BrE, but /ki-**bosh**/ is also an acceptable AmE pronunciation.
