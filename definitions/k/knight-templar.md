# Knight Templar

Knight Templar. The traditional plural is *Knights Templars* (pluralizing both the noun *knight* and the postpositive adjective *templar*). This has been so since the 1600s.

The historic *Knights Templars* were members of a military and monastic Augustinian order founded in 12th-century Jerusalem during the First Crusade. Members were called the Knights of the Temple or *Knights Templars* because their quarters were in a palace next to the building called Solomon's Temple. The order was suppressed in the 1300s for religious and political reasons.

Although a modern international organization called the Knights Templar (no *s*) exists, its connection to the monastic order is merely apocryphal. So although many writers err, the differing plural spellings for the ancient order and the modern organization should be carefully observed.
