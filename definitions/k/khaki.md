# khaki

khaki \(= a brownish-yellow fabric of twilled cotton or wool\) is frequently misspelled *\*kahki*—e.g.: "Tuesday is D-day. But don't look for Audie Murphy or a whole lot of *kahki* \[read *khaki*\]." Lawrence van Gelder, "The Glory and Glamour of Dietrich," *N.Y. Times*, 31 Mar. 1996, § 12, at 51.

Language-Change Index

*khaki* misspelled *\*kahki*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*khaki* vs. *\*kahki*\): 2,601:1
