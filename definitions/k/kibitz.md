# kibitz

kibitz \(= \[1\] to comment unhelpfully and annoyingly when someone is doing something \[originally during a card game\]; or \[2\] to speak tediously about obvious things\) is a Yiddishism introduced into AmE in the 1890s. The word is accented on the first syllable: /**kib**-itz/. The agent noun is *kibitzer* /**kib**-it-zәr/. The variant spellings *\*kibbitz* \(vb.\) and *\*kibbitzer* \(n.\) have not become predominant across World Englishes.

Current ratio in print \(*kibitz* vs. *\*kibbitz*\): 5:1   
Current ratio in print \(*kibitzer* vs. *\*kibbitzer*\): 9:1
