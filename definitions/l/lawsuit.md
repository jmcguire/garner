# lawsuit

lawsuit. Journalists often misuse *lawsuit* \(one word in both AmE and BrE\) for *complaint* or *petition* \(= the paper that is filed to start a lawsuit\)—e.g.:

• "In its 18-page *lawsuit* \[read *complaint*\], Viacom-owned CBS alleged: ‘"Celebrity" was consciously designed to mimic "Survivor" and unfairly trade on its success.' " Meg James, "CBS Sues to Block New ABC Program," *L.A. Times*, 7 Nov. 2002, Bus. §, pt. 3, at 3.

• "The allegations in his 144-page *lawsuit* \[read *complaint*\] read like a Robin Cook novel." Lynne Tuohy, "Pfizer Trials Called Cruel, Immoral," *Hartford Courant*, 6 Dec. 2002, at A1.

• " ‘Defendants knew he couldn't control his gambling addiction,' according to the 29-page *lawsuit* \[read *complaint*\]." Becky Yerak, "Gamblers Sue over Addiction," *Detroit News*, 8 Dec. 2002, at D1.

The *lawsuit* is the whole process; the document \(however many pages long\) is only an instrumental part of it. See **case \(d\)**.
