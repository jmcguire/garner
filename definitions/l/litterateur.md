# litterateur

litterateur; **literatus**; **literato**; **\*literateur**. English speakers have been indecisive in arriving at a standard term to denote a literary person. Collectively, of course, such people are literati. \(See **literati**.) But what is a single member of the group? The normal choice today is *litterateur*, a gallicism dating from the late 1700s. The older latinism *literatus*, a synonym dating from the early 1600s, is the singular of *literati*. But since the early 1800s, it hasn't been as common as *litterateur*. The Italianate *literato*, which also forms the plural *literati*, has also been used, along with another Italian form—*letterato*. In any event, all these words are assuredly literarisms. If you must use one, stick to *litterateur*, which sometimes takes an acute accent \(*littérateur*\)—but not normally in English. See diacritical marks.
