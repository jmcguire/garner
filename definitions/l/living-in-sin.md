# living in sin

living in sin. This phrase, which reached its zenith in the 1800s, is on the wane. Even the Church of England has proclaimed that *living in sin* is a "most unhelpful" way of describing unmarried couples who cohabit. The Church estimates that four out of five couples today live together before marrying. A major Church report in 1995 therefore concluded that the phrase should be dropped. Ruth Gledhill, " ‘Living in Sin' Is No Longer Sinful, Says Church Report," *Times* (London), 7 June 1995, at 1.
