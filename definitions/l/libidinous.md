# libidinous

libidinous; **libidinal**. The adjective *libidinous* \(= lustful, lewd, and lascivious\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1400s, has always been a disparaging term to describe someone with an unbridled sexual desire. *Libidinal*, introduced in the early 1920s as a psychoanalytical term, is the neutral adjective meaning "of, relating to, or involving the sex drive \(libido\)." So one is deprecatory, the other neutral. By 1930 the neutral term \(*libidinal*\) had overtaken the old term conveying moral disapproval \(*libidinous*\). Today, *libidinal* occurs in print about three times as often as its lustier counterpart. The differentiation between the terms took hold from the time *libidinal* was introduced.
