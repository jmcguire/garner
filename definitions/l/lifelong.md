# lifelong

lifelong; **livelong**. *Lifelong* = lasting for all or most of one's life <Seymour's lifelong dream was to conduct the New York Philharmonic>. *Livelong* = \(of a time period, esp. a day or a night\) whole, entire <"the eyes of Texas are upon you, all the livelong day">. *Livelong* was once more prevalent than *lifelong*, especially during the early 1800s, but then the fortunes of these two words reversed about 1860. Since then, instances of *lifelong* have steadily risen in modern print sources. Confusion of these words isn't as uncommon as it ought to be—e.g.:

 - "Born in Providence, a son of the late Peter Gomes and the late Mary Fortes, he had been a *livelong* \[read *lifelong*\] resident of Providence, moving to Brockton eight years ago." "Late Obituary: Bernard D. ‘Chicken' Fortes," *Providence J.-Bull.*, 18 Jan. 1995, at C6.

 - "Berning pursued a *livelong* \[read *lifelong*\] interest in the golf swing." Larry Bohannan, "Making Ends Meet," *Desert Sun* \(Palm Springs\), 12 Feb. 2002, at C8.

 - "A native and *livelong* \[read *lifelong*\] resident of the Tampa area, he attended Riverview High School." "McGowan, Mitchell Curtis" \(obit.\), *St. Petersburg Times* \(Fla.\), 4 Sept. 2002, at B7.
