# licensor

licensor (= someone who grants a license to another), an agent noun dating from the mid-1600s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since the late 1880s and in BrE since about 1940. The older spelling, \**licenser*, has been in retreat probably because *licensor* answers to *licensee* more neatly as a terminological correlative. See -er (a).

 - Current ratio in print (*licensor* vs. \**licenser* in World Englishes): 12:1
