# lingerie

lingerie. This French word emerged in the English language in the 1830s as a word denoting linen clothes. It soon became a euphemism for *women's underwear*. A true French pronunciation is difficult in English, since the first syllable is a French nasal with no precise equivalent in English. Although the French pronunciation is something like /la\[*n*\]-**zhree**/—and that is the approximate BrE pronunciation—the established AmE pronunciation is /lon-jә-**ray**/. No advertiser would consider affecting a French pronunciation for an American audience. See hypercorrection (k).
