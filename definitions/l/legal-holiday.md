# legal holiday

legal holiday; **public holiday**; **bank holiday**. These phrases are essentially synonymous. The first two are mostly AmE phrases denoting a day designated by law as a holiday, accompanied by the closing of most public offices and paid leave for most public employees. Observance of a legal holiday by the private sector is voluntary. A legal or public holiday may be established by the national government (e.g., July 4 as Independence Day) or a state government (e.g., March 2 as Texas Independence Day).

*Bank holiday* is a mostly BrE term denoting a weekday when most businesses (not just banks) are closed. *Bank holidays* are standardized in many countries, but are not known by this name in the U.S.
