# laureled

laureled (= \[1\] overgrown with evergreen trees or shrubs of the genus *Laurus*; \[2\] crowned with a leafy wreath; or \[3\] honored, illustrious), a past-participial adjective dating from the 1600s but not in widespread use until 1800, is preferably so spelled only in AmE. (See spelling (c).) Across World Englishes generally, the BrE spelling *laurelled* predominates.

 - Current ratio in print (*laurelled* vs. *laureled* in World Englishes): 3:1
 - Current ratio in print (*laureled* vs. *laurelled* in AmE): 1.04:1
