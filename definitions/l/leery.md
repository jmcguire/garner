# leery

leery (= wary \[of someone or something\] by reason of distrust; suspicious), an adjective dating from the early 1700s, was originally slang that became especially popular during the 1800s and 1900s. The word derives from the cognate adjective *leer*, meaning "looking askance" or "oblique and indirect." Today the word has lost its slanginess but probably remains a casualism. The variant spelling **leary*, which enjoyed brief popularity from the 1890s to about 1900, is now relatively uncommon.

 - Current ratio in print (*leery* vs. \**leary*): 24:1
