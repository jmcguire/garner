# lemma

lemma (= \[1\] a proposition either assumed or proved in establishing a theorem; \[2\] a proposition used as a heading within a composition; or \[3\] a headword in a glossary or dictionary), a Greek loanword dating in English from the late 1500s, has two plurals: *lemmas* and \**lemmata*. The former has strongly predominated since the mid-1700s. See plurals (b).

The verb *lemmatize* (BrE *lemmatise*) is stressed on the first syllable, not the second, across World Englishes.

 - Current ratio in print (*lemmas* vs. \**lemmata*): 6:1
