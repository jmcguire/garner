# lagoon

lagoon (= \[1\] a shallow lake, channel, or pond connected to a larger body of water; or \[2\] a discrete area of seawater separated from the rest of the sea by a coral reef, an atoll, or sand dunes), a French loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has always been predominantly so spelled. The French and Italian etymons from which *lagoon* is derived remain variants in English: \**lagune* (Fr.) and \**laguna* (Ital.). The anglicized spelling *lagoon* didn't take hold until the mid-1700s.

 - Current ratio in print (*lagoon* vs. \**laguna* vs. \**lagune*): 276:3:1
