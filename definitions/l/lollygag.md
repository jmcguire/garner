# lollygag

lollygag (= \[1\] to fool around; or \[2\] to engage in idle inactivity to delay getting down to work), an AmE colloquialism dating from the mid-1800s, has been predominantly so spelled since the 1960s. Before that, \**lallygag* was the usual spelling. The word is of uncertain etymology.

 - Current ratio in print (*lollygag* vs. \**lallygag*): 14:1
