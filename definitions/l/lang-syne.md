# lang syne

lang syne \[lit., "long since" in Middle English\] \(= times past\), a Scotticism dating in Modern English from the late 1600s, is a term that many American desktop dictionaries over the years have recorded as a solid compound: \**langsyne*. But the one-word version, whose heyday in BrE was 1810 to 1910, has never predominated. The full phrase, of course, is *auld lang syne* \[lit., "old long since"\], which is typically heard only on December 31.

 - Current ratio in print \(*lang syne* vs. \**langsyne*\): 12:1
