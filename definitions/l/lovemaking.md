# lovemaking

lovemaking \(= \[1\] traditionally, courtship; or \[2\] modernly, sexual activity—esp., copulation\), a compound dating from the 1400s, has been predominantly so written, as solid, since about 1972. Before that, the hyphenated variant *love-making* was standard. Sense 2, suggesting sexual intercourse as an act of love, dates only from the 1920s.

Current ratio in print \(*lovemaking* vs. *love-making*\): 4:1
