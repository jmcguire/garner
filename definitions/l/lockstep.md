# lockstep

lockstep (= \[1\] a way of marching side by side with others with perfectly synchronized movements; or \[2\] a standard, exceptionless procedure that ensures uniformity), a compound dating from about 1800, has been predominantly so written, as solid, since about 1960. The earlier forms were \**lock-step* (predominant from about 1830 to 1960) and \**lock step* (predominant before 1830). Yet as the second ratio below suggests, the 130-year predominance of \**lock-step* may be misleading because many instances doubtless involved phrasal adjectives. It would be appropriate if the modern solidity of the term could be exceptionless.

 - Current ratio in print (*lockstep* vs. \**lock-step* vs. \**lock step*): 10:2.1:1
 - Current ratio in print (*in lockstep with* vs. \**in lock step with* vs. \**in lock-step with*): 8:1.1:1
