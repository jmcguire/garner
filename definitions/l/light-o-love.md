# light-o'-love

light-o'-love \(= \[1\] a paramour, esp. a fickle one; or \[2\] a prostitute\), a literary term dating from the 1500s and still used infrequently, predominantly has the *of* reduced through elision to *o'*. \**Light-of-love* is a variant. The plural is *light-o'-loves*, not \**lights-o'-love*.

Current ratio in print \(*light-o'-love* vs. \**light-of-love*\): 1.3:1
Current ratio in print \(*light-o'-loves* vs. \**lights-o'-love*\): 6:1
