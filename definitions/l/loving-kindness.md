# loving-kindness

loving-kindness (= tender, affectionate behavior from one with a benevolent temperament), a biblical expression dating in English from the early 1500s, has been predominantly so hyphenated since 1820. Before that, \**loving kindness* was the usual form. The solid spelling \**lovingkindness*, a compound dating from the late 1600s, hasn't been accepted kindly, consistently remaining a second runner-up. Cf. **lovesick**.

 - Current ratio in print (*loving-kindness* vs. \**loving kindness* vs. \**lovingkindness*): 1.2:1.2:1
