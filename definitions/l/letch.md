# letch

letch \(= a lascivious, lubricious man\), a respelled back-formation from *lecher*, gained popularity in the mid-1800s and then again especially after 1940. The product of apocope \(the loss of the final sound in a word\), *letch* remains a casualism that occurs less commonly in print than the full word *lecher*, but more commonly than the alternative back-formation \**lech*.

Current ratio in print \(*a lecher* vs. *a letch* vs. *a \*lech*\): 4:1.3:1
