# levy, vt

levy, v.t., = (1) "to impose (as a fine or a tax) by legal sanction" \<the court levied a fine of $500\>; (2) "to conscript for service in the military" \<the troops were soon levied\>; (3) "to wage (a war)" \<the rebels then levied war against the government\>; or (4) to take or seize (property) in execution of a judgment \<the creditor may levy on the debtor's assets\>.

In sense 1, this verb is sometimes mangled through object-shuffling: "He quit hours after the football program was *levied* with sanctions that included a two-year ban on post-season play, a loss of 10 scholarships for each of the next two recruiting classes, and removal from the Pac-10's television revenue-sharing pool for 1993, costing the program an estimated $1.4 million." Jim Cour, "Coach Quits in Protest," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 23 Aug. 1993, at C1. A program (or person) is not *levied with* a penalty; rather, the penalty is *levied against* the program (or person).

The word is pronounced /**le**-vee/; it's sometimes wrongly pronounced like the surname *Levy* (usually /**lee**-vee/).
