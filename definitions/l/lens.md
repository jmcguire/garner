# lens

lens. So spelled—not *\*lense*. But the misspelling occurs fairly often, as something like a back-formation from the plural—e.g.:

 - "Raunchy Lisa ‘Left Eye' Lopes—who got her nickname after wearing a condom over one *lense* \[read *lens*\] of her glasses—makes no apologies for her behavior." John Dingwall, "Latest Flame," *Daily Record* \(Baltimore\), 8 Feb. 1996, at 23.

 - "You will . . . pay a price for such surreal sensationalism—a single *lense* \[read *lens*\] is $150." Sylvi Capelaci, "For Your Eyes Only," *Toronto Sun*, 24 June 1997, at 42.

 - "His \[Alex Rocco's\] most famous role came in 1972's ‘The Godfather,' in which he played the humbled casino owner who meets his fate on a massage table, with a bullet through a thick eyeglass *lense* \[read *lens*\]." "Was Moe Greene in ‘The Godfather'?" *Wash. Post*, 21 July 2015, at B4.

## Language-Change Index

*lens* misspelled *\*lense*: Stage 1
