# legislator

legislator. In proper governmental terms, a governor is the executive, not a legislator: "Utah Gov. Mike Leavitt and *other legislators* \[read *several legislators*\] discuss their support for a balanced-budget amendment with reporters on Thursday." "EPA Has Been Flexible on Missions, Official Says," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 10 Feb. 1995, at A10 (photo caption). The caption-writer wasn't observing the separation of the branches of government. But *lawmaker* (an arguably broader term) might have been permissible there.
