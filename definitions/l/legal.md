# legal

legal; **lawful**; **licit**. *Legal* is the broadest term, meaning either (1) "of, relating to, or involving law, falling within the province of law," or (2) "established, permitted, or not forbidden by law." These two senses are used with about equal frequency.

*Lawful* and *licit* share sense 2 of *legal*: "according or not contrary to law, permitted by law." *Lawful* is quite common \<driving in a lawful manner\>. The least frequent of these three terms is *licit* \<licit acts\> \<the licit use of force\>, which usually occurs in direct contrast to *illicit*.

*Lawful* should not be used in sense 1 of *legal*, as it sometimes is—e.g.: "The judgment must be affirmed if there is sufficient evidence to support it on any *lawful* \[read *legal*\] theory, and every fact issue sufficiently raised by the evidence must be resolved in support of the judgment." See **illegal**.
