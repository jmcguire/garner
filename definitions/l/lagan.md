# lagan

lagan /**lag**-әn/ \(= goods abandoned at sea but made recoverable by attaching them to a buoy or other floating object\), a legal term dating from the 1600s, has been predominantly so spelled only since about 1890. Before that—and overwhelmingly from 1760 to 1820—the usual spelling was \**ligan* \(now a variant\). Among the other variants are \**lagon*, \**ligen*, and \**lagend*. There used to be an officer at Calais whose job was to collect and account for lagan—he was called the *lagander*—but this isn't done in the days of the Chunnel.

 - Current ratio in print \(*lagan* vs. \**ligan* vs. \**lagon* vs. \**ligen* vs. \**lagend*\): 35:34:16:13:1
