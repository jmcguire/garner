# lifeline

lifeline \(= \[1\] a rope used for saving people in danger, esp. at sea; \[2\] a means of escape from a difficult situation; or \[3\] in palmistry, a crease in the palm thought to represent the duration and ease of one's life\), a compound dating from about 1700, has been predominantly so written, as solid, since about 1950 in AmE and in BrE. The earlier forms, \**life line* and \**life-line*, are now but variants.

 - Current ratio in print \(*lifeline* vs. \**life line* vs. \**life-line*\): 25:1.5:1
