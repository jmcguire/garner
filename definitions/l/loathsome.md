# loathsome

loathsome. So spelled, even though the first syllable sounds like the verb rather than the adjective (see **loathe**). Perhaps as a result of the sound association, many writers misspell the word *\*loathesome*—e.g.:

 - "An array of *loathesome* \[read *loathsome*\] characters drifts through this anthropology of the urban undead." Chris Kidler, "Tama Janowitz's ‘A Certain Age,' " *Baltimore Sun*, 8 Aug. 1999, at F10.

 - "Severed Fingers and Toes (Gasworks) are extremely *loathesome* \[read *loathsome*\]-looking gummy chews, available at party stores." Charles Perry, "Extreme Treats," *L.A. Times*, 27 Oct. 1999, at H1.

 - "The characters we first meet in ‘Nurse Betty' are terminally dim, risible or *loathesome* \[read *loathsome*\]." James Verniere, "Zellweger Is a Reason to Love ‘Nurse Betty,' " *Boston Herald*, 8 Sept. 2000, at S21.

## Language-Change Index

 - *loathsome* misspelled *\*loathesome*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*loathsome* vs. *\*loathesome*): 118:1
