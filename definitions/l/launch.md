# launch

launch, v.t. By the early 20th century, *launch* had become a vogue term when used in the sense "to begin, initiate"—e.g.:

 - "USAir Chairman Seth Schofield . . . is visiting Pittsburgh to *launch* a new service to Mexico City today." Steve Creedy, "Travel Agents Slap Airlines over Fee Caps," *Pitt. Post-Gaz.*, 15 Feb. 1995, at F1.

 - "DuPage County is *launching* an innovative ‘last chance' educational initiative for students expelled from school because of drug selling." Casey Banas, "Last Chance Offered to Kids Facing Expulsion," *Chicago Trib.*, 12 Feb. 1997, at D1.

The related noun <the first launch in California will be in San Diego> is much the same.
