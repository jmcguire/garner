# liege

liege, a term recorded in English from the late 1200s, is a kind of contronym: it can denote either a medieval vassal sworn to allegiance to a feudal lord or the feudal lord to whom the vassal swears allegiance. In fuller phrasing, the vassal is a *liegeman*, the lord a *liege lord*. The main point of this entry, however, is to note the pronunciation: /leej/—not /leezh/. Cf. **siege**.
