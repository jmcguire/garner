# laundromat

laundromat is sometimes misspelled *\*laundry mat*—e.g.:

• "Winkler got the idea for immortalizing T-shirts one day during the spin cycle at the *laundry mat* \[read *laundromat*\]." "Last-Minute Gifts Tip for Holiday Procrastinators," *Virginian-Pilot & Ledger Star* \(Norfolk\), 22 Dec. 1996, at 6.

• "He followed the scent to the vent fan on the nearby *laundry mat* \[read *laundromat*\], then to the dryers inside where bulging pillow cases were tumbling." R.K. Shull, "Waiting for the Smoke to Clear," *Indianapolis News*, 19 July 1999, at C1.

• "Someone stole a car from a *laundry mat* \[read *laundromat*\]." "Police Log," *Orlando Sentinel*, 2 Sept. 1999, at D10.

• "The Rizzo family returned to the *laundry mat* \[read *laundromat*\]." Alex Lizzi Jr., *Bonding the Rizzo's* \[read *Rizzos'*\] *Family Vacation* 100 \(2007\).

Although *laundromat* appears to be a portmanteau word, perhaps derived from *laundry* and *automat*, it is actually a servicemark for a brand of washing machines, and, briefly, a chain of do-it-yourself laundries.
