# leniency

leniency; **\*lenience**. The former has been standard since 1800. The latter is a needless variant. E.g.: "Shas Party leader Aryeh Deri . . . is alleged to have demanded Bar-On's appointment in expectation of *lenience* \[read *leniency*\] in his own trial on charges of fraud in his party's finances." Barton Gellman, "Netanyahu's Indictment Sought," *Wash. Post*, 17 Apr. 1997, at A1.

## Language-Change Index

*\*lenience* for *leniency*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*leniency* vs. *\*lenience*\): 16:1
