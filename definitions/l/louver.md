# louver

louver (= an adjustable slat of wood, glass, etc.—usu. one of many—sloping to modify the degree of light or outside air that comes into a room), a French loanword dating in English from the 1300s, has been predominantly so spelled in AmE since the late 1930s. The BrE spelling has always been predominantly *louvre*. See -er (b); see also gallicisms.
