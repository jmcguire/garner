# lineament

lineament /**lin**-ee-ә-mәnt/ \(= \[1\] a feature of someone's body, esp. of the face, usu. with regard to its outline or silhouette; or \[2\] a typical quality or distinctive feature\), a French loanword dating in English from the 1400s, is so spelled. The term is normally used in the plural <the lineaments of his youthful face>. It is often misspelled and mispronounced as if it were the word \**linament*.

Current ratio in print \(*lineaments* vs. \**linaments*\): 542:1
