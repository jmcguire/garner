# lardon

lardon /**lahrd**-әn/ or /**lahr**-dahn/ \(= a fatty strip of pork used for flavoring\), a cookery term dating from the 1400s, was originally so spelled. But for a century, from the 1870s to the 1970s, the variant spelling \**lardoon* \(traceable to the 1700s\) became predominant—especially in AmE. Since about 1973, *lardon* \(the form of the French etymon\) has gained a decisive predominance throughout the English-speaking world.

Current ratio in print \(*lardon* vs. \**lardoon*\): 7:1
