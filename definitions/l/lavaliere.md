# lavaliere

lavaliere; **lavalier**; \***lavalliere**; **\*lavallière**. *Lavaliere* is the conventional spelling for the word meaning \(1\) "a microphone that is clipped to clothing and used typically in broadcast studios and theaters"; or \(2\) "a style of jewelry and clothing." \(The microphone was so named because it was originally hung around the neck like jewelry.\) In the 2010s, the term predominantly lost its final *e*: *lavalier* surged in popularity—like a bold cavalier.

Although the term derives from the name of a French courtesan, Louise de la Vallière \(1644–1710\), the anglicized *lavaliere* is pronounced /lav-ә-**leer**/. The last two spellings among the headwords are overt gallicisms \(pronounced /lah-vahl-**yair**/\)—and mere variants in English.

 - Current ratio in print \(*lavalier* vs. *lavaliere* vs. \**lavalliere* vs. \**lavallière*\): 44:31:3:1
