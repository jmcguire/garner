# landsman

landsman; **landman**. A *landsman* \(dating in its modern senses from the 1600s\) is either a fellow countryman or someone contrasted with a *seaman*. A *landman* \(dating in its current sense from the 1920s\) is someone whose work involves acquiring oil-and-gas leases, negotiating arrangements for the development of leases, and managing mineral-leased properties. In most parts of the U.S. in which landmen are active, the title of *landman* has been decisively adopted by women as well as men. Although \**landperson* has occasionally surfaced since the 1960s, *landman* remains the usual term. \(See sexism \(c\).) By contrast, **landsperson* is so uncommon as not to register in big-data searches.

Current ratio in print \(*landman* vs. \**landperson*\): 204:1
