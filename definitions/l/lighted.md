# lighted

lighted; **lit**. Both are standard past-tense and past-participial forms. The most traditional forms are *lighted* as an adjective <a lighted torch> and as a past participle <have you lighted the fire yet?>. *Lit* is unimpeachable as a past tense—e.g.: "He *lit* another cigarette, inhaling deeply as the executives ran through several more subjects." Ken Auletta, "Beauty and the Beast," *New Yorker*, 16 Dec. 2002, at 65, 67. See irregular verbs.

Although *he lighted the fire* was the predominant phrase in print sources from 1700 to 1900, *he lit the fire* became predominant beginning about 1915. Let your ear guide you.

 - Current ratio in print \(*he lit the* vs. *he lighted the*\): 3:1
