# ledger line

ledger line \(= in musical notation, a short line added above or below a musical staff to show a note outside the range of the normal staff\) has been predominantly so spelled for most of its history since being introduced about 1700. According to the *OED*, "the origin of this use is unclear," but the editors add that "the common statement that it represents *leger*, ‘light, slight,' is baseless." In fact, the variant \**leger line*, reflecting that etymological error, was especially common from 1895 to the early 1970s, at times predominating.

 - Current ratio in print \(*ledger line* vs. \**leger line*\): 4:1
