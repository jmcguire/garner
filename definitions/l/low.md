# low

low; **lowly**. Each can function as both adjective and adverb: *a low profile*; *the supplies ran low*; *a lowly peasant*; *soar lowly through the clouds*. Because of the potential ambiguity, ensure that either word's meaning is clear from the context. Be aware that in the adjectival uses, *low* tends to connote literal or figurative spatiality <shooting from a low position>, while *lowly* connotes inferior status <a lowly position within the organization>.
