# locator

locator \(= \[1\] someone who finds something, esp. for a fee; or \[2\] someone who fixes the future situation or course of settlers, a building, a railroad, a mine, etc. by survey or otherwise\), a Latin loanword dating in English from the 1600s, has always been predominantly so spelled. **Locater* is a variant. See -er \(a\).

Current ratio in print \(*locator* vs. \**locater*\): 25:1
