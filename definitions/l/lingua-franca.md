# lingua franca

lingua franca \(= a language used as a common or commercial tongue among people whose first languages are diverse\), a phrase borrowed from Italian in the early 1600s, has two plurals in English: *lingua francas* and \**linguae francae*. Neither was used much at all until the 1900s, and from 1920 until about 1967 the two forms contended closely. Since then, *lingua francas* \(the anglicized plural\) has strongly predominated. See plurals \(b\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*lingua francas* vs. \**linguae francae*\): 6:1
