# lambaste

lambaste; **lambast**. Although both spellings are well attested, *lambaste* is truer to the putative etymology (*lam* "to beat" \+ *baste* "to thrash") and, in any event, has vastly predominated in AmE since the mid-20th century. *Lambast*, however, has been the usual form in BrE since the late 1960s. Across World Englishes today, the two spellings occur with almost equal frequency.

The word is pronounced /**lam**-bast/ or /**lam**-bayst/. The second is the most frequent pronunciation; that explains, no doubt, why people often hesitate to spell the word *lambaste*. The traditional pronunciation, rarely heard today, stresses the second syllable: /lam-**bayst**/.
