# lingo

lingo (= odd, minimally comprehensible language used by some group of people), a humorous or mildly disparaging term dating from the 1600s, has two plural spellings: *lingoes* and *lingos*. From about 1795 to 1870, *lingos* was the more usual plural. After about 1870, *lingoes* strongly predominated—though since about 1980 the two have been in close competition for predominance. See plurals (d).

 - Current ratio in print (*lingoes* vs. *lingos*): 1.05:1
