# -laced

-laced. Although *straitlaced* is formed from an adjective \(*strait*\), the modern combining form -*laced* \(meaning "filled with"\) should follow a noun: *arsenic-laced meal*, *metaphor-laced speech*, etc. It shouldn't be combined with an adjective—e.g.: "After the game, Isaiah Rider, who scored 16 points, questioned his teammates' will to win with a *profane-laced* \[read *profanity-laced*\] diatribe." "Shaq's Monster Night Lifts L.A.," *Austin Am.-Statesman*, 30 Dec. 1999, at D4.
