# leitmotif

leitmotif /**līt**-moh-teef/ \(= a recurrent musical or metaphorical theme associated throughout the work with a specific character or situation\) is the standard spelling. *\*Leitmotiv*, though more faithful to the German loanword, is but a variant in English. That is, the word was anglicized to make it align with an older import into the English language: *motif*. In fact, though, the meaning of *leitmotif* \(which smacks of sesquipedality\) is so close to that of *motif* that outside highly specialized musical contexts, it is rarely needed—except to signal that you're a genuine intellectual. See germanicisms.

Current ratio in print \(*leitmotif* vs. \**leitmotiv*\): 4:1
