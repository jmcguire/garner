# zero

zero. When used as an adjective \(as it rarely is\), *zero* should modify a plural noun, not a singular one. The only number that takes a singular noun is *one*. E.g.: "In 1985, New York City had 71 days that were out of compliance with the EPA standard for carbon monoxide; that number declined to two days in 1991 and *zero day* \[read *zero days* or *no days*\] last year." Gregg Easterbrook, "Winning the War on Smog," *Newsweek*, 23 Aug. 1993, at 29.

The plural is *zeros*, not *\*zeroes*—although *zeroes* is the correct verb form. See plurals \(d\).

 - Current ratio in print \(*two zeros* vs. *\*two zeroes*\): 5:1
