# zwieback

zwieback \(= a sweetened bread that is baked and then sliced and toasted\) is sometimes misspelled *\*zweiback*, and has been since the late 1800s in both AmE and BrE. E.g.: "At his school, *zweiback* \[read *zwieback*\], a type of German bread, was the preferred snack." Jane Tinsley Swope, "Calvert and Hillyer," *Baltimore Sun*, 26 Oct. 1994, at A15. The word is pronounced /**swee**-bak/ or /**swī**-bak/.

Current ratio in print \(*zwieback* vs. \**zweiback*\): 34:1
