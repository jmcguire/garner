# necessitate

necessitate (= to make necessary) is often inferior to *require*—e.g.: "A bored tunnel also *necessitates* \[read *requires*\] deeper stations, which cost more to build and ventilate." Mei-Ling Hopgood, "When the Tracks Meet the Trees," *St. Louis Post-Dispatch*, 24 Aug. 1997, at B1. Yet *require* isn't always a satisfactory substitute—e.g.: "Early-season struggles could *necessitate* a change." Mark Didtler, "USF Starts, and So Does Barnhardt," *Orlando Sentinel*, 6 Sept. 1997, at B4. What explains the difference? *Necessitate* sounds less rigid and absolute than *require*.
