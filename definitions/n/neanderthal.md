# Neanderthal

Neanderthal \(= \[1\] a hominid that lived some 30,000 to 200,000 years ago; \[2\] a big, ugly, and stupid man; or \[3\] a person with backward ideas and resolute opposition to change, esp. progressive change\) is a technical term in sense 1 and a disparaging label in senses 2 and 3. The word is predominantly pronounced /nee-**an**-dәr-thahl/ in AmE and /nee-**an**-dәr-tahl/ in BrE. The latter is a slightly anglicized version of the German place name for which the species was named. Many American scientists have adopted this pronunciation, perhaps to dissociate themselves from the derogatory connotations of senses 2 and 3.

That pronunciation is also reflected in the variant spelling *Neandertal*, which has long been the German spelling. Since the beginning of the 20th century, some English-speaking scholars have adopted this spelling.

Current ratio in print \(*Neanderthal* vs. *Neandertal*\): 5:1
