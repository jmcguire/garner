# nostrum

nostrum \(/**nos**-trәm/ or /**noh**-strәm/\), meaning either "a quack medicine" or "a panacea," forms the plural *nostrums*, not *\*nostra*—e.g.: "If it wants to move into the global economy, \[India\] must give up many of the *nostrums*, such as the need to preserve small businesses, which are deeply enshrined in its social policy." Peter Montagnon, "Old Protectionism Restricts Progress," *Fin. Times*, 19 Nov. 1996, at 4. See plurals \(b\). Cf. **rostrum**.
