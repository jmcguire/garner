# neologist

neologist; **\*neologizer**. The first is standard, the second a needless variant. E.g.: "Sir Thomas Elyot, the great *neologizer* \[read *neologist*\], deliberately set out to improve and promote English by introducing new words into the language." Ronald Wardhaugh, *Proper English: Myths and Misunderstandings About Language* 76 \(1999\).

Current ratio in print \(*neologist* vs. \**neologizer*\): 5:1
