# no love lost

no love lost (now denoting dislike or enmity), a phrase dating from the 1600s, is a curious expression. *OED2* called it "an ambiguous phrase which has been employed with two contrary implications." From the 1600s to the 1800s, the phrase often meant that affection was mutual—that not one bit of potential affection was squandered. That sense has long been obsolete. Today the usual meaning is that the people mentioned have no love at all for each other \<what with all the constant skirmishes, there is no love lost between them\>.

Since about 1980, the phrase has sometimes been mistakenly written \**no love loss*, doubtless because it has been misheard. This is a kind of mondegreen.

 - Current ratio in print (*no love lost between* vs. \**no love loss between*): 279:1
