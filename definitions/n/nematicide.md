# nematicide

nematicide /ni-**mat**-i-sīd/ or /**nem**-ә-tә-sīd/ (= a substance or preparation that kills worms), a hybrid combining Greek and Latin elements and dating in English from the late 1800s, has been predominantly so spelled only since about 1963 in BrE and 1976 in AmE. In the years before, \**nematocide* and \**nemacide* were common forms.

 - Current ratio in print (*nematicide* vs. \**nematocide* vs. \**nemacide*): 639:99:1
