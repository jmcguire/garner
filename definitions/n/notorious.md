# notorious

notorious may mean either "infamous" (the usual sense) <a notorious killer> or "famous" (an archaic sense). The word's negative connotations are firmly established in modern usage. Yet the corresponding noun, *notoriety*, is more neutral <achieved notoriety by saving several lives>. Gradually, though, the noun is becoming tinged with the unpleasant connotations of its adjectival form <achieved notoriety for drug-peddling charges>. Yet if *notoriety* can be equivocal, *notoriousness* removes all doubt. See noun suffixes (c).
