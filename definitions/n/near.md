# near

near should not be used adverbially in place of *nearly*. That use of the word is dialectal—e.g.: "It's long all right, but not *near* \[read *nearly*\] as long as the money shadow Tiger figures to cast by the time he's ready for the rocking chair alongside the 19th hole." Peter Finney, "Year One: A Gauge into Woods' Future," *Times-Picayune* \(New Orleans\), 27 Aug. 1997, at D1. See dialect.

## Language-Change Index

*near* misused for the adverb *nearly*: Stage 1
Current ratio in print \(*not nearly as* vs. *\*not near as*\): 36:1
