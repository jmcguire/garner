# neuron

neuron (= a nerve cell that sends messages from one part of the body to another, esp. to and from the brain), a Greek loanword dating in English from the 1880s, has been predominantly so spelled since about 1905 in AmE and 1970 in BrE. Before those dates, \**neurone* was the usual spelling in both major varieties of English. Like *vitamin* (originally \**vitamine*), the word lost its final *e*.

The corresponding adjective has been predominantly *neuronal* since the late 1930s in AmE and the early 1940s in BrE. The earlier form was \**neuronic*—now a needless variant.

 - Current ratio in print (*neuron* vs. \**neurone*): 22:1
 - Current ratio in print (*neuronal* vs. \**neuronic*): 504:1
