# niggardly

niggardly \(= grudging, stingy\) derives from an Old Norse word \(*hnøggr* "covetous, stingy"\); it has nothing to do with the racial slur that it sounds similar to. E.g.: "A tall, heavy-set, good-looking Irishman, he was never *niggardly* about attorney fees." Murray T. Bloom, *The Trouble with Lawyers* 272 \(1970\). Even so, prudent speakers and writers have come to shun it just to avoid misunderstandings. The word has been in decline in English-language print sources since 1950. See prejudiced and prejudicial terms.

Unfortunately, the word itself is sometimes misspelled because of such misunderstandings—e.g.: "Mr. Graham carries a reputation for being *niggeredly* \[read *niggardly*\] but he insists there is a difference between shrewd investment and frivolous spending." "Soccer: No Denying Graham's Right to Title," *Daily Telegraph*, 13 May 1991, at 35.
