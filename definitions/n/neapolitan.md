# Neapolitan

Neapolitan. As a noun, *Neapolitan* denotes an inhabitant of Naples, Italy. \(See demonyms.\) As an adjective, it identifies the noun it goes with as being from or connected with Naples. *Neapolitan* ice cream, with layers of different flavors \(commonly chocolate, vanilla, and strawberry\), originated in the 1800s, supposedly in Naples. The word is pronounced /nee-ә-**pol**-i-tәn/, not /nee-ah-/ or /nee-oh-/.

The term is misspelled *\*Neopolitan* surprisingly often—e.g.:

 - " ‘Italian,' once a derogatory ethnic label, is now understood to have emerged as a social construct derived from many regional identities—*Neopolitan* \[read *Neapolitan*\], Calabrian, Sicilian—that once characterized early immigration from Italy to the United States." Alex P. Oberle & Daniel D. Arreola, "Resurgent Mexican Phoenix," *Geographical Rev.*, 1 Apr. 2008, at 171.

 - "That classic *Neopolitan* \[read *Neapolitan*\] ice-cream trio is delicious on the eye, too." "Color My Wedding," *Brides*, Sept.–Oct. 2008, at 140.

 - "It was not unlike floating through an enormous block of *Neopolitan* \[read *Neapolitan*\] ice cream." Helen Olsson, "Nebraska, with a Paddle," *N.Y. Times*, 3 Oct. 2008, at F1.
