# nickel

nickel \(= \[1\] a hard silver-white metal often used in alloys; or \[2\] a five-cent coin in the U.S. or Canada\), a Swedish loanword dating in English from the mid-1700s, has always been so spelled—originally shortened from the German compound *kupfernickel* "copper nickel." The word is sometimes misspelled \**nickle*.

The phrasal verb *nickel-and-dime* \(= to deal with so cheaply or parsimoniously as to be ineffective or grossly unfair\) forms the past tense *nickel-and-dimed*, not \**nickeled-and-dimed* or \**nickeled-and-dime*. \**Nickeled-and-dime* doesn't occur in edited sources but is fairly common in unedited prose.

 - Current ratio in print \(*nickel* vs. \**nickle*\): 290:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*nickel-and-dime* vs. \**nickle-and-dime*\): 85:1
 - Current ratio in print \(*nickel-and-dimed* vs. \**nickeled-and-dimed*\): 6:1
