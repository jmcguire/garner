# novella

novella \(= a piece of written fiction longer than a short story but shorter than a novel, usu. 7,500 to 40,000 words\), an Italian loanword, dates in English from the late 1600s. Until the 1950s the Italianate plural *novelle* was usual, but by 1958 the anglicized plural *novellas* had become predominant.

The similar word \**novelette*, dating from the 1780s, was more common than *novella* through the late 1950s. Today, however, it's but a needless variant. And **novellette* is merely a misspelling.

Current ratio in print \(*novella* vs. \**novelette* vs. \**novellette*\): 2,680:173:1
Current ratio in print \(*two novellas* vs. *two novelle*\): 30:1
