# needle in a haystack

needle in a haystack; **needle in a bottle of hay**. Although the latter expression looks odd to most people, it's the older one. The word *bottle* is used there in the sense "bundle"—a sense that is now obsolete (except in this expression). The phrase *needle in a haystack* predominates in modern usage.

 - Current ratio in print (*needle in a haystack* vs. *needle in a bottle of *): 51:1
