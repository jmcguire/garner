# novitiate

novitiate; \***noviciate**. This word—meaning (1) "the period during which a person is a novice"; (2) "a building where novices are housed"; or (3) "a novice"—is preferably spelled *novitiate*. *\*Noviciate* is a variant spelling that was common from only about 1750 to 1850.

The word is sometimes misspelled *\*novitate*—e.g.: "Last year the case was transferred to the Diocese of Allentown, since that is where Ciszek was born and where he is buried at the Jesuit *novitate* \[read *novitiate*\]." Anne Rodgers-Melnick, "Journey Toward Sainthood Long, Complex," *Pitt. Post-Gaz.*, 14 Sept. 1997, at A11.

 - Current ratio in print (*novitiate* vs. *\*noviciate*): 11:1
