# nightie

nightie (= a woman's or child's nightgown), a diminutive dating from the mid-1800s, was originally spelled *nighty* as often as *nightie*, and until the 1920s it wasn't a sex-specific term. Beginning in the 1920s, though, the spelling *nightie* became predominant—and the term became overwhelmingly associated with a woman's nightdress, and sometimes a child's.

 - Current ratio in print (*in a nightie* vs. *in a nighty*): 11:1
 - Current ratio in print (*in her nightie* vs. *in his nightie*): 57:1
