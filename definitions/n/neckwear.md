# neckwear

neckwear \(= clothing worn about the neck\) is sometimes misspelled *\*neckware*, on the false analogy of *hardware* or *software*—e.g.:

 - "Ms. Keys, 31, had been a *neckware* \[read *neckwear*\] designer, concentrating on women's scarves, when someone gave her husband a fancy-looking Italian tie." James Barron, "Oh, to Be Sneaky and Well Dressed," *N.Y. Times*, 1 Sept. 1996, § 1, at 47.

 - "Dean Mullin, owner of Knotz Apparel Corp., a New Hope *neckware* \[read *neckwear*\] and graphic design company, said HPNet called him Tuesday." John Wilen, "ISP Abandons Clients to Move West, Fast," *Phil. Bus. J.*, 28 Mar. 1997, at 9.

 - "After her stellar performance Sunday in the women's slopestyle competition, she has a new piece of *neckware* \[read *neckwear*\]: an Olympic gold medal." Rick Maese, "Anderson Rides ‘Good Vibes' to Snowboarding Gold," *Wash. Post*, 10 Feb. 2014, at D7.

Language-Change Index

*\*neckware* for *neckwear*: Stage 1
