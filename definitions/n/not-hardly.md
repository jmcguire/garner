# \*not hardly

\*not hardly. This robust barbarism is fine if your purpose is to show dialect, but it doesn't otherwise belong in the serious writer's toolbox—e.g.:

 - "Is there a more logical place for Moorpark than the Marmonte League? *Not hardly.* \[Read *Hardly.*\] But logic often has nothing to do with it." Paige A. Leech, "Time to Get Real on Realignment," *L.A. Times*, 16 Feb. 1997, at C10.

 - "End of the problem? *Not hardly.* \[Read *Hardly*.\] In February, the state filed criminal charges." Jerry Spangler, "Fight Brews over Out-of-State Spirits," *Deseret News* (Salt Lake City), 29 July 1997, at B4.

 - "Liberal alarmist hype? *Not hardly*. \[Read *Hardly*.\]" Cy Wood, "Indescribably Inane," *Valley Times-News* (Lanett, Ala.), 6 Aug. 2015, News §, at 4.

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*not hardly* for *hardly*: Stage 1
 - Current ratio in print (*can hardly* vs. *\*can't hardly*): 75:1
