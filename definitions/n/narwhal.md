# narwhal

narwhal /**nahr**-wahl/ \(= an arctic whale whose male has a long spiral tusk extending from its upper jaw\), a Scandinavian loanword dating in English from the mid-1600s, was adapted from its etymon in Danish and Norwegian: *narhval* "corpse whale." Variant spellings in English are \**narwal* \(serious competition for the standard spelling from 1780 to 1845\) and \**narwhale* \(a fully anglicized spelling that never caught on\).

Current ratio in print \(*narwhal* vs. \**narwhale* vs. \**narwal*\): 165:31:1
