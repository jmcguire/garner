# nursing one's wounds

nursing one's wounds. Occasionally, by malapropism, the phrase is mangled into *\*nurturing one's wounds*, as if wounds were to be cultivated and developed further—e.g.: "The other young women *nurtured* \[read *nursed*\] his wounds and cooled his skin with sponges soaked in a special herbal solution." Lawrence Patrick, *Inner Journey* 26 \(2003\).

## Language-Change Index

 - *\*nurturing wounds* for *nursing wounds*: Stage 1
