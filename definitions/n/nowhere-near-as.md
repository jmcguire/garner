# nowhere near as

nowhere near as is a casualism for *not nearly as*. During the 20th century, it became fairly common in both AmE and BrE—e.g.:

 - "Admittedly, Tony Blair is *nowhere near as* \[or *not nearly as*\] terrifying as Mrs. T." Nicholas Lloyd, "Spin Doctors in Overdose," *Times* (London), 6 Aug. 1997, at 21.

 - "The technology, which is *nowhere near as* \[or *not nearly as*\] sophisticated as a flux generator bomb, could easily move from law enforcement to the criminal and terrorist population." "Cyber Terrorism," *Am. Banker*, 8 Sept. 1997, at 43.
