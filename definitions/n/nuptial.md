# nuptial

nuptial; **nuptials**. Although *nuptial* is in good use as an adjective, the noun *nuptials* (= wedding) is generally a pomposity to be avoided. Though exceedingly common in 18th-century English, it waned in the 1800s. It should be left to its ineradicable place in newspaper reports of weddings, where grubstreet hacks engage in (not to say become engaged to) inelegant variation.

*Nuptial* is pronounced /**nәp**-shәl/—not /**nәp**-shә-wәl/ or (worse) /-chә-/. See **prenuptial**.
